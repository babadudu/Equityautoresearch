# Morris Chang — Computer History Museum Oral History
**Date**: 2007
**Source**: [Computer History Museum](https://www.computerhistory.org/collections/catalog/102658129)

## Context
An oral history interview conducted by the Computer History Museum covering Morris Chang's 25-year career at Texas Instruments, the origins of learning curve pricing theory, and the early history of the semiconductor industry. This is one of the earliest comprehensive English-language interviews documenting Chang's formative experiences before founding TSMC.

## Key Themes Covered
- TI career (1958-1983): From engineer to VP of worldwide semiconductor operations
- Learning curve pricing: Chang's deep study of how cumulative production volume drives cost reduction
- The economics of semiconductor manufacturing scale
- Early semiconductor industry structure and competition
- Decision to leave TI and the path to founding TSMC

## Notable Context
Chang's experience at TI — particularly his mastery of learning curve economics — became the intellectual foundation for TSMC's pure-play foundry model. His insight that a dedicated foundry could accumulate production volume faster than any IDM's internal fab would later prove to be TSMC's most powerful competitive advantage.
