# CC Wei — On Intel Foundry Competition
**Date**: January 2026
**Source**: [WCCFTech](https://wccftech.com/tsmcs-ceo-says-intel-foundry-wont-be-competitive-by-just-throwing-money/)
**Context**: Q4 2025 earnings call and post-call analysis
**Format**: Earnings call Q&A

---

## On Intel Foundry's Competitiveness

When asked whether Intel's investment could close the gap with TSMC:

> "In the development of semiconductor technology to this point, receiving investment has not helped to improve competitiveness, right?"

Wei's core argument: money alone cannot buy semiconductor manufacturing excellence — it requires decades of accumulated learning, talent cultivation, and process optimization.

---

## "Not Worried"

When pressed on whether TSMC is concerned about Intel Foundry's progress:

> "Not worried."

---

## Context

This exchange was significant because:
1. Intel 18A had just completed tape-outs for Panther Lake and Clearwater Forest
2. Intel Foundry was targeting 2027 breakeven
3. Wei's dismissive response contrasted with Intel CEO Lip-Bu Tan's optimism
4. The "investment ≠ competitiveness" argument echoed Morris Chang's learning curve philosophy

Wei's two-word response ("Not worried") became widely quoted as emblematic of TSMC's confidence in its structural advantages.
