# Morris Chang & C.C. Wei — TSMC Sports Day 2024
**Date**: October 28, 2024
**Source**: [Digitimes — TSMC Sports Day](https://www.digitimes.com/news/a20241028PD200/taiwan-tsmc-morris-chang-ceo-2024.html)
**Setting**: Annual TSMC Sports Day event at TSMC headquarters, Hsinchu
**Format**: Public speeches to 80,000+ employees and families

---

## Morris Chang — Founder's Address

### On 2024 Performance
> "2024 would be another record-breaking year for TSMC, though the toughest challenges are yet to come."

### On Sports Day
> Sports Day is "the most exciting day of his year" because "employees' enthusiasm, teamwork, and athletic spirit bring him immense joy."

### On Globalization
> "In the semiconductor industry, especially in advanced semiconductor process technology, globalization is dead and free trade is dead."

### On TSMC's Future
> "If we accept this challenge, we will succeed, supported by our outstanding teams and leadership."

### Humor
After receiving applause, Chang humorously added:
> "Fortunately, the response was enthusiastic; otherwise, I would have to settle accounts later."

---

## C.C. Wei — Chairman's Address

### Personal Milestone
> "TSMC has come a long way over 37 years, and I have waited 26 years to stand here."

This remark was particularly poignant — Wei joined TSMC in 1998, and the 2024 Sports Day was his first as both Chairman and CEO (dual role assumed June 2024).

### On Team Spirit
> "Sports Day serves as a reunion for the TSMC family."

He encouraged employees to "continue to embody the 'One Team' spirit and press forward."

### 2024 Milestones Highlighted
Wei announced key achievements:
- 2nm (N2) technology on track with A16 process unveiled
- Japan fab (JASM Kumamoto) opening
- Germany (ESMC Dresden) groundbreaking
- US Arizona facility advancements

---

## Context

The 2024 Sports Day was notable for the contrast between the two leaders:
- **Chang** (age 93): Reflecting on decades of achievement while warning of unprecedented challenges ahead
- **Wei** (age 71): Celebrating his ascension to the dual Chairman-CEO role and looking forward to the AI era

The event drew attention because Chang's "toughest challenges yet to come" comment was interpreted as referring to: (1) geopolitical tensions around overseas expansion, (2) the unprecedented scale of AI-driven CapEx ($38-42B in 2025), and (3) cross-cultural management challenges as TSMC globalizes.
