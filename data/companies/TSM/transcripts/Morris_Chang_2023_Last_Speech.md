# Morris Chang's Last Speech — Full Text Summary
**Date**: April 2021 (delivered); Published 2023-04
**Source**: [Interconnected Blog — Morris Chang's Last Speech](https://interconnected.blog/morris-changs-last-speech/)
**Setting**: Speech to Taiwanese government officials and business leaders, age 90
**Format**: Public speech, approximately 45 minutes

---

## Part 1: Semiconductor History

Chang opened by tracing the entire history of semiconductors from the 1948 transistor invention at Bell Labs:

> "Shockley was their leader, and Bardeen and Brattain didn't really like him, but the three of them collaborated to invent the transistors."

He recalled meeting Intel co-founders in 1958:
> "We were all young and excited, and thought we were the 'Favored Children of Heaven.'"

---

## Part 2: Moore's Law

Chang offered a nuanced view of Moore's Law — not as physical law but as industry-driven prediction:
> "It was at first only a prediction, which might not have been true. In fact, if MOS was not invented... Moore's Law would not have been accurate."

---

## Part 3: TSMC's Business Model

On identifying TSMC's core insight:
> "Since our customers are semiconductor companies, if they originally make their own wafer but now we make wafer too, then our competitors are those in charge of wafer manufacturing inside semiconductor companies."

> "Although the upside was that there were no competitors, the downside was that there were no customers either."

The biggest strategic discovery:
> "The biggest discovery of a business model is who your customer is and how you make money."

---

## Part 4: Taiwan vs. US Manufacturing Advantages

Chang was extraordinarily direct about Taiwan's structural superiority in semiconductor manufacturing:

> "American talents are not as good as Taiwanese ones, whether it's engineers, technicians, foremen, or operators."

> "The commitment to manufacturing is a great advantage of Taiwan."

He explained the geographic advantage:
> "TSMC's three manufacturing centers often have thousands of engineers whose location assignment changes but who do not have to move their family."

> "Taiwan's advantages that I just talked about are America's weaknesses."

> "Culture, habits, and language are all different."

On US efforts to reshore semiconductor manufacturing:
> "a very expensive exercise in futility"

> "Short-term subsidies cannot make up for long-term competitive disadvantages."

---

## Part 5: Competition Assessment

On Samsung:
> "Samsung Electronics is a strong competitor of TSMC."

On China:
> "Mainland is not yet a rival, especially in terms of wafer manufacturing."

He drew a clear distinction between "real competitors" (Samsung) and "restricted potential participants" (China under sanctions).

---

## Part 6: Globalization & Identity

> "Globalization is almost dead. Free trade is almost dead."

On his personal identity:
> "My identity has always been an American. There is nothing else."

> "I am from the United States, and my dream is to build a wafer fab in the United States."

---

## Part 7: Taiwan's Future

When asked about finding another "protective mountain" (護國神山) industry for Taiwan, Chang was blunt:
> "Difficult!"
