# TSMC Q4 2024 Earnings Call Transcript
**Date**: January 16, 2025
**Source**: [Motley Fool Transcript](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/01/16/taiwan-semiconductor-manufacturing-tsm-q4-2024-ear/) | [TSMC IR PDF](https://investor.tsmc.com/chinese/encrypt/files/encrypt_file/reports/2025-01/84aeb15bbe33894365d33f52e027c5268ba95dcf/TSMC%204Q24%20Transcript.pdf)
**Speakers**: C.C. Wei (Chairman & CEO), Wendell Huang (VP & CFO)

---

## Opening Remarks

### Wendell Huang (CFO)
> "Fourth quarter revenue increased 14.3% sequentially in NT, supported by strong demand for our industry-leading 3-nanometer and 5-nanometer technologies."

**Q4 2024 Results:**
- Gross margin: 59% (up 120 basis points sequentially)
- Operating margin: 49%
- EPS: TWD 14.45

**Full Year 2024:**
- Revenue: USD 90 billion (30% growth)
- Gross margin: 56.1%
- Free cash flow: TWD 870 billion
- Advanced technologies (7nm and below): 69% of total wafer revenue (up from 58% in 2023)

### C.C. Wei (CEO)
> "2024 was a mixed year of recovery for the global semiconductor industry. AI-related demand shrunk, while other applications saw only very mild recovery."

> "TSMC's revenue increased 30% year over year in U.S. dollar term, outperforming the foundry industry growth" of approximately 6%.

---

## 2025 Outlook

> "We expect 2025 to be another strong growth year for TSMC and forecast our full year revenue to increase by close to mid-20s percent."

The Foundry 2.0 industry is expected to grow 10% year-over-year in 2025.

---

## AI Demand & Revenue Projections

> "AI accelerators accounted for close to mid-teens percent of our total revenue in 2024. Even after more than tripling in 2024, we forecast our revenue from AI accelerators to double in 2025."

Long-term guidance:
> "AI accelerator revenue to approach a mid-40% CAGR for the five-year period starting off the already higher base of 2024."

Five-year overall guidance:
> "For the five-year period starting from 2024, we expect our long-term revenue growth to approach a 20% CAGR in U.S. dollar term, fueled by all four of our growth platforms."

---

## N2 Technology Timeline

> "N2 is well on track for volume production in second half of 2025 as scheduled with the ramp profile similar to N3."

Performance: "10 to 15 speed improvement at the same power, or 20% to 30% power improvement."

---

## US Manufacturing Strategy

> "Our first fab has already entered the high-volume production in 4Q '24, utilizing N4 process technology with a yield comparable to our fabs in Taiwan."

On overseas costs:
> "Overseas fab costs are higher for everyone, including TSMC and all other semiconductor manufacturers."

**Pricing philosophy for US fabs:**
> "Do we charge a little bit higher? Yes, we did because we have a value of geographics of flexibility, right? And you guys know Made in U.S.A is a premium product."

---

## Overseas Fab Cost Structure (Huang)

> "The U.S. fab cost, it is more expensive in the U.S., mainly because of several reasons. Number one, the smaller scale, right? Now number two, the higher price in the supply chain; and number three, the very early stage of the ecosystem."

Margin dilution guidance:
> "2% to 3% margin dilution impact from the ramp-up of our overseas fabs" annually through 2030.

Additional headwinds:
> "Inflationary costs, including higher electricity prices in Taiwan to impact our gross margin by at least 1% in 2025."

---

## Competition / Intel Relationship

On Intel Foundry (which is also a TSMC customer):
> "They are our very good customers. I like them, and they are very important to TSMC's business also."

---

## Geopolitics & Government Relations

> "We have a very frank and open communication with the current government and with the future one also. I cannot say anything more than that."

On memory/AI export controls:
> "We are working with all the memory suppliers, all of them... all of them are working with TSMC."

---

## Supply Chain & Capacity

> "We have a very tight capacity and cannot even meet customers' need."

On cost improvement efforts:
> "We continue to improve. That improved the cost structure, both in Taiwan and in the U.S. And we also try very hard to find out new methodology..."

---

## Capital Investment Commitment

> "Even as we invest for the future growth with this higher level of CapEx spending in 2025, we remain committed to delivering profitable growth to our shareholders."

### 2025 CapEx Plan (Huang)
> "We expect our capital budget to be between USD 38 billion and USD 42 billion as we invest to capture the future growth."

Approximately 70% allocated for advanced process technologies.

---

## Long-Term Gross Margin (Huang)

> "53% and higher is achievable" when excluding foreign exchange impacts.

---

## Q1 2025 Guidance (Huang)

> "First quarter revenue to be between USD 25 billion and USD 25.8 billion, which represents a 5.5% sequential decline or a 34.7% year-over-year increase at the midpoint."

Gross margin: 57-59%; Operating margin: 46.5-48.5%.
