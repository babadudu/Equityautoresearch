# CC Wei — National Science and Technology Conference Speech
**Date**: December 16-17, 2024
**Source**: [Taipei Times](https://www.taipeitimes.com/News/front/archives/2024/12/17/2003828622) | [Taiwan News](https://www.taiwannews.com.tw/news/5996159) | [Tom's Hardware](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/artificial-intelligence/elon-musk-prioritizing-humanoid-robot-advances-says-tsmc-ceo-tsmc-will-supply-the-chips-if-the-price-is-right)
**Setting**: National Science and Technology Conference, Taipei
**Format**: Public keynote speech

---

## AI & Customer Insights

> "In the past two months, our customers shared a lot of their future plans with me. Artificial intelligence [AI] and AI applications were the most talked about subjects in our conversation."

---

## Elon Musk & Humanoid Robots

Wei revealed a recent conversation with "the world's richest guy" (widely interpreted as Elon Musk):

> "I just talked to the world's richest guy the other day. He told me [developing] versatile robots is the direction he is striving toward, not cars."

> "I asked him what worries him the most about developing such robots; he said lack of chip supply would be his biggest concern."

Wei responded with characteristic directness and humor:
> "Chip supply should not be a problem as long as he pays."

---

## Taiwan's Future in Robotics & AI

Wei identified versatile service robots as a major opportunity for Taiwan's tech ecosystem:
> "Versatile service robots [are] the next new business opportunity for Taiwan, as such robots require good AI chips, software and precision machinery."

He emphasized Taiwan's strengths in the AI chip ecosystem:
> "In my opinion, we [Taiwan] can harness our strength in the AI area. We have good chip design houses capable of designing all sorts of products."

---

## Drones

Wei also advised Taiwan to invest in drone technology:
> "Taiwanese software developers and precision machinery manufacturers should allocate as much resources as possible" to the drone sector.

---

## Context

This speech was significant because it revealed:
1. Wei's direct relationship with the world's top technology CEOs (beyond just Apple/NVIDIA)
2. TSMC's positioning for the next wave of AI demand beyond cloud computing — humanoid robots and edge AI
3. Wei's pragmatic, customer-driven approach: "as long as he pays"
4. His vision for Taiwan's broader technology ecosystem, not just TSMC

Tesla's Optimus robot plans called for low-volume production in 2025 for internal use, with high-volume external production targeted for 2026 — all requiring TSMC's advanced chips.
