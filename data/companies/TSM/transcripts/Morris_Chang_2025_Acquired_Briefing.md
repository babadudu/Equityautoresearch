# Morris Chang — Acquired Briefing Interview Analysis
**Date**: 2025-01
**Source**: [Acquired Briefing](https://www.acquiredbriefing.com/p/the-morris-chang-interview)

## Key Quotes

"It was only the very initial plan."
— On the suggestion TSMC would stop at two fabs. Chang always intended massive scale.

"I was a serious student of learning curve and I would never stop at just two."
— On his commitment to scale economics from day one.

"I decided that I would take half of what Apple asked for... He said, well, I think you can eliminate your dividend. Your shareholders will understand that. I said, well, no."
— On negotiating capacity allocation with Apple while maintaining financial discipline.

"The R&D director was not concerned at all about having his planned budget cut back... He has been working at 8%. It has been like that, and that is what propelled our R&D effort."
— On the institutional impact of his fixed 8% R&D rule.

"I worked out a number. I also knew that NVIDIA customers were after them. They had demands on NVIDIA too. So I used all the intelligence I could get."
— On strategically resolving the NVIDIA dispute after his 2009 return.

"IBM still consider themselves to be the senior partner in any partnership they established... We declined without having to think about it at all."
— On rejecting IBM's co-development proposal, preserving TSMC's independent R&D.

"it's the first time in a long, long time that I have talked so long."
— Closing remark, reflecting the depth of the conversation.

## Context
This briefing analyzes Chang's Acquired podcast interview, highlighting his scale ambition, Apple negotiations, NVIDIA crisis resolution, and IBM partnership refusal as defining leadership moments.
