# CC Wei — The Register Interview: AI Chip Shortage Prediction
**Date**: July 18, 2024
**Source**: [The Register](https://www.theregister.com/2024/07/18/tsmc_ceo_predicts_ai_chip/)
**Context**: Q2 2024 earnings call and analyst Q&A
**Format**: Earnings call with subsequent media analysis

---

## CoWoS Capacity & AI Chip Shortage Timeline

On when supply and demand will balance:
> "I hope in 2025 or 2026 we can reach the balance."

On CoWoS packaging capacity expansion:
> "From last year to this year, we have more than doubled" the CoWoS capacity, and expects next year to double again.

---

## Geopolitical Strategy — Unwavering Expansion

When Morgan Stanley analyst Charlie Chan asked about geopolitical risks and potential tariffs (following Trump's comments about Taiwan and TSMC), Wei responded:

> "Okay, Charlie. So far, we did not change any of our original plan of expansion of our overseas fab. We continue to expand in Arizona, in Kumamoto and maybe [in] future in Europe. No change to our strategy. We continue our current practice."

---

## Tariff Position

> "On the tariff, not that we know of. Normally, if there's an import tariff, the customers will be responsible for that, but no discussion, nothing."

---

## AI Demand — All Roads Lead to TSMC

> "Whether AI applications use ASICs or graphics processors, they all require very leading-edge technology and are working with TSMC."

---

## Context

This interview came during Q2 2024 — a pivotal quarter where TSMC's AI/HPC demand accelerated dramatically. Wei's responses demonstrated:
1. **Operational focus**: CoWoS capacity doubling year-over-year
2. **Geopolitical composure**: No strategy changes despite Trump's rhetoric
3. **Strategic positioning**: TSMC captures all AI chip architectures (GPU + ASIC)
4. **Pragmatic optimism**: Supply-demand balance expected 2025-2026
