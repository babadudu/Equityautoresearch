## GLP-1 Market Duopoly Structure

### Extreme Concentration: HHI ~5,098

The GLP-1 market exhibits an extreme duopoly structure. As of 2025, Eli Lilly (~57%) and Novo Nordisk (~43%) together account for over **95%** of GLP-1 market revenue ([CNBC, 2026](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/04/eli-lilly-novo-nordisk-earnings-glp1-market.html); [Redbock, 2025](https://redbock.com/news/glp-1-medications-a-market-on-the-rise/)).

**HHI Concentration Analysis**: Using Lilly 57% and Novo 43%, HHI = 57² + 43² = 3,249 + 1,849 = **~5,098** — far exceeding the US DOJ "highly concentrated" threshold (HHI > 2,500). This places GLP-1 in the oligopoly category under regulatory definitions. However, two key distinctions from a traditional oligopoly apply: (1) TAM is still rapidly expanding (new patients far outnumber share-stealing dynamics), and (2) competition is primarily driven by clinical data rather than price wars.

### CEO Perspective on Competition

David Ricks has expressed a surprisingly welcoming attitude toward more entrants: he believes the market is far from saturated, and that "someone has to do this work" — more participants expanding the market matters more than market share competition ([T. Rowe Price, 2025-11](https://www.troweprice.com/en/us/insights/the-long-view-eli-lilly)). He has articulated a niche segmentation framework: "If there's 200 chronic diseases that are downstream affected by obesity, surely Novo and Lilly will have left one or two behind... I think some companies will choose to niche into one of those, try to get pricing power [and] own a narrow but focused segment. That's viable" ([BioSpace/JPM26, 2026-01](https://www.biospace.com/business/jpm26-lilly-plots-path-to-dominance-as-obesity-market-enters-a-new-era)).

### Patent Timeline Asymmetry

A key structural advantage for Lilly: Novo's semaglutide core patent is expiring in 2026 in India and will expire in China, Brazil, Canada, and Turkey — countries collectively representing **40% of global population and 33% of global obesity population** ([IQVIA, 2026-01](https://www.iqvia.com/locations/emea/blogs/2026/01/outlook-for-obesity-in-2026)). By contrast, tirzepatide's core patents are estimated to extend to ~2036 ([CEN/ACS, 2025-12](https://cen.acs.org/pharmaceuticals/Looming-GLP-1-drug-patent/103/web/2025/12)), giving Lilly a minimum ten-year exclusivity window.
