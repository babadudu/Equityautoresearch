## eBay Market Structure & HHI Concentration Analysis (US & Global E-commerce, 2024–2025)

### Overview

The global e-commerce marketplace exhibits a "top-heavy, long-tail fragmented" structure. From the 1990s when only eBay and Amazon existed as major platforms, the 2020s have produced a multipolar landscape. Pierre Omidyar observed the market's self-organizing nature at the company's founding: "Commerce and trade is at the base of all human activity... fundamentally everything we do in human activities is related to trade" ([Academy of Achievement](https://achievement.org/achiever/pierre-omidyar/)). This belief drove him to build a marketplace based on community trust rather than Amazon's "retailer-as-platform" model.

### US E-commerce Market Concentration (2024)

US e-commerce market concentration can be quantified via CR metrics and the Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI). Based on [DemandSage 2024 market share data](https://www.demandsage.com/online-shopping-market-share/):

| Rank | Platform | US E-commerce Market Share | E-commerce Sales |
|------|----------|--------------------------|------------------|
| 1 | Amazon | 37.6% | $447.4B |
| 2 | Walmart | 6.4% | $76.2B |
| 3 | Apple | 3.6% | $42.8B |
| 4 | **eBay** | **3.0%** | **$35.7B** |
| 5 | Target | 1.9% | $22.6B |
| **CR5** | | **52.5%** | |

Source: [DemandSage Online Shopping Market Share 2026](https://www.demandsage.com/online-shopping-market-share/)

**HHI Estimate**: Using the above market shares, US e-commerce HHI ≈ 37.6² + 6.4² + 3.6² + 3.0² + 1.9² + other (~47.5% fragmented) ≈ 1,414 + 41 + 13 + 9 + 4 + ~50 (long-tail estimate) ≈ **~1,531**. Under US Department of Justice standards (HHI <1,500 = low concentration; 1,500–2,500 = moderate concentration; >2,500 = high concentration), US e-commerce sits at the **moderate concentration boundary**. Amazon alone contributes 92% of the HHI, creating a classic "one dominant player, several secondary players" structure. eBay ranks fourth with 3.0% market share, but holds significantly higher concentration in its core categories (used/collectibles/auto parts).

### Global Competitive Landscape

The **global picture** is more multipolar. Chinese companies (Alibaba, Pinduoduo, JD) dominate China; Amazon leads Europe and the Americas; eBay accounts for only ~1.2% of global e-commerce GMV ($79.6B / $6.86T). However, in used goods and collectibles sub-markets, eBay's concentration is significantly higher—estimated at 30–40% of the global online trading cards market and 15–20% of the online auto parts market.

| Platform | 2024–2025 GMV Estimate | Positioning | Core Strength |
|----------|----------------------|-------------|---------------|
| Amazon | ~$700B+ | New goods, full-category, Prime ecosystem | Logistics speed, category breadth |
| Alibaba (Taobao+Tmall) | ~$900B+ | China domestic + cross-border | China market dominance |

| Etsy | ~$13B | Handcrafts/vintage | Creator community |

### eBay's Differentiated Position

eBay's differentiation lies in its "horizontal marketplace + vertical depth" model. CEO Jamie Iannone explicitly highlighted eBay's inventory structure as its fundamental differentiator in the Q4 2025 earnings call: "Our inventory is fundamentally different from most marketplaces. It is 90% non-new and seasoned" ([eBay Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-18](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/18/ebay-ebay-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)). He added: "Our scale and our value proposition have really positioned us as a leader in this space" ([eBay Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-18](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/18/ebay-ebay-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)).

Iannone explained the high value of enthusiast buyers using cross-category spending data in a Semafor interview: "if we have a handbag buyer come in who'll spend $1,500 in handbags, they'll spend over $5,000 in other categories" ([Semafor, 2025-10-09](https://www.semafor.com/article/10/09/2025/how-jamie-iannone-made-ebay-an-investor-collectible)). In his 2021 shareholder letter, he quantified enthusiast buyer value: "Enthusiast buyers drive 71% of our GMV, on average shopping... over 30 days each year" ([LinkedIn, 2021](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/empowering-economic-opportunity-all-jamie-iannone)).

In a CNN interview, Iannone highlighted eBay's 30-year data advantage: eBay possesses "decades of product listings and customer interactions to train AI models, along with 2.4 billion active listings and 134 million buyers"—30 years of transaction data is a barrier difficult for new entrants to replicate ([CNN Business, 2025-11-14](https://www.cnn.com/2025/11/14/tech/ebay-ai-shopping)). In his 2021 annual report, he provided a full-platform scale picture: "over 147 million active buyers and 17 million sellers across 190 markets" with "approximately 1.5 billion live listings" ([LinkedIn, 2021](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/empowering-economic-opportunity-all-jamie-iannone)).

### De Minimis Impact on Competitive Structure

The elimination of the de minimis tariff exemption has produced structural competitive effects. [FinancialContent analysis (2026-02-20)](https://markets.financialcontent.com/stocks/article/finterra-2026-2-20-resilience-in-re-commerce-an-in-depth-analysis-of-ebay-inc-ebay) noted the new tariffs "inadvertently benefited eBay by leveling the playing field against ultra-low-cost importers"—Chinese low-price direct sales platforms like Temu and SHEIN that relied on the de minimis duty-free threshold were directly impacted, while eBay's predominantly used/non-new inventory structure (90% non-new) was less affected.
