## eBay Regulatory Environment (2025–2026)

The ecommerce industry faces regulatory risks across four major dimensions: tariff policy, platform liability, data privacy, and tax compliance. Below in order of impact significance.

### 1. Tariffs & De Minimis Changes (Major 2025 Inflection)

On August 29, 2025, the United States fully eliminated the de minimis exemption for imports under $800 — the most significant policy change affecting global ecommerce. During the transition period, a flat tariff of $80–$200 per item was added, subsequently transitioning to full ad valorem tariffs ([CedCommerce, 2025](https://cedcommerce.com/blog/de-minimis-rule-ends-what-sellers-must-know-and-do-before-august-29/); [Fortune, 2025-08-29](https://fortune.com/2025/08/29/etsy-ebay-shein-de-minimis-tariff-exemption-ends-adding-charges/)).

Iannone described the immediate impact in the Q3 2025 earnings call: "The recent elimination of the de minimis exemption for imports under $800 has created incremental cost" and further noted: "we saw a deceleration in year-over-year volume growth starting in September in key markets importing into the U.S. after the removal of the de minimis exemption" ([eBay Q3 2025 Earnings Call, 2025-10-29](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/29/ebay-ebay-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)). He warned Q4 would face "a full quarter of impact from the removal of global de minimis exemption, versus a single month." CFO Peggy Alford added: "we expect to face incremental headwinds from annualizing breakage associated with the global de minimis changes."

By the Q4 2025 earnings call, Iannone disclosed eBay's active response: "We accelerated our product roadmap for cross-border solutions to help our sellers navigate new tariffs and trade policy changes" ([eBay Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-18](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/18/ebay-ebay-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)). Alford identified the specific international impact path: "Challenging macroeconomic conditions in the UK and Germany and a deceleration in our cross-border volume growth due to U.S. trade policies" compressed international GMV.

Former CFO Steve Priest had already warned in the Q4 2024 earnings call: "Facing the uncertainty around U.S. tariffs and de minimis changes... contemplating a range of outcomes for tariffs in our guidance based on what we can see and forecast" ([Digital Commerce 360, 2025-03-10](https://www.digitalcommerce360.com/2025/03/10/ebay-sales-revenue-gmv-q4-2024/)). VP John Egbert provided quantitative context in the same call: goods from China represent approximately "about 5% of total GMV", and "About 75% of eBay volume from China is forward deployed" in US warehouses ([Digital Commerce 360, 2025-03-10](https://www.digitalcommerce360.com/2025/03/10/ebay-sales-revenue-gmv-q4-2024/)).

**Net impact on eBay**: Short-term negative for cross-border volume; structurally positive as eBay's 90% non-new inventory model is less exposed than Temu/SHEIN's new-goods direct-import model.

### 2. EU Digital Services Act (DSA) & Digital Markets Act (DMA)

The EU Digital Services Act (DSA) became fully applicable on February 17, 2024, adding obligations for online marketplaces including transparency reporting, content moderation, and seller verification (Know Your Business Customer, KYBC). DSA applies tiered regulation by platform scale, with the strictest "Very Large Online Platform (VLOP)" obligations applying to platforms with over 45 million average monthly active EU users.

**eBay's DSA Compliance Status**: Based on data from June–November 2025, eBay's EU monthly average active users **did not reach the 45 million threshold**, classifying eBay as a "Large Online Platform" rather than VLOP — exempting it from VLOP's additional systemic risk assessments and external audit obligations ([eBay DSA Compliance Page](https://www.ebayinc.com/company/digital-services-act/)). eBay has published transparency reports for 2024 and 2025 (including quantitative and qualitative data) and continuously monitors user count changes. By contrast, Amazon and AliExpress have been designated as VLOPs, facing stricter scrutiny.

eBay's specific compliance measures include: (1) **VeRO Program** (Verified Rights Owner Program) for IP protection and counterfeit removal; (2) **Regulatory Portal** — consumer protection tool strengthening product safety oversight; (3) founding signatory of the EU anti-counterfeit MoU and **EU Product Safety Pledge** ([eBay DSA Compliance Page](https://www.ebayinc.com/company/digital-services-act/)).

**Risks and opportunities coexist**: Seller verification and product safety obligations increase compliance costs (especially identity verification for cross-border sellers), but also raise buyer trust in the platform — benefiting eBay's Authenticity Guarantee strategy. VLOP non-compliance maximum penalty is **6% of global annual revenue** ([EU Digital Services Act](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/digital-services-act)).

The EU Digital Markets Act (DMA) primarily targets "gatekeepers" (Apple, Google, Meta, Amazon). eBay has not been designated a gatekeeper, but should monitor DMA's indirect impact on platform interoperability.

### 3. Tax Compliance: 1099-K Threshold Stabilization (Major Positive)

The 2025 One Big Beautiful Bill (OBBB) **retroactively restored** the 1099-K reporting threshold to pre-reform levels: third-party settlement organizations (TPSOs) need only report to the IRS when annual total payments exceed **$20,000 AND exceed 200 transactions** — the previously planned phase-down from $20,000 to $600 was fully cancelled ([IRS 1099-K FAQs under OBBB](https://www.irs.gov/newsroom/irs-issues-faqs-on-form-1099-k-threshold-under-the-one-big-beautiful-bill-dollar-limit-reverts-to-20000); [TaxesForExpats 2026 Analysis](https://www.taxesforexpats.com/articles/tax-reform-2025/form-1099-k-threshold-rollback-600-rule-reversed-in-latest-tax-reform.html)).

This is a **significant structural positive** for eBay. eBay's C2C seller base includes a large number of casual sellers (annual sales $1,000–$15,000). If the threshold had dropped to $600, millions of sellers would have been triggered to file 1099-K for the first time — the tax complexity and psychological burden could have caused a "seller exodus" directly impacting GMV. OBBB stabilized the threshold, eliminating this tail risk ([FinancialContent Analysis, 2026-02-20](https://markets.financialcontent.com/stocks/article/finterra-2026-2-20-resilience-in-re-commerce-an-in-depth-analysis-of-ebay-inc-ebay)).

Key details: (1) threshold is **calculated per platform**, not aggregated across platforms ([Millan CPA 2025 Guide](https://millancpa.com/insights/form-1099-k-changes-for-2025-what-businesses-and-sellers-must-know/)); (2) credit card transactions have **no threshold** — acquiring banks must report regardless of amount; (3) **some states have lower thresholds** — Massachusetts, Virginia, Maryland, and others have thresholds as low as $600, meaning eBay sellers may still be triggered at the state level ([1800Accountant 2026 Update](https://1800accountant.com/blog/irs-1099-reporting-changes-2026)).

### 4. INFORM Consumers Act (Effective June 2023)

The INFORM Consumers Act took effect June 27, 2023, requiring online marketplaces to verify the identity of "high-volume sellers" (200+ units sold AND $5,000+ in 12 months), and to disclose seller names and addresses to buyers when annual revenue reaches $20,000. eBay has achieved full compliance, establishing seller verification processes and annual review mechanisms ([eBay Seller Center — INFORM Consumers Act](https://www.ebay.com/sellercenter/resources/inform-consumers-act)).

eBay actively supported the INFORM Act during the legislative process — lobbying through the eBay Main Street policy advocacy team, viewing it as a tool to combat counterfeits and stolen goods, helping raise platform trust ([eBay Main Street — INFORM](https://www.ebaymainstreet.com/news-events/new-inform-consumers-act-bill-introduced)). The FTC has stated it will "enforce the Act to the fullest extent possible" ([Avalara INFORM Analysis](https://www.avalara.com/blog/en/north-america/2023/01/congress-requires-marketplaces-to-monitor-high-volume-marketplace-sellers.html)).

**Net impact: positive** — (a) compliance costs manageable (eBay already had VeRO and other seller verification mechanisms); (b) raises buyer trust; (c) creates larger compliance burden for smaller/emerging marketplace platforms, indirectly reinforcing eBay's market position.

### 5. Data Privacy (GDPR / CCPA) and Compliance Costs

For eBay, GDPR compliance primarily affects: (1) **Ad targeting constraints** — GDPR requires explicit user consent for behavioral tracking and ad targeting. eBay's first-party advertising business (Promoted Listings, ~$2.1B revenue in 2025) relies on in-platform behavioral data, which is relatively less affected by GDPR since the data is generated within eBay's own ecosystem (first-party data) rather than cross-site tracking. (2) **Compliance costs** — large enterprise annual GDPR compliance costs are estimated at $1.7M–$70M depending on data processing scale ([Sprinto GDPR Cost 2025](https://sprinto.com/blog/gdpr-compliance-cost/); [Usercentrics GDPR Cost Guide](https://usercentrics.com/knowledge-hub/cost-of-gdpr-compliance/)). eBay's GDPR compliance cost as a percentage of revenue is very low (<0.5%). (3) **AI training data compliance** — GDPR Article 22 restricts "purely automated decision-making"; eBay must ensure AI-driven pricing recommendations and search ranking functions do not constitute discriminatory automated decisions for EU users.

**Net impact on eBay**: GDPR compliance costs are manageable, and eBay's first-party data model (not relying on third-party cookies) gives it a relative advantage as privacy regulation tightens — advertisers are shifting from platforms dependent on third-party data toward marketplaces with first-party data.

### 6. FTC v. Amazon Antitrust Case (Trial Scheduled October 2026)

eBay holds only 3.0% US ecommerce market share, far below antitrust scrutiny thresholds. However, the FTC's antitrust case against Amazon — if successful — could fundamentally reshape ecommerce competition and provide significant indirect benefits to eBay.

In September 2023, the FTC joined by 18 state attorneys general and Puerto Rico filed an antitrust suit against Amazon ([FTC v. Amazon.com, Inc.](https://www.ftc.gov/legal-library/browse/cases-proceedings/1910129-1910130-amazoncom-inc-amazon-ecommerce)), alleging Amazon illegally maintained monopoly power in two related markets — "online retail services" and "marketplace services". Core allegations: (a) **Anti-discounting measures** — Amazon penalizes sellers offering lower prices on other platforms (through search demotion or Buy Box removal), preventing sellers from offering lower prices on eBay and competing platforms; (b) **Prime-FBA bundling** — tying Prime badge (which drives ~70% of purchase decisions) to Amazon's own logistics service (FBA), crowding out third-party logistics options; (c) **Seller fee inflation** — total fees Amazon charges third-party sellers (including advertising fees, FBA fees, commissions) rose from ~19% in 2014 to ~45% in 2024 ([Antitrust Attorney Analysis](https://www.theantitrustattorney.com/unpacking-the-implications-of-the-ftcs-antitrust-case-against-amazon-for-online-marketplace-competition/)).

**Case timeline**: A federal judge ruled the case could proceed to trial in September 2024; **the antitrust trial is scheduled for October 2026** ([TechPolicy.Press FTC v. Amazon Tracker](https://www.techpolicy.press/tracker/ftc-v-amazoncom/)). In a separate matter, the FTC reached a historic **$2.5 billion settlement** with Amazon in September 2025 over Amazon Prime "dark patterns" (difficulty cancelling subscriptions) — the largest consumer protection settlement in FTC history ([FTC Press Release, 2025-09](https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2025/09/ftc-secures-historic-25-billion-settlement-against-amazon)) — signaling continued escalation of regulatory enforcement against tech platforms.

**Potential impact on eBay**: If the FTC antitrust case succeeds or reaches behavioral remedies, it may require Amazon to (i) eliminate anti-discounting provisions (allowing sellers to freely price on eBay, potentially offering lower prices there); (ii) unwind Prime-FBA bundling (reducing seller dependency on Amazon logistics, making multi-platform selling more viable); (iii) restrict use of third-party seller data for own-brand products ([PYMNTS Analysis](https://www.pymnts.com/cpi-posts/npacking-the-implications-of-the-ftcs-antitrust-case-against-amazon-for-online-marketplace-competition/)). Any of these could reduce seller lock-in to the Amazon ecosystem, generating incremental migration of sellers and buyers to eBay. Iannone's "We don't compete with our sellers" positioning ([LinkedIn, 2021](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/empowering-economic-opportunity-all-jamie-iannone)) becomes a clear differentiator in this context — eBay has no private label brands and does not compete with sellers.

### 7. EU VAT in the Digital Age (ViDA)

On March 11, 2025, the EU officially passed the VAT in the Digital Age (ViDA) package — the largest reform to the EU VAT system since 1993 ([EU Taxation and Customs Union, 2025-03-11](https://taxation-customs.ec.europa.eu/news/adoption-vat-digital-age-package-2025-03-11_en)). ViDA introduces three major changes for ecommerce marketplace platforms:

(a) **Expanded deemed supplier obligations** (framework effective April 14, 2025; some measures implemented July 2028): Online marketplace platforms are treated as "deemed suppliers" in specific contexts, responsible for collecting and remitting VAT. Notably, **eBay's C2C secondhand goods sellers and sellers located within the EU are not currently in the primary expansion scope of deemed supplier obligations** — the ViDA deemed supplier expansion primarily targets accommodation and passenger transport platforms ([VATCalc ViDA Analysis](https://www.vatcalc.com/eu/eu-marketplace-deemed-supplier-for-eu-sellers-vat-2025/)). However, the possibility of future expansion to all C2C goods transactions remains.

(b) **One-Stop-Shop (OSS) expansion** (implemented July 2028): Single VAT filing window expanded to more cross-border transaction types, including B2B cross-border own-inventory movements — significantly reducing the multi-country VAT registration burden for cross-border sellers ([EU ViDA Implementation](https://taxation-customs.ec.europa.eu/taxation/vat/vat-digital-age-vida_en)). eBay's sellers (90%+ export internationally) will directly benefit from OSS simplification.

(c) **E-invoicing and digital reporting** (phased implementation 2030–2035): Requires structured e-invoicing for cross-border B2B transactions and real-time reporting to the EU central database — this will increase platform technical compliance costs, but large platforms (like eBay) can spread costs across scale ([PwC Digital Marketplaces Tax Demands](https://www.pwc.com/gx/en/issues/transformation/digital-marketplaces-tax-legal-regulatory-demands.html)).

**Net impact on eBay: neutral to positive** — eBay already has robust cross-border VAT processing infrastructure (IOSS registration, automatic VAT calculation), and OSS expansion benefits smaller sellers' compliance (indirectly encouraging more sellers onto the platform). Main risk: if future deemed supplier obligations expand to all C2C goods transactions, eBay would need to assume massive VAT collection and remittance obligations.
