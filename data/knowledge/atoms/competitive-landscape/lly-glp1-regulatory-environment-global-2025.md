## LLY GLP-1 Global Regulatory Environment (2025–2026)

### United States (FDA)

**IRA (Inflation Reduction Act) Drug Price Negotiation**

Medicare drug price negotiations have commenced. In November 2025, both Lilly and Novo Nordisk signed agreements with the U.S. government to include GLP-1 anti-obesity drugs under Medicare Part D coverage (previously, Medicare did not cover obesity drugs). Post-negotiation, the monthly cost dropped to **$245** (patient out-of-pocket ~$50), representing approximately a 75% reduction from the ~$1,000/month list price ([JPMorgan, 2026](https://www.jpmorgan.com/insights/global-research/current-events/obesity-drugs); [Motley Fool, 2025-11](https://www.fool.com/investing/2025/11/11/win-intense-battle-rule-glp-1-drug-lly-nvo-pfe/)).

CEO David Ricks framed this as a deliberate volume-for-price trade: "We're excited to get going on that; that'll start in the spring, and we lowered our prices to get that done," estimating Medicare access could reach "20 to 30 million new patients... Just on Medicare" ([T. Rowe Price, 2025-11](https://www.troweprice.com/en/us/insights/the-long-view-eli-lilly)).

**Quantified Policy Risk Exposure**: IRA negotiations reduced GLP-1 net pricing by approximately **60–75%** (from ~$1,000 → $245/month). ADA Ventures estimates the Medicare negotiated discount represents **71% off list price** ([ADA Ventures](https://www.adaventures.com/the-glp-1-economy)). Lilly's strategic calculation is that volume from 20–30M newly covered Medicare patients more than offsets price compression.

**MAHA (Make America Healthy Again) Movement**

Ricks expressed strong alignment with the RFK Jr.-led health policy agenda: "At the top level, we have a huge amount of alignment. Everyone in our company wakes up every day trying to make people healthy" and "Medicine isn't always the answer. Science tells us that already, so that's easy to embrace" ([Washington Reporter, 2025-03](https://washingtonreporter.news/p/interview-eli-lilly-ceo-david-ricks)).

**FDA Accelerated Approval & Compounding Pharmacy Enforcement**

- April 2025: FDA removed GLP-1 drugs from the shortage list
- May 2025: Enforcement grace period for compounding pharmacies ended ([IQVIA, 2026-01](https://www.iqvia.com/locations/emea/blogs/2026/01/outlook-for-obesity-in-2026))
- Novo Nordisk's oral Wegovy received a National Priority Voucher for accelerated review ([IQVIA, 2026-01](https://www.iqvia.com/locations/emea/blogs/2026/01/outlook-for-obesity-in-2026))

**LillyDirect (DTC Model)**

Lilly operates a direct-to-consumer channel that bypasses traditional insurance intermediaries, selling at ~$549/month. Ricks described the consumer appeal: "People skip all this other morass and getting a 'this is not a bill' piece of paper. They're just like, 'Here's my Visa card number.'" ([Cheeky Pint/All-In, 2024](https://cheekypint.substack.com/p/dave-ricks-ceo-of-eli-lilly-on-glp)).

### Europe (EMA)

Europe is the second-largest GLP-1 market, but approval and reimbursement timelines lag the U.S. significantly. The EMA's obesity drug guidelines have **not been updated since 2016** ([IQVIA, 2026-01](https://www.iqvia.com/locations/emea/blogs/2026/01/outlook-for-obesity-in-2026)), creating regulatory uncertainty for new formulations and indication expansions. Mounjaro has been approved in major European markets (Germany, UK, France) for diabetes; Zepbound's obesity indication is under country-by-country review.

Key 2025 milestone: **Italy became the first European nation to legally define obesity as a "chronic progressive disease"** ([IQVIA, 2026-01](https://www.iqvia.com/locations/emea/blogs/2026/01/outlook-for-obesity-in-2026)) — a legislative precedent that could accelerate insurance coverage expansion across Europe.

The UK market is particularly active: GLP-1 sales are projected to surpass **£2B by end-2025** ([IQVIA, 2026-01](https://www.iqvia.com/locations/emea/blogs/2026/01/outlook-for-obesity-in-2026)). A notable anomaly: Mounjaro's UK retail price **increased by up to 170%** in September 2025 ([IQVIA, 2026-01](https://www.iqvia.com/locations/emea/blogs/2026/01/outlook-for-obesity-in-2026)), the reverse of the U.S. pricing trend.

Novo Nordisk has filed with EMA for oral semaglutide 25mg obesity indication.

### Japan (PMDA)

Japan is Lilly's fourth-largest market (2025 revenue ~$2.7B, ~4.1% of total). **Zepbound (tirzepatide) has received PMDA approval for chronic obesity** ([IQVIA, 2026-01](https://www.iqvia.com/locations/emea/blogs/2026/01/outlook-for-obesity-in-2026)) — a significant international expansion milestone.

Japan applies a lower obesity threshold (BMI ≥25 vs. U.S. BMI ≥30), implying a proportionally larger addressable patient population relative to total population. Japanese drug prices are adjusted by the government on a biennial cycle — predictable but structurally deflationary over time.

### China (NMPA)

China is the world's second-largest pharmaceutical market and the most competitive GLP-1 battleground. Lilly's 2025 China revenue was approximately $2.3B (~3.5% of total), but domestic competitors are rapidly scaling. **Innovent Biologics' mazdutide** (dual-receptor GLP-1+Glucagon) delivered 18.55% weight loss over 60 weeks (vs. 3.02% placebo) in the GLORY-2 trial and has received NMPA acceptance ([IQVIA, 2026-01](https://www.iqvia.com/locations/emea/blogs/2026/01/outlook-for-obesity-in-2026)).

Critically, the **semaglutide core patent expires in 2026 in China, India, Brazil, Canada, and Turkey** — countries that collectively represent **40% of global population and 33% of global obesity** ([IQVIA, 2026-01](https://www.iqvia.com/locations/emea/blogs/2026/01/outlook-for-obesity-in-2026)). A wave of generics in these markets will reshape the global competitive landscape.

Ricks took a harsh stance on Chinese-sourced compounded products circulating in the U.S.: "Most of them are sourced out of Chinese labs that aren't even approved in China, let alone in the U.S." ([Washington Reporter, 2025-03](https://washingtonreporter.news/p/interview-eli-lilly-ceo-david-ricks)).

### Insurance Coverage as the Structural Bottleneck

Insurance coverage remains the single largest structural constraint on GLP-1 market growth. Ricks explained the fundamental tension: "There's a blurry picture here of insurance and health care schemes that cover the comorbidities of obesity" ([T. Rowe Price, 2025-11](https://www.troweprice.com/en/us/insights/the-long-view-eli-lilly)) — insurers pay for diabetes and heart disease complications but may not cover obesity itself as a standalone indication.

Ricks' strategy for unlocking international coverage is government-to-government negotiation rather than company-to-government: "A company to government discussion we're never going to win, but a government to government discussion, we could win" ([Washington Reporter, 2025-03](https://washingtonreporter.news/p/interview-eli-lilly-ceo-david-ricks)).
