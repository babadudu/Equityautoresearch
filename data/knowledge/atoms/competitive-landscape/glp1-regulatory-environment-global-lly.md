## GLP-1 Global Regulatory Environment

### United States (FDA)

**IRA Drug Price Negotiation**: Medicare drug price negotiations have launched. In November 2025, both Lilly and Novo Nordisk signed agreements with the US government to include GLP-1 anti-obesity drugs in Medicare Part D coverage (previously Medicare did not cover obesity drugs). Post-negotiation monthly cost: **$245** (patient co-pay $50), approximately 75% below the original ~$1,000/month price ([JPMorgan, 2026](https://www.jpmorgan.com/insights/global-research/current-events/obesity-drugs); [Motley Fool, 2025-11](https://www.fool.com/investing/2025/11/11/win-intense-battle-rule-glp-1-drug-lly-nvo-pfe/)).

Lilly's strategic posture is "trade price for volume": Medicare coverage potentially reaches "20 to 30 million new patients... Just on Medicare" (David Ricks, [T. Rowe Price, 2025-11](https://www.troweprice.com/en/us/insights/the-long-view-eli-lilly)). Per ADA Ventures estimates, the Medicare negotiation final discount is **71%** off list price ([ADA Ventures](https://www.adaventures.com/the-glp-1-economy)).

**Quantified policy risk exposure**: IRA negotiations reduced GLP-1 net pricing by approximately **60-75%** (from ~$1,000 → $245/month). However, the volume upside from 20-30M newly eligible Medicare patients is projected to exceed price compression effects.

Ricks on Medicare coverage: "We're excited to get going on that; that'll start in the spring, and we lowered our prices to get that done" ([T. Rowe Price, 2025-11](https://www.troweprice.com/en/us/insights/the-long-view-eli-lilly)). On timing: "We're excited to get going. We expect this to be effective by July 1" ([Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-04](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/04/eli-lilly-lly-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)).

**MAHA (Make America Healthy Again) Movement**: Ricks expressed strong alignment with RFK Jr.-led health policy: "At the top level, we have a huge amount of alignment. Everyone in our company wakes up every day trying to make people healthy" and "Medicine isn't always the answer. Science tells us that already, so that's easy to embrace" ([Washington Reporter, 2025-03](https://washingtonreporter.news/p/interview-eli-lilly-ceo-david-ricks)).

**FDA Accelerated Approval and Compounding Pharmacy Regulation**: FDA removed GLP-1 drugs from the shortage list in April 2025; the enforcement grace period for compounding pharmacies ended in May 2025 ([IQVIA, 2026-01](https://www.iqvia.com/locations/emea/blogs/2026/01/outlook-for-obesity-in-2026)). Novo's oral Wegovy received a National Priority Voucher for accelerated review ([IQVIA, 2026-01](https://www.iqvia.com/locations/emea/blogs/2026/01/outlook-for-obesity-in-2026)).

**LillyDirect (DTC Model)**: Bypasses the traditional insurance system, selling directly to consumers at ~$549/month, circumventing PBM intermediaries. By January 2026, over 1 million people per month are purchasing medications through LillyDirect.

### Europe (EMA)

Europe is the second-largest GLP-1 market, but approval and reimbursement pace lags the US. EMA obesity drug guidelines have **not been updated since 2016** ([IQVIA, 2026-01](https://www.iqvia.com/locations/emea/blogs/2026/01/outlook-for-obesity-in-2026)), creating regulatory uncertainty for new formulations and indication expansions. Mounjaro has been approved for diabetes in major European markets (Germany, UK, France); Zepbound's obesity indication is under country-by-country review.

Notably, **Italy in 2025 became the first European country to legally define obesity as a 'chronic progressive disease'** ([IQVIA, 2026-01](https://www.iqvia.com/locations/emea/blogs/2026/01/outlook-for-obesity-in-2026)) — a legislative milestone that could catalyze insurance coverage across Europe. The UK market is especially active: GLP-1 sales are projected to exceed £2B by end-2025 ([IQVIA, 2026-01](https://www.iqvia.com/locations/emea/blogs/2026/01/outlook-for-obesity-in-2026)), though an interesting inverse dynamic emerged where Mounjaro's UK retail price **increased by up to 170%** in September 2025 ([IQVIA, 2026-01](https://www.iqvia.com/locations/emea/blogs/2026/01/outlook-for-obesity-in-2026)). Novo has filed for EMA approval of oral semaglutide 25mg for the obesity indication.

### Japan (PMDA)

Japan is Lilly's fourth-largest market (~$2.7B revenue in 2025, representing 4.1% of total). **Zepbound (tirzepatide) has received PMDA approval for chronic obesity** ([IQVIA, 2026-01](https://www.iqvia.com/locations/emea/blogs/2026/01/outlook-for-obesity-in-2026)), a significant international expansion milestone. Japan's obesity threshold is lower (BMI ≥25 = obese, vs. BMI ≥30 in the US), meaning the potential target population is proportionally larger relative to total population. Drug prices are adjusted biannually by the government — pricing pressure is more predictable than in the US/Europe but trends toward long-term compression.

### China (NMPA)

China is the world's second-largest pharmaceutical market and the most competitive GLP-1 battleground. Lilly's 2025 China revenue is approximately $2.3B (3.5% of total), but domestic competitors are rapidly emerging. **Innovent's mazdutide** (dual receptor GLP-1+Glucagon) demonstrated 60-week weight loss of **18.55%** (vs. placebo 3.02%) in the GLORY-2 trial and has been filed with NMPA ([IQVIA, 2026-01](https://www.iqvia.com/locations/emea/blogs/2026/01/outlook-for-obesity-in-2026)).

Semaglutide's core patent expires **in 2026 in China, India, Brazil, Canada, and Turkey** — countries collectively representing **40% of global population and 33% of global obesity population** ([IQVIA, 2026-01](https://www.iqvia.com/locations/emea/blogs/2026/01/outlook-for-obesity-in-2026)). Generic drug entry will reshape market dynamics in these regions.

Ricks on Chinese compounding products: "Most of them are sourced out of Chinese labs that aren't even approved in China, let alone in the U.S." ([Washington Reporter, 2025-03](https://washingtonreporter.news/p/interview-eli-lilly-ceo-david-ricks)).

### Global Regulatory Risk Quantification Summary

| Region | Core Regulatory Risk | Opportunity | Revenue Exposure |
|--------|---------------------|-------------|------------------|
| United States | IRA negotiation → net price -60-75% | Medicare coverage → 20-30M new patients | 67% (~$43.8B) |
| Europe | EMA guidelines outdated (2016); fragmented country reimbursement | Italy legislation precedent → European coverage expansion | 15% (~$10B) |
| Japan | Biannual drug price adjustment pressure | PMDA approved Zepbound for obesity | 4% (~$2.7B) |
| China | Semaglutide 2026 patent expiry; domestic competition (mazdutide) | Massive TAM; lower BMI threshold | 3.5% (~$2.3B) |
| Other Emerging | Patent expiry + generics | Large untapped population | 10% (~$6.4B) |

### Insurance Coverage as Structural Bottleneck

Ricks identifies insurance coverage as the biggest structural constraint: "There's a blurry picture here of insurance and health care schemes that cover the comorbidities of obesity" ([T. Rowe Price, 2025-11](https://www.troweprice.com/en/us/insights/the-long-view-eli-lilly)) — insurers pay for diabetes and heart disease but not necessarily for "obesity" itself. His strategy involves pushing for government-level negotiations: "A company to government discussion we're never going to win, but a government to government discussion, we could win" ([Washington Reporter, 2025-03](https://washingtonreporter.news/p/interview-eli-lilly-ceo-david-ricks)).
