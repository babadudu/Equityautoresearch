## GLP-1 Market Structure: Dual Oligopoly & Pipeline Competition (2025)

### Extreme Concentration: Dual Oligopoly

The GLP-1 market exhibits an extreme dual-oligopoly structure. As of 2025, Eli Lilly (~57%) and Novo Nordisk (~43%) together account for over **95%** of GLP-1 market revenue ([CNBC, 2026](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/04/eli-lilly-novo-nordisk-earnings-glp1-market.html); [Redbock, 2025](https://redbock.com/news/glp-1-medications-a-market-on-the-rise/)).

### HHI Concentration Analysis

Using Lilly 57% and Novo 43% estimates: **HHI = 57² + 43² = 3,249 + 1,849 = ~5,098** — far exceeding the U.S. DOJ "highly concentrated" threshold (HHI > 2,500). This places the GLP-1 market firmly in oligopoly territory under regulatory definition.

However, this oligopoly differs from traditional concentrated markets in two critical ways:
1. **TAM is still rapidly expanding** — new patients vastly outnumber share-switchers, meaning both players can grow simultaneously
2. **Competition is driven by clinical data, not price wars** — the primary battleground is head-to-head trial outcomes, not margin compression

As Amgen (MariTide), Pfizer (Metsera pipeline), and others enter between 2027–2032, HHI is projected to decline to the 3,000–4,000 range, though Lilly+Novo's dual dominance is unlikely to change fundamentally in the near term.

### CEO Perspective on Competition

Lilly CEO David Ricks expressed a surprisingly welcoming view of new entrants: he argues that an undersaturated market means more participants expand the pie rather than steal share — "someone has to do this work" ([T. Rowe Price, 2025-11](https://www.troweprice.com/en/us/insights/the-long-view-eli-lilly)). However, Ricks is far more concerned about compounding pharmacy threats: "There's just too much opaque pricing and profit taking" ([Washington Reporter, 2025-03](https://washingtonreporter.news/p/interview-eli-lilly-ceo-david-ricks)), and specifically about counterfeit/unapproved compounded semaglutide: "You've created basically a shadow generic industry undermining patents itself" and "Most of them are sourced out of Chinese labs that aren't even approved in China, let alone in the U.S." — warning of safety risks from what he estimates as up to one to two million Americans using unproven compounds ([Washington Reporter, 2025-03](https://washingtonreporter.news/p/interview-eli-lilly-ceo-david-ricks); [Cheeky Pint/All-In, 2024](https://cheekypint.substack.com/p/dave-ricks-ceo-of-eli-lilly-on-glp)).
