## Overview

Unlike the U.S. duopoly, international warehouse club markets are far more fragmented. Costco faces different local competitors in each market. The contrast reveals both Costco's global competitive strengths and the specific challenges it faces as a relatively late international entrant.

## China: The Critical Strategic Battleground

Sam's Club in China has become one of Walmart's fastest-growing businesses globally. According to SCMP, Sam's Club China FY2025 comparable sales grew approximately 13%, far exceeding the global average ([SCMP, 2025](https://www.scmp.com/business/china-business/article/3343683/walmarts-sams-club-cracks-china-market-formula-even-foreign-retailers-shut-shop)). With only 7 stores in China versus Sam's Club's 62, Costco has a clear first-mover disadvantage.

However, the crowded scene on the first day of Costco's Shanghai store opening in 2019 — when operations had to be temporarily suspended due to the crowd — demonstrates the enormous potential of the Costco model in China. The key question is whether expansion speed can match demand.

## Management Philosophy on Competition

Vachris takes an extremely pragmatic approach to competition: "Our culture is very simple … it focuses back on doing the right thing." ([South Sound Biz, 2024](https://www.southsoundbiz.com/profiles/ron-vachris-costco-interview/article_7c03e854-37d9-11ef-b403-1fab84c884aa.html)) His philosophy: there is no need to deliberately "beat" competitors — just continuously create value for members and market share will follow naturally.

Sinegal articulated the essential difference between Costco and Walmart/Sam's Club: "Wal-Mart is a terrific company. But their model is different. They want to be all things to all people. We want to be all things to a smaller group of people." ([Fast Company, 2004/07](https://www.fastcompany.com/47593/costco-love))

Jelinek's attitude on membership fee increases illustrated the same culture. When asked in a rare CNBC interview whether Costco would raise fees to cope with inflation, he responded: "We think about it every year. Right now, in terms of the membership fee, it is not on the table right at the moment." ([CNBC, 2022/07/11](https://www.cnbc.com/2022/07/11/4-things-costco-ceo-craig-jelinek-said-in-a-rare-interview-on-the-economy-retail-trends-and-more.html)) — embodying Costco's culture of absorbing short-term profit pressure rather than easily passing costs to members.

## Structural Observation: Costco vs. B2B-Focused Competitors

A key insight from the international landscape: most of Costco's international "competitors" (Metro AG, Makro) primarily target SMB businesses (B2B) rather than end consumers (B2C). This means the membership-model warehouse club space for consumers remains largely uncontested in most international markets — Costco's biggest international rival is Sam's Club, operating the same model, not legacy B2B wholesalers.

This structural observation supports Costco's long-term international expansion thesis: the most direct competition exists only in markets where Sam's Club has already entered (U.S., China). In Japan, South Korea, and Europe, Costco faces format-differentiated competitors with limited direct pricing competition.
