## eBay Vertical Category Market Concentration Analysis

eBay's overall ecommerce market share is only ~1.2% (global) / ~3% (US), but its concentration in core vertical categories is dramatically higher. The following analyzes eBay's competitive position and market structure by category.

### Trading Cards Market (Global, ~$13B)

The global trading card market reached approximately **$13 billion** in 2024 ([PR Newswire / Market Decipher, 2025](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/2025---trading-cards-market-size-is-estimated-at-13-billion-in-2024-and-is-expected-to-grow-at-a-cagr-of-8-5--during-the-forecast-period-market-decipher-302361699.html)), with a CAGR of 8.5%, projected to reach $21 billion by 2034 ([Yahoo Finance / Custom Market Insights](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/latest-global-trading-card-games-083000470.html)). Sports trading cards reached $12.98B in 2023 with a CAGR of ~19% ([Straits Research](https://straitsresearch.com/report/collectible-card-games-market)). Pokémon holds more than 12% market share in the trading card game (TCG) sub-segment ([GM Insights](https://www.gminsights.com/industry-analysis/trading-card-games-market)).

eBay is the world's largest online trading card exchange platform — in 2024 it acquired Goldin Auctions (top sports card auction house) and partnered with PSA (Professional Sports Authenticator) to launch in-platform grading services (having processed over 600,000 cards since launch) ([Sportico, 2025](https://www.sportico.com/business/commerce/2025/ebay-sports-cards-growth-1234850708/)). eBay trading cards have achieved double-digit growth for nine consecutive quarters, with Pokémon GMV growing triple digits YoY for three consecutive quarters as of Q3 2025 ([eBay Q3 2025 Earnings Call](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/29/ebay-ebay-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)). eBay is recognized by the industry as the world's largest online trading card secondary market platform ([Fortune, 2025-07-31](https://fortune.com/2025/07/31/ebay-earnings-live-sports-cards-trading-pokemon/)).

Comparing eBay's $10B collectibles annual business (including trading cards and other collectibles) against the global $13B trading card market, and considering online transactions represent approximately 50–60% of total, eBay's estimated market share in the **online trading card market is 25–35%**. Major competitors include Whatnot (valuation $5B, live auction model), Fanatics (sports card direct sales), and Courtyard (selling $50M in cards and comics monthly) ([Fortune, 2025-07-31](https://fortune.com/2025/07/31/ebay-earnings-live-sports-cards-trading-pokemon/)). eBay's competitive response includes launching **eBay Live** live trading functionality.

By Q2 2025, the collectibles category grew 10%, while the rest of the platform grew only ~1% — a gap of ~11 percentage points ([Fortune, 2025-07-31](https://fortune.com/2025/07/31/ebay-earnings-live-sports-cards-trading-pokemon/)).

**Online trading card market HHI estimate: ~2,000–2,500** (moderate to high concentration), with eBay, Whatnot, and TCGPlayer/Amazon forming a CR3 of ~70–80%.

### Online Auto Parts Aftermarket (US, ~$220B Third-Party Marketplace)

The global ecommerce automotive aftermarket reached approximately **$74 billion** in 2024 ([Polaris Market Research](https://www.polarismarketresearch.com/industry-analysis/e-commerce-automotive-aftermarket)), with the US market at approximately $62 billion ([Precedence Research](https://www.precedenceresearch.com/e-commerce-automotive-after-market)). Third-party marketplace platforms (including eBay, Amazon, etc.) collectively account for $21.8B, or 49% of US aftermarket ecommerce ([TMCapital Automotive Aftermarket eCommerce Trends 2024–2025](https://www.tmcapital.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/12/2024-2025-eCommerce-Trends-in-the-Automotive-Aftermarket-1.pdf)). Online sales are projected to represent 40% of the aftermarket by 2026 (vs. only 25% in 2021) ([Hedges & Company](https://hedgescompany.com/blog/2020/07/auto-parts-industry-analysis-16-billion-2020/)). The market is highly fragmented — beyond eBay and Amazon, major participants include RockAuto, AutoZone, O'Reilly, Advance Auto Parts, CarParts.com, and dozens of vertical retailers ([TMCapital](https://www.tmcapital.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/12/2024-2025-eCommerce-Trends-in-the-Automotive-Aftermarket-1.pdf)).

| **eBay Motors** | ~$10B+ GMV | ~45–50% | 20-year first-mover advantage, 110M+ parts listings, vehicle fitment tools |
| Amazon Automotive | ~$8–10B | ~35–40% | Prime logistics, all-category coverage |
| RockAuto / Other Verticals | ~$3–5B | ~15–20% | Deep specialist selection, low pricing |
| **CR3** | | **~90%+** | |
| **HHI Estimate** | | **~3,500–4,000** | **Highly concentrated** (eBay² + Amazon² + other² ≈ 2,200 + 1,400 + 400) |

Sources: [MyListerHub 2025](https://www.mylisterhub.com/articles/why-every-auto-parts-business-should-be-selling-on-ebay-in-2025); [TMCapital](https://www.tmcapital.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/12/2024-2025-eCommerce-Trends-in-the-Automotive-Aftermarket-1.pdf); [X-Cart Platform Comparison](https://www.x-cart.com/blog/where-to-sell-car-parts-online.html)

eBay Motors launched in 2000, giving it a 20+ year first-mover advantage. With over 110 million active parts listings and at least one part sold per second, eBay holds a **leadership position** in online auto parts marketplace (HHI >2,500 = highly concentrated). Amazon has increased investment in vehicle fitment data tools in recent years, but eBay's inventory depth in modification and non-standard parts remains far ahead. eBay Motors annual GMV exceeds $10B, with 40 million active buyers and 110 million+ active parts listings, making auto parts eBay's **#1 category by volume and revenue** ([MyListerHub, 2025](https://www.mylisterhub.com/articles/why-every-auto-parts-business-should-be-selling-on-ebay-in-2025); [LinkedIn, 2021](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/empowering-economic-opportunity-all-jamie-iannone)). In 2024, eBay further expanded into whole-vehicle transactions via the acquisition of the Caramel platform.

### Sneaker Resale Market (Global, ~$6B)

The global sneaker resale market reached approximately **$6 billion** in 2025 ([ShelfTrend 2025 Marketplace Analysis](https://www.shelftrend.com/fashion/sneaker-resale-profit-margins-2025-marketplace-analysis)). The global secondhand sneaker market (including offline) was $4.5B in 2023, projected to reach $14.3B by 2032 ([Dataintelo](https://dataintelo.com/report/global-secondhand-sneakers-market)). Market structure has moved from StockX dominance in 2020–2021 toward a multi-polar configuration.

| Platform | Positioning | Fee Rate | Competitive Position |
|----------|-------------|----------|---------------------|
| **StockX** | Real-time buy/sell, price discovery algorithm | 12–19% | Limited-edition dominant player, ~$540M revenue 2023 ([ShelfTrend](https://www.shelftrend.com/fashion/sneaker-resale-profit-margins-2025-marketplace-analysis)) |
| **GOAT** | International coverage, full price range | 9.5–25% | Secondhand + new integration, international market leader |
| **eBay** | Authenticity Guarantee $100+ | 13% | Low-fee advantage; instant payment attracts casual sellers ([ShelfTrend](https://www.shelftrend.com/fashion/sneaker-resale-profit-margins-2025-marketplace-analysis)) |
| P2P channels (Reddit/FB) | No intermediary | 0% | Continuously eroding platform share |

eBay holds a moderate position in sneaker resale (estimated ~15–20% market share), below StockX but above most vertical platforms. eBay's Authenticity Guarantee (third-party expert verification for $100+ sneakers) is a core differentiator. Iannone noted in a ChannelX interview that the authentication strategy began with sneakers: "We started with watches and sneakers... the results have been phenomenal. We're seeing 20-plus percent growth in those categories" ([ChannelX, 2022-01](https://channelx.world/2022/01/exclusive-interview-with-jamie-iannone-ebay-ceo/)). However, in 2025 the sneaker resale market broadly slowed — "only 47% of new releases are profitable for resellers" ([ShelfTrend](https://www.shelftrend.com/fashion/sneaker-resale-profit-margins-2025-marketplace-analysis)) — shifting transaction volume from speculation-driven to personal-use-driven.

**HHI estimate: ~1,500–2,000** (moderate concentration)

### Pre-Owned Luxury Watch Market (Global, ~$24B)

Global secondhand luxury watch transaction value reached **$22.84 billion** in 2024, with a 2025 estimate of **$23.71 billion** ([Chrono24 Secondary Watch Market Report H1 2025](https://about.chrono24.com/en/press/Secondary-Watch-Market-Report-H1-2025)). Rolex holds ~33.7% brand share in the secondhand market, with Omega in second place and growing steadily ([Chrono24-Fratello Report H1 2025](https://about.chrono24.com/en/press/Secondary-Watch-Market-Report-H1-2025)).

| Platform | Positioning | Estimated Annual Transaction Volume |
|----------|-------------|-------------------------------------|
| Chrono24 | World's largest watch marketplace (1.6M+ cumulative transactions) | ~$3–5B |
| WatchBox | High-end curation + trade-in | ~$1–2B |
| eBay (Authenticity Guarantee) | Third-party authentication for $2,000+ watches | ~$1–2B |
| Watchfinder (Richemont) | Certified refurbished | ~$0.5–1B |
| Brand CPO (Rolex CPO, etc.) | Brand-direct certification | Rapidly growing |

Source: [BusinessWire Pre-owned Luxury Watches Market Report 2024–2029](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20241112100822/en/Pre-owned-Luxury-Watches-Market-Report-2024-2029-Featuring-Chrono24-WatchBox-eBay-CME-Crown-Caliber-Watchfinder-TrueFacet-Govberg-The-Watch-Club-and-Watches-of-Switzerland---ResearchAndMarkets.com); [Chrono24 H1 2025](https://about.chrono24.com/en/press/Secondary-Watch-Market-Report-H1-2025)

eBay's estimated watch resale market share is ~5–8% — below Chrono24 but comparable to WatchBox. Gen Z preference for design-driven slim watches (Cartier search volume up 4x) is pushing market structure away from Rolex dominance toward diversification ([JCK, 2025](https://www.jckonline.com/editorial-article/gen-z-reshapes-watch-market/)). eBay's Authenticity Guarantee covers $2,000+ watches, verified by independent experts at Authenticity Guarantee centers — this trust infrastructure positions eBay to continue gaining penetration as the recommerce trend expands.

**HHI estimate: ~1,000–1,500** (fragmented)

**Key Insight**: eBay ranks only fourth in overall ecommerce (3.0%), but in its strategically focused vertical categories, market structure ranges from "fragmented" to "highly concentrated" — with eBay holding a leadership position (CR1 or CR2) in online auto parts and trading card marketplaces. This is the structural foundation of Iannone's vertical depth strategy.
