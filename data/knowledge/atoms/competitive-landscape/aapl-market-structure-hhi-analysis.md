## Apple Market Structure: HHI Concentration Analysis

Apple's competitive environment exhibits dramatically different concentration profiles depending on which product segment and geography is measured. Using the Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI), the divergence between US and global smartphone markets is especially stark.

### Interpreting the HHI Divergence

The US DOJ defines HHI >2,500 as a "highly concentrated market." The US smartphone market's HHI exceeding 4,200 is one of the quantitative foundations underlying the DOJ's antitrust lawsuit filed against Apple in March 2024. Apple holds approximately 57% US smartphone unit share, and an estimated 85% of global smartphone profits — a profit-share-to-unit-share ratio that is extraordinary by any historical standard.

The critical insight is that Apple's market power is *geographically and economically segmented*: highly dominant in the US and in the global premium ($800+) price tier, but relatively limited in emerging markets. The global shipment HHI of ~1,700 (moderate concentration) reflects Samsung, Xiaomi, and others' strength in volume-driven, lower-ASP segments where Apple strategically chooses not to compete.

Tim Cook has consistently articulated this positioning as deliberate strategy rather than inability to compete on volume: "We've never been about market share. We've been about making the best products" ([CNBC interview, 2023](https://www.cnbc.com/tim-cook-interview/)).

### Digital Services Market Structure

In digital services, Apple faces a fragmented competitive landscape — Google (search/YouTube), Amazon (cloud/streaming), Microsoft (cloud/productivity), Spotify (music), and Netflix (video) each dominate their respective verticals. Apple competes not by outright leading any single service category, but by leveraging device pre-installation and ecosystem integration to maintain distribution advantages within its installed base.

The App Store take rate (15–30%) faces dual pressure from Epic Games litigation and EU Digital Markets Act (DMA) enforcement, potentially fragmenting Apple's services distribution moat.

### PC Market Context

In the PC market, Lenovo leads with 24.9% shipment share (70.8M units), followed by HP and Dell at approximately 20–22% each. Apple Mac holds approximately 8% global unit share ([IDC via Thurrott, January 2026](https://www.thurrott.com/hardware/331635/idc-pc-sales-rose-8-1-percent-in-2025-9-6-percent-in-q4)). However, the shipment share figure materially understates Apple's competitive position in the high-end creator segment, where Apple Silicon's performance-per-watt leadership has enabled Mac to build qualitative differentiation that unit share metrics do not capture.

### China Competitive Dynamics

China represents Apple's most intense competitive environment. Huawei's return with the Mate 60/70 series (leveraging SMIC's 7nm-class process) has re-intensified competition in the premium China segment. Cook acknowledged the pressure while maintaining long-term conviction: "The competitive environment there is the most competitive in the world... that remains to be the case" ([Apple Q3 FY2024 Earnings Call / Six Colors, 2024-08](https://sixcolors.com/post/2024/08/this-is-tim-q3-2024-analyst-call-transcript/)), adding: "We continue to be confident in the long-term opportunity in China. I don't know how every chapter of the book reads but we're very confident in the long term" ([ibid](https://sixcolors.com/post/2024/08/this-is-tim-q3-2024-analyst-call-transcript/)).
