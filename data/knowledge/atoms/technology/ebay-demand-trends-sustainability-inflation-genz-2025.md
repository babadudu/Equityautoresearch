## eBay Demand Trends: Sustainability, Inflation Trade-Down, and Gen Z Recommerce Adoption (2025)

Three structural demand trends converge to create a favorable macro backdrop for eBay's recommerce-focused marketplace strategy, each reinforcing the platform's differentiated positioning versus commodity e-commerce incumbents.

---

### Trend 1: Accelerating Sustainable Consumption

Consumer preference for refurbished, secondhand, and sustainably-sourced goods is accelerating across product categories:

- Refurbished electronics: YoY growth of **+18%**
- Reused fashion: YoY growth of **+22%**
- Sustainability-focused listings as a share of eBay's catalogue: **5–6%** ([ElectroIQ, 2025](https://electroiq.com/stats/ebay-statistics/))

eBay has measured and publicized the environmental impact of its C2C marketplace. CEO Jamie Iannone quantified in the 2021 annual report: "540,000 metric tons of carbon emissions were avoided" through C2C sales ([LinkedIn, 2021](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/empowering-economic-opportunity-all-jamie-iannone)). By 2025, eBay estimated cumulative positive environmental impact approaching **$25 billion** over five years ([eBay Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-18](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/18/ebay-ebay-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)).

This sustainability narrative serves a dual strategic function: it differentiates eBay from Amazon and Temu on brand values (not just price/selection), and it provides a regulatory and ESG narrative that resonates with institutional investors and policy-makers increasingly focused on circular economy metrics.

---

### Trend 2: Inflation-Driven Consumer Trade-Down

Persistent inflation in key markets — particularly Western Europe — has driven consumers toward secondhand and refurbished goods as a cost-saving strategy. Iannone confirmed this trend's durability on the Q3 2025 earnings call: even "in a challenging environment, macro environment in Europe," enthusiast buyers "don't churn on the marketplace" ([eBay Q3 2025 Earnings Call, 2025-10-29](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/29/ebay-ebay-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)).

This is a structural, not cyclical, demand driver for eBay in two respects:
1. **Counter-cyclical resilience**: In recessionary or inflationary environments, recommerce platforms benefit from demand migration from new goods. eBay's GMV is partially insulated from consumer downturns in a way that pure new-goods retailers are not.
2. **Buyer acquisition**: Inflation-driven first-time secondhand buyers often develop lasting recommerce habits, expanding the total addressable buyer base for platforms like eBay.

US online resale market growth accelerated to **+23% in 2024** — the strongest growth since 2021 — with the market projected to reach $40 billion by 2029 at a 13% CAGR ([ThredUp 2025 Resale Report](https://www.thredup.com/resale)).

---

### Trend 3: Gen Z Becoming the Primary Consumer Cohort

Gen Z's relationship with recommerce is qualitatively different from prior generations: they treat secondhand as a **preferred** consumption mode, not an alternative or compromise. Key data:

- Nearly **80% of Gen Z and millennials** self-identify as participants in the recommerce movement ([ThredUp 2025 Resale Report](https://www.thredup.com/resale))
- **58% of Gen Z consumers** have used AI shopping tools ([EComposer](https://ecomposer.io/blogs/ecommerce/ai-in-ecommerce-statistics)), making eBay's AI investment directly aligned with its target growth demographic
- Global secondhand apparel CAGR of 16.1% (2025: $199B → 2031: $486B) is substantially driven by Gen Z demand ([GlobeNewsWire](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/01/23/3224864/28124/en/Secondhand-Apparel-Market-Analysis-Report-2026-A-485-Billion-Market-by-2031-Driven-by-Gen-Z-Rise-of-Digital-Resale-Platforms-Trade-in-Programs-and-Demand-for-Sustainable-and-Afford.html))

The generational shift creates an unusual strategic opportunity: eBay's existing brand identity (founded on C2C secondhand and auction dynamics) aligns with Gen Z's preference in ways that Amazon's logistics-first, new-goods identity does not. Iannone articulated this directly in a 2022 Financial Times interview: "We're leaning into where Gen Z and millennials are... there's a bigger focus on sustainability, and 're-commerce'. eBay is really the pioneer... that gives us an opportunity" ([Financial Times via PYMNTS, 2022-10-21](https://www.pymnts.com/news/ecommerce/2022/ebay-bids-on-luxury-recommerce-to-woo-young-shoppers/)).

The convergence of Gen Z's recommerce preference with their high AI tool adoption (58%) means eBay's dual investment in recommerce infrastructure and AI-powered discovery tools (eBay.ai chatbot, Magical Listing, Agentic Commerce) is optimally targeted at the highest-growth consumer segment.

### Synthesis: Three Trends × eBay's Positioning

| Demand Trend | Structural Driver | eBay Advantage | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sustainable consumption | Environmental regulation + Gen Z values | 30-year secondhand brand, $25B impact narrative | Competitors launching sustainability lines |
| Inflation trade-down | Macro pressure on real wages in EU/US | Recommerce >40% of GMV, counter-cyclical protection | Economic recovery could reduce new-to-secondhand migration |
| Gen Z recommerce adoption | Generational preference shift, not just trend | Pioneer positioning, Authenticity Guarantee trust layer | Depop, ThredUp, Poshmark competing for fashion/apparel vertical |

eBay's recommerce share of GMV rose from approximately one-third to over 40% between 2020 and 2025, with the trajectory still upward. The combination of structural demand tailwinds (all three trends are secular, not cyclical) and eBay's early-mover investments in recommerce infrastructure (Authenticity Guarantee, Focus Categories strategy, AI-powered listing tools) positions the company to compound share in its differentiated segment even as GMV growth in the broader commodity e-commerce market moderates.
