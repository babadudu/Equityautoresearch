## Costco E-Commerce Penetration & Digital Transformation FY2018–FY2025

### E-Commerce S-Curve: Slow but Steady

Costco's e-commerce development follows a typical "late entrant catch-up" curve. The FY2025 10-K reveals e-commerce accounts for approximately ~7% of net sales ([Costco 10-K FY2025, SEC EDGAR](https://www.sec.gov/cgi-bin/browse-edgar?action=getcompany&CIK=0000909832&type=10-K&dateb=&owner=include&count=10)).

E-commerce revenue grew from $5.5B in FY2018 to $18.9B in FY2025, a seven-year increase of 244%, but as a percentage of total revenue only rose from 4% to 7%.

### The "Low Penetration" Paradox

This low penetration rate appears to be a weakness but actually reflects the **irreplaceability** of Costco's physical warehouses — the in-store member experience ("treasure hunt" effect, sampling, gas stations, instant use) cannot be replicated online. The "lower half" of the e-commerce S-curve — AI personalized recommendations, supply chain automation, last-mile delivery — is redefining the online opportunity for warehouse clubs. Although Costco is a late entrant, its 146 million cardholders' data asset provides the foundation for future acceleration.

### Member Digitalization & App Adoption

Costco's member base continues expanding while digital adoption accelerates:

| Metric | FY2022 | FY2023 | FY2024 | FY2025 | Q1 FY2026 |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|----------|
| Paid Members | 65.8M | 71M | 76.2M | ~78M | 81.4M |
| Total Cardholders | 118M | 128M | 137M | ~140M | 145.9M |
| U.S./Canada Renewal Rate | 92.5% | 92.7% | 92.9% | 92.9% | ~93% |

Sources: [Costco Q1 FY2026 Earnings Release](https://investor.costco.com/news/news-details/2025/Costco-Wholesale-Corporation-Reports-First-Quarter-Fiscal-Year-2026-Operating-Results/default.aspx), [Fifth Person](https://fifthperson.com/costco-business-model/)

The digital membership card launched in 2024 was a significant milestone — members no longer need to carry physical cards, using the app to enter and checkout. From FY2022 to Q1 FY2026, paid members grew from 65.8M to 81.4M (CAGR ~6%), and total cardholders rose from 118M to 145.9M. This reflects that Costco's membership "adoption curve" remains in a stable upward phase — particularly international markets (global renewal rates rising from 88% to ~91%) which still have significant penetration headroom.

### Omnichannel Strategy Context

CEO Ron Vachris listed digital experience improvement as one of his three top priorities, but emphasized he would not sacrifice physical warehouse core values for e-commerce ([Fortune, 2024/05/28](https://fortune.com/2024/05/28/costco-ceo-ron-vachris-lifer-forklift-driver/)). Vachris also noted that the pace of opening 24–25 new warehouse stores per year can be maintained for another 5–10 years ([Fast Company, 2025](https://www.fastcompany.com/91336649/ron-vachris-coscto-world-changing-ideas-2025)), meaning the warehouse-centric member acquisition model will continue driving the demand curve upward.

This dual-track approach — steady physical expansion + cautious digital enhancement — distinguishes Costco from pure-play e-commerce retailers and from Walmart's more aggressive omnichannel buildout.
