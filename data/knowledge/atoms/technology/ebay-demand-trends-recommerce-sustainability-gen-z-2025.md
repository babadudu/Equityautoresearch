## eBay Demand Trends: Recommerce, Sustainability, Inflation Trade-Down, and Gen Z (2025)

Three demand-side trends are structurally favorable for eBay's marketplace model through the late 2020s: the acceleration of sustainable consumption, inflation-driven trade-down behavior, and the generational shift as Gen Z becomes a primary consumer cohort. These trends reinforce eBay's positioning in recommerce and align with its AI investment trajectory.

### Trend 1: Sustainable Consumption Accelerating

Refurbished electronics are growing YoY at +18% and reused fashion at +22%, with sustainability-focused listings accounting for 5–6% of eBay's total inventory ([ElectroIQ, 2025](https://electroiq.com/stats/ebay-statistics/)). The global secondhand apparel market alone is projected to grow from $199B in 2025 to $486B in 2031 at a 16.1% CAGR ([GlobeNewsWire](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/01/23/3224864/28124/en/Secondhand-Apparel-Market-Analysis-Report-2026-A-485-Billion-Market-by-2031-Driven-by-Gen-Z-Rise-of-Digital-Resale-Platforms-Trade-in-Programs-and-Demand-for-Sustainable-and-Afford.html)).

eBay has quantified its environmental contribution. In his 2021 annual letter, CEO Jamie Iannone documented: "540,000 metric tons of carbon emissions were avoided" through C2C sales on the platform ([LinkedIn, 2021](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/empowering-economic-opportunity-all-jamie-iannone)). By 2025, eBay estimated its five-year cumulative positive environmental impact had approached **$25 billion** ([eBay Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-18](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/18/ebay-ebay-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)). Recommerce now accounts for over 40% of eBay's GMV in FY2025, up from approximately one-third five years earlier.

Iannone positioned eBay as the structural beneficiary of the sustainability shift in a 2022 Financial Times interview: "We're leaning into where Gen Z and millennials are... there's a bigger focus on sustainability, and 're-commerce'. eBay is really the pioneer... that gives us an opportunity" ([Financial Times via PYMNTS, 2022-10-21](https://www.pymnts.com/news/ecommerce/2022/ebay-bids-on-luxury-recommerce-to-woo-young-shoppers/)).

This strategic positioning is reinforced by eBay Authenticity Guarantee—a trust infrastructure for high-value secondhand categories (sneakers, watches, trading cards, luxury handbags) that addresses the core friction in recommerce: buyer confidence in item authenticity and condition.

### Trend 2: Inflation-Driven Trade-Down Behavior

Persistent inflation in the US and Europe is driving consumers toward secondhand and refurbished goods as a cost-saving strategy. This dynamic creates counter-cyclical demand resilience for eBay—when macroeconomic conditions deteriorate, eBay's value proposition as a source of discounted goods strengthens.

Iannone confirmed this resilience on the Q3 2025 earnings call: even "in a challenging environment, macro environment in Europe," enthusiast buyers on eBay "don't churn on the marketplace" ([eBay Q3 2025 Earnings Call, 2025-10-29](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/29/ebay-ebay-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)). The "enthusiast buyer" segment—eBay's core high-frequency purchaser cohort across categories like motorsports parts, collectibles, and refurbished electronics—demonstrates lower price sensitivity and higher retention than casual shoppers, providing a stable demand floor.

US online resale grew by 23% in 2024, the fastest rate since 2021, and is projected to reach $40B by 2029 at a 13% CAGR ([ThredUp 2025 Resale Report](https://www.thredup.com/resale)). The trade-down trend is additive to the sustainability trend—consumers saving money and reducing environmental impact simultaneously.

### Trend 3: Gen Z Becomes the Primary Consumer Cohort

Nearly **80% of Gen Z and millennial consumers** identify as participants in the recommerce movement ([ThredUp 2025 Resale Report](https://www.thredup.com/resale)), representing a fundamental shift in purchasing norms. For these cohorts, secondhand is a preference category rather than a compromise—a structural change from prior generations where resale carried a stigma.

Critically, 58% of Gen Z consumers have already used AI shopping tools ([EComposer](https://ecomposer.io/blogs/ecommerce/ai-in-ecommerce-statistics)), which means eBay's dual investment in recommerce and AI is directly aligned with its most important emerging customer segment. Gen Z buyers are simultaneously the most sustainability-oriented, the most comfortable with AI-mediated shopping, and the most likely to engage with secondhand and collectible categories—eBay's core strengths.

This generational alignment has strategic compounding effects: as Gen Z's purchasing power grows through the late 2020s, the structural tailwinds for eBay's recommerce-first positioning strengthen. The cohort's preference for AI-assisted discovery also supports eBay's agentic commerce investments.

The three trends are mutually reinforcing: Gen Z's sustainability preferences drive recommerce adoption; inflation makes the economic case for secondhand more compelling; and AI tools lower the friction for Gen Z buyers and sellers to transact on eBay's platform. Together, they create a structural demand environment that is more favorable for eBay in the 2025–2030 period than at any time since the platform's Web 1.0 dominance in the early 2000s.
