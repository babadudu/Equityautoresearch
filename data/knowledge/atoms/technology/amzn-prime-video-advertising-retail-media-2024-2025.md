## Amazon Prime Video Advertising & Retail Media Technology (2024–2025)

### Amazon's Advertising Technology Stack

Amazon's advertising business is built on a structurally differentiated asset: first-party purchase intent data at scale. Unlike social or search advertising, Amazon can offer advertisers direct reach to consumers who are actively in the purchase funnel. The full-funnel advertising stack includes:

- **Sponsored Products**: Search-result placements within Amazon's marketplace (bottom-of-funnel, high intent)
- **Sponsored Brands**: Brand-level awareness placements across Amazon properties (mid-funnel)
- **Amazon DSP (Demand Side Platform)**: Programmatic advertising across Amazon-owned and third-party inventory (upper-funnel)
- **Prime Video Ads**: Premium streaming video inventory with first-party audience targeting (upper/mid-funnel)

This stack allows Amazon to offer advertisers a closed-loop attribution capability unavailable on traditional TV, social, or search platforms: ad exposure can be directly connected to purchase outcomes on Amazon's marketplace.

### Prime Video Advertising: Launch and Scale

**Launch Timeline**: In January 2024, Amazon introduced an ad-supported tier for Prime Video in the United States, United Kingdom, Germany, and Canada. The rollout subsequently expanded to France, Italy, Spain, Mexico, Australia, and additional markets, reaching 16 markets total by end of 2024.

Unlike Netflix's opt-in ad tier (requiring subscribers to downgrade), Amazon made ad-supported viewing the default for existing Prime Video subscribers, with an opt-out premium tier available for an additional monthly fee. This default-on approach drove immediate scale.

**Audience Scale**: As of Q4 2025, Prime Video advertising reaches **315 million average monthly viewers** globally ([MediaPost, Nov 2025](https://www.mediapost.com/publications/article/410592/amazon-prime-video-ad-reach-jumps-nearly-60-to-31.html)). This represents 58% growth from approximately 200 million monthly viewers in April 2024—a scale-up from initial launch levels driven by geographic expansion and subscriber adoption.

For competitive context, Netflix's ad-supported tier reached approximately 190 million monthly active users as of late 2025. Prime Video's 315 million monthly viewers represents a ~125 million person advantage over Netflix in ad-accessible streaming audiences.

**Jeremy Helfand, VP of Prime Video Advertising**, described the convergence of capabilities: "We're just beginning to see what's possible when premium entertainment, engaged viewers, and innovative ad-technology converge with relevant and performant advertising at this unprecedented scale." ([MediaPost, Nov 2025](https://www.mediapost.com/publications/article/410592/amazon-prime-video-ad-reach-jumps-nearly-60-to-31.html))

### Revenue Trajectory: Prime Video Ads

MoffettNathanson Research projections for Prime Video advertising revenue:

| Year | Estimated Revenue |
|---|---|
| 2024 | ~$3.3 billion |
| 2025 | ~$5.2 billion |
| 2026 | ~$7.1 billion |

([Variety, Jan 2024](https://variety.com/2024/tv/news/amazon-prime-video-advertising-revenue-forecast-1235888805/))

The 2024–2026 revenue ramp from $3.3B to $7.1B represents approximately 115% cumulative growth over two years. This growth is driven by three factors: geographic expansion (16+ markets), increasing advertiser adoption as measurement tools mature, and the premium CPM commanded by a platform that can connect video ad exposure to purchase outcomes.

### Retail Media: The First-Party Data Moat

Retail media is the fastest-growing sub-segment of digital advertising, projected to represent over 20% of total digital ad spend by 2026. Amazon is the dominant retail media network globally, with structural advantages that are difficult to replicate:

1. **Purchase data depth**: Amazon's product catalog spans hundreds of millions of SKUs across all consumer categories. This granularity allows audience segmentation unavailable on any other platform—advertisers can target consumers who purchased a competitor's product, who are in a replenishment cycle, or who have expressed intent across related categories.

2. **Closed-loop measurement**: Advertisers can measure revenue outcomes (not just clicks or impressions) for campaigns running both on Amazon's properties and through Amazon DSP on third-party sites.

3. **Flywheel reinforcement**: Advertising revenue subsidizes lower marketplace fees or pricing, attracting more sellers and shoppers, which generates more purchase data, which improves ad targeting, which attracts more advertising spend.

### Competitive Positioning vs. Streaming Rivals

The Prime Video advertising scale advantage over Netflix (~125 million additional monthly viewers) is significant but the more defensible advantage is attribution capability. Netflix, Disney+, and other streaming platforms serve advertising on top of entertainment content, but they lack the purchase data that makes Amazon's advertising uniquely performant for consumer goods advertisers.

For a packaged goods company advertising detergent, Amazon can demonstrate that a Prime Video ad campaign drove incremental purchases on Amazon's marketplace. No competing streaming platform offers this closed-loop measurement. This measurement advantage supports premium CPM pricing and drives allocations away from linear TV and other digital video platforms.

### Incremental Margin Profile

Advertising is Amazon's highest-margin business segment. Unlike AWS (which requires significant ongoing infrastructure capex) or fulfillment (which has high variable costs), advertising is monetizing an existing asset—the audience already paying for Prime subscriptions. Prime Video advertising revenue is incremental to subscription revenue without proportional incremental cost. The margin expansion from advertising revenue growth is disproportionate relative to its share of total Amazon revenue.
