## Amazon Advertising Technology: Retail Media Network and Prime Video Ad Expansion

### Retail Media: Structural Advantage

Retail media is the fastest-growing subcategory of digital advertising. Amazon's structural advantage in this market derives from its first-party purchase intent data — advertisers can target consumers at the precise moment of commercial intent rather than relying on inferred interest signals. This is qualitatively superior to search or social advertising targeting.

Amazon's advertising product suite provides full-funnel coverage:
- **Sponsored Products**: Cost-per-click ads appearing within search results and product pages
- **Sponsored Brands**: Brand awareness ads featuring custom creative, appearing prominently in search
- **Amazon DSP (Demand Side Platform)**: Programmatic display and video advertising using Amazon's first-party audience data, deployable both on Amazon properties and across third-party publishers

Amazon Advertising generated **$56.2B in revenue for full-year 2024** (estimated), making it the third-largest digital advertising business globally behind Alphabet and Meta.

### Prime Video Advertising: The 2024–2025 Expansion

The most significant incremental advertising inventory expansion in Amazon's recent history is the introduction of advertising within Prime Video.

**Rollout timeline:**
- **January 2024**: Launched ad-supported tier in the United States, United Kingdom, Germany, and Canada
- **2024–2025**: Expanded to France, Italy, Spain, Mexico, Australia, and additional markets, reaching **16 markets total** by end of 2025

**Scale achieved:**
- As of Q4 2025, Prime Video advertising reaches **315 million average monthly viewers** globally ([MediaPost, Nov 2025](https://www.mediapost.com/publications/article/410592/amazon-prime-video-ad-reach-jumps-nearly-60-to-31.html))
- This represents **58% growth** from the 200 million viewers reported in April 2024
- For context, Netflix's ad-supported tier reaches approximately **190 million monthly active users** — Prime Video's advertising reach exceeds Netflix by approximately 125 million viewers

**Revenue forecasts (MoffettNathanson Research):**
- 2024: ~$3.3B ([Variety, Jan 2024](https://variety.com/2024/tv/news/amazon-prime-video-advertising-revenue-forecast-1235888805/))
- 2025: ~$5.2B
- 2026: ~$7.1B

Prime Video Advertising VP Jeremy Helfand stated: "We're just beginning to see what's possible when premium entertainment, engaged viewers, and innovative ad-technology converge with relevant and performant advertising at this unprecedented scale." ([MediaPost, Nov 2025](https://www.mediapost.com/publications/article/410592/amazon-prime-video-ad-reach-jumps-nearly-60-to-31.html))

### Competitive Positioning in Streaming Advertising

Amazon entered the streaming advertising market from a position of existing scale — Prime Video's subscriber base (~200M+ Prime members globally) meant that the advertising launch immediately reached an audience comparable to or exceeding all streaming competitors except Netflix.

Key differentiation from pure-play streaming advertisers:
1. **First-party purchase data**: Amazon can close the loop between ad exposure and actual purchase on Amazon.com, providing measurable attribution that linear TV and most streaming competitors cannot match
2. **Prime ecosystem integration**: Advertising buys can be directly linked to sponsored product campaigns, enabling unified omnichannel attribution
3. **DSP reach extension**: Amazon DSP allows the same audience data to be deployed across third-party inventory, extending reach beyond Amazon-owned properties

### Rufus: AI Extending Advertising Value at the Consumer Layer

Amazon's AI shopping assistant Rufus — launched in 2024 — was tracking "to deliver over $10 billion in incremental annualized sales" as of Q3 2025 ([Q3 2025 Earnings Call](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/31/amazon-amzn-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)). Rufus represents the application of AI to the discovery and conversion funnel, complementing the advertising technology stack by improving the efficiency of advertiser spend through better consumer-product matching.
