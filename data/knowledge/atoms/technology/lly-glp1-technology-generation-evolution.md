## GLP-1 Drug Technology: Three Generations

The evolution of GLP-1 (glucagon-like peptide-1) receptor agonist technology can be understood through three distinct generations, each delivering significant improvements in clinical efficacy and reducing patient burden.

### Generation Overview

| Generation | Representative Drugs | Mechanism | Dosing Frequency | Avg. Weight Loss | Key Breakthrough |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1st Gen** (2005–2016) | Byetta, Victoza, Trulicity | Single GLP-1 receptor | Daily 1–2x / Weekly 1x | 5–8% | Commercial validation of GLP-1 |
| **2nd Gen** (2017–2023) | Ozempic/Wegovy (Novo); **Mounjaro/Zepbound (Lilly)** | Single GLP-1 (Novo); **Dual GIP+GLP-1 (Lilly)** | Weekly injection | 15–20% | Doubled clinical weight loss; obesity indication approved |
| **3rd Gen** (2025+) | Oral Wegovy (approved); Orforglipron (Lilly); Retatrutide (Lilly); MariTide (Amgen) | Oral GLP-1; Triple receptor (GIP+GLP-1+GCG); Antibody-peptide conjugate | Daily oral / Monthly injection | 12–24% | **Injection-to-oral transition**; more potent multi-targeting; longer-acting (monthly injection) |

*Sources: [Redbock, 2025](https://redbock.com/news/glp-1-medications-a-market-on-the-rise/), [IQVIA, 2026-01](https://www.iqvia.com/locations/emea/blogs/2026/01/outlook-for-obesity-in-2026)*

### Second Generation: Tirzepatide's Dual-Receptor Breakthrough

Lilly's tirzepatide (Mounjaro/Zepbound) represents the defining breakthrough of the second generation. Its dual GIP+GLP-1 receptor mechanism delivered an average weight loss of **"20% of body weight"** ([Fortune, 2024-11](https://fortune.com/2024/11/13/eli-lilly-ceo-dave-ricks/)), with patients reducing caloric intake by approximately 800 kcal/day. CEO David Ricks emphasized the superior tolerability: **"people lose the weight and they don't feel miserable"** ([Cheeky Pint/All-In, 2024](https://cheekypint.substack.com/p/dave-ricks-ceo-of-eli-lilly-on-glp)). This represents a fundamental efficacy gap versus the single-receptor semaglutide.

### Third Generation: Lilly's Three Parallel Platforms

Lilly has simultaneously advanced three distinct third-generation technology platforms:

**1. Orforglipron (Oral Small-Molecule GLP-1)**
- Phase 3 trials completed (ATTAIN-1/2)
- 36mg dose: ~11.2% weight loss at 72 weeks; ~1.48% HbA1c reduction at 40 weeks
- FDA approval anticipated 2026
- Sources: [NEJM, 2025](https://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMoa2511774); [Lancet, 2025](https://www.thelancet.com/journals/lancet/article/PIIS0140-6736(25)02165-8/abstract)
- Weight loss lower than injectable tirzepatide (~11% vs ~20%), but Ricks positions it against semaglutide (Ozempic), targeting the large population with injection phobia or preference for oral administration

**2. Retatrutide (Triple Receptor: GIP+GLP-1+Glucagon)**
- Phase 2 data: weight loss up to **24%** — the highest of any GLP-1-class drug ([Redbock, 2025](https://redbock.com/news/glp-1-medications-a-market-on-the-rise/))
- Phase 3 ongoing; estimated launch 2027–2028
- Glucagon receptor addition enhances energy expenditure; described as a candidate for "ultimate obesity drug"

**3. $55B Manufacturing Capacity Expansion**
- Confirmed in Q4 2025 earnings call — the largest capacity expansion in pharmaceutical history
- 2025 sellable GLP-1 doses ~1.8x higher than 2024; supply bottleneck progressively resolving
