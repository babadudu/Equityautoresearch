## GLP-1 Demand Structural Shifts: The Indication Stacking Strategy

### Cognitive Revolution: Obesity as Chronic Disease

The most important structural shift on the demand side is the reclassification of obesity from a "lifestyle problem" to a "chronic metabolic disease." This reframing is the primary driver of GLP-1 long-term adoption and insurance coverage expansion.

Ricks has consistently emphasized this cognitive shift: **"The drugs work and they add to the diet exercise regime for people who are already overweight"** ([Fortune, 2024-11](https://fortune.com/2024/11/13/eli-lilly-ceo-dave-ricks/)). Italy codified obesity as a chronic disease in law in 2025 ([IQVIA, 2026-01](https://www.iqvia.com/locations/emea/blogs/2026/01/outlook-for-obesity-in-2026)), a legal precedent that may catalyze global insurance coverage shifts.

**S-curve comparison to statins:** GLP-1 technology diffusion mirrors the historical trajectory of statins. When Mevacor launched in 1987, high cholesterol was viewed as a lifestyle issue, not a disease requiring medication. By 2006, Lipitor was the world's best-selling drug (peak $12.9B), with over 200 million statin users. GLP-1 is repeating this path — with a larger TAM (1 billion obese globally vs. ~300–400 million hypercholesterolemia patients at the statin peak) and faster technology iteration (three generations in 20 years vs. 30 years for statins). IQVIA designated 2025 as the completion point for GLP-1's transition from "niche to mainstream" ([IQVIA, 2026-01](https://www.iqvia.com/locations/emea/blogs/2026/01/outlook-for-obesity-in-2026)).

### Tirzepatide: Diabetes Prevention Evidence

In August 2024, a three-year SURMOUNT-1 extension study showed tirzepatide reduced type 2 diabetes incidence risk by **94%**. Ricks described this as: **"This is a profound result"** ([CNBC, 2024-08-20](https://www.cnbc.com/video/2024/08/20/eli-lilly-ceo-david-ricks-on-3-year-obesity-drug-study-this-is-a-profound-result.html)). This repositions GLP-1 from a "weight loss drug" to a "chronic disease prevention drug," expanding both the prescriber base and payer justification.

### Alzheimer's: The Second Growth Curve

Lilly's Kisunla (donanemab) targets Alzheimer's amyloid plaques, achieving **"more than 80% reduction in [amyloid] like a year. Even to undetectable levels"** ([T. Rowe Price, 2025-11](https://www.troweprice.com/en/us/insights/the-long-view-eli-lilly)).

Ricks defines the next decade as a "decade of the brain," stating: **"brain health is probably the biggest category"** ([Fortune, 2024-11](https://fortune.com/2024/11/13/eli-lilly-ceo-dave-ricks/)). The Alzheimer's opportunity metrics:
- 55 million Alzheimer's patients globally
- Current penetration rate <1%
- Potential market: $50B+

### Indication Expansion Ripple Effects

Clinical evidence for GLP-1 is expanding beyond obesity into:
- Cardiovascular risk reduction
- NASH/MASH (non-alcoholic steatohepatitis)
- Sleep apnea
- Addictive behaviors

Each new indication significantly expands TAM and attracts new payers.

### Addiction Indications: The Next TAM Expansion

Perhaps the most strategically significant demand expansion is GLP-1's emerging role in addiction treatment. In December 2024, Ricks publicly announced at the Economic Club: **"These medicines can be useful for other things we don't think about connected to weight"**, describing GLP-1 drugs as **"anti-hedonics"** — **"They're reducing that desire cycle. So next year, you'll see Lilly start large studies in alcohol abuse and nicotine use, even in drug abuse"** ([Economic Club DC, 2024-12-10](https://www.economicclub.org/events/david-ricks); [IBJ, 2024-12](https://www.ibj.com/articles/lilly-approves-15-billion-in-share-buybacks-raises-dividend)).

If addiction indications achieve clinical validation, GLP-1 TAM expands dramatically:
- 29 million Americans with alcohol use disorder
- 31 million Americans using tobacco products
- Multi-hundred-million globally with substance use disorders

### The Indication Stacking Analogy: Keytruda Precedent

This "indication stacking" strategy parallels Keytruda's expansion in oncology — from one cancer type progressively to 30+ indications, ultimately becoming the world's best-selling drug. GLP-1 drugs exhibit the same structural pattern: a platform mechanism that validates across multiple disease categories, each validation expanding TAM, prescriber confidence, and payer coverage.

The GLP-1 demand expansion trajectory:
1. **Type 2 diabetes** (initial approval)
2. **Obesity** (major TAM expansion, ~2023)
3. **Cardiovascular risk reduction** (SURMOUNT/SELECT trials)
4. **Diabetes prevention** (94% risk reduction, 2024)
5. **NASH/MASH, sleep apnea** (ongoing trials)
6. **Addiction behaviors** (Lilly initiating large studies, 2025)
7. **Potential neurological applications** (speculative, early signals)

Each step up this ladder represents not just incremental revenue but potentially step-change TAM expansion and reclassification of the drug category itself.
