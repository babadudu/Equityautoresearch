## Eli Lilly Geographic Revenue Breakdown FY2023–FY2025

Eli Lilly operates as a single business segment (human pharmaceuticals), selling products in approximately 95 countries. The United States consistently represents the core market (~2/3 of revenue), but international markets are accelerating as GLP-1 products receive approvals and manufacturing capacity is released.

### Geographic Strategy by Region

**United States (66.7% of 2025 revenue):** The primary commercial market for Mounjaro/Zepbound. Starting in 2026, Medicare Part D coverage of obesity medications will add 20–30 million potential patients. The LillyDirect direct-to-consumer channel accelerates access for out-of-pocket patients. US share rose from 63.9% in 2023 to 67.4% in 2024, reflecting Mounjaro/Zepbound's early ramp concentrated in the US market, before moderating slightly to 66.7% in 2025 as international markets began accelerating.

**Europe (17.7% of 2025 revenue):** The fastest-growing region in 2025 (+67%), driven by Mounjaro's diabetes indication rollout in Germany, UK, and France, plus country-by-country Zepbound obesity approvals. Italy's 2025 legislation classifying obesity as a chronic disease may accelerate insurance coverage expansion across Europe ([IQVIA, 2026-01](https://www.iqvia.com/locations/emea/blogs/2026/01/outlook-for-obesity-in-2026)). Europe's growth trajectory signals a structural re-rating as reimbursement frameworks adapt to GLP-1 therapies.

**Japan (3.3% of 2025 revenue):** Zepbound has received PMDA approval for chronic obesity ([IQVIA, 2026-01](https://www.iqvia.com/locations/emea/blogs/2026/01/outlook-for-obesity-in-2026)), but growth remains moderate (+18%), partly due to biennial drug price adjustment (yakka revision) pressure. Japan defines obesity at BMI ≥25, which means a proportionally larger share of the population qualifies as a target patient population relative to Western markets.

**China (3.0% of 2025 revenue):** Competitive dynamics are intensifying. Innovent Biologics' mazdutide (GLP-1/glucagon dual agonist) has been accepted for NMPA review, and semaglutide's core patent is set to expire in China in 2026 ([IQVIA, 2026-01](https://www.iqvia.com/locations/emea/blogs/2026/01/outlook-for-obesity-in-2026)). CEO David Ricks has taken a hard line on Chinese-sourced counterfeit GLP-1 products, stating: "Most of them are sourced out of Chinese labs that aren't even approved in China" ([Washington Reporter, 2025-03](https://washingtonreporter.news/p/interview-eli-lilly-ceo-david-ricks)). China remains a subscale market relative to Lilly's global footprint.

**Other International (9.3% of 2025 revenue):** Emerging markets including the Middle East, Latin America, and Southeast Asia are growing strongly (+42%), primarily driven by GLP-1 first launches. The global unserved patient population remains vast, and Ricks has identified emerging markets as the largest long-term incremental opportunity.

### Geographic Mix Trend Interpretation

The US share rose from 63.9% (2023) → 67.4% (2024), reflecting Mounjaro/Zepbound's US-first commercial ramp. The slight decline to 66.7% in 2025 marks the beginning of international rebalancing as European and other international markets accelerate. This trend is expected to continue through 2026–2028 as more international markets receive approvals and Medicare volume-price trade-offs take effect domestically.

The tirzepatide franchise (Mounjaro + Zepbound combined) reached $36.5B in 2025, representing 56% of total company revenue—making it the absolute core of Lilly's business. This concentration creates both opportunity (continued GLP-1 penetration globally) and risk (single-molecule dependence, competitive threats from next-generation agents including oral formulations and once-monthly injectables). International markets grew faster than the US in Q4 2025 (+43% internationally), confirming the geographic rebalancing trend.
