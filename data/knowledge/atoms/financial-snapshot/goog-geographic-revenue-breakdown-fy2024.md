## Alphabet (GOOG) Geographic and Segment Revenue Breakdown — FY2024

### Geographic Revenue (FY2024)

Alphabet reported total revenue of approximately $350.0 billion in FY2024, a +14% year-over-year increase. Revenue is reported across four geographic segments:

| Region | Revenue (USD billions) | Share | YoY Growth | Source |
|--------|----------------------|-------|------------|--------|
| United States | ~$171.5B | 49% | +18% | [Alphabet 10-K 2024](https://s206.q4cdn.com/479360582/files/doc_financials/2024/q4/goog-10-k-2024.pdf) |
| EMEA | ~$101.5B | 29% | +12% | [Alphabet 10-K 2024](https://s206.q4cdn.com/479360582/files/doc_financials/2024/q4/goog-10-k-2024.pdf) |
| APAC | ~$56.0B | 16% | +10% | [Alphabet 10-K 2024](https://s206.q4cdn.com/479360582/files/doc_financials/2024/q4/goog-10-k-2024.pdf) |
| Other Americas | ~$21.0B | 6% | +8% | [Alphabet 10-K 2024](https://s206.q4cdn.com/479360582/files/doc_financials/2024/q4/goog-10-k-2024.pdf) |
| **Total** | **$350.0B** | **100%** | **+14%** | |

The United States share increased from 47% in FY2023 to 49% in FY2024, reflecting accelerated AI-driven domestic advertising and cloud demand. The U.S. grew fastest at +18%, outpacing EMEA (+12%), APAC (+10%), and Other Americas (+8%).

Alphabet reports revenue across three main segments: Google Services, Google Cloud, and Other Bets.

| Other Bets | ~$1.9B | <1% | — | [Alphabet Q4 2024 Press Release](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1652044/000165204425000010/googexhibit991q42024.htm) |
| **Total** | **$350.0B** | **100%** | **+14%** | |

**Key observations:**
- Google Search & other remains the dominant revenue driver at 58% of total, growing a healthy +13% YoY.
- YouTube ads at ~$36.5B (+14%) continues to outpace the broader advertising market.
- Google Network (-3%) is the only declining segment, reflecting secular headwinds in third-party display/programmatic advertising.
- Subscriptions, Platforms & Devices at +16% is the fastest-growing component within Google Services, driven by YouTube Premium, Google One, and hardware.
- Google Cloud at $43.2B (+31%) is the standout growth engine, now representing 12% of total revenue.

### Segment Operating Income (FY2024)

| Segment | Operating Income (USD billions) | Operating Margin | YoY Change |
|---------|--------------------------------|-----------------|------------|
| Google Services | $121.3B | 39.8% | +26% |
| Google Cloud | $6.1B | 14.1% | +259% (from $1.7B) |
| Other Bets | ~-$4.6B | — | Ongoing losses |
| Corporate | ~-$10.4B | — | — |
| **Total** | **$112.4B** | **32.1%** | **+33%** |

**Google Cloud profitability inflection:** The Cloud segment's profit trajectory represents one of the most significant shifts in Alphabet's financial profile:
- FY2022: Operating loss of ~$3.0B
- FY2023: Operating income of $1.7B (first meaningful profit)
- FY2024: Operating income of $6.1B (14.1% margin)

This +259% YoY profit growth at Google Cloud — with margins expanding from ~5% to 14.1% — validates the segment's scale economics and positions it as a meaningful contributor to consolidated operating income alongside the dominant Google Services segment.

Google Services maintained a robust 39.8% operating margin, up from ~36% in FY2023, reflecting operating leverage as revenue grew faster than cost base.

**Total consolidated operating margin** expanded to 32.1% in FY2024, up from ~27% in FY2023, driven by both segment margin expansion and the rapid growth of high-margin Cloud profits.

---

### Summary

FY2024 marks a year of broad-based strength for Alphabet: the U.S. accelerated its share of geographic revenue to 49%, Google Cloud crossed $43B in revenue with a 14% operating margin (up from near-zero two years prior), and total consolidated operating income reached $112.4B (+33% YoY). The only structural headwind is Google Network (-3%), which represents an ongoing secular shift away from third-party programmatic advertising toward direct formats (Search, YouTube, Subscriptions).
