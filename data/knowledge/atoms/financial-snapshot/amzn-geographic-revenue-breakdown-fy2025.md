## Amazon Geographic & Segment Revenue Breakdown — FY2025

### Operating Segment Revenue

Amazon reports revenue across three operating segments. In FY2025, total consolidated revenue reached **$716.9B**, up **+12% YoY** from $638.0B in FY2024. Source: [AMZN 2025 10-K](https://www.sec.gov/cgi-bin/browse-edgar?action=getcompany&CID=1018724&type=10-K)

| Segment | FY2025 Revenue | Mix | FY2024 Revenue | Mix | YoY |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| North America | $426.3B | 59.5% | $387.7B | 60.8% | +10% |
| International | $161.9B | 22.6% | $143.5B | 22.5% | +13% |
| AWS | $128.7B | 18.0% | $107.6B | 16.9% | +20% |
| **Total** | **$716.9B** | **100%** | **$638.0B** | **100%** | **+12%** |

AWS was the fastest-growing segment at +20% YoY, followed by International at +13% and North America at +10%. AWS's revenue share expanded from 16.9% to 18.0% over the year.

### Operating Income by Segment

Source: [AMZN Q4 2025 Press Release](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1018724/000101872426000002/amzn-20251231xex991.htm)

| Segment | FY2025 OI | FY2024 OI | YoY | OI Margin FY2025 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| North America | $29.6B | $25.0B | +18% | 6.9% |
| International | $4.7B | $3.8B | +24% | 2.9% |
| AWS | $45.6B | $39.8B | +15% | 35.4% |
| **Total** | **$79.9B** | **$68.6B** | **+16%** | **11.1%** |

Key profit structure observation: **AWS contributes 57% of total operating income** ($45.6B / $79.9B) while representing only 18% of revenue — a dramatic profit concentration. AWS's 35.4% operating margin compares to 6.9% for North America and 2.9% for International.

International operating income grew the fastest at +24% YoY, suggesting improving unit economics outside North America as the segment scales.

### International Market Strategy Observations

**US dominance persists**: The United States accounts for approximately 69% of Amazon's total revenue, reflecting the company's still-concentrated North American base.

**International growth acceleration**: The International segment grew +13% YoY versus +10% for North America, indicating the international expansion is gaining momentum.

**European dual engines**: Germany ($40.9B) and the UK ($37.9B) together account for roughly half of International segment revenues, serving as Amazon's twin pillars in Europe.

**India as a long-term growth bet**: Despite contributing only ~0.5% of total revenue, India is considered Amazon's next billion-user market opportunity. Amazon has cumulatively invested over **$6.5 billion** in India since 2014. The low current revenue base reflects early-stage monetization rather than lack of strategic priority.

**Japan maturity**: Japan ($27.4B) is Amazon's most established Asia-Pacific market, characterized by high e-commerce penetration and strong Prime membership retention.

### Profit Structure Summary

AWS is the profit engine of Amazon's business model:
- **18% of revenue → 57% of operating income**
- 35.4% operating margin vs. 11.1% consolidated margin

Advertising Services, while not separately reporting operating income, is estimated by market analysts to carry **50–60% operating margins**, suggesting it may be Amazon's highest-margin business by percentage. If accurate, Advertising ($68.6B revenue) could contribute operating income comparable to or exceeding North America's retail operations on a margin-adjusted basis.
