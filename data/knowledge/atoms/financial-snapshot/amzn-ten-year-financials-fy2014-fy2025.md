## Amazon 10-Year Financial History & Inflection Points (FY2014–FY2025)

Amazon's twelve-year financial trajectory is a textbook case of transitioning from "loss-driven growth" to "profit explosion." Revenue compounded at 21% annually from $89B in 2014 to $717B in 2025, but the evolution of net income is even more dramatic — from a net loss of $241M in 2014 to net income of $77.7B in 2025.

### Five Major Inflection Points

#### 1. 2015 — First Large-Scale Profitability

AWS was disclosed separately for the first time, reporting full-year revenue of $7.9B and operating income of $1.86B. The market saw Amazon's "hidden profit engine" for the first time, and the stock rose 118% for the year. Bezos wrote in his letter to shareholders: "AWS is a $5 billion business and still growing fast. Born in 2006, it's now big enough to stand on its own." ([2015 Letter to Shareholders](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1018724/000119312516530910/d168744dex991.htm))

The gross margin expansion from 29.5% (2014) to 33.0% (2015) reflected the shift in revenue mix toward higher-margin cloud services. Operating income jumped from $0.18B to $2.23B — a 12x increase in a single year.

#### 2. 2018 — AWS + Advertising Profit Explosion

Operating income surged from $4.1B to $12.4B (+202%), driven primarily by AWS scale economics and rapid growth in the advertising business. Advertising was first disclosed as "Other Revenue" exceeding $10B. This was the year investors recognized Amazon's three-engine model: retail (scale/logistics), AWS (cloud margin), and advertising (high-margin attach).

Gross margins crossed 40% for the first time (40.2%), reflecting the growing contribution of services revenue relative to product revenue.

#### 3. 2020 — COVID E-Commerce Boom

Revenue grew 38% YoY to $386B — Amazon's largest absolute revenue increase in history. Amazon hired over 250,000 warehouse workers within weeks as the logistics network approached capacity limits. Bezos wrote in the 2020 Letter to Shareholders: "If you want to be successful in business, you have to create more than you consume. Your goal should be to create value for everyone you interact with." ([2020 Letter to Shareholders](https://www.aboutamazon.com/news/company-news/2020-letter-to-shareholders))

Despite the revenue surge, gross margins dipped slightly to 39.6% (from 42.0% in 2021 direction), reflecting the massive labor and logistics costs of scaling fulfillment capacity during the pandemic.

#### 4. 2022 — Rivian Write-Down + Logistics Overcapacity

Net income turned negative at -$2.7B. Primary causes: (1) a $12.7B write-down on Rivian investment (Amazon had invested in the EV truck startup as part of its delivery fleet strategy); (2) post-pandemic logistics overcapacity as Amazon had nearly doubled its fulfillment network in 2020–2021 and needed to absorb the excess capacity.

This was the most difficult year for Andy Jassy following his succession of Bezos as CEO in July 2021. Operating income collapsed from $24.9B (2021) to $12.3B (2022) — a 51% decline — despite revenue growing 9% to $514B. Operating margin fell to 2.4%, a decade low.

The $12.7B Rivian write-down was a non-cash mark-to-market loss reflecting the decline in Rivian's stock price from its November 2021 IPO peak.

#### 5. 2024–2025 — Record Margin Highs

Operating margin rebounded from 2.4% (2022) to 11.1% (2025). Jassy's cost-control initiatives proved effective:
- Regionalization of logistics network (reducing inter-regional shipments)
- Layoffs of 27,000 employees (early 2023)
- Organizational flattening (reducing management layers)
- AWS AI demand drove cloud revenue acceleration to 20% YoY growth

Gross margins crossed 49% in 2025, reflecting continued mix shift toward advertising and AWS. Net income of $77.7B in 2025 represents a 326x multiple over the $0.24B net loss in 2014 — a transformation achieved in just eleven years.

### Key Margin Trend Analysis

**Gross Margin Expansion (29.5% → 49.3%, 2014–2025):** The nearly 20 percentage point improvement reflects Amazon's successful shift from a low-margin product retailer to a high-margin services company. AWS (estimated ~60–65% gross margin) and Advertising (estimated ~75%+ gross margin) now generate a disproportionate share of total profit despite being smaller revenue contributors than retail.

**Operating Leverage:** The operating income CAGR (2014–2025) substantially exceeded revenue CAGR. Revenue grew 8x; operating income grew from near-zero to $79.9B. This is the compound effect of fixed-cost leverage in both the logistics network (once built, incremental shipments are near-zero marginal cost) and AWS (data center depreciation is largely fixed).

**EPS Volatility:** The wide EPS swings (from -$5.36 in 2022 to $7.17 in 2025, post-split adjusted) illustrate how investment impairments (Rivian), one-time charges, and operating leverage interact. The 2022 trough was largely non-cash; underlying cash generation remained positive throughout.
