## eBay Geographic Revenue Breakdown and GMV Trends FY2024–FY2025

### Overview

eBay operates a marketplace platform spanning 190 markets, with revenue split roughly 50/50 between the United States and international markets. As of FY2024, the US accounted for 50.9% of total revenue, with the UK, China, and Germany as the three largest international contributors. CEO Jamie Iannone described the scale in eBay's 2021 annual report as "over 147 million active buyers and 17 million sellers across 190 markets" ([LinkedIn, 2021](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/empowering-economic-opportunity-all-jamie-iannone)). The high degree of cross-border coverage creates significant inter-regional traffic flows on the platform.

### FY2025 Geographic GMV Performance

**US Acceleration:** The US exhibited strong momentum throughout FY2025. US GMV growth accelerated to approximately 13% in Q3 2025 ([eBay Q3 2025 Earnings Call, 2025-10-29](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/29/ebay-ebay-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)) and further accelerated to approximately **19%** in Q4 2025 ([eBay Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-18](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/18/ebay-ebay-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)).

**International Divergence:** International markets told a materially different story. Q3 2025 international GMV grew approximately 4% on an FX-neutral basis, but Q4 2025 international GMV declined approximately 1% on an organic FX-neutral basis ([eBay Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-18](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/18/ebay-ebay-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)).

CEO Iannone acknowledged European weakness on the Q4 2025 earnings call: "Europe has been more challenged as consumer confidence remains low and retail sales trends are subdued there" ([eBay Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-18](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/18/ebay-ebay-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)).

CFO Steve Alford provided additional specificity, identifying dual headwinds: "Challenging macroeconomic conditions in the UK and Germany and a deceleration in our cross-border volume growth due to U.S. trade policies" ([eBay Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-18](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/18/ebay-ebay-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)).

### China Revenue: Cross-Border Export Model

It is important to note that eBay's China revenue classification reflects an export-driven cross-border model, not domestic Chinese e-commerce activity. Chinese-origin sellers listing goods for purchase by US and European buyers generate this revenue.

VP John Egbert disclosed in the Q4 2024 earnings call that goods sourced from China represent approximately **5% of total GMV**, and of that volume, **75% had already been "forward deployed" to US warehouses** prior to sale — meaning the de minimis tariff exposure for these goods was lower than the headline China GMV figure might suggest ([Digital Commerce 360, 2025-03-10](https://www.digitalcommerce360.com/2025/03/10/ebay-sales-revenue-gmv-q4-2024/)).

CEO Iannone further clarified in the Q3 2025 earnings call that cross-border headwinds from de minimis removal extended beyond China, citing "sellers in Japan and Canada being amongst the most impacted" ([eBay Q3 2025 Earnings Call, 2025-10-29](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/29/ebay-ebay-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)).

### Motors: Global GMV Contribution

eBay's Motors category (auto parts and vehicles) carries significant global scale. Iannone noted in the annual report: "40 million buyers with over $10 billion in GMV annually" ([LinkedIn, 2021](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/empowering-economic-opportunity-all-jamie-iannone)). The 2024 acquisition of Caramel extended eBay's Motors offering into full vehicle transactions.

### Key Takeaways

1. **US/international split is approximately 50/50** at the revenue level, but FY2025 GMV trends show a widening divergence favoring the US (+19% GMV in Q4 2025 vs. -1% international organic FX-neutral).
2. **UK and Germany** are the two largest individual international revenue contributors and are the primary sources of international pressure in 2025, driven by weak consumer confidence and trade policy headwinds.
3. **China revenue is a cross-border export line**, not a measure of eBay's domestic Chinese market presence; 75% of China-sourced goods were already US-warehoused as of Q4 2024.
4. **De minimis policy changes** create cross-border headwinds affecting sellers in China, Japan, and Canada — not only Chinese sellers.
5. **Motors GMV** exceeds $10B annually with 40M buyers, representing a large and globally distributed business within eBay's category mix.
