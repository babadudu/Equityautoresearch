## Eli Lilly 10-Year Financial History FY2015–FY2025

### Overview

Eli Lilly's decade-long financial trajectory is one of the most dramatic transformations in large-cap pharma history. From a mid-sized specialty pharma navigating patent cliffs in 2015, LLY emerged by 2025 as the dominant global GLP-1 franchise, posting revenue of $65.18B and an operating margin approaching 40%. The central driver: tirzepatide (Mounjaro for diabetes, Zepbound for obesity), which became the fastest-ramping drug launch in pharmaceutical history.

### Key Inflection Points

**1. 2017: David Ricks Becomes CEO**

Ricks took the helm in January 2017 and initiated a "science-first" strategic transformation. He streamlined the executive team, eliminated non-core assets, and concentrated R&D investment on high-conviction programs — most critically, the GIP/GLP-1 dual agonist that would become tirzepatide. The 2017 net loss (-$0.19 EPS) was a one-time distortion from U.S. tax reform, not a reflection of underlying business deterioration; operating income that year still grew to $4.04B.

**2. 2016/2021: Tirzepatide Clinical Breakthroughs**

Early phase data on tirzepatide began generating internal confidence around 2016. The watershed moment came in 2021, when Phase 3 SURPASS trial results showed tirzepatide delivered weight loss of up to 22.5% — outcomes that exceeded semaglutide benchmarks and reshaped analyst expectations for the entire obesity pharmacology field. The stock re-rated sharply in 2021 as the market began pricing in blockbuster potential.

**3. 2022: Mounjaro FDA Approval**

Monunjaro (tirzepatide for Type 2 diabetes) received FDA approval in May 2022, initiating the commercial phase of LLY's GLP-1 transformation. The launch trajectory was steep: physicians and patients who had seen SURPASS data were primed. Revenue from Mounjaro contributed significantly to 2022 results even on a partial-year basis.

**4. 2023–2024: Zepbound Launch + GLP-1 Supercycle**

Zepbound (tirzepatide for obesity/weight management) received FDA approval in November 2023 and launched in December 2023. The combination of Mounjaro + Zepbound drove a demand surge that outpaced manufacturing capacity. In 2024, the two products combined for approximately $16.5B in revenue, propelling total company revenue to $45.04B (+32% YoY) and operating income to $12.90B. The gross margin expanded to 81.3%, reflecting the premium pricing of the tirzepatide franchise.

**5. 2025: Global GLP-1 Dominance Confirmed**

By 2025, the tirzepatide franchise (Mounjaro + Zepbound) reached approximately $36.5B in combined revenue, representing a market share of ~57% in the GLP-1 category. Total company revenue grew 45% YoY to $65.18B. Operating income of $26.30B implied an operating margin of ~40.4% — a level more typical of software platforms than pharmaceutical manufacturers. Gross margin reached 83.0%, the highest in LLY's modern history.

### Margin Expansion Analysis

**Gross Margin Trajectory (2015–2025):**
- 2015: 73.3%
- 2020: 78.1% (+480 bps over 5 years)
- 2025: 83.0% (+490 bps over the next 5 years)

The steady improvement reflects: (1) mix shift toward high-ASP GLP-1 products, (2) manufacturing scale benefits as tirzepatide volumes grew, and (3) reduced drag from older lower-margin legacy brands.

**Operating Margin Trajectory:**
- 2015: ~17.2% ($3.43B / $19.96B)
- 2020: ~25.3% ($6.21B / $24.54B)
- 2024: ~28.6% ($12.90B / $45.04B)
- 2025: ~40.4% ($26.30B / $65.18B)

The acceleration in operating leverage from 2024 to 2025 is notable: revenue grew $20.1B while operating income grew $13.4B, implying an incremental operating margin of ~67% on the marginal revenue dollar. This suggests the fixed cost base (R&D, SG&A, manufacturing overhead) was largely set, and tirzepatide volume growth flowed disproportionately to the bottom line.

**EPS Compound Growth:**
- From $2.26 (2015) to $22.95 (2025)
- 10-year CAGR: approximately 26% on diluted EPS
- Excluding the 2017 tax anomaly and 2019 divestiture gain, the core EPS CAGR is ~24%

The 2022–2025 revenue CAGR of ~32% is exceptional for a company that was already generating $28B+ in revenue at the start of that period. It places LLY in rare company among large-cap biopharma transformations.

### Contextual Notes for Investors

- **2019 Net Income Anomaly**: The $8.32B net income in 2019 (vs. $5.20B operating income) reflects significant asset divestiture gains (Elanco Animal Health spinoff proceeds), not recurring earnings power. Core EPS was closer to $5.50–$6.00.
- **2017 EPS Anomaly**: The -$0.19 EPS reflects a one-time deferred tax charge from the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act, not operational deterioration. Operating income grew normally that year.
- **Supply Constraints (2023–2024)**: Despite the revenue figures, LLY was supply-constrained on tirzepatide through much of 2023–2024. Manufacturing expansion (Research Triangle Park, Lebanon IN, Alzey Germany) was a multi-billion dollar capital program that will continue to shape capex through 2026.
- **Competitive Context**: Novo Nordisk (NVO) remained the other dominant GLP-1 player throughout this period with semaglutide (Ozempic/Wegovy). LLY's 57% market share figure for 2025 implies NVO's share contracted as tirzepatide's clinical superiority drove prescriber switching.
