TSMC's financial trajectory is currently defined by the massive AI/HPC (High-Performance Computing) super-cycle, which has pushed the company to historic highs in revenue and capital efficiency.

### 2026 Financial Projections (Base Case)
- **Revenue**: $150 Billion (NT$4,950B), implying ~30% YoY growth following a $115B result in 2025. This is driven by N2 ramp-up and CoWoS capacity doubling.
- **Gross Margin**: Expected at 61% (Base) to 65% (Bull). While overseas fab startups dilute margins by 2-4 percentage points, this is offset by the mix shift toward highly profitable 3nm/2nm nodes.
- **CapEx**: Record-breaking $52-$56 billion budget for 2026 to support the 2-3 year design lead times required by AI customers (Source: https://investor.tsmc.com/english/quarterly-results/2025/q4).
- **Net Margin**: 46%, yielding NT$439 EPS.

### Revenue Segmentation (2024-2026E)
- **HPC**: Now the primary driver, growing from 51% of 2024 revenue to an estimated 58% by 2026. AI accelerators alone are seeing 54-56% CAGR (2024-2029).
- **Smartphone**: Stabilizing at 24-30% of revenue, anchored by Apple’s exclusive relationship.
- **Geographic Mix**: North America (primarily US) accounted for 70% of 2024 revenue, while China fell to 9% due to export controls.

### Valuation and DCF Model
Using a WACC of 9% (inclusive of a geopolitical risk premium) and a terminal growth rate of 2.5%, a 5-year explicit DCF model yields a fair value range of **NT$6,550 – NT$7,380**. However, this DCF result is heavily compressed by the current $50B+ annual CapEx cycle. Multiples-based valuation provides a different picture:
- **P/E Approach**: Trading at ~24.5x Forward P/E (as of March 2026), TSMC is at a discount to semiconductor peers (industry median ~29x). Applying a 25x target to 2026E EPS yields a target price of **NT$10,975**.
- **EV/EBITDA**: Currently ~20x, compared to a 5-year mean of 13.2x, reflecting the structural shift in ROIC (which hit a record 41.5% in 2025).
- **PEG Ratio**: At ~1.0x, TSMC trades at a 48% discount to the Information Technology sector average (~1.9x), a "Taiwan Discount" that factors in the tail risk of a Taiwan Strait conflict (Source: https://www.ainvest.com/news/taiwan-semiconductor-manufacturing-tsm-geopolitical-undercurrents-driving-discount-2505/).
