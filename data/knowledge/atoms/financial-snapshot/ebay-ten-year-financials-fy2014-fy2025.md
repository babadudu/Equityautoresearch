## eBay Ten-Year Financial History FY2014–FY2025

### Overview: Three Structural Eras

eBay's financial history divides into three distinct eras defined by major corporate restructuring events:

1. **PayPal Symbiosis Era (–2015):** Total revenues exceeded $15B when PayPal was included. The combined entity masked the standalone marketplace's economics.
2. **Multi-Divestiture Era (2015–2020):** PayPal was spun off in July 2015; StubHub was sold to viagogo for $4.05B in 2019; eBay Classifieds Group was sold to Adevinta for $9.2B in 2021. Revenues normalized to the $8–10B range reflecting the pure marketplace business.
3. **Iannone Transformation Era (2020–present):** CEO Jamie Iannone's "tech-led reimagination" focused on core marketplace, with take rate expansion from ~12% to 14.0% driving revenue growth independent of GMV acceleration.

### Key Inflection Points

#### 1. 1995–1998 — Founding and IPO
Pierre Omidyar launched eBay as a side experiment. By mid-1997, the platform had 150,000 users and 794,000 daily auction listings. In 1997, Omidyar raised $4.5M from Benchmark Capital (selling 22% equity). The September 1998 IPO priced at $18/share; within four months the stock reached $300 ([Entrepreneur](https://www.entrepreneur.com/growing-a-business/pierre-omidyar/197554)). Omidyar on the company's accidental scale: "I didn't set out to create a huge business on eBay. When it happened, I just took advantage of it" ([Entrepreneur](https://www.entrepreneur.com/growing-a-business/pierre-omidyar/197554)). During the 1999 system outage crisis, Omidyar maintained conviction: "I've always had... unshakable faith that it's going to endure" ([Academy of Achievement](https://achievement.org/achiever/pierre-omidyar/)).

#### 2. 2002 — PayPal Acquisition
eBay acquired PayPal for $1.5B, solving the online payments friction problem and inaugurating the "eBay + PayPal" symbiotic era that inflated consolidated revenues to $15B+. This combination would define eBay's financials for over a decade before being unwound.

#### 3. 2015 — PayPal Spin-Off
Under pressure from activist investor Carl Icahn, eBay spun off PayPal in July 2015. Revenue declined from the consolidated $15B+ level to the $8–9B range reflecting the standalone marketplace. eBay lost a major growth engine but gained strategic focus on its core marketplace business. Operating margins initially held in the 26–27% range as cost structures adjusted.

#### 4. 2019 — StubHub Divestiture ($4.05B)
The sale of StubHub to viagogo for $4.05B removed ticketing revenue, causing reported revenue to decline from $10.74B (2018 peak) to $9.74B (2019). Gross margins improved slightly as the higher-margin marketplace business became a larger share of the mix.

#### 5. 2020 — Iannone Appointment + COVID Surge
New CEO Jamie Iannone arrived with a mandate for "tech-led reimagination" — finding a company he described as having grown "too complacent" ([Semafor, 2025-10-09](https://www.semafor.com/article/10/09/2025/how-jamie-iannone-made-ebay-an-investor-collectible)). COVID drove GMV up 21% in 2020, with non-COVID categories subsequently pulling back in 2021–2022. The net income figure of ~$5.7B in 2020 was significantly inflated by Classifieds divestiture-related gains.

#### 6. 2021 — Classifieds Group Divestiture ($9.2B)
The $9.2B sale of eBay Classifieds Group to Adevinta completed eBay's transformation into a "pure marketplace" company. The transaction generated massive non-recurring gains (net income of $13.6B in 2021, EPS of $0.38 due to diluted share count methodology), but obscured the underlying operating performance. On an adjusted basis, operating income reached $2.92B with a 28.1% operating margin — the highest in the modern era.

#### 7. 2022–2024 — Take Rate Expansion Era
With GMV growth slowing to low single digits post-COVID, eBay pivoted to monetization efficiency. Take rate expanded from approximately 12% to 13.8% by FY2024, enabling revenue to grow from $9.80B (2022) to $10.28B (2024) despite subdued volume growth. Advertising revenue reached $1.6B in 2024. The 2022 net loss of -$1.27B reflected Adevinta and other investment impairments rather than operating deterioration.

#### 8. 2025 — Focus Category Acceleration
FY2025 marked the clearest inflection in the Iannone transformation. Full-year revenue reached $11.1B with a 27.8% operating margin — the best margin profile since the 2021 divestiture year. GMV grew to $79.6B (+7% YoY). Take rate reached 14.0%. Q4 2025 revenue exceeded $3.0B for the first time, growing +15% YoY, with U.S. GMV accelerating to +19% ([eBay FY2025 Results](https://www.ebayinc.com/stories/news/ebay-inc-reports-fourth-quarter-and-full-year-2025-results/)). Focus categories (Motors Parts & Accessories, Luxury, Refurbished, Trading Cards, and other verticals) accelerated from ~5% to ~10% GMV growth ([Semafor, 2025-10-09](https://www.semafor.com/article/10/09/2025/how-jamie-iannone-made-ebay-an-investor-collectible)). Diluted EPS of $5.52 represented a new high-water mark for the standalone marketplace era.

Iannone's year-end summary: "2025 was a milestone for eBay, and our results reflect the strength of our strategy and the disciplined execution behind it... eBay is the strongest position it has been in years" ([Business Chief, 2025](https://businesschief.com/news/ebay-ceo-why-was-2025-a-milestone-for-the-commerce-leader)).

### Structural Margin Observations

- **Gross margin improvement:** From ~64–65% (2015–2016) to ~72–73% (2023–2025), reflecting the progressive divestiture of lower-margin businesses (StubHub event ticketing, Classifieds) and growing high-margin advertising revenue.
- **Operating margin volatility:** Ranges from 19.2% (2023, operating investment cycle) to 28.1% (2021, peak post-restructuring), with 2025's 27.8% suggesting the business has returned to peak efficiency.
- **Take rate as the growth lever:** In the absence of GMV acceleration, take rate expansion from 12% → 14.0% over 2020–2025 has been the primary revenue growth mechanism, contributing approximately $150–200M in incremental annual revenue per 10bps of take rate improvement at current GMV levels.
- **Advertising as structural margin driver:** The $1.6B advertising business (2024) carries near-100% incremental margins and represents eBay's highest-quality revenue stream, increasingly resembling the retail media model pioneered by Amazon.
