## Turning Point 1: 2019–2020 — EUV + 5G + COVID Triple Catalyst

TSMC pioneered volume EUV production (N7+/N5), coinciding with the 5G upgrade cycle and pandemic-driven digitalization demand. Revenue surged from $35.6B to $47.7B (+34%), initiating a super-growth cycle. Morris Chang later reflected on this period: "There's a tide in the affairs of man, which taken at its flood leads on to fortune." — TSMC captured the "sweet timing" of EUV volume production. ([Source: Acquired Podcast, 2025-01](https://www.acquired.fm/episodes/tsmc-founder-morris-chang))

Key metrics at the inflection:
- Revenue CAGR 2014–2019: ~7.4% (steady, node-driven)
- Revenue jump 2019→2020: +34% in a single year
- Gross margin recovery: 46.0% (2019) → 53.1% (2020), as EUV yield matured
- ROIC: ~22% trough in 2019 (heavy EUV capex load) → ~28% recovery in 2020

## Turning Point 2: 2022 — Peak Pricing Power

During the global chip shortage, TSMC raised advanced process pricing significantly (reportedly 10–20%), driving gross margin to a historical high of 59.6% and ROIC to 37.3%. CFO Wendell Huang articulated TSMC's pricing philosophy: "Our pricing will remain strategic, not opportunistic to earn our value." — emphasizing disciplined pricing: earning value, not price gouging. ([Source: The Next Platform, 2026-01-16](https://www.nextplatform.com/compute/2026/01/16/tsmc-has-no-choice-but-to-trust-the-sunny-ai-forecasts-of-its-customers/4092173))

CEO C.C. Wei added at the Q4 2024 earnings call: "Even as we invest for the future growth with this higher level of CapEx spending in 2025, we remain committed to delivering profitable growth to our shareholders." ([Source: TSMC Q4 2024 Transcript](https://investor.tsmc.com/chinese/encrypt/files/encrypt_file/reports/2025-01/84aeb15bbe33894365d33f52e027c5268ba95dcf/TSMC%204Q24%20Transcript.pdf))

2022 standout metrics:
- Gross margin: 59.6% (all-time high at that point)
- Operating margin: 49.5%
- Net income: NT$993B (nearly doubling from 2020's NT$518B)
- EPS: NT$191.5 (vs. NT$100.0 in 2020)
- ROIC: 37.3% — exceptional capital efficiency

---

## Turning Point 3: 2023 — Inventory Correction Year

Weakened consumer electronics demand triggered customer destocking, causing revenue to decline -4.5% YoY. ROIC fell from 37.3% to 25.2%. However, this year marked the beginning of the AI demand explosion — NVIDIA's order volume surged dramatically, laying the groundwork for the 2024 rebound.

2023 correction metrics:
- Revenue: $70.45B (−$3.4B YoY)
- Gross margin contraction: 59.6% → 54.4% (−520bps)
- Operating margin: 42.6% (vs. 49.5% in 2022)
- ROIC trough: 25.2%
- Net income: NT$852B (−NT$141B YoY)

Despite the correction, TSMC maintained >50% gross margins — testament to structural pricing power even in down cycles.

---

## Turning Point 4: 2024–2025 — AI Supercycle

AI/HPC demand explosion drove revenue growth of 34% (2024) and 32% (2025). By 2025, TSMC's revenue exceeded NT$3.8 trillion, net income surpassed NT$1.7 trillion, and ROIC leapt to 41.5% — an all-time high.

CEO C.C. Wei at the Q4 2025 earnings call: "We believe the AI is real. Not only real, it is starting to grow into our daily life."

On the $52–56B capex plan, Wei acknowledged: "I'm also very nervous about it. You bet, because we have to invest about $52 billion to $56 billion for the capex. If we didn't do it carefully, that would be a big disaster to TSMC for sure." But his conclusion: "I want to make sure that my customers' demand is real. So I talked to those cloud service providers — all of them. The answer is that I'm quite satisfied with the answer. They showed me the evidence that AI really helps their businesses." ([Source: The Next Platform, 2026-01-16](https://www.nextplatform.com/compute/2026/01/16/tsmc-has-no-choice-but-to-trust-the-sunny-ai-forecasts-of-its-customers/4092173))

Wei added a characteristically direct verification note: "I also double checked their financial status — they are very rich." — confirming hyperscaler customers have sufficient financial capacity to sustain long-term investment. ([Source: The Next Platform, 2026-01-16](https://www.nextplatform.com/compute/2026/01/16/tsmc-has-no-choice-but-to-trust-the-sunny-ai-forecasts-of-its-customers/4092173))

2024–2025 AI supercycle metrics:
- 2024 revenue: $88.34B (+34% YoY); ROIC: 30.9%
- 2025 revenue: ~$115B+ (+32% YoY); ROIC: 41.5% (all-time high)
- 2025 gross margin: 59.9% (matching 2022 peak)
- 2025 operating margin: 50.8% (surpassing all prior years)
- 2025 EPS: NT$331.3 (vs. NT$226.3 in 2024)

## Structural Observations

**ROIC as a cycle indicator**: ROIC dips during heavy capex ramp years (2019: ~22%, 2023: 25.2%) and peaks when capacity meets demand (2022: 37.3%, 2025: 41.5%). The 2025 ROIC of 41.5% while simultaneously spending $52–56B capex is historically exceptional.

**Margin floor**: Even in the 2023 correction, TSMC maintained 54.4% gross margins — a structural floor reflecting irreplaceable advanced process leadership.

**Revenue CAGR summary**:
- 2014–2019: ~7.4% (mature growth, process node cadence)
- 2019–2022: ~27.5% (EUV + COVID + pricing power)
- 2022–2023: −4.5% (inventory correction)
- 2023–2025: ~28% (AI supercycle)
- 2014–2025 full period: ~15.2% CAGR
