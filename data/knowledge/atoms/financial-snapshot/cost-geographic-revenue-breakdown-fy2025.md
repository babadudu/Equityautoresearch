### Geographic Revenue Concentration

Costco's revenue is heavily concentrated in North America. The FY2025 10-K reveals that the US and Canada combined account for **86%** of net sales. International operations (including Canada) represent **27%** of net sales but **34%** of operating income, indicating that international segments carry higher margins than the North American average.

California alone accounts for **26%** of US net sales, representing meaningful geographic concentration risk within the domestic segment.

### International Expansion Runway & Strategy

**CEO Ron Vachris on expansion pace:** "We see some good runway" for opening 24–25 warehouses annually over the next 5–10 years. ([South Sound Biz, 2024](https://www.southsoundbiz.com/profiles/ron-vachris-costco-interview/article_7c03e854-37d9-11ef-b403-1fab84c884aa.html))

**Former CEO Craig Jelinek on Asia-Pacific:** In a 2022 CNBC interview, Jelinek confirmed the Asia-Pacific region's role as a growth engine and referenced China's enormous market potential. ([CNBC, 2022/12/19](https://www.cnbc.com/video/2022/12/19/watch-cnbcs-full-interview-with-costco-ceo-craig-jelinek.html))

**China landmark moment:** The 2019 opening of the Shanghai Minhang store became a defining event in Costco's international expansion — the store was forced to temporarily suspend operations on its first day due to overwhelming crowd turnout.

**Vachris on tariff mitigation via regional sourcing:** "We continue to move more Kirkland Signature product sourcing into the countries or regions where the items are sold, and this is helping us to lower costs and mitigate some of the potential impacts of tariffs." ([Quartr, Q3 FY2025 Earnings Call](https://quartr.com/insights/business-philosophy/ron-vachris-from-forklift-driver-to-ceo-at-costco))

This statement reflects Costco's deliberate **regionalized sourcing** supply chain strategy for its private-label Kirkland Signature brand — decoupling manufacturing geography from sales geography to reduce tariff exposure and potentially improve unit economics in non-US markets.

### Key Observations

1. **Margin asymmetry**: International contributes 27% of net sales but 34% of operating income — suggesting structurally higher warehouse-level profitability outside North America, possibly due to lower competition density and stronger membership retention in markets like Japan, Korea, and Taiwan.
2. **California concentration risk**: A single US state (California) drives 26% of US net sales (~19% of total), creating geographic earnings concentration.
3. **E-commerce acceleration**: At +16.1% comparable growth, e-commerce is growing ~2× faster than in-warehouse comps, though it remains only ~7% of net sales.
4. **Steady store cadence**: 24 net new warehouses in FY2025 is consistent with the long-run 25/year target Vachris has articulated, with international skewing toward Asia-Pacific and Latin America.
5. **Membership fee growth**: Membership fees grew +10% YoY to $5.3B, outpacing net sales growth (+8.1%), reflecting the September 2024 fee increase ($65→$65 domestic Gold Star; Executive $130→$130) taking effect plus member count expansion.
