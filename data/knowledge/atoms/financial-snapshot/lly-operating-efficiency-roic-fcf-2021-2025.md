### ROIC Analysis

Lilly's ROIC troughed in 2023 at 10.21%, primarily because R&D investment accelerated (tirzepatide multiple Phase 3 trials plus early-stage $50B CapEx spend) while GLP-1 revenue had not yet fully materialized. ROIC rebounded to 16.97% in 2024 and surged to **25.18%** in 2025 — a level that ranks among the top tier of large-cap pharmaceuticals (peer comparison: Novo Nordisk ~30%, Pfizer ~5%, Merck ~18%). ROE exceeding 100% reflects Lilly's leveraged capital structure (D/E ~1.6x), but ROIC strips out leverage effects; the 25%+ reading confirms that Lilly's core business capital efficiency is exceptionally high.

### Operating Leverage Analysis

In 2025, revenue grew 45% while operating income grew more than 100%, demonstrating the pharmaceutical industry's characteristic extreme operating leverage. Once a drug receives approval and reaches market, incremental production carries near-zero marginal cost, meaning almost all incremental revenue flows through to profit. The 2026 Performance Margin guidance of 46–47.5% signals that operating efficiency continues to accelerate.

CEO Dave Ricks has demonstrated clear awareness of this dynamic: *"We spend almost 25% of sales this year on R&D... We'll spend $14 billion this year total"* ([All-In/Cheeky Pint, 2024](https://cheekypint.substack.com/p/dave-ricks-ceo-of-eli-lilly-on-glp)). However, Ricks frames the high R&D rate as a moat rather than a burden: *"If we're good, we can direct that at problems that are both big and solvable in a way governments never could"* ([Chief Executive, 2025](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/)).

### CapEx Investment vs. Efficiency

The $50B CapEx program temporarily suppresses FCF but is designed to remove the supply bottleneck that has been constraining revenue. Ricks reported that incretin sellable doses in H1 2025 were *"produced more than 1.6 times"* compared to the prior year period ([S&P Global, 2025](https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/research/2025/08/eli-lilly-set-to-leapfrog-novo-nordisk-in-weight-loss-drug-race)), indicating capacity expansion is rapidly converting to revenue.

This represents a classic "invest now, harvest later" cycle. During the CapEx peak (2024–2027), ROIC remains somewhat suppressed by the large invested capital base. Once plants come online, ROIC could climb further toward 30%+ as the incremental return on the new manufacturing assets materializes. Ricks has characterized this as the largest capacity expansion in pharmaceutical history (see also §1.3 of the source report).

### Key Takeaways

1. **Gross margin expansion** (76% → 83% over 2021–2025) is structural, driven by GLP-1 products carrying higher margins than the legacy portfolio.
2. **Operating margin** nearly doubled from 23.4% (2023 trough) to 40.3% (2025) in just two years — among the fastest margin expansions in large-cap pharma history.
3. **ROIC of 25%+** in 2025, excluding leverage, confirms genuine capital efficiency at the business level, not a financial engineering artifact.
4. **R&D and SG&A as a percent of revenue are declining** as the revenue denominator grows faster than these cost lines, a sustainable dynamic as long as GLP-1 volume continues to scale.
5. **2026 Performance Margin guidance of 46–47.5%** suggests management expects further significant margin improvement, implying the operating leverage cycle has not yet peaked.
