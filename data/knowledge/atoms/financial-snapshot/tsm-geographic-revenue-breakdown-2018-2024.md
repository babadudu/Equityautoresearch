## TSMC Geographic Revenue Breakdown (2018–2024)

### Overview

TSMC's revenue center of gravity has shifted dramatically from Asia toward North America over the 2018–2024 period, primarily driven by AI/HPC customers (NVIDIA, AMD, Broadcom) and Apple. The following data captures a seven-year trend in geographic revenue distribution.

### Key Observations

**North America (70% of 2024 revenue):**
- Absolute revenue grew 130% from 2018 to 2023 (NT$633B → NT$1,409B)
- Share rose from ~60% to 70% over the period
- Q4 single-quarter share reached as high as 76%, driven by Apple seasonal effects
- Growth underpinned by AI/HPC customer concentration: NVIDIA, AMD, Broadcom, and Apple

**China (11% of 2024 revenue):**
- Absolute revenue still grew ~50% in absolute terms from 2018 to 2023 (NT$176B → NT$267B)
- However, share roughly halved from ~17% to 11%
- Decline reflects TSMC's active compliance with US export controls on advanced process nodes for Chinese customers
- AI advanced-node orders shifted to North American customers; China revenue increasingly concentrated in mature nodes

**Japan (~5% of 2024 revenue):**
- FY2021–2023 CAGR of approximately 30%
- Growth driven by JASM (Japan Advanced Semiconductor Manufacturing) Kumamoto fab effect
- Beneficiary of Japan's semiconductor revival policy and government subsidies

**Regional CAGR 2021–2023 Ranking:**
Japan ~30% > North America ~22% > EMEA ~20% > Taiwan ~11% > China ~9%

### Structural Interpretation

The geographic shift is not simply customer-driven but reflects geopolitical realignment. US export controls imposed from 2022 onward effectively redirected TSMC's most advanced process capacity (3nm, 5nm) away from Chinese customers toward US hyperscalers and fabless AI chip designers. The result is a revenue structure increasingly correlated with US technology spending cycles rather than the broader Asian electronics ecosystem that historically anchored TSMC's customer base.
