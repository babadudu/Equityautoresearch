### Key Observations

**iPhone** remains the largest segment but is experiencing slow share erosion — from 52.3% in FY2023 to 50.4% in FY2025 — as Services grows faster. Absolute revenue grew only modestly (+4.2% YoY in FY2025), reflecting a maturing smartphone upgrade cycle.

**Services** is the fastest-growing segment at +13.5% YoY in FY2025, reaching $109.2B and now representing 26.2% of total revenue, up from 22.2% in FY2023. This structural shift is significant: Services carries substantially higher gross margins (~74% estimated) versus hardware (~37%), making it the primary driver of overall margin expansion. The segment crossed the $100B annual threshold for the first time in FY2025.

**Wearables, Home & Accessories** is the only segment in decline, falling -3.6% YoY to $35.7B, its second consecutive year of contraction. The AirPods and Apple Watch categories face saturation in core markets; Vision Pro has not yet contributed material revenue.

**Mac** rebounded strongly at +12.4% YoY to $33.7B, driven by the M3/M4 chip upgrade cycle and continued enterprise adoption of Apple Silicon-based laptops.

**iPad** returned to modest growth (+5.0% YoY to $28.0B) after a weak FY2024, supported by the iPad Pro (M4) and iPad Air (M2) refresh cycle.

### Three-Year Revenue Trend

Total Apple revenue grew from $383.3B (FY2023) to $416.2B (FY2025), a cumulative gain of +8.6% over two years. The mix shift toward Services is structurally positive for margins and cash flow quality, as recurring subscription revenue is less capital-intensive and more predictable than hardware cycles.
