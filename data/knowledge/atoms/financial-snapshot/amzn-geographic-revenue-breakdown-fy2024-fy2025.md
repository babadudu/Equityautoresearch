## Amazon Geographic & Segment Revenue Breakdown FY2024–FY2025

### Operating Segment Revenue

Amazon reports three operating segments: North America, International, and AWS. For FY2025, total revenue reached **$716.9B**, up 12% year-over-year from $638.0B in FY2024.

| Segment | FY2025 Revenue | Share | FY2024 Revenue | Share | YoY |
|---------|---------------|-------|---------------|-------|-----|
| North America | $426.3B | 59.5% | $387.7B | 60.8% | +10% |
| International | $161.9B | 22.6% | $143.5B | 22.5% | +13% |
| AWS | $128.7B | 18.0% | $107.6B | 16.9% | +20% |
| **Total** | **$716.9B** | **100%** | **$638.0B** | **100%** | **+12%** |

Source: [AMZN 2025 10-K](https://www.sec.gov/cgi-bin/browse-edgar?action=getcompany&CID=1018724&type=10-K)

### Key Strategic Observations

**AWS as the profit engine:** AWS accounts for only 18% of total revenue but contributes 57% of total operating income ($45.6B of $79.9B), with a 35.4% operating margin — far exceeding the North America segment (6.9%) and International segment (2.9%).

**Advertising as the hidden high-margin business:** Advertising Services grew 22% YoY to $68.6B in FY2025, making it the fastest-growing major segment. Market estimates suggest advertising operating margins of 50–60%, implying it may be Amazon's highest-margin business line — though operating income is not separately disclosed for this category.

**International acceleration:** The International segment grew 13% YoY in FY2025 vs. 10% for North America, signaling accelerating international expansion. International operating income also grew faster (+24% YoY) as the segment continues its path toward margin parity with North America.

**European dual-engine:** Germany ($40.9B) and the UK ($37.9B) together account for approximately 49% of International segment revenue (excluding AWS), functioning as Amazon's twin anchors in Europe.

**India: long-term bet, currently sub-scale:** Despite a cumulative investment of over $6.5B in India since 2014, India's marketplace revenue remains under 0.5% of Amazon's consolidated total. However, India is strategically positioned as a next-billion-user market with high long-term growth potential.

**Japan: mature Asia-Pacific market:** Japan ($27.4B) is Amazon's most established Asia-Pacific market, characterized by high e-commerce penetration and strong Prime membership retention.

**US concentration risk:** The United States accounts for approximately 68.7% of FY2024 revenue (~$438B), reflecting significant geographic concentration. Any US-specific regulatory action, consumer spending slowdown, or competitive pressure would have outsized impact on consolidated results.
