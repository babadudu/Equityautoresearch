### Regional Commentary

**Americas (FY2025: $178.4B, 42.9% of revenue, YoY +6.8%)**

The Americas remains Apple's core revenue base. iPhone holds approximately 55–60% smartphone market share in the United States. CFO Kevan Parekh reported September quarter records in FY2025 Q4: "We set September quarter records in the Americas, Europe, Japan, and the rest of Asia-Pacific and grew in the vast majority of markets we track" ([Apple Q4 FY2025 Earnings Call / Six Colors, 2025-10](https://sixcolors.com/post/2025/10/this-is-tim-transcript-of-apples-q4-2025-financial-call/)). CEO Tim Cook announced a major domestic investment commitment: "We're putting $600 billion into the U.S. over the next four years" ([GMA, 2026-03-17](https://abcnews.com/GMA/Culture/tim-cook-talks-apples-50th-anniversary-future-ai/story?id=131137939)), covering supply chain diversification, data centers, and Apple Silicon manufacturing.

**Europe (FY2025: $111.0B, 26.7% of revenue, YoY +9.6%)**

Europe is Apple's fastest-growing mature market; FY2025 revenue crossed $111B for the first time. Parekh highlighted Mac outperformance and broad geographic strength: "We grew in every geographic segment and saw double-digit growth in Europe, Greater China, and the rest of Asia-Pacific" ([Apple Q3 FY2025 Earnings Call / Six Colors, 2025-07](https://sixcolors.com/post/2025/07/this-is-tim-transcript-of-apples-q3-2025-financial-call/)). Services also set new all-time records in Europe in Q4 FY2025 ([Apple Q4 FY2025 Earnings Call / Six Colors, 2025-10](https://sixcolors.com/post/2025/10/this-is-tim-transcript-of-apples-q4-2025-financial-call/)). A structural risk: EU Digital Markets Act (DMA) regulation is forcing Apple to open sideloading and third-party payment options, which may compress App Store take rates over the medium term.

**Greater China (FY2025: $64.4B, 15.5% of revenue, YoY -3.9%)**

China revenue has declined for two consecutive years: FY2023 $72.6B → FY2024 $67.0B → FY2025 $64.4B, a cumulative decline of -11.3%. Primary drivers are the Huawei Mate 60/70 series re-entering the high-end segment and geopolitically-driven consumer sentiment shifts. Cook acknowledged pressure but expressed optimism on iPhone 17 reception: "I was just there. It's incredibly vibrant and dynamic... The iPhone 17 family has been very well received there. We do believe that we'll return to growth in Q1" ([Apple Q4 FY2025 Earnings Call / Six Colors, 2025-10](https://sixcolors.com/post/2025/10/this-is-tim-transcript-of-apples-q4-2025-financial-call/)). Cook also noted a favorable but price-band-limited effect from Chinese government subsidies: "The subsidies play a favorable role... they only apply to certain price ranges" ([same source](https://sixcolors.com/post/2025/10/this-is-tim-transcript-of-apples-q4-2025-financial-call/)). In October 2025, Cook visited Beijing and pledged increased investment in China despite tariff threats ([Bloomberg, 2025-10-15](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-10-15/apple-s-cook-vows-to-boost-chinese-investment-during-visit)).

**Japan (FY2025: $28.7B, 6.9% of revenue, YoY +14.6%)**

Japan was Apple's fastest-growing geographic segment in FY2025, with iPhone maintaining >50% smartphone market share domestically. Parekh confirmed Japan set a September quarter record in Q4 FY2025 ([Apple Q4 FY2025 Earnings Call / Six Colors, 2025-10](https://sixcolors.com/post/2025/10/this-is-tim-transcript-of-apples-q4-2025-financial-call/)). The yen's relative weakness vs. prior years may have contributed to currency tailwinds on reported USD figures.

**Rest of Asia Pacific — India Highlighted (FY2025: $33.7B, 8.1% of revenue, YoY +9.9%)**

India is the highest-priority growth market within this segment. Parekh reported an all-time revenue record in India in Q4 FY2025: "We set an all-time revenue record in India" ([Apple Q4 FY2025 Earnings Call / Six Colors, 2025-10](https://sixcolors.com/post/2025/10/this-is-tim-transcript-of-apples-q4-2025-financial-call/)). Cook had framed India's strategic importance as early as Q2 FY2024: "I see it as an incredibly exciting market and it's a major focus for us" ([BusinessToday, 2024-05-03](https://www.businesstoday.in/technology/news/story/apple-ceo-tim-cook-very-very-pleased-on-strong-double-digit-growth-in-india-428090-2024-05-03)). Critically, India has also transitioned from a sales market to a core manufacturing hub: Cook disclosed in Q3 FY2025 that "The majority of the iPhones sold in the U.S. have a country of origin of India" ([Apple Q3 FY2025 Earnings Call / Six Colors, 2025-07](https://sixcolors.com/post/2025/07/this-is-tim-transcript-of-apples-q3-2025-financial-call/)). Foxconn plans to double India iPhone production to 25–30 million units by end of 2025 ([Yahoo Finance, 2025](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/apple-smashes-india-production-records-123035455.html)).

### Key Takeaways

1. **China structural decline**: Greater China has shed ~$8B in annual revenue over two years and its mix has dropped from 18.9% to 15.5%. The Huawei competitive threat and geopolitical overhang remain unresolved.
2. **Europe as offsetting growth driver**: Europe's +9.6% YoY growth and expanding mix (24.6% → 26.7%) is partially absorbing China headwinds, but DMA-driven App Store margin pressure is a medium-term risk.
3. **Services mix expansion**: Services grew from 22.2% to 26.2% of revenue over three years — a structurally higher-margin mix shift that supports gross margin expansion even as hardware growth moderates.
4. **India dual role**: India now functions as both a high-growth sales market (record revenue) and the primary manufacturing origin for US-bound iPhones — a strategic hedge against US-China tariff risk.
5. **Japan outperformance**: Japan's 14.6% YoY growth was the strongest of any region in FY2025, though its 6.9% revenue share limits total impact.
