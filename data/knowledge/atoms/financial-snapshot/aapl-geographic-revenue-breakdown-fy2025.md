### Americas (FY2025: $178.4B, 42.9% of total, YoY +6.8%)

The Americas is Apple's largest and most profitable home market. iPhone commands approximately 55–60% smartphone market share in the United States. CFO Kevan Parekh reported at the FY2025 Q4 earnings call: "We set September quarter records in the Americas, Europe, Japan, and the rest of Asia-Pacific and grew in the vast majority of markets we track" ([Apple Q4 FY2025 Earnings Call / Six Colors, 2025-10](https://sixcolors.com/post/2025/10/this-is-tim-transcript-of-apples-q4-2025-financial-call/)).

CEO Tim Cook announced a major domestic investment commitment: "We're putting $600 billion into the U.S. over the next four years" ([GMA, 2026-03-17](https://abcnews.com/GMA/Culture/tim-cook-talks-apples-50th-anniversary-future-ai/story?id=131137939)), encompassing supply chain diversification, data centers, and Apple Silicon manufacturing. This signals both a geopolitical hedge and a response to reshoring policy pressures.

---

### Europe (FY2025: $111.0B, 26.7% of total, YoY +9.6%)

Europe is Apple's fastest-growing mature market, with FY2025 revenue surpassing $111B for the first time. Parekh highlighted double-digit growth during FY2025 Q3: "We grew in every geographic segment and saw double-digit growth in Europe, Greater China, and the rest of Asia-Pacific" ([Apple Q3 FY2025 Earnings Call / Six Colors, 2025-07](https://sixcolors.com/post/2025/07/this-is-tim-transcript-of-apples-q3-2025-financial-call/)). Mac performance in Europe was particularly strong, and Services achieved all-time revenue records.

**Regulatory headwind:** The EU Digital Markets Act (DMA) forces Apple to permit app sideloading and third-party payment processors, creating structural pressure on App Store take rates over the medium term. This risk is not yet visible in reported revenue but is a key bear case for Services margin expansion in Europe.

---

### Greater China (FY2025: $64.4B, 15.5% of total, YoY -3.9%)

Greater China is Apple's most contested region. Revenue has declined for two consecutive years, from $72.6B in FY2023 to $64.4B in FY2025, a cumulative decline of -11.3%. The primary drivers are:
1. **Huawei's return** — The Mate 60 and Mate 70 series (featuring domestically produced Kirin chips) recaptured premium Android share.
2. **Consumer sentiment** — Geopolitical tensions have contributed to a shift toward domestic brands in some segments.

Cook acknowledged the pressure but expressed optimism about the iPhone 17 cycle: "I was just there. It's incredibly vibrant and dynamic... The iPhone 17 family has been very well received there. We do believe that we'll return to growth in Q1" ([Apple Q4 FY2025 Earnings Call / Six Colors, 2025-10](https://sixcolors.com/post/2025/10/this-is-tim-transcript-of-apples-q4-2025-financial-call/)).

On government subsidies: "The subsidies play a favorable role... they only apply to certain price ranges" ([same source](https://sixcolors.com/post/2025/10/this-is-tim-transcript-of-apples-q4-2025-financial-call/)). Chinese government trade-in subsidy programs have partially offset demand weakness at qualifying price points.

In October 2025, Cook visited Beijing and pledged to increase investment in China ([Bloomberg, 2025-10-15](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-10-15/apple-s-cook-vows-to-boost-chinese-investment-during-visit)), despite ongoing tariff risks. Greater China's share of total revenue has fallen from 18.9% (FY2023) to 15.5% (FY2025), reducing but not eliminating Apple's exposure to China-specific risks.

---

### Japan (FY2025: $28.7B, 6.9% of total, YoY +14.6%)

Japan was Apple's highest-growth region in FY2025. iPhone commands over 50% smartphone market share in Japan, one of the highest penetration rates globally. Parekh confirmed Japan set a September quarter record in FY2025 Q4 ([Apple Q4 FY2025 Earnings Call / Six Colors, 2025-10](https://sixcolors.com/post/2025/10/this-is-tim-transcript-of-apples-q4-2025-financial-call/)). The yen's depreciation may have contributed to unit volume as Japan-priced iPhones became more attractively priced relative to USD-equivalent alternatives, though currency translation works in reverse for reported USD revenue.

---

### Rest of Asia Pacific / India (FY2025: $33.7B, 8.1% of total, YoY +9.9%)

This segment includes India, Australia, Southeast Asia, and other Asia Pacific markets. India is the strategically critical component.

**India as sales market:** Cook positioned India as a top strategic priority at the FY2024 Q2 earnings call: "I see it as an incredibly exciting market and it's a major focus for us" ([BusinessToday, 2024-05-03](https://www.businesstoday.in/technology/news/story/apple-ceo-tim-cook-very-very-pleased-on-strong-double-digit-growth-in-india-428090-2024-05-03)). Parekh confirmed India set an all-time revenue record in FY2025 Q4: "We set an all-time revenue record in India" ([Apple Q4 FY2025 Earnings Call / Six Colors, 2025-10](https://sixcolors.com/post/2025/10/this-is-tim-transcript-of-apples-q4-2025-financial-call/)).

**India as manufacturing base:** Cook disclosed a pivotal supply chain shift during FY2025 Q3: "The majority of the iPhones sold in the U.S. have a country of origin of India" ([Apple Q3 FY2025 Earnings Call / Six Colors, 2025-07](https://sixcolors.com/post/2025/07/this-is-tim-transcript-of-apples-q3-2025-financial-call/)). India has thus transitioned from a consumer market opportunity to a dual-role strategic asset — both a high-growth sales market and a primary iPhone manufacturing hub for the U.S. market. Foxconn plans to double India's iPhone production capacity to 25–30 million units by end of 2025 ([Yahoo Finance, 2025](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/apple-smashes-india-production-records-123035455.html)).

### Geographic Mix Summary and Key Trends

| Trend | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Americas stability | Share held flat ~42-43%; remains high-margin core |
| Europe outperformance | Fastest-growing mature market; DMA is medium-term risk |
| China structural decline | -11.3% cumulative FY23–25; Huawei + geopolitics |
| Japan strength | Highest YoY growth in FY2025 at +14.6% |
| India dual-role | All-time sales record AND majority U.S. iPhone source |

Apple's geographic revenue is becoming less China-dependent and more diversified across Europe and emerging Asia, a deliberate strategic repositioning that reduces geopolitical concentration risk.
