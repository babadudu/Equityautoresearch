| Return Source | Contribution |

| Share Buybacks (~6%/year pace) | 5% |
| Dividend | 1.8% |

The buyback contribution is the single largest driver, reflecting eBay's aggressive capital return program. At ~6% annual share count reduction, buybacks mechanically boost per-share metrics even in a low-growth environment. This is a critical feature of the eBay investment thesis: the stock can generate double-digit returns with minimal organic growth if buybacks are sustained.

## Valuation Summary & Investment Implications

eBay at ~$62 (March 2026) appears roughly fairly valued relative to its own historical multiples and the DCF base case. The investment case is not predicated on growth reacceleration — it relies on capital return execution:

- **Buyback yield:** ~6%/year provides a mechanical earnings-per-share tailwind
- **Dividend yield:** ~1.8% adds income component
- **FCF generation:** ~$2.6B/year provides the fuel for sustained capital return
- **Valuation floor:** P/FCF of ~11x is near the low end of historical range, limiting downside in a no-growth scenario

The key risks to the valuation are:
1. GMV acceleration failing to materialize, causing earnings estimates to be cut
2. Rising interest rates compressing multiples and increasing WACC
3. Competitive pressure from Amazon, Temu, and Shein eroding eBay's casual seller base
4. Buyback program being curtailed due to leverage concerns or acquisition activity

The key upside catalysts are:
1. AI-driven personalization lifting conversion rates and buyer retention
2. Vertical category (motors, luxury goods, trading cards) monetization improvement
3. Interest rate normalization compressing discount rates
4. Strategic partnership or platform deal announcement
