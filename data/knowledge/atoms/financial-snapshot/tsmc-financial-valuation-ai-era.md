### The Revenue and Margin Explosion

Driven by the structural shift toward AI/HPC (High-Performance Computing), TSMC’s financial profile has reached historic highs. HPC surpassed smartphone revenue to become the company's largest platform, representing 51% of total revenue in 2024 and accelerating to 60% by Q2 2025. 

In 2024, TSMC generated $88.3 billion in revenue (+34% YoY). Estimates for 2025 sit at ~$115 billion (+32% YoY), with management providing a robust 2026 projection of roughly $150 billion (+30% YoY). Profitability has expanded simultaneously. Operating leverage and aggressive value-based pricing—particularly for N3 and upcoming N2 wafers—pushed gross margins to 62.3% in Q4 2025, well above CFO Wendell Huang's stated long-term structural floor of 53%. Net margins achieved an astonishing 48.3%.

### The CapEx vs. Free Cash Flow Tension

The primary friction in TSMC's financial model is its immense capital intensity. To support N2 mass production and a doubling of CoWoS packaging capacity, TSMC set a staggering 2026 CapEx budget of $52-$56 billion, utilizing ~36% of projected revenues. 

While TSMC’s Return on Invested Capital (ROIC) hit a record 41.5% in 2025, demonstrating exceptional capital efficiency, the absolute scale of CapEx deeply compresses Free Cash Flow (FCF). Operating cash flow of roughly $85B in 2026 is eroded by $54B in CapEx, leaving an estimated FCF of $31B (an FCF margin of ~20.7%). Consequently, TSMC trades at a lofty Trailing Price-to-FCF ratio of ~48.8x, implying an FCF yield of barely 2.1%. Bears argue this makes TSMC an expensive asset for yield-focused investors, though bulls point to the massive $291 billion NTD in customer prepayments (as of 2024) that effectively de-risks this capacity buildout.

### Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) and Multiples Valuation

A 5-year explicit DCF model (WACC 9.0%, terminal growth 2.0-3.0%) values TSMC’s equity at $1.02T to $1.16T, implying a share price of NT$6,500 to NT$7,400. This is significantly below current market trading levels (~NT$10,500), primarily because the DCF penalizes the heavy CapEx years of 2026-2027. 

However, relative valuation metrics paint a more bullish picture. TSMC currently trades at a Forward P/E of ~24.5x (based on 2026E EPS). Compared to semiconductor peers like NVIDIA (~35x), ASML (~30x), and AMD (~30x), TSMC exhibits a pronounced "Taiwan Discount." Its EV/EBITDA of ~20x sits well below the peer average of ~39x. The Price-to-Earnings-to-Growth (PEG) ratio is around 1.0x, representing a 48% discount relative to the broader IT sector. 

### The Geopolitical Risk Premium (WACC adjustment)

The discrepancy between TSMC's stellar fundamentals (30% growth, 60%+ margins) and its compressed multiples is entirely a function of geopolitical risk. A sensitivity analysis reveals that raising the WACC from a baseline 9% to 10% (incorporating a higher Taiwan blockade risk premium) shrinks the fair value by approximately 11%. Conversely, a "peace dividend" scenario that harmonizes TSMC's P/E multiple with fabless peers (28-30x) yields a target price exceeding NT$13,000. 

Ultimately, TSMC presents an internal rate of return (IRR) of roughly 18% in a base-case scenario over the next three years, assuming CapEx normalizes by 2028 and structural AI demand holds. But as long as the geopolitical overhang exists, TSMC will likely remain structurally underpriced relative to the hyperscale tech giants that rely entirely on its silicon.
