Advanced process nodes (7nm and below) now account for 69% of total revenue in 2024, expected to reach ~70% in 2025. The 5nm node is the single largest revenue contributor at ~37%, while 3nm is the fastest-growing, projected to rise from 18% to 23% by Q3 2025.

### Customer Concentration

Top 10 customers accounted for **76% of revenue in 2024**, up from 70% in 2023 and 68% in 2022 — a consistent trend toward greater concentration.

- **Largest customer (est. Apple):** ~22% of revenue
- **Second largest (est. NVIDIA):** <12% of revenue
- **Third largest (est. AMD):** <10% of revenue

Source: [TechSoda](https://techsoda.substack.com/p/explainer-tsmcs-2024-annual-report)

### Structural Implications

The combination of HPC at 51% of platform mix and advanced nodes at 69% of process mix reveals a TSMC that is increasingly a pure-play AI infrastructure supplier at the leading edge. The customer concentration trend (76% from top 10) introduces earnings sensitivity to Apple's iPhone cycle and NVIDIA's GPU upgrade cadence. Mature node revenue (~31%) provides a floor but is strategically less critical and faces margin pressure from new entrants and Chinese foundry competition.
