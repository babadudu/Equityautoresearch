## Eli Lilly Geographic Revenue Breakdown FY2023–FY2025

Eli Lilly operates as a single business segment (human pharmaceuticals), selling products in approximately 95 countries worldwide. The United States remains the core market (~2/3 of revenue), but international markets are accelerating as GLP-1 products receive regulatory approvals and manufacturing capacity expands.

### Regional Strategy Details

**United States (66.7% of 2025 revenue)**
The commercial core for Mounjaro and Zepbound launches. Starting in 2026, Medicare Part D coverage of obesity drugs is expected to add 20–30 million potential patients. LillyDirect, the company's direct-to-consumer channel, accelerates access for out-of-pocket patients. Revenue grew +39% in 2024 and +43% in 2025, reflecting the initial GLP-1 ramp being US-centric before international approvals broadened.

**Europe (17.7% of 2025 revenue)**
The fastest-growing region in 2025 at +67%, driven by Mounjaro's diabetes indication rollout in Germany, the UK, and France, as well as country-by-country obesity approvals for Zepbound. Italy passed legislation in 2025 classifying obesity as a chronic disease, which could catalyze broader insurance reimbursement across Europe ([IQVIA, 2026-01](https://www.iqvia.com/locations/emea/blogs/2026/01/outlook-for-obesity-in-2026)). Europe's share declined from 18.1% in 2023 to 15.4% in 2024 as US growth outpaced it, then rebounded to 17.7% in 2025 as European launches accelerated.

**Japan (3.3% of 2025 revenue)**
Zepbound received PMDA approval for chronic obesity ([IQVIA, 2026-01](https://www.iqvia.com/locations/emea/blogs/2026/01/outlook-for-obesity-in-2026)), though growth remains moderate at +18% due in part to biennial drug price adjustment pressures. Japan defines obesity at BMI ≥25 (versus ≥30 in Western markets), suggesting a proportionally larger addressable population.

**China (3.0% of 2025 revenue)**
The most challenged international market. Domestic GLP-1 competition is intensifying — Innovent Biologics' mazdutide has been accepted for NMPA review, and semaglutide's core patents are set to expire in China in 2026 ([IQVIA, 2026-01](https://www.iqvia.com/locations/emea/blogs/2026/01/outlook-for-obesity-in-2026)). CEO David Ricks has taken a hard line on Chinese counterfeit GLP-1 products: "Most of them are sourced out of Chinese labs that aren't even approved in China" ([Washington Reporter, 2025-03](https://washingtonreporter.news/p/interview-eli-lilly-ceo-david-ricks)). China's revenue share declined from 4.5% in 2023 to 3.0% in 2025.

**Other International (9.3% of 2025 revenue)**
Emerging markets including the Middle East, Latin America, and Southeast Asia delivered strong +42% growth in 2025, primarily driven by first-time GLP-1 launches. Ricks views emerging markets as the largest long-term incremental opportunity given the vast untapped populations. Revenue nearly doubled from $2.95B in 2023 to $6.06B in 2025.

### Geographic Mix Trend Interpretation

US mix rose from 63.9% in 2023 to 67.4% in 2024 as Mounjaro and Zepbound launched domestically ahead of international approvals, then dipped to 66.7% in 2025 as European and Other International markets began to accelerate. This rebalancing trend is expected to continue through 2026–2028 as more international markets obtain regulatory approval and the Medicare volume-for-price trade-off takes effect in the US.
