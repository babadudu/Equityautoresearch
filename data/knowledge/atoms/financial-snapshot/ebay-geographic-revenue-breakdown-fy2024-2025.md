## eBay Geographic Revenue Breakdown and Category Mix FY2024–FY2025

### Geographic Revenue Overview

eBay's revenue is roughly split between US and international markets, though strong US growth in FY2025 tilted the balance further toward the domestic market. The platform serves 190 markets, and CEO Jamie Iannone described its scale in eBay's 2021 annual report as "over 147 million active buyers and 17 million sellers across 190 markets" ([LinkedIn, 2021](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/empowering-economic-opportunity-all-jamie-iannone)). The high degree of cross-border coverage means eBay's geographic segments have significant cross-traffic between them.

### FY2025 Geographic Performance (GMV Growth)

US GMV growth accelerated sharply through FY2025. In Q3 2025, US GMV accelerated to approximately 13% ([eBay Q3 2025 Earnings Call, 2025-10-29](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/29/ebay-ebay-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)), and in Q4 2025 it further accelerated to approximately **19%** ([eBay Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-18](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/18/ebay-ebay-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)).

International performance diverged sharply. In Q3 2025, international GMV grew approximately 4% on a FX-neutral basis. However, in Q4 2025, international GMV declined approximately 1% on an organic FX-neutral basis, driven by weak European consumer confidence and tariff headwinds ([eBay Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-18](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/18/ebay-ebay-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)).

CEO Iannone acknowledged the European weakness directly in the Q4 2025 earnings call: "Europe has been more challenged as consumer confidence remains low and retail sales trends are subdued there" ([eBay Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-18](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/18/ebay-ebay-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)).

CFO Steve Alford provided additional detail on the specific pressure points: "Challenging macroeconomic conditions in the UK and Germany and a deceleration in our cross-border volume growth due to U.S. trade policies" ([eBay Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-18](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/18/ebay-ebay-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)).

### China Revenue: Cross-Border Export Model

The $1.17B China revenue line in FY2024 (11.4% of total) reflects an export-oriented, cross-border model rather than domestic Chinese commerce. Chinese sellers sell to US and European buyers — not Chinese consumers buying domestically.

VP John Egbert disclosed in the Q4 2024 earnings call that goods sourced from China represent approximately 5% of total GMV, and that 75% of this inventory had already been "forward deployed" to US warehouses ([Digital Commerce 360, 2025-03-10](https://www.digitalcommerce360.com/2025/03/10/ebay-sales-revenue-gmv-q4-2024/)). This forward-deployment strategy partially insulates eBay from de minimis tariff risk on China-origin goods, as warehouse-held inventory crosses the border under different customs treatment.

Cross-border trade disruption from US trade policy was not limited to China-origin sellers. Iannone disclosed in the Q3 2025 earnings call that "sellers in Japan and Canada being amongst the most impacted" by the removal of the de minimis exemption ([eBay Q3 2025 Earnings Call, 2025-10-29](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/29/ebay-ebay-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)).

### Motors (Automotive) Global Scale

In eBay's 2021 annual report, Iannone highlighted the global reach of the Motors category: "40 million buyers with over $10 billion in GMV annually" ([LinkedIn, 2021](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/empowering-economic-opportunity-all-jamie-iannone)). This positions eBay's automotive parts and vehicles business as one of the largest online automotive marketplaces globally.

### Trading Cards: 2025 Tailwind, 2026 Deceleration Expected

The Collectibles & Trading Cards category was a significant outperformer in FY2025, with Pokémon cards posting triple-digit GMV growth. CFO Alford provided explicit forward guidance on expected normalization in the Q4 2025 earnings call: "We are expecting a deceleration in Pokémon growth in 2026, just given the triple-digit growth that we saw in 2025. Although, I will point out that we expect healthy overall growth in trading cards" ([eBay Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-18](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/18/ebay-ebay-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)).

### Key Structural Observations

1. **US/International divergence**: US GMV accelerated to ~19% in Q4 2025 while international organic GMV turned negative — the widest geographic split in recent history.
2. **Europe macro headwinds**: UK and Germany, representing ~24% of total FY2024 revenue combined, are both under pressure from weak consumer sentiment and trade policy disruption.
3. **China revenue is export revenue**: The 11.4% China share reflects cross-border seller activity, not Chinese consumer demand — making it sensitive to US trade policy rather than Chinese GDP.
4. **Focus categories concentrate GMV**: At ~67% of GMV, focus categories now dominate eBay's mix, with Electronics (28%) and Fashion (17%) as the two largest pillars.
5. **Collectibles becoming material**: Trading cards have scaled to a $10B+ annual business, creating both a growth tailwind in 2025 and a base-effect headwind in 2026.
