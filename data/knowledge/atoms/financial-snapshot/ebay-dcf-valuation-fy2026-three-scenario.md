## DCF Three-Scenario Analysis

| Assumption | Bear | Base | Bull |
|------------|------|------|------|
| Revenue CAGR (5-year) | 2% | 4% | 6% |
| Terminal operating margin | 20% | 23% | 26% |
| Terminal growth rate | 2.0% | 2.5% | 3.0% |
| WACC | 8.4% | 8.4% | 8.4% |
| **Target price** | **$47** | **$72** | **$95** |
| **IRR vs $62** | **-2%** | **+10%** | **+18%** |

**Scenario interpretation**:

- **Bear ($47)**: 2% revenue CAGR with 20% terminal margins implies continued secular GMV pressure from Amazon and Chinese cross-border platforms (Temu, Shein), margin compression from investments, and no meaningful multiple re-rating. At $62, this scenario implies a ~-2% IRR — a modest loss.

- **Base ($72)**: 4% revenue CAGR with 23% terminal margins reflects stabilization in core marketplace volumes, continued focus category growth (collectibles, parts & accessories), and steady monetization gains from AI-powered listing tools and advertising. IRR of ~10%.

- **Bull ($95)**: 6% revenue CAGR with 26% terminal margins requires successful execution of AI-driven buyer/seller tools, meaningful GMV recovery in non-focus categories, and potential monetization upside from advertising or financial services. IRR of ~18%.

## Sensitivity Matrix — Target Price vs Revenue Growth × WACC

| | WACC 7.5% | WACC 8.4% | WACC 9.5% |
|---|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Revenue growth 2% | $55 | $47 | $40 |
| Revenue growth 4% | $83 | $72 | $62 |
| Revenue growth 6% | $110 | $95 | $82 |

**Sensitivity observations**:

1. **Revenue growth is the dominant driver**: Moving from 2% to 6% CAGR at base WACC (8.4%) shifts the target price from $47 to $95 — a 102% range. This dwarfs the WACC sensitivity.

2. **WACC sensitivity is meaningful but secondary**: At 4% revenue growth, a 200bps WACC shift (7.5% to 9.5%) moves the target from $83 to $62 — a 25% range. Rising rates represent a real headwind.

3. **At current price ($62), breakeven requires ~4% revenue growth at base WACC**: The $62 intersection sits at the base WACC / 4% growth cell, confirming the stock is priced for the base case with minimal margin of safety.

4. **Downside protection is limited at low growth scenarios**: At 2% revenue growth across all WACC assumptions, fair value ranges from $40–$55, implying 11–35% downside from $62. Bears who believe eBay cannot exceed 2% revenue growth have a strong case for avoiding the stock.

## Investment Conclusion

eBay at ~$62 (March 2026) appears **fairly valued** on a multi-method basis, with the triangulated fair value range of $54–$73 centered on the current price. The DCF base case yields a ~10% IRR, driven primarily by buybacks (5%) rather than growth (3%). 

The risk/reward is asymmetric but not compelling: the bull case ($95, +53%) requires sustained 6% revenue growth that eBay has not demonstrated in recent years, while the bear case ($47, -24%) is achievable with continued GMV stagnation at 2% growth. 

For income-oriented investors, the combination of ~1.8% dividend yield and ~6% annual buyback yield (~7.8% total capital return yield) provides a floor thesis. For growth investors, eBay's peer discount (14.7x P/E vs. Amazon 60x, MercadoLibre 55x) is justified by structurally lower growth expectations rather than being an opportunity.
