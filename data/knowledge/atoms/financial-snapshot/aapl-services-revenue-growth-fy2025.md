## Apple Services Segment: Revenue Growth and Margin Profile FY2025

### Overview

Apple's Services segment generated $109.2 billion in revenue in FY2025, representing 26.2% of total Apple revenue. Year-over-year growth was +13.5%, making Services the fastest-growing business line at Apple. The segment carries a gross margin of approximately 70%, dramatically outpacing the Hardware segment's ~36% gross margin.

### Services Sub-Segments

The Services category encompasses a broad portfolio of recurring and transactional revenue streams:

- **App Store** — The largest sub-segment within Services by revenue. Apple charges a take rate of 15–30% on transactions processed through the App Store.
- **iCloud** — Cloud storage subscription service for Apple device users.
- **Apple Music** — Music streaming service with over 100 million subscribers as of FY2025.
- **Apple TV+** — Original video streaming service.
- **Apple Pay** — Mobile and contactless payment platform.
- **AppleCare** — Extended warranty and device support subscription.
- **Apple Arcade** — Games subscription service.
- **Apple News+** — News and magazine subscription service.
- **Apple Fitness+** — Fitness and workout streaming service.
- **Google Search Revenue Share** — Google pays Apple an estimated ~$20 billion annually to remain the default search engine on Apple devices and browsers. This is one of the highest-margin revenue streams within Services.

### Subscriber Base

As of the end of FY2025, the number of paid subscriptions across Apple's platform exceeded **975 million**. This figure includes subscriptions across all of Apple's own services (Music, TV+, Arcade, iCloud, News+, Fitness+) as well as third-party subscriptions transacted through the App Store.

### Management Commentary

Tim Cook commented on the Services business during Apple's Q4 FY2025 Earnings Call (2025-10-30):

> "Services set an all-time revenue record, reflecting the strength of our ecosystem and the value our customers find in our offerings."

### Strategic Significance: Apple's Hidden Transformation

The rise of Services represents a structural transformation in Apple's profit composition. In FY2015, Services accounted for approximately 8.5% of total Apple revenue. By FY2025, that share had grown to 26.2% — a tripling of the revenue mix contribution over a decade.

Because Services carries a ~70% gross margin versus ~36% for Hardware, its growing share is structurally accretive to Apple's blended gross margin. This ongoing mix shift means Apple's overall profitability profile increasingly resembles a software and services company rather than a pure hardware manufacturer — even as device revenue remains the foundation of the installed base.

The recurring, subscription-based nature of Services also reduces revenue volatility compared to hardware upgrade cycles, providing Apple with more predictable cash flows and greater earnings stability across economic cycles.
