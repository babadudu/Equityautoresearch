## TSMC in the Allied Framework and Global Industry Dependency Analysis

### US-Japan-Taiwan Triangle: The Multilateral Alliance Architecture

**Chip 4 Alliance**: In 2022, the United States proposed the "Chip 4 Alliance" composed of the US, Japan, South Korea, and Taiwan, aimed at building a semiconductor supply chain security system dominated by democratic nations. These four economies collectively account for 82% of the global semiconductor market, 74% of the semiconductor global value chain, 84% of chip design, 77% of manufacturing equipment, and 99% of memory chips. ([Source: Fortune Asia, 2024-03-28](https://fortune.com/asia/2024/03/28/chip-4-alliance-us-korea-japan-taiwan-semiconductors-china-opec-cartel-for-digital-age/))

**TSMC Japan Footprint (JASM)**: TSMC's Kumamoto fab (JASM), a joint venture with Sony and Denso, began mass production in 2024 and received approximately 50% fab construction subsidies from the Japanese government. In February 2026, CEO Wei met with Japanese Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi to discuss TSMC's Japan expansion and deepening Taiwan-Japan semiconductor cooperation. ([Source: Prime Minister's Office of Japan, 2026-02-05](https://japan.kantei.go.jp/104/actions/202602/05hyoukei.html))

**US-Japan/Korea Technology Prosperity Deals (TPD)**: In October 2025, the United States signed "Technology Prosperity Deals" with Japan and South Korea separately, covering cooperation frameworks across AI, semiconductors, quantum computing, biotech, space, and 6G. ([Source: TechCrunch, 2025-10-29](https://techcrunch.com/2025/10/29/us-signs-collaboration-agreements-with-japan-and-south-korea-for-ai-chips-and-biotech/))

**Quad and AUKUS Frameworks**: Both the US-Japan-India-Australia Quad and the US-UK-Australia AUKUS security alliance have incorporated semiconductor supply chain security into their strategic agendas. According to The Diplomat (2021), proposals for a "Semi-Quad" semiconductor alliance aim to ensure supply chain resilience and deter aggression through economic interdependence via regional ally cooperation. ([Source: The Diplomat — Semi-Quad Alliance](https://thediplomat.com/2021/05/time-for-a-semi-quad-alliance/))

**Taiwan's New Southbound Policy+**: Taiwan has expanded economic linkages with Southeast and South Asia, combined with regional frameworks like the Quad, to build a multi-layered security network of "deterrence through interdependence." ([Source: PacForum](https://pacforum.org/publications/yl-blog-114-a-world-reliant-on-taiwans-semiconductor-industry-amid-chinese-aggression/))

### Three Strategic Effects on TSMC from the Allied Architecture

The multilateral allied system (Chip 4 / Quad / AUKUS / TPD) produces three strategic effects for TSMC:

1. **Demand Lock-In**: Allied nations' semiconductor policies (CHIPS Act $52.7B, EU Chips Act €43B, Japan semiconductor promotion ¥3.9 trillion) all treat TSMC as the flagship partner, giving TSMC the highest priority for fab construction subsidies and policy support within the allied circle.

2. **Economization of Security Commitments**: According to The Diplomat (2024), TSMC's massive investments in the US, Japan, and Europe make these countries' interests in Taiwan's security more concrete — no longer merely an abstract commitment to "freedom and openness" but hundreds of billions of dollars in direct economic interests.

3. **Technology Standards Authority**: Export control coordination under the Chip 4 framework (US-Japan-Netherlands trilateral EUV export restrictions) means TSMC's dependency on ASML EUV equipment flows only to allied nations, creating institutionalized guarantees for the technology barrier. ([Source: The Diplomat — Silicon Shield 2.0](https://thediplomat.com/2024/09/silicon-shield-2-0-a-taiwan-perspective/); [TechCrunch, 2025-10-29](https://techcrunch.com/2025/10/29/us-signs-collaboration-agreements-with-japan-and-south-korea-for-ai-chips-and-biotech/))

Wei's response to ally strategy in the Q4 2024 earnings call was notably cautious: "We have a very frank and open communication with the current government and with the future one also. I cannot say anything more than that." — this phrasing reflects TSMC's unique position within the allied system: it is the object everyone is competing to attract, but must also maintain a delicate balance between the US and China. ([Source: TSMC Q4 2024 Transcript](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/01/16/taiwan-semiconductor-manufacturing-tsm-q4-2024-ear/))

### Quantifying Industry Dependency on TSMC Advanced Processes

TSMC's advanced processes (sub-7nm) have penetrated virtually all global high-tech industries, forming a deep dependency structure. According to Hudson Institute (2025), "a significant disruption could affect $1.6 trillion, or roughly 8%, of America's annual gross domestic product" — an impact scale exceeding the 2008 financial crisis or 2020 COVID lockdowns. ([Source: Hudson Institute, 2025](https://www.hudson.org/technology/losing-taiwan-semiconductor-would-devastate-us-economy-riley-walters))

According to Vision of Humanity (2025), Taiwan accounts for 20% of global semiconductor manufacturing, 37% of logic chips, and 92% of advanced logic chips (sub-10nm). Taiwan and South Korea together produce 100% of sub-10nm semiconductors. ([Source: Vision of Humanity](https://www.visionofhumanity.org/the-worlds-dependency-on-taiwans-semiconductor-industry-is-increasing/))
