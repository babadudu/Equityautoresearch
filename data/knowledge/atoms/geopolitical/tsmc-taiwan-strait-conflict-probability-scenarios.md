**Key Interpretation**: CFR's "even chance" assessment requires careful reading — it refers to "China escalating pressure causing serious crisis" not "full invasion." According to Recorded Future, the probability of a full-scale invasion in the short term (2025–2026) remains low, but risk continues rising post-2027. The most likely near-term action is a maritime and airspace "quarantine," as its mobilization cost is low and interference effect is high.

### Insurance and Shipping Risk Data

According to Nature Communications (2025), the estimated expected trade disruption value from maritime chokepoint geopolitical risks is approximately $192 billion annually, with the Taiwan Strait and Suez Canal as primary risk sources. Economic losses from shipping delays, rerouting, higher insurance rates, and trade disruptions are estimated at $10.7 billion annually. ([Source: Nature Communications, 2025](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-025-65403-w))

### Taiwan Energy Vulnerability Amplifies Blockade Risk

According to Global Taiwan Institute (2026), Taiwan imports approximately 97% of its energy, and under a blockade scenario could maintain only approximately two weeks of energy supply. Taiwan Power Company accumulated losses exceeding NT$350 billion ($11.08 billion USD) from 2022–2024, reflecting financial pressure from energy price subsidies. ([Source: Global Taiwan Institute, 2026-02](https://globaltaiwan.org/2026/02/taiwans-bumpy-road-to-energy-resilience/))

| Scenario | Blockade Scope | Duration | TSMC Revenue Impact (Annualized) | Global Supply Chain Impact | Probability Assessment |
|----------|---------------|----------|----------------------------------|---------------------------|------------------------|

| Full blockade (naval/air quarantine) | Complete ban on imports/exports | 3–12 months | −60~80% ($69–92B) | Global semiconductor supply collapse, GDP loss $5T | Low (10–20% within 5 years) |
| Military conflict (invasion/bombardment) | Physical infrastructure attack | Uncertain | Taiwan capacity zeroed (~$92B, ~80% of total) | Global GDP loss ~$10T | Very low (<10% within 5 years) |

**Note**: The above are estimation models, not precise predictions. Probability assessments synthesize public evaluations from CFR, Recorded Future, and risk advisory firms. TSMC's overseas capacity (US + Japan + Germany) could maintain approximately $20–30B in annualized revenue under the most extreme scenarios, but would require years to expand to Taiwan's production scale.

According to Rhodium Group, a Taiwan Strait disruption would cause global economic losses of $2.7 trillion (year one), with the US bearing approximately $1.6 trillion of the impact. According to ISDP (2025), a chip traveling from design to consumer may cross 70 national borders — Taiwan as the most critical manufacturing node means its disruption would trigger cascading collapse across the global supply chain. ([Source: ISDP, 2025-04](https://www.isdp.eu/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/Brief-TSMC-Apr-15-2025-new.pdf); [Vision of Humanity](https://www.visionofhumanity.org/the-worlds-dependency-on-taiwans-semiconductor-industry-is-increasing/))
