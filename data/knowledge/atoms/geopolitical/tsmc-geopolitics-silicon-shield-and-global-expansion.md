TSMC sits at the epicenter of the US-China technology rivalry, utilizing its "Silicon Shield" status while under intense pressure to diversify its manufacturing footprint.

### The Silicon Shield Theory
The theory posits that TSMC’s control over 92% of the world’s advanced logic chips (<10nm) makes Taiwan too economically vital to be attacked without causing a global economic collapse. A Taiwan Strait disruption would risk ~$1.6 trillion in annual US GDP and ~$5 trillion in global economic loss (Source: https://rhg.com/research/taiwan-economic-disruptions/). However, critics argue this creates a "Silicon Trap" where China might accelerate its timeline as the US builds domestic capacity (Source: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/391522122_Silicon_Shield_or_Silicon_Trap?).

### Global Expansion Strategy
Under pressure to achieve "geographic flexibility," TSMC is investing heavily outside Taiwan:
- **United States (Arizona)**: A $165 billion total investment across 6 planned fabs. Fab 21 Phase 1 (N4P) has achieved a 92% yield, remarkably 4 percentage points higher than equivalent Taiwan facilities (Source: https://www.techpowerup.com/328123/tsmc-arizona-achieves-4-higher-yields-than-taiwanese-facilities-marking-progress-for-us-silicon-manufacturing).
- **Japan (Kumamoto)**: JASM (joint venture with Sony/Denso) received ~50% government subsidies. Its first fab began mass production in 2024.
- **Germany (Dresden)**: ESMC project targeting 22/28nm for automotive and industrial sectors, backed by the European Chips Act.

### Regulatory and Sanctions Environment
- **Export Controls**: US restrictions on sub-7nm AI chips to China have reduced TSMC’s China revenue from 22% (2020) to 9% (2024). C.C. Wei maintains that AI growth will continue "dramatically" even if the China market is unavailable (Source: https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/17/tsmc-tsm-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/).
- **Taiwan’s N-2 Rule**: Taiwan law mandates that TSMC’s overseas technology must be at least two generations behind its most advanced node in Taiwan to preserve the island's strategic value. While Fab 21 in Arizona will run 3nm in 2027, Taiwan will already be at the 1.4nm (A14) stage (Source: https://taiwannews.com.tw/news/6267804).
- **Supply Chain Vulnerability**: ASML's EUV tools in Taiwan are equipped with "remote disable" switches, ensuring that an occupying force could not gain operational control of the fabs (Source: https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2024-05-21/asml-can-disable-chip-making-machines-if-china-invades-taiwan).
