## Eli Lilly Business Model DNA

Eli Lilly is a pure-play innovative pharmaceutical company—it does not participate in generics or consumer health products. Its business model follows a virtuous cycle: high R&D investment → patent-protected pricing power → high gross margins → reinvestment into R&D. This model is distinct from diversified pharma peers and results in concentrated exposure to both clinical risk and commercial upside.

### R&D Scale as Competitive Moat

R&D investment is Lilly's most central competitive investment. CEO Dave Ricks stated: "We spend almost 25% of sales this year on R&D" and "We'll spend $14 billion this year total" ([All-In/Cheeky Pint, 2024](https://cheekypint.substack.com/p/dave-ricks-ceo-of-eli-lilly-on-glp)). He placed this in perspective: this amount is "above Germany and Japan as a nation and headed closer to the NIH" ([Chief Executive, 2025](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/)).

Ricks describes the binary nature of drug discovery: "It's literally like the card flipping over and you win the hand or you don't." He emphasizes what makes pharma unique among industries: "Our business is one of the few businesses that you do actual discovery... Something never happened before—and now it's happened." ([Chief Executive, 2025](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/)). The largest single R&D cost item is late-stage clinical trials: "Most of that is the last step."

### Manufacturing CapEx: $50B Five-Year Agenda

Capacity expansion is a second major investment axis. Lilly announced a "$50 billion CapEx agenda... over five years" ([T. Rowe Price, 2025](https://www.troweprice.com/en/us/insights/the-long-view-eli-lilly)) including four new factories. Ricks described the physical constraint: "We have announced investments of $23 billion in new capital in the United States for factories. We can't go faster. There's no more vendors to build plants faster than we're building right now" ([Economic Club DC, 2024-12-10](https://www.economicclub.org/events/david-ricks)).

By the 2026 JPMorgan Healthcare Conference, the investment was yielding results: "We brought a lot of capacity online and solved the supply issues. This gave prescribers and consumers a lot of confidence in starting therapy" ([PharmExec/JPM26, 2026-01](https://www.pharmexec.com/view/jp-morgan-2026-eli-lilly-continuing-glp1-success)). Lilly produced "more than 1.6 times the amount of salable incretin doses during the first half of 2025 when compared to the first half of 2024" ([CNBC/S&P Global, 2025](https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/research/2025/08/eli-lilly-set-to-leapfrog-novo-nordisk-in-weight-loss-drug-race)).

### LillyDirect: The DTC Distribution Breakthrough

Lilly's LillyDirect platform allows patients to purchase GLP-1 drugs directly with a credit card, bypassing insurance and pharmacy benefit managers (PBMs). Ricks describes the consumer experience: "People skip all this other morass and getting a 'this is not a bill' piece of paper. They're just like, 'Here's my Visa card number.'" ([All-In/Cheeky Pint, 2024](https://cheekypint.substack.com/p/dave-ricks-ceo-of-eli-lilly-on-glp)).

By January 2026, DTC had achieved remarkable scale. At the JPMorgan conference, Ricks announced: "Basically a million people a month go online and buy GLP-1 medicine directly from a drug manufacturer." He added that vials on LillyDirect had become "basically the second top-selling obesity drug next to Lilly Zepbound in an auto injector, and that's a profound achievement in one year" ([PharmExec/JPM26, 2026-01](https://www.pharmexec.com/view/jp-morgan-2026-eli-lilly-continuing-glp1-success)). The DTC channel reduces intermediary costs, accelerates patient access, and—critically—establishes a direct sales infrastructure for the upcoming oral GLP-1 launch.

### Oral GLP-1 Pricing Philosophy: Mass-Market Positioning

Orforglipron (oral GLP-1, expected ~2026) is positioned explicitly as a mass-market product. Ricks stated: "Our goal will be to reach the masses with that product, and so we need a pricing strategy in concert with that" ([Fortune Well, 2025-01](https://fortune.com/well/article/eli-lilly-weight-loss-pill-orforglipron-glp1-2026/)). The manufacturing logic supports lower pricing: "We all know the production systems for injectables have really been stretched... With oral technology we can make more and get this medicine to more people around the world" ([Fortune Well, 2025-01](https://fortune.com/well/article/eli-lilly-weight-loss-pill-orforglipron-glp1-2026/)).

Ricks distilled the pricing philosophy: "It's all about price at the pump. When people go to the pharmacy counter, how can they have a consistent and low cost for benefits that are helping their health?" ([Bloomberg/JPM, 2025-01-13](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/videos/2025-01-13/lilly-ceo-on-fighting-cancer-and-obesity-drug-pricing-video)). Medicare expansion of coverage further broadens access: "That opens up things pretty wide, and we'll see where we can go from there" ([CNBC, 2026-01-30](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/30/eli-lilly-ceo-david-ricks-medicare-coverage-obesity-pills.html)).

### Insulin Pricing Transparency: Rebuilding Trust

Ricks candidly acknowledged the historical insulin pricing problem: "Our actual net on insulin really hasn't changed. It's like $30–$40, but the list price got up to 275" and "We competed on this spread... We kept raising the list price" ([All-In/Cheeky Pint, 2024](https://cheekypint.substack.com/p/dave-ricks-ceo-of-eli-lilly-on-glp)). In 2023, Lilly proactively reduced Humalog's list price to $35, a move designed to rebuild public trust. This transparency-first posture has become part of Lilly's public identity under Ricks.
