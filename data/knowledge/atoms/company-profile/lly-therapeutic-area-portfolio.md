## Eli Lilly Therapeutic Area Portfolio

Lilly organizes its business around six therapeutic areas, each targeting major areas of global unmet medical need. As of 2025, the portfolio is heavily concentrated in cardiometabolic (GLP-1) but is actively diversifying into neuroscience and oncology for long-term durability.

### 1. Cardiometabolic (Diabetes & Obesity) — ~60% of 2025 Revenue

This is Lilly's core engine, driven by tirzepatide, a dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist. Two products:

- **Mounjaro** (tirzepatide for type 2 diabetes): 2025 revenue $23.0B — one of the best-selling drugs globally.
- **Zepbound** (tirzepatide for obesity): 2025 revenue $13.5B — reached this scale within two years of launch.

CEO Dave Ricks described tirzepatide's clinical impact: "It's reducing weight on average, 20% of body weight" ([Fortune, 2024](https://fortune.com/2024/11/13/eli-lilly-ceo-dave-ricks/)). The mechanism represents a generational leap from earlier GLP-1 monotherapy; the dual-agonist approach produces superior weight loss outcomes versus semaglutide monotherapy in head-to-head data.

**Generational transition:** Trulicity (dulaglutide, prior-generation GLP-1) is being deliberately cannibalized by Mounjaro—revenue fell from $7.1B in 2023 to $5.3B in 2024 (−26%). Ricks views this replacement as both medically appropriate and strategically necessary. The willingness to accelerate self-cannibalization reflects Lilly's confidence in tirzepatide's superiority and its focus on clinical outcomes over portfolio protection.

### 2. Oncology — ~9% of 2025 Revenue

- **Verzenio** (abemaciclib, CDK4/6 inhibitor for breast cancer): 2025 revenue $5.7B. Positioned in early-stage breast cancer adjuvant therapy, a growing indication that extends the addressable population beyond metastatic disease.
- Pipeline breadth: Ricks cited "60+ cancer studies" underway ([Chief Executive, 2025](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/)), spanning immunotherapy, targeted therapy, and combinations.

### 3. Immunology — ~5% of 2025 Revenue

- **Taltz** (ixekizumab for psoriasis and psoriatic arthritis): 2024 revenue $3.3B (+18% YoY).
- Next-generation: lebrikizumab in atopic dermatitis is expanding the immunology franchise into dermatology adjacencies.

### 4. Neuroscience — Largest Long-Term Optionality

- **Kisunla** (donanemab for early Alzheimer's disease): FDA-approved in 2024, representing ~30 years of Lilly research in Alzheimer's. Ricks: "We've been studying... Alzheimer's for basically 30 years at Lilly" ([T. Rowe Price, 2025](https://www.troweprice.com/en/us/insights/the-long-view-eli-lilly)).
- Clinical results: amyloid plaque clearance of "more than 80% reduction in like a year. Even to undetectable levels" ([T. Rowe Price, 2025](https://www.troweprice.com/en/us/insights/the-long-view-eli-lilly)).
- Ricks framed the next decade as "the decade of the brain... brain health is probably the biggest category" ([Fortune, 2024](https://fortune.com/2024/11/13/eli-lilly-ceo-dave-ricks/)).

### 5. Cardiovascular/Metabolic — Partnership with Boehringer Ingelheim

- **Jardiance** (empagliflozin, SGLT2 inhibitor for diabetes and heart failure): 2024 revenue $3.3B (+22% YoY), co-promoted with Boehringer Ingelheim. SGLT2 inhibitors and GLP-1 agonists are increasingly used in combination, giving Lilly a comprehensive cardiometabolic portfolio spanning two drug classes.

### Portfolio Strategy: Concentration Now, Diversification Building

Lilly's 2025 revenue is highly concentrated (tirzepatide alone represents ~45%+ of total revenue), which creates both upside and concentration risk. The strategic logic: use tirzepatide cash flows to fund Alzheimer's and oncology pipelines that can sustain growth post-tirzepatide patent expiry. The neuroscience bet is particularly long-dated but potentially transformative given the scale of Alzheimer's unmet need and Lilly's unique 30-year data advantage in the indication.
