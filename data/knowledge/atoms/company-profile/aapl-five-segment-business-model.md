## Apple's Five Business Segments (FY2025)

Apple's business is organized into five segments, each playing a distinct role within its broader ecosystem.

---

### iPhone — $209.6B (50.4% of revenue)

iPhone is the central entry point to Apple's ecosystem and the world's best-selling consumer electronics product. FY2025 iPhone revenue was $209.6B, up 4.2% year-over-year, with record shipments of 247 million units. iPhone's success stems not just from the hardware itself, but from its role as the "hub" of the Apple ecosystem — every iPhone connects users to the App Store, iCloud, Apple Pay, Apple Music, and other services.

Tim Cook reflected on Apple's product philosophy in a 2026 Fortune interview: "That one plus one is equal to three, not two. That if you share an idea and debate it, it gets bigger and better" ([Fortune, 2026-03-11](https://fortune.com/2026/03/11/apple-ceo-tim-cook-late-predecessor-steve-jobs-unusual-advice-never-ask-what-i-would-do/)). This "the whole is greater than the sum of its parts" ethos is embodied in iPhone's integration with the Apple ecosystem.

Key product milestones:
- **iPhone 15 Pro (2023):** First titanium chassis, A17 Pro chip, USB-C port (complying with EU's USB-C mandate)
- **iPhone 16 (2024):** First iPhone series with integrated Apple Intelligence features

iPhone ASP is estimated at approximately $850–$900 (derived from $209.6B revenue / 247M units), roughly 1.5–2x the ASP of leading Android flagships.

---

### Services — $109.2B (26.2% of revenue)

Services is Apple's fastest-growing segment and its highest-margin business. FY2025 Services revenue was $109.2B, up 13.5% year-over-year, with gross margins of approximately 70% — far above the ~36% hardware gross margin.

Services encompasses:
- **App Store** (largest Services revenue contributor; take rate 15–30%)
- **iCloud** storage subscriptions
- **Apple Music** (over 100 million subscribers)
- **Apple TV+** streaming
- **Apple Pay** transaction fees
- **AppleCare** extended warranty
- **Apple Arcade**, **Apple News+**, **Apple Fitness+**
- **Google search revenue sharing** (estimated ~$20B/year, disclosed in DOJ antitrust proceedings)

As of end of FY2025, paid subscriptions across Apple's platform exceeded 975 million.

Tim Cook stated on the Q4 FY2025 earnings call (2025-10-30): "Services set an all-time revenue record, reflecting the strength of our ecosystem and the value our customers find in our offerings."

The structural significance: Services has grown from 8.5% of Apple's revenue in FY2015 to 26.2% in FY2025. This shift is fundamentally changing Apple's margin profile — as Services revenue grows, blended gross margins expand and earnings become more recurring and predictable.

---

### Mac — $33.7B (8.1% of revenue)

Mac has been revitalized by Apple Silicon. FY2025 revenue was $33.7B, up 12.4% year-over-year — the second-fastest growing segment after Services. Since the M1 chip launch in late 2020, Mac has transformed from "a derivative of the Intel ecosystem" into the "flagship vehicle for Apple's chip ecosystem."

The M-series chips deliver a performance-per-watt ratio that significantly outperforms Intel and AMD. MacBook Air achieves over 18 hours of battery life in a fanless design. The M3, M3 Pro, and M3 Max series extended Mac's lead in the professional creator market (video editing, 3D rendering, AI development).

Apple SVP of Hardware Engineering John Ternus said at WWDC 2023: "Apple silicon has completely transformed the Mac experience, and we're just getting started" ([WWDC 2023 Keynote](https://www.apple.com/apple-events/), 2023-06-05).

---

### iPad — $28.0B (6.7% of revenue)

FY2025 iPad revenue was $28.0B, up 5.0% year-over-year, ending two consecutive years of decline. The 2024 launch of the M4-powered iPad Pro (Apple's thinnest product ever) and the new iPad Air reinvigorated upgrade demand. iPad maintains dominance in the education market and holds over 50% revenue share in the premium tablet segment.

---

### Wearables, Home & Accessories — $35.7B (8.6% of revenue)

This segment includes Apple Watch, AirPods, HomePod, Apple TV, Beats, and accessories. FY2025 revenue was $35.7B, down 3.6% year-over-year — the only declining segment. Apple Watch maintains approximately 50% revenue share in the global smartwatch market; AirPods holds a leadership position in the TWS (true wireless stereo) earphone market. Growth has stalled due to market maturation and a slower pace of innovation.

Tim Cook, in a 2023 interview on the Dua Lipa podcast, addressed tech product usage: "I don't want people using them too much... Go out and spend it in nature" ([9to5Mac, 2023-11-17](https://9to5mac.com/2023/11/17/tim-cook-dua-lipa-interview/)). This reflects Apple's brand positioning around "technology in balance with life" — a differentiation strategy that also underpins Apple Watch's health and wellness features.
