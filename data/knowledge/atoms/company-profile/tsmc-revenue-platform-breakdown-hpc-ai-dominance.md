## TSMC Revenue Platform Breakdown: HPC/AI Dominance and Diversified End Markets

### HPC (High Performance Computing) — Primary Growth Engine

HPC has surpassed smartphones to become TSMC's largest revenue platform. In full-year 2024, HPC represented 51% of revenue; by Q2 2025, it climbed to 60%. HPC encompasses AI accelerators (GPU/TPU/ASIC), CPUs, FPGAs, and networking chips. NVIDIA's H100/B100/GB200 series, AMD's MI300X, and Broadcom's custom AI ASICs are all manufactured by TSMC.

CEO C.C. Wei disclosed a key growth data point in the Q4 2025 earnings call: AI accelerator revenue is expected to grow at a 2024–2029 CAGR of 54–56%, significantly above the prior estimate of 45%. ([Source: TSPA Semiconductor Substack](https://tspasemiconductor.substack.com/p/from-tsmcs-earnings-to-the-ustaiwan))

Wei identified a critical structural feature of HPC demand in the Q2 2024 earnings call: "Whether AI applications use ASICs or graphics processors, they all require very leading-edge technology and are working with TSMC." — Regardless of how the AI chip routing debate (GPU vs. ASIC) resolves, the technology requirements converge on TSMC advanced processes, making TSMC the indispensable manufacturer for the entire AI ecosystem. ([Source: TSMC Q2 2024 Transcript](https://investor.tsmc.com/english/encrypt/files/encrypt_file/reports/2024-08/5122725a56670882d777a8e8bfe0ed247cc55330/TSMC%202Q24%20Transcript.pdf))

Wei also described his demand verification methodology: "I spend a lot of time in the last three to four months talking to my customers and end customers' customer. I want to make sure that my customers' demand is real." He noted a telling downstream signal: "they are making even more money than TSMC" — the profitability of downstream AI service providers validates demand sustainability. ([Source: The Next Platform, 2026-01-16](https://www.nextplatform.com/compute/2026/01/16/tsmc-has-no-choice-but-to-trust-the-sunny-ai-forecasts-of-its-customers/4092173); [TSPA Semiconductor](https://tspasemiconductor.substack.com/p/from-tsmcs-earnings-to-the-ustaiwan))

A unique competitive advantage source was revealed in the Q3 2025 earnings call: "As process technology complexity increases, the engagement lead time with customer is now at least 2 to 3 years in advance. Therefore, we probably get the deepest and widest look possible in the industry." — A 2–3 year customer engagement lead time gives TSMC unmatched visibility into future technology demand, far exceeding any competitor. ([Source: TSMC Q3 2025 Transcript](https://investor.tsmc.com/english/encrypt/files/encrypt_file/reports/2025-10/6860312f04fd291d0f26b46c1234f84e6332717e/TSMC%203Q25%20Transcript.pdf))

### Smartphones — Stable Base

The smartphone platform (35% of full-year 2024 revenue) is anchored by Apple as the largest single customer. Apple's A-series and M-series chips are exclusively manufactured by TSMC, and Apple is typically TSMC's first adopter of each new process generation. ([Source: TSMC Q4 2024 Management Report](https://investor.tsmc.com/english/encrypt/files/encrypt_file/reports/2025-01/2d8b2bb6fc3b5887d24ae0635f639c1cdca834f3/4Q24ManagementReport.pdf))

Morris Chang's autobiography (Volume 2) disclosed that in 2010, Jeff Williams proactively expressed Apple's interest in having TSMC manufacture iPhone logic chips, and in 2011 Tim Cook told Chang not to worry about Intel competition. This relationship illustrates the deep interdependency between TSMC and its anchor smartphone customers.

MediaTek and Qualcomm flagship smartphone chips are also produced at TSMC. In TSMC's 2024 Annual Report, the 3nm process node represented 18% of revenue, described as "driven by smartphone and High Performance Computing applications." ([Source: TSMC 2024 Annual Report](https://investor.tsmc.com/sites/ir/annual-report/2024/2024%20Annual%20Report_E.pdf))

### IoT and Automotive — Long-Tail Growth

IoT (~6% of revenue) and automotive (~5%) represent smaller shares but provide a stable demand foundation for mature processes (28nm–16nm). Automotive chips carry stringent quality requirements (zero-defect targets) and extended qualification cycles, creating meaningful barriers to entry.

In the Q3 2025 earnings call, Wei emphasized the importance of customer breadth: "We have more than 500 different customers across all the end market segments." — 500+ customers prevents over-dependence on any single application domain. ([Source: TSMC Q3 2025 Transcript](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/17/tsmc-tsm-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/))

### Advanced Packaging — Emerging Structural Moat

CoWoS and InFO advanced packaging have become a critical TSMC differentiator. Capacity expanded from ~13,000 wafers/month at end-2023 to ~70,000–75,000 wafers/month by end-2025. CFO Wendell Huang noted that "roughly 10-20% of capex in 2025 will go into advanced packaging/test facilities" — advanced packaging has graduated from an add-on service to a strategic investment priority. ([Source: TechSoda](https://techsoda.substack.com/p/explainer-tsmcs-2024-annual-report); [App Economy Insights, 2025](https://www.appeconomyinsights.com/p/tsmc-ai-megatrend-engine))

### Process Node Revenue Mix (2024)

Advanced processes (≤7nm) represented 69% of 2024 revenue. Within that: 3nm = 18%, 5nm = ~37%. This mix shift toward leading-edge nodes is the structural driver of TSMC's improving gross margin profile, as advanced nodes command significantly higher wafer ASPs.

### Revenue Concentration Risk

Top-10 customers account for 76% of revenue (2024), up from 70% (2023) and 68% (2022). Apple ~22%, NVIDIA <12%, AMD <10%. While concentration risk is real, the flip side is that these are the world's most strategically important chip companies — and their growing dependency on TSMC advanced manufacturing deepens the moat rather than creating a vulnerability, particularly given the 2–3 year lead time structure for process engagement.
