## Google (Alphabet) Unit Economics and Cost Structure

### Key Unit Economics Metrics

**Search ARPU**
With ~4.6 billion global monthly active users, Search ad revenue of ~$253B (2025E) implies an annual advertising value per user (ARPU) of approximately **$55** (+25% vs. 2024's ~$44). US ARPU is significantly higher (estimated $180+), while emerging markets (India, Southeast Asia) are materially lower.

**YouTube ARPU**
YouTube has 2.5B+ monthly active users. 2025 total revenue (ads + subscriptions) exceeded $60B, implying ARPU of ~$24/year. Premium subscribers (~100M users, ~$139/year annual fee) command significantly higher ARPU.

**Cloud Customer Retention**
Google Cloud's Net Revenue Retention Rate is estimated at >120%, meaning existing customers naturally increase spending by 20%+ per year (land-and-expand model). Ashkenazi corroborated: "Google Cloud backlog increased 18% sequentially in Q2 and 38% year over year, reaching $106 billion." ([Q2 2025 Earnings Call, Jul 2025](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/07/23/alphabet-googl-q2-2025-earnings-call-transcript/))

**TAC (Traffic Acquisition Cost)**
2024 TAC was approximately $51B (~22% of Search + Network revenue), primarily paid to Apple (Safari default search) and Android OEMs. The Apple payment is estimated at ~$20B annually—a critical but expensive component of Google's search moat.

**AI Inference Cost Deflation**
Pichai revealed a dramatic cost optimization figure at Q4 2025: "We were able to lower Gemini serving unit costs by 78% over 2025 through model optimizations." ([Q4 2025 Earnings Call, Feb 2026](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/04/alphabet-googl-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)) This is the critical enabling condition for AI Overviews to expand usage scenarios without sacrificing margins—AI search's marginal cost is falling rapidly.

**Gemini User Scale**
"The Gemini app now has more than 450 million monthly active users with strong engagement growth." ([Q3 2025 Earnings Call, Nov 2025](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/11/27/alphabet-googl-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/))

### Cost Structure Evolution in the AI Era

The AI era introduces two new major cost components:

**1. Inference Compute Costs (CapEx + Depreciation)**
Ashkenazi acknowledged: "Depreciation increased $1.3 billion year over year to $5 billion, reflecting a growth rate of 35%." ([Q2 2025 Earnings Call, Jul 2025](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/07/23/alphabet-googl-q2-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)) However, the 78% unit cost reduction is offsetting this pressure.

**2. Content Licensing Costs**
Pichai disclosed at DealBook: "We are licensing data from Reddit, we license data from AP, we license data from New York Times." ([NYT DealBook Summit, Dec 2024](https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/sundar-pichai-on-a-i-regulation-and-whats-next-for-google/id1718472373?i=1000679408558)) This implies content acquisition costs may become a new line item in the search business as AI Overviews increasingly relies on high-quality sourced content.

Pichai described AI's impact on overall efficiency: "It's an accelerator. People will be able to do more, which means maybe we'll create new products." ([Bloomberg Tech Summit, June 2025](https://singjupost.com/transcript-of-sundar-pichai-on-future-of-ai-antitrust-and-privacy/))

### CapEx Investment Cycle and Returns

CapEx of ~$75B (original 2025 plan) was raised mid-year. Ashkenazi at Q1 2025: "We still expect to invest approximately $75 billion in CapEx this year." ([Q1 2025 Earnings Call, Apr 2025](https://www.rev.com/transcripts/alphabet-q1-2025-earnings-call)) At Q2, revised upward: "We now expect to invest approximately $85 billion in CapEx in 2025, up from $75 billion estimate." ([Q2 2025 Earnings Call, Jul 2025](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/07/23/alphabet-googl-q2-2025-earnings-call-transcript/))

Pichai's investment philosophy is asymmetric: "The risk of underinvesting is dramatically greater than the risk of overinvesting for us here, even in scenarios where if it turns out that we are overinvesting, we clearly — these are infrastructure which are widely useful for us." ([CNBC, 2024](https://www.cnbc.com/2024/07/23/alphabet-moves-past-stumbles-in-ai-to-deliver-positive-story-to-investors.html))

Ashkenazi addressed ROIC concerns at Q3 2025: "We have a rigorous framework and approach by which we evaluate these long-term investments." ([Q3 2025 Earnings Call, Nov 2025](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/11/27/alphabet-googl-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/))

**2026 CapEx escalation:** Ashkenazi announced: "For the full year 2026, we expect CapEx to be in the range of $175 billion to $185 billion." ([Q4 2025 Earnings Call, Feb 2026](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/04/alphabet-googl-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)) This is double the 2025 level, reflecting accelerating AI infrastructure competition. However, Cloud backlog of $240B (4x+ annual Cloud revenue) provides visibility into demand.

### Shareholder Return Mechanism

Ashkenazi confirmed at Q1 2025: "We returned value to shareholders...in the form of $15.1 billion in share repurchases" and "Our board declared a 5% increase in our quarterly dividend." ([Q1 2025 Earnings Call, Apr 2025](https://www.rev.com/transcripts/alphabet-q1-2025-earnings-call)) Full-year 2024 buybacks totaled $62.2B (~3% of market cap). Combined buybacks and dividends imply an annualized shareholder return yield of approximately 3–4%.
