## TSMC Organizational Culture: Core Values, Talent Strategy, and Incentive Architecture

TSMC's organizational culture is inseparable from the vision of founder Morris Chang, anchored in four core values: **Integrity** (誠信正直), **Commitment** (承諾), **Innovation** (創新), and **Customer Trust** (客戶信任). These are not merely stated principles — they are operationalized through deliberate talent strategies, a multi-layered incentive system, and a counter-cyclical investment philosophy that has defined the company across decades.

### Employee Base and Retention

As of December 31, 2024, TSMC employed **83,825 people globally**, an increase of 7,347 (+9.6%) year-over-year. The company operates 12 300mm wafer fabs and 4 200mm fabs in Taiwan, plus overseas facilities in the United States, Japan, China, and Germany, making it one of Taiwan's largest private employers. ([TSMC 2024 Annual Report](https://investor.tsmc.com/sites/ir/annual-report/2024/2024%20Annual%20Report_E.pdf); [TweakTown, 2024](https://www.tweaktown.com/news/104783/tsmc-has-24-semiconductor-fabs-under-construction-worldwide-83-825-employees-in-total/index.html))

Annual employee turnover has historically been maintained at **4–5%**, far below the global semiconductor industry average of approximately 10–15%. Even during the fierce global tech talent competition of 2021, TSMC's overall turnover reached only 6.8%, though the new-hire turnover was higher at 17.6%, reflecting the adaptation challenges of high-intensity fab shift work. ([Taiwan News, 2022-07-02](https://www.taiwannews.com.tw/news/4586957); [TSMC ESG Report](https://esg.tsmc.com/en-US/sustainability-roles/admired-employer))

Morris Chang has articulated the structural basis for Taiwan's talent advantage. In a Brookings Institution lecture, he stated: *"Dedicated engineers are Taiwan's advantage."* He further explained the geographic compactness that enables efficient engineer deployment: *"TSMC's three manufacturing centers often have thousands of engineers whose location assignment changes but who do not have to move their family."* — a logistical advantage that is structurally impossible to replicate in the geographically dispersed fab footprint of the United States. ([Brookings Institution, April 2022](https://www.brookings.edu/wp-content/uploads/2022/04/Vying-for-Talent-Morris-Chang-20220414.pdf))

Chang also articulated a counter-intuitive layoff philosophy in the Acquired podcast that reveals deep respect for human capital: *"People will not respect us if we lay off by performance ratings. Because it's very subjective."* And: *"If you need the people back within a year, you shouldn't lay off."* During the 2008–2009 financial crisis, this philosophy enabled TSMC to retain its core engineering team, preserving the institutional knowledge needed for the subsequent technology surge. ([Acquired Podcast, January 2025](https://www.acquired.fm/episodes/tsmc-founder-morris-chang))

### Compensation Structure: Three-Layer Model

TSMC's compensation system operates on three layers: **fixed salary + performance bonus + employee profit sharing**. The profit-sharing component is the most powerful retention and motivation tool.

Under TSMC's corporate charter, **not less than 1%** of annual earnings must be allocated to employee profit sharing, while director compensation is capped at 0.3%. ([TSMC 2024 Annual Report](https://investor.tsmc.com/sites/ir/annual-report/2024/2024%20Annual%20Report_E.pdf); [TSMC PR, 2024](https://pr.tsmc.com/english/news/3104))

For fiscal year 2024, total employee profit sharing reached **NT$70.3 billion** (~US$2.1 billion), distributed in cash in July 2025 — a historic figure reflecting TSMC's record profitability during the AI supercycle. This represented approximately 32% growth from 2023's ~NT$53.4 billion. ([TSMC 2025 AGM Agenda](https://investor.tsmc.com/sites/ir/shareholders-meeting/2025-06-03/2025AGM_Agenda_wmn.pdf); [SemiWiki Forum](https://semiwiki.com/forum/threads/tsmc-board-of-directors-meeting-resolutions-2024.19576/))

CFO Wendell Huang noted in the annual report that the significant increase in G&A expenses was driven by two main factors: *"The increase in G&A was driven by overseas fab startup costs and employee profit sharing."* G&A rose 35.6% year-over-year to NT$25,425M in 2024, with employee profit sharing expanding in direct proportion to net income growth. ([TechSoda](https://techsoda.substack.com/p/explainer-tsmcs-2024-annual-report))

Additional compensation components include allowances, cash bonuses, tuition assistance, referral bonuses, an Employee Stock Purchase Plan (ESPP), an Employee Stock Ownership Trust (ESOT), and retirement contributions. ([TSMC ESG Report](https://esg.tsmc.com/en-US/sustainability-roles/admired-employer))

### Equity Incentives: Restricted Stock Awards (RSA) with ESG Linkage

Since 2022, TSMC has replaced stock options with **Restricted Stock Awards (RSA)** as its primary equity incentive vehicle. In fiscal year 2023, **2,960,000 RSA shares** were granted; the authorized upper limit for fiscal year 2024 was **4,185,000 shares** (ordinary shares).

A distinctive feature of TSMC's RSA program is its **ESG adjustment mechanism**: the Compensation and Human Resources Development Committee may adjust the RSA grant quantity by up to ±10% based on the company's ESG achievements. This directly links executive and senior employee compensation to sustainability targets, embedding ESG performance into the incentive architecture rather than treating it as a separate compliance obligation. ([TSMC PR, 2024](https://pr.tsmc.com/english/news/3104); [Abachy, 2024](https://abachy.com/news/tsmc-board-directors-approves-key-resolutions-2024-meeting))

### Global Cultural Building: Intercultural Programs and Flexible Benefits

As TSMC's overseas expansion accelerated — Arizona (USA), Kumamoto (Japan), Dresden (Germany) — cross-cultural management emerged as a core organizational challenge. In 2024, TSMC launched the **Intercultural Learning Program**, structured around eight themes including cross-cultural awareness, adaptability, and inclusive leadership, targeting the global employee base.

In parallel, TSMC introduced the **tFlex Global Flexible Benefits Program**, providing each full-time employee with **US$250 per year** in flexible benefit points, applicable across four categories: medical insurance, family care, wellness, and development & volunteering. By November 2024, the program had received **81,828 applications** totaling approximately **NT$4.7 billion**. ([TSMC ESG Report](https://esg.tsmc.com/en-US/sustainability-roles/admired-employer); [TSMC ESG Articles](https://esg.tsmc.com/en-US/articles/334))

CEO C.C. Wei articulated the globalization vision in a CommonWealth Magazine interview: *"Turning Taiwan's TSMC into the World's TSMC."* — signaling that geographic expansion demands parallel cultural internationalization, not just capacity deployment. ([CommonWealth Magazine, 2025-07-10](https://english.cw.com.tw/article/article.action?id=4215))

Wei reinforced a collaborative organizational philosophy in a Fortune Asia interview, framing TSMC's success as an ecosystem achievement: *"The semiconductor industry has never fought alone. Our success is built on the joint efforts of our colleagues, our customers, and our suppliers."* ([Fortune Asia, 2025-07-30](https://fortune.com/asia/2025/07/30/tsmc-ai-boom-cc-wei-wendell-huang-global-500/))

### Counter-Cyclical Investment as Cultural Expression

One of the clearest expressions of TSMC's culture is its willingness to invest aggressively during industry downturns. During 2023, when global semiconductor demand was weak and consumer electronics inventory was bloated, most industry peers reduced capital expenditure. TSMC maintained full-year CapEx of approximately **NT$850 billion (~US$28 billion)**, pressing forward with the Arizona and Kumamoto facilities.

For the 2025–2026 cycle, facing planned CapEx of **$52–56 billion**, Wei personally visited all major cloud service provider (CSP) customers to validate demand before committing. His conclusion: *"I also double checked their financial status — they are very rich."* ([The Next Platform, 2026-01-16](https://www.nextplatform.com/compute/2026/01/16/tsmc-has-no-choice-but-to-trust-the-sunny-ai-forecasts-of-its-customers/4092173))

This counter-cyclical discipline paid off when AI demand exploded in 2024, allowing TSMC to capture a 34% revenue increase because capacity had been pre-positioned. The Semiconductor Industry Association (SIA) recognized both Wei and former Chairman Mark Liu with the **Robert N. Noyce Award** — the industry's highest honor — for their contributions to the global semiconductor ecosystem. ([SIA](https://www.semiconductors.org/tsmcs-dr-c-c-wei-and-dr-mark-liu-to-receive-sias-highest-honor/))

### Cultural Moat Summary

TSMC's organizational culture constitutes a durable competitive moat through several reinforcing mechanisms:

1. **Profit-sharing alignment**: 83,000+ employees share directly in TSMC's profitability, creating company-wide incentive alignment unusual at this scale.
2. **Low voluntary attrition**: 4–5% annual turnover preserves institutional knowledge and process expertise that took decades to accumulate.
3. **Geographic talent density**: Taiwan's compact geography enables engineer reassignment without family relocation, a structural efficiency that overseas fabs cannot replicate.
4. **ESG-linked equity**: RSA adjustments tied to sustainability performance embed long-term thinking into the compensation structure.
5. **Counter-cyclical discipline**: Willingness to invest through downturns — rooted in Chang's "don't lay off unless you won't need them back" philosophy — ensures TSMC emerges from every cycle with a larger technology lead.
