## Amazon's Six Business Segments (FY2025)

Amazon's business is organized into six distinct revenue streams, each functioning as an independent flywheel that also reinforces the others.

---

### 1. E-Commerce Retail (Online Stores + Physical Stores) — FY2025 Revenue $291.9B, 40.7% of Total

Amazon's retail business is the world's largest e-commerce platform, offering direct (1P) sales across hundreds of millions of SKUs. Founded in 1995 as an online bookstore, Amazon progressively expanded into the "Everything Store." The core retail strategy centers on a three-pillar value proposition: low prices, wide selection, and fast delivery.

The logistics network includes over 110 fulfillment centers, over 1,000 delivery stations, and a nationwide same-day delivery hub footprint.

Jeff Bezos articulated the enduring retail logic: *"There are three things that will never change: customers will always want lower prices, faster delivery, and more selection. I can guarantee that no customer ten years from now will say, 'I love Amazon, I just wish the prices were higher.'"* ([2012 re:Invent Keynote](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=O_hy2Q-x-xo))

**Physical Stores** includes Whole Foods Market (acquired 2017 for $13.7B), Amazon Go, and Amazon Fresh. Whole Foods gave Amazon a physical retail entry point and fresh grocery supply chain capabilities.

---

### 2. Third-Party Seller Services — FY2025 Revenue $172.2B, 24.0% of Total

Third-party sellers account for over 60% of total units sold on Amazon's platform. Amazon provides Fulfillment by Amazon (FBA), advertising tools, and brand management services, charging commissions and service fees in return.

This segment is the core of Amazon's flywheel: more sellers → more selection → more customers. Third-party unit share grew from 3% in 2000 to over 60% in 2025, the most powerful empirical validation of the platform's self-reinforcing model.

The effective take rate across commissions + FBA fees + advertising is approximately 15%, and has been gradually rising.

---

### 3. Amazon Web Services (AWS) — FY2025 Revenue $128.7B, 18.0% of Total

AWS is the world's largest cloud infrastructure provider, offering over 200 services spanning compute, storage, databases, AI/ML, analytics, and security. Founded in 2006 from an internal initiative to externalize Amazon's infrastructure capabilities — a quintessential expression of Bezos's "Day 1" philosophy.

AWS's competitive moat rests on three pillars:
- **Scale advantages**: 31% global market share drives structural cost leadership
- **Service breadth**: 200+ services create deep customer lock-in
- **Global infrastructure**: 34 geographic regions provide compliance and latency coverage

Andy Jassy, who built AWS from 2006 to 2021 before becoming Amazon CEO, explained the AI infrastructure thesis: *"You have to build the right set of primitives and that's what we've been doing with SageMaker and Bedrock. You have to have your infrastructure modernized for AI."* ([SiliconANGLE, Dec 2024](https://siliconangle.com/2024/12/05/exclusive-amazon-ceo-andy-jassy-reveals-aws-strategy-building-enterprise-ai-platform/)) — infrastructure modernization and AI adoption are not alternatives but mutual prerequisites.

**Q4 2025 Performance**: AWS revenue growth accelerated to 24% YoY (fastest in 13 quarters), reaching a $142B annualized run rate with a 35% operating margin. Jassy on the Q4 2025 earnings call: *"AWS growth continued to accelerate to 24%, the fastest we've seen in thirteen quarters. AWS is now a $142 billion annualized run rate business."* ([Q4 2025 Earnings Call](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/05/amazon-amzn-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/))

Amazon's proprietary chip business (Graviton + Trainium) crossed $10B in annual revenue run rate, growing triple digits YoY: *"Our chips business, inclusive of Graviton and Trainium, is now over $10 billion in annual revenue run rate growing triple-digit percentages year over year."*

---

### 4. Advertising Services — FY2025 Revenue $68.6B, 9.6% of Total

Amazon's advertising business has been the fastest-growing segment over the past five years (FY2020: $21.5B → FY2025: $68.6B, ~26% CAGR). Its core advantage: Amazon owns the world's largest purchase-intent database. Users searching on Amazon already have explicit buying intent, which drives conversion rates and ROAS materially above Google Search and Meta.

Key developments:
- **Prime Video ads** (launched 2025): Expands addressable ad inventory into streaming video
- **Amazon DSP**: Allows advertisers to place Amazon shopping-data-targeted ads off-platform

CFO Brian Olsavsky noted the scale of advertising growth: *"Advertising revenue grew 22% in the fourth quarter and we added over $12 billion of incremental revenue in 2025 alone."* ([Q4 2025 Earnings Call](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/05/amazon-amzn-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)) — the single-year advertising increment alone represents nearly one-tenth of AWS's total annual revenue, illustrating the flywheel accelerating profit diversification.

---

### 5. Subscription Services — FY2025 Revenue $49.6B, 6.9% of Total

Includes Amazon Prime membership fees, Audible, Kindle Unlimited, Amazon Music, and Prime Video subscriptions. Prime is the adhesive of Amazon's flywheel — over 200 million paid members globally, with Prime members averaging ~$1,400/year in spending vs. ~$600 for non-members.

Prime Video has become a top-5 global streaming platform, backed by exclusive sports rights including NFL Thursday Night Football.

Bezos explained Prime's strategic logic in his 2016 Letter to Shareholders: *"We want Prime to be such a good value, you'd be irresponsible not to be a member."* ([2016 Letter to Shareholders](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1018724/000119312517120198/d373368dex991.htm))

---

### 6. Other (Exploratory) — FY2025 Revenue $5.9B, 0.8% of Total

Includes Project Kuiper (satellite broadband, in development) and Zoox (autonomous vehicles, in development). Amazon Care has been shut down. This segment reflects Amazon's ongoing bet-placing on long-duration bets consistent with its Day 1 culture.
