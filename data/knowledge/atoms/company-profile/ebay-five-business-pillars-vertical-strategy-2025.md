## eBay's Five Business Pillars and Vertical Focus Strategy

eBay's marketplace is organized around five strategically distinct business pillars. CEO Jamie Iannone summarized the overarching mission in eBay's 2021 annual report: "Every day, around the world, we connect people and build communities to create economic opportunity for all" ([LinkedIn, 2021](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/empowering-economic-opportunity-all-jamie-iannone)). The platform hosts over 2.4 billion active listings, serving 134 million active buyers and 18–20 million active sellers.

Iannone's core strategy is category-by-category deep cultivation — investing disproportionate product and marketing resources into verticals where eBay has structural advantages (unique/hard-to-find inventory, high-trust needs, enthusiast communities) rather than competing head-on with Amazon in commodity goods.

---

### 1. Auto Parts & Accessories (~12% GMV, $10B+)

eBay is one of the world's largest online auto parts marketplaces, with over 400 million parts listings. Iannone quantified this category in the 2021 annual report: "40 million buyers with over $10 billion in GMV annually" ([LinkedIn, 2021](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/empowering-economic-opportunity-all-jamie-iannone)). The category serves a complete ecosystem from DIY owners to professional mechanics, spanning OEM replacement parts to performance modifications. In 2024, auto parts grew 8% YoY.

Iannone emphasized in the Q4 2024 earnings call that eBay is actively "Reestablishing eBay as a trusted place to shop for auto parts" ([Digital Commerce 360, 2025-03-10](https://www.digitalcommerce360.com/2025/03/10/ebay-sales-revenue-gmv-q4-2024/)). In 2024, eBay also acquired Caramel — a vehicle transaction platform — extending eBay Motors into full vehicle sales beyond just parts.

Auto parts exemplifies eBay's vertical focus strategy most clearly: a category where unique inventory breadth, trust mechanisms, and specialist buyer demand create defensible differentiation from generalist retailers.

---

### 2. Collectibles & Trading Cards (~12–13% GMV, $10B Annual Business)

Trading cards (Pokémon, sports cards, Magic: The Gathering) are among eBay's fastest-growing categories. Trading card GMV has accelerated for **10 consecutive quarters** as of mid-2025 ([Fortune, 2025-07-31](https://fortune.com/2025/07/31/ebay-earnings-live-sports-cards-trading-pokemon/)). By 2025, eBay had grown collectibles into a **$10 billion annual business** ([Grokipedia](https://grokipedia.com/page/Jamie_Iannone)). The collectibles category grew 10% while the rest of the platform grew approximately 1% — a gap of ~11 percentage points ([Fortune, 2025-07-31](https://fortune.com/2025/07/31/ebay-earnings-live-sports-cards-trading-pokemon/)).

eBay's competitive differentiation in collectibles includes: PSA/BGS graded card dedicated search filters, third-party authentication services, and the eBay Vault (secure physical storage). Since PSA launched on eBay in Q3 2024, over 500,000 graded cards have been sold through the platform ([Sportico, 2025](https://www.sportico.com/business/commerce/2025/ebay-sports-cards-growth-1234850708/)). Enthusiast buyers in this category exhibit exceptionally high stickiness, with average spend far exceeding general users.

eBay faces emerging competition in collectibles from Whatnot (valued at $5B, focused on live auction streaming), Fanatics (direct sports card sales), and Courtyard (selling $50M/month in cards and comics). eBay's response is **eBay Live** — a live commerce feature. Iannone stated: "We have already seen significant evidence that live commerce can deepen engagement among eBay enthusiasts and unlock even greater velocity in our strongest verticals" ([Fortune, 2025-07-31](https://fortune.com/2025/07/31/ebay-earnings-live-sports-cards-trading-pokemon/)). eBay's structural advantages in this category include 30 years of transaction data, global reach across 190 markets, and the acquisition of Goldin (a premier sports card auction house) bringing high-end category integration.

The strategic rationale for collectibles goes beyond category GMV: deep enthusiast buyers in collectibles demonstrate cross-category spend 3–4x that of general buyers, creating a platform-wide value multiplier.

Founder Pierre Omidyar's earliest observations on eBay anticipated this dynamic. The first item ever sold on AuctionWeb was a broken laser pointer ($14.83), and the buyer told him "I'm a collector of broken laser pointers." Omidyar concluded: "It became apparent very quickly... in order to do a trade you actually have to get to know that person and build a trusting relationship first" ([Academy of Achievement](https://achievement.org/achiever/pierre-omidyar/)). Trust as the foundation for collectibles commerce is embedded in eBay's DNA from day one.

### 3. Certified Luxury Goods (~5% GMV)

Since 2020, eBay has operated the **Authenticity Guarantee** program, providing third-party professional authentication for sneakers ($100+), watches ($2,000+), and handbags ($500+). This service substantially increased consumer trust in second-hand luxury goods on the platform.

Iannone articulated the strategic rationale in a Financial Times interview: "We're leaning into where Gen Z and millennials are... there's a bigger focus on sustainability, and 're-commerce'. eBay is really the pioneer... that gives us an opportunity" ([Financial Times via PYMNTS, 2022-10-21](https://www.pymnts.com/news/ecommerce/2022/ebay-bids-on-luxury-recommerce-to-woo-young-shoppers/)). In a ChannelX interview, Iannone confirmed the strategy began with sneakers and watches before expanding to handbags ([ChannelX, 2022-01](https://channelx.world/2022/01/exclusive-interview-with-jamie-iannone-ebay-ceo/)).

### 4. Refurbished Electronics & Sustainable Goods (~28% GMV in Electronics)

Electronics is eBay's largest single category by GMV (28%). Within electronics, refurbished and certified refurbished products (sold under the **eBay Refurbished** banner) grew 18% YoY. eBay partners with Apple, Dyson, Samsung and other major brands to offer manufacturer-certified refurbished products with warranties and return protections.

Iannone quantified the sustainability angle in the 2021 annual report: "540,000 metric tons of carbon emissions were avoided" through consumer-to-consumer sales ([LinkedIn, 2021](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/empowering-economic-opportunity-all-jamie-iannone)). Sustainability-focused listings now represent 5–6% of all platform listings ([ElectroIQ, 2025](https://electroiq.com/stats/ebay-statistics/)). This category aligns eBay with secular recommerce and circular economy trends.

### 5. Advertising Business (~$1.6B Revenue, High-Growth)

eBay's first-party advertising business (Promoted Listings) has been the largest driver of revenue growth over the past three years. Full-year 2024 advertising revenue was approximately $1.6B (~2.4% of GMV), with Q4 2024 advertising revenue of $445M (YoY +12%). By Q4 2025, quarterly advertising revenue had climbed further to **$544M** ([FinancialContent, 2026-03-12](https://markets.financialcontent.com/talkmarkets/article/marketminute-2026-3-12-ebay-defies-e-commerce-slump-with-3-billion-q4-revenue-and-strategic-pivot-to-gen-z-enthusiasts)). 3.4 million sellers actively use advertising products on the platform.

Advertising revenue is nearly pure margin and is the primary driver of eBay's expanding take rate. Iannone noted in Q4 earnings commentary that Promoted Listings deliver an average 25% sales lift for participating sellers ([ElectroIQ, 2025](https://electroiq.com/stats/ebay-statistics/)).

### 6. Depop Acquisition — Gen Z Fashion Platform (Expected Close Q2 2026)

On February 18, 2026, eBay announced the acquisition of **Depop** from Etsy for **$1.2 billion in cash** — a social-first second-hand fashion marketplace with Gen Z as its core demographic ([DNYUZ, 2026-02-18](https://dnyuz.com/2026/02/18/etsy-sells-depop-a-secondhand-clothing-app-popular-with-gen-z-to-ebay/); [Euronews, 2026-02-19](https://www.euronews.com/business/2026/02/19/ebay-snaps-up-depop-in-1bn-bid-for-gen-z-wallets)).

Depop's profile at acquisition:
- **7 million active buyers**, **3M+ active sellers**
- 90% of users under age 34
- **$1B GMS in 2025**, with US sales growing +60% YoY

Iannone positioned Depop as a "natural extension" of eBay's recommerce strategy: "We are confident that as part of eBay, Depop will be even more well-positioned for long-term growth, benefiting from our scale, complementary offerings and operational capabilities" ([Euronews, 2026-02-19](https://www.euronews.com/business/2026/02/19/ebay-snaps-up-depop-in-1bn-bid-for-gen-z-wallets)). He specifically highlighted Depop's role in closing a demographic gap: "millennials and Gen Z and young women in particular, which is still an underrepresented demographic for us" ([DNYUZ, 2026-02-18](https://dnyuz.com/2026/02/18/etsy-sells-depop-a-secondhand-clothing-app-popular-with-gen-z-to-ebay/)).

Depop will retain its independent brand and London headquarters while integrating eBay's logistics network, payment processing, and Authenticity Guarantee infrastructure. The global secondhand apparel market is projected to reach $350B by 2028 ([BizTech, 2026](https://biztechcommunity.com/news/the-1-2bn-bet-on-gen-z-what-depop-brings-to-ebay/)). This acquisition — eBay's largest M&A since the 2021 Classifieds divestiture — signals Iannone's strategy shifting from purely organic vertical investment to inorganic expansion for demographic reach. Notably, Etsy originally acquired Depop for $1.625B and sold it at $1.2B, a $425M loss, reflecting Depop's underperformance under Etsy's ownership.

### 7. Agentic Commerce — AI-Driven Next-Generation E-Commerce

In January 2025, eBay partnered with OpenAI to launch **Agentic Commerce**, enabling AI agents to autonomously search, compare prices, and place orders on behalf of buyers. Iannone described the strategic vision: "This agentic approach to ecommerce could create an entirely new channel for discovery and shopping online... we anticipate this collaboration could expand the reach of our sellers, giving more buyers exposure to eBay's unique and differentiated inventory" ([Digital Commerce 360, 2025-03-10](https://www.digitalcommerce360.com/2025/03/10/ebay-sales-revenue-gmv-q4-2024/)). eBay simultaneously launched five additional AI features including AI-powered shipping estimates and a shopping chatbot ([CNN Business, 2025-11-14](https://www.cnn.com/2025/11/14/tech/ebay-ai-shopping)).
