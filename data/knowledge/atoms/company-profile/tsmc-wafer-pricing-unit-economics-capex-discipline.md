## TSMC Wafer Pricing, Unit Economics, and CapEx Return Discipline

### Wafer Pricing Trajectory: Exponential Value Creation per Node

TSMC's wafer pricing increases sharply with each process node generation, reflecting the exponential R&D investment and yield complexity required. According to analyst estimates:

- **3nm (N3):** ~$18,000–$20,000 per wafer — representing a 3x+ increase over 10 years ([Source: Tom's Hardware, 2025](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/tsmcs-wafer-pricing-now-usd18-000-for-a-3nm-wafer-increased-by-over-3x-in-10-years-analyst))
- **2nm (N2, estimated):** >$30,000 per wafer — 50%+ premium over N3 ([Source: Tom's Hardware](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/tsmc-may-increase-wafer-pricing-by-10-for-2025-report))

For 2026, TSMC plans sub-5nm advanced process price increases of 5–10%, while CoWoS advanced packaging prices are set to rise by ~20%. ([Source: SmBom, 2025](https://www.smbom.com/news/13847); [TrendForce, 2025-10-08](https://www.trendforce.com/news/2025/10/08/news-tsmcs-2nm-reportedly-up-10-20-far-below-rumored-50-3-7nm-to-rise-single-digit-in-2026/))

### CapEx Return Framework

CFO Wendell Huang articulated TSMC's philosophy on capital intensity directly: "A higher level of capital expenditures is always going to be correlated with higher growth opportunities in the following years. As long as we believe there are business opportunities, we will not hesitate to invest." ([Source: App Economy Insights, 2025](https://www.appeconomyinsights.com/p/tsmc-ai-megatrend-engine))

Huang made a specific commitment on return discipline: "If we do our job right, the growth of our business should outpace the growth of the CapEx." — Even as CapEx climbs to $52–56B in 2026, revenue growth is expected to exceed capital expenditure growth. ([Source: TSMC Q3 2025 Transcript](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/17/tsmc-tsm-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/))

CEO Wei reinforced commitment to profitable growth despite the higher investment cycle: "Even as we invest for the future growth with this higher level of CapEx spending in 2025, we remain committed to delivering profitable growth to our shareholders." ([Source: TSMC Q4 2024 Transcript](https://investor.tsmc.com/chinese/encrypt/files/encrypt_file/reports/2025-01/84aeb15bbe33894365d33f52e027c5268ba95dcf/TSMC%204Q24%20Transcript.pdf))

### The Long-Term Gross Margin Floor

On the structural profitability floor, CFO Huang stated: "Thus, even with the unfavorable foreign exchange rate, we believe a long-term gross margin of 53% and higher remains well achievable." — This 53% floor reflects the baseline economics of TSMC's pure-play foundry model. The current 62%+ gross margin reflects AI supercycle tailwinds on top of this structural base. ([Source: TSMC Q2 2025 Transcript](https://investor.tsmc.com/chinese/encrypt/files/encrypt_file/reports/2025-07/1f4f86c935f1de837672a6973154e64b26bdae57/TSMC%202Q25%20Transcript.pdf))

Pricing discipline is also notable: When rumors of a 20% across-the-board wafer price increase circulated, Huang pushed back: "Pricing is only one element of our broader profitability... the more decisive driver of profitability is a high utilization rate supported by strong demand and disciplined capacity planning." ([Source: TrendForce, 2026-01-15](https://www.trendforce.com/news/2026/01/15/news-tsmc-addresses-speculations-of-a-20-wafer-asp-increase-says-price-is-not-the-main-profit-driver/))

### Operating Cost Structure (2024)

- **R&D:** NT$21,812M (up 12% YoY) — the 8% of revenue discipline established by Morris Chang, maintained regardless of cycle
- **G&A and Marketing:** NT$25,425M (up 35.6% YoY) — driven by overseas fab startup costs and employee profit sharing, per CFO Huang ([Source: TechSoda](https://techsoda.substack.com/p/explainer-tsmcs-2024-annual-report))
- **Customer Prepayments:** NT$291.1B received in 2024 — customers prepaying cash to secure future fab capacity, a reflection of extreme supply tightness and TSMC's irreplaceable manufacturing position

### N2 Node as the Next Long-Lifecycle Revenue Driver

Wei expressed high conviction on N2 pricing durability in the Q3 2025 earnings call: "we believe N2, N2P, A16 and its derivatives will propel our N2 family to be another large and long-lasting node." — N2 family is expected to become another multi-year revenue and pricing anchor, similar to N5's longevity. ([Source: TSMC Q3 2025 Transcript](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/17/tsmc-tsm-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/))

### The 8% R&D Discipline as Institutional Moat

Morris Chang's fixed ~8% R&D-to-revenue ratio is a structural mechanism for maintaining technology leadership across cycles. He explained the rationale: "I wanted to make him at ease, you know what I mean? He doesn't have to argue, he doesn't have to request every year." — By institutionalizing the ratio, TSMC removes the political and cyclical risk of R&D budget cuts, ensuring continuous generational investment in process technology. ([Source: Acquired Podcast, 2025-01](https://podscripts.co/podcasts/acquired/tsmc-founder-morris-chang))

This discipline, combined with value-based pricing and utilization-focused profitability management, forms the structural foundation of TSMC's durable competitive advantage in the global semiconductor foundry industry.
