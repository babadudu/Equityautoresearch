## Eli Lilly Business Model DNA

Eli Lilly operates across six major therapeutic areas, each targeting some of the world's largest unmet medical needs. The company is a pure-play innovative pharmaceuticals enterprise — it does not participate in generics or consumer health — built on a core flywheel: high R&D investment → patent-protected pricing power → high gross margins → reinvestment into R&D.

---

### Segment 1: Cardiometabolic (Diabetes & Obesity) — ~60% of 2025 Revenue

This is Lilly's core engine, driven by tirzepatide, a dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist. **Mounjaro** (tirzepatide for type 2 diabetes) generated $23.0B in 2025 revenue, making it one of the world's best-selling drugs. **Zepbound** (tirzepatide for obesity) generated $13.5B in 2025 revenue — a remarkable scale achieved within just two years of launch.

CEO Dave Ricks explained the mechanism's revolutionary nature: "It's reducing weight on average, 20% of body weight" ([Fortune, 2024](https://fortune.com/2024/11/13/eli-lilly-ceo-dave-ricks/)).

**Generational product transition:** The previous-generation GLP-1 agent Trulicity (dulaglutide) is being deliberately replaced by Mounjaro. Trulicity revenue fell from $7.1B in 2023 to $5.3B in 2024 (-26%). Ricks is unapologetic: tirzepatide's clinical superiority makes the cannibalization rational and necessary.

**Insulin pricing transparency:** Ricks has been candid about historical pricing dysfunction in the insulin market: "Our actual net on insulin really hasn't changed. It's like $30–$40, but the list price got up to 275" — "We competed on this spread... We kept raising the list price" ([All-In/Cheeky Pint, 2024](https://cheekypint.substack.com/p/dave-ricks-ceo-of-eli-lilly-on-glp)). Lilly proactively capped Humalog at $35 in 2023 to rebuild public trust.

---

### Segment 2: Oncology — ~9% of 2025 Revenue

**Verzenio** (abemaciclib, CDK4/6 inhibitor) is Lilly's flagship oncology product, generating $5.7B in 2025 revenue. It is positioned in early-stage adjuvant breast cancer treatment. Lilly's oncology pipeline is expanding: Ricks noted "60+ cancer studies" underway ([Chief Executive, 2025](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/)), spanning immunotherapy, targeted therapy, and multiple tumor types.

---

### Segment 3: Immunology — ~5% of 2025 Revenue

**Taltz** (ixekizumab) for psoriasis and arthritis generated $3.3B in 2024 revenue (+18% YoY). Lilly is developing next-generation immunology assets including lebrikizumab for atopic dermatitis, expanding its presence beyond the existing Taltz franchise.

---

### Segment 4: Neuroscience — Largest Long-Term Growth Optionality

**Kisunla** (donanemab) received FDA approval in 2024 for early Alzheimer's disease, representing the culmination of three decades of neuroscience investment. Ricks stated: "We've been studying... Alzheimer's for basically 30 years at Lilly" ([T. Rowe Price, 2025](https://www.troweprice.com/en/us/insights/the-long-view-eli-lilly)). Clinical data shows the drug achieves "more than 80% reduction in like a year. Even to undetectable levels" of amyloid plaques.

Ricks has framed the next decade as "the decade of the brain... brain health is probably the biggest category" ([Fortune, 2024](https://fortune.com/2024/11/13/eli-lilly-ceo-dave-ricks/)).

---

### Segment 5: Cardiovascular & Metabolic (Jardiance Alliance)

**Jardiance** (empagliflozin), co-developed with Boehringer Ingelheim, generated $3.3B in 2024 revenue (+22% YoY), used for diabetes and heart failure. The emerging clinical practice of combining SGLT2 inhibitors with GLP-1 agonists gives Lilly a complete cardiometabolic product matrix.

## Strategic Investment Pillars

### 1. R&D Scale as Competitive Moat

Lilly's R&D investment is the single most important competitive barrier. Ricks has emphasized: "We spend almost 25% of sales this year on R&D" and "We'll spend $14 billion this year total" ([All-In/Cheeky Pint, 2024](https://cheekypint.substack.com/p/dave-ricks-ceo-of-eli-lilly-on-glp)). This sum is "above Germany and Japan as a nation and headed closer to the NIH" ([Chief Executive, 2025](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/)).

### 2. Manufacturing Capacity Expansion ($50B CapEx Agenda)

Demand for incretin therapies has outpaced supply. Lilly announced a "$50 billion CapEx agenda... over five years" ([T. Rowe Price, 2025](https://www.troweprice.com/en/us/insights/the-long-view-eli-lilly)) encompassing four new manufacturing facilities. By mid-2025, the company "produced more than 1.6 times the amount of salable incretin doses during the first half of 2025 when compared to the first half of 2024" ([CNBC/S&P Global, 2025](https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/research/2025/08/eli-lilly-set-to-leapfrog-novo-nordisk-in-weight-loss-drug-race)).

### 3. LillyDirect — Direct-to-Consumer Disruption

LillyDirect is a platform allowing patients to purchase Lilly medications directly using a credit card, bypassing insurance and pharmacy benefit managers (PBMs). Ricks described patient reaction: "People skip all this other morass and getting a 'this is not a bill' piece of paper. They're just like, 'Here's my Visa card number.'" ([All-In/Cheeky Pint, 2024](https://cheekypint.substack.com/p/dave-ricks-ceo-of-eli-lilly-on-glp)). DTC pricing of ~$549/month vs. ~$1,000 net through traditional channels demonstrates meaningful PBM cost elimination.

### 4. The Fundamental Nature of Drug Discovery

Ricks distinguishes pharmaceuticals from other industries: "Our business is one of the few businesses that you do actual discovery... Something never happened before — and now it's happened." The binary outcome of drug development is explicit: "It's literally like the card flipping over and you win the hand or you don't" ([Chief Executive, 2025](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/)). This unpredictability justifies the diversified pipeline approach and sustained R&D spend even when individual programs fail.

## Business Model Summary

Lilly's model can be summarized as: **patent-protected discovery → monopoly pricing window → 83% gross margins → reinvestment into the next discovery cycle**. The tirzepatide franchise has compressed years of revenue growth into a single product cycle, providing capital to fund the next generation (Alzheimer's, next-gen obesity, oncology). The $50B manufacturing CapEx investment, the LillyDirect DTC channel, and the 30-year neuroscience bet all reflect a management team willing to invest far ahead of the revenue curve.
