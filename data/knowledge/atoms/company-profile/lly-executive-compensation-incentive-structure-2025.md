## Eli Lilly Executive Compensation & Incentive Structure (2025)

### Overview: Three-Dimensional Pay Design

Lilly's executive compensation philosophy links short-term operational performance, pipeline innovation progress, and long-term shareholder return in a single integrated framework. The design deliberately avoids over-weighting any single metric, reducing the risk of gaming or short-termism. The annual bonus explicitly covers "a mix of measures on three dimensions: operations; progress within our innovation pipeline; and shareholder return" ([2026 Proxy Statement](https://www.stocktitan.net/sec-filings/LLY/def-14a-eli-lilly-co-definitive-proxy-statement-45fcdb58ab68.html)).

### Annual Bonus: 218% of Target Payout in 2025

The annual bonus is governed by a three-dimensional scorecard:
1. **Operational performance** — product revenues and EPS (adjusted)
2. **Pipeline progress** — clinical milestones, regulatory approvals, and late-stage trial advancement
3. **Shareholder return** — relative and absolute TSR components

The compensation committee adjusts reported revenue and EPS results "in certain circumstances to eliminate the distorting effect of certain unusual items," ensuring the incentive reflects core operational performance rather than one-time events ([2026 Proxy Statement](https://www.stocktitan.net/sec-filings/LLY/def-14a-eli-lilly-co-definitive-proxy-statement-45fcdb58ab68.html)).

The 2025 payout at **218% of target** reflects Lilly's exceptional year: Mounjaro (tirzepatide for diabetes) and Zepbound (tirzepatide for obesity) drove revenue growth well above plan, multiple pipeline assets advanced to late-stage trials, and LLY stock significantly outperformed the S&P 500 and pharmaceutical peer group.

### Long-Term Incentive (LTI) Structure: SVA and RVA

Lilly replaces traditional RSUs and stock options with two bespoke three-year performance award vehicles, both capped at 200% of target:

**1. Shareholder Value Award (SVA)**
- Measures absolute stock price appreciation over a rolling three-year performance period
- 2025 payout: **200% of target** (the maximum)
- Reflects LLY stock's extraordinary appreciation — the share price grew more than 10× between 2022 and 2025, driven by the GLP-1 revolution
- By tying directly to absolute stock price rather than earnings, SVA ensures management benefits only when shareholders benefit

**2. Relative Value Award (RVA)**
- Measures total shareholder return (TSR) relative to a pharmaceutical peer group median over a rolling three-year period
- 2025 payout: **200% of target** (the maximum)
- The relative framing guards against windfall payouts from sector-wide tailwinds — executives must outperform peers, not merely ride a bull market
- Lilly's TSR dramatically exceeded the peer group median over 2022-2025, driven by tirzepatide's commercial success and the GLP-1 category expansion

Both instruments carry a hard ceiling at 200% of target. This prevents unlimited upside that could incentivize excessive risk-taking, while still delivering substantial rewards for genuine outperformance. The three-year vesting horizon discourages short-term stock price manipulation and aligns with the multi-year timelines of pharmaceutical product launches.

### Management Alignment: Personal Stake

Beyond the formal compensation structure, Ricks's own disclosed ownership stake in Lilly (approximately $100M+ per Proxy Statement filings) provides an additional alignment mechanism that operates independently of the formal incentive architecture. Executives who own substantial personal equity in their companies face the same wealth consequences as ordinary shareholders — they cannot simply collect bonus income while stock underperforms. This owner-operator alignment is particularly credible when, as at Lilly, the stock has compounded dramatically and the CEO has maintained rather than sold down the position.

### Design Philosophy: Why This Structure Works

The combination of:
- **Low fixed pay** (base = ~5% of total) — eliminates entitlement; keeps fixed cost base lean
- **Multi-dimensional annual bonus** — prevents gaming any single metric; rewards balanced operational execution
- **Dual three-year LTI vehicles** (SVA + RVA) — links pay to both absolute and relative shareholder outcomes over meaningful time horizons
- **Hard 200% cap on LTIs** — limits excessive risk appetite while delivering strong upside for genuine outperformance
- **Adjusted EPS/revenue metrics** — removes noise from one-time items, ensuring incentives reflect sustainable performance

...creates a compensation architecture that successfully aligns CEO incentives with long-term shareholder value creation without distorting incentives toward short-term earnings manipulation or excessive risk-taking. In the context of a pharmaceutical company where a single pipeline failure can destroy years of value and a single breakthrough can create hundreds of billions in market capitalization, this balance is especially important.
