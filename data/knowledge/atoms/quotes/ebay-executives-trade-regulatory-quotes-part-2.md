## eBay Executive Team — Trade Policy & Regulatory Quotes

### Context
Beyond CEO Jamie Iannone, several eBay executives provided critical quantitative and qualitative color on the company's exposure to U.S. trade policy changes, including the de minimis exemption elimination and tariff uncertainty. These quotes span Q4 2024 (early warning) through Q4 2025 (confirmed impact).

---

### Peggy Alford (CFO, succeeded Steve Priest in 2025)

**Q3 2025 — Annualizing headwinds from de minimis:**
> "We expect to face incremental headwinds from annualizing breakage associated with the global de minimis changes."

— Peggy Alford, eBay CFO, [eBay Q3 2025 Earnings Call, 2025-10-29](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/29/ebay-ebay-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)

*Note: "Breakage" in this context refers to seller and buyer activity that dropped off following the de minimis change — Alford is signaling that the year-over-year comp gets harder as 2026 anniversaries the full impact.*

**Q4 2025 — International GMV compression:**
> "Challenging macroeconomic conditions in the UK and Germany and a deceleration in our cross-border volume growth due to U.S. trade policies."

— Peggy Alford, eBay CFO, [eBay Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-18](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/18/ebay-ebay-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)

*Note: This quote identifies a dual headwind on international GMV — macro weakness in eBay's two largest European markets (UK and Germany) compounded by U.S. trade policy reducing inbound cross-border flows.*

---

### Steve Priest (former CFO, through early 2025)

**Q4 2024 Earnings Call — Early tariff uncertainty warning:**
> "Facing the uncertainty around U.S. tariffs and de minimis changes... contemplating a range of outcomes for tariffs in our guidance based on what we can see and forecast."

— Steve Priest, former eBay CFO, [Digital Commerce 360, 2025-03-10](https://www.digitalcommerce360.com/2025/03/10/ebay-sales-revenue-gmv-q4-2024/)

*Note: Priest's language — "range of outcomes" — reflects that as of early 2025, the magnitude of de minimis changes was still uncertain. This was one of the earliest public acknowledgments by eBay's finance team of tariff risk as a scenario-planning input.*

---

### John Egbert (VP, Investor Relations)

**Q4 2024 — China GMV quantification:**
> "About 5% of total GMV" comes from China-origin merchandise.

> "About 75% of eBay volume from China is forward deployed" in U.S. warehouses.

— John Egbert, eBay VP, [Digital Commerce 360, 2025-03-10](https://www.digitalcommerce360.com/2025/03/10/ebay-sales-revenue-gmv-q4-2024/)

*Note: These two data points together define eBay's structural de minimis exposure. If 5% of GMV comes from China, and 75% of that is already forward-deployed in U.S. warehouses, then only ~25% of 5% (~1.25% of total GMV) is directly exposed to de minimis changes on cross-border shipments. This materially limits the maximum financial impact.*

---

### Investment Analysis Context

The sequence of quotes across Q4 2024 → Q3 2025 → Q4 2025 reveals an evolving management narrative:
- **Q4 2024** (Priest): Uncertainty; range of outcomes contemplated
- **Q3 2025** (Iannone + Alford): Impact confirmed, volume deceleration visible in September
- **Q4 2025** (Iannone + Alford): Full-quarter impact absorbed; product roadmap accelerated as response

The forward-deployed inventory buffer (Egbert's 75% figure) significantly limits eBay's structural downside, but the residual ~1-2% of GMV at risk from direct-ship China inventory, combined with macro weakness in UK/Germany, created a convergence of headwinds in H2 2025.
