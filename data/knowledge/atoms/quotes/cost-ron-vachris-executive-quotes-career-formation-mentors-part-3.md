# Ron Vachris Executive Quotes — Career Formation, Mentors & Leadership Philosophy (Part 3)

## Background

Ron Vachris (born 1965, Staten Island, NY) became Costco's third CEO in January 2024 after 41 years with the company, starting as a 17-year-old part-time forklift driver at Price Club in 1982. The quotes below span his career formation, key inflection points, and leadership philosophy as distilled from interviews (2024–2025).

---

## On Discovering His Calling at Price Club (1982)

Vachris joined Price Club intending only to work through the school semester, but was quickly captivated by the warehouse club model:

> "I thought, I'll do this while I'm going to school...But I found I was learning more here in real life than I was at school about business."
— Ron Vachris, [Cosmos Philly, 2024](https://cosmosphilly.com/ron-vachris-costco-greek-american-ceo/)

**Context:** He never left. This 1982 decision initiated a 41-year "lifer" journey through Price Club and Costco.

---

## On Becoming a Store Manager (1992)

Promotion to store GM at Price Club in 1992 was a defining moment:

> "[That year] really developed my leadership skills."
— Ron Vachris, [CNBC, 2025/12/12](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/12/12/costco-ceo-i-developed-my-leadership-skills-doing-this-job-in-1992.html)

**Context:** Managing his first store taught Vachris the unit-level accountability that would anchor his operational philosophy for the rest of his career.

---

## On Accepting the Real Estate Role Assigned by Jeff Brotman (2015)

Co-founder Jeff Brotman surprised Vachris by assigning him to lead Costco's real estate division — entirely outside his operations background:

> "Don't be afraid to get outside your comfort zone if somebody has confidence in you."
— Ron Vachris, [South Sound Biz, 2024](https://www.southsoundbiz.com/profiles/ron-vachris-costco-interview/article_7c03e854-37d9-11ef-b403-1fab84c884aa.html)

**Context:** Vachris described his initial reaction as "shocked." He later credited this cross-functional stretch assignment — overseeing all global site selection, construction, and property operations — as giving him a holistic understanding of the business that pure operations experience could not have provided.

Reflecting on working alongside Brotman during this period:

> "One of the best educations I had in global business, was working alongside one of the founders."
— Ron Vachris, [South Sound Biz, 2024](https://www.southsoundbiz.com/profiles/ron-vachris-costco-interview/article_7c03e854-37d9-11ef-b403-1fab84c884aa.html)

---

## On Culture as Top Priority After Becoming CEO (2024)

Upon assuming the CEO role in January 2024, Vachris identified three priorities: culture, international expansion, and e-commerce. On the first:

> "Keeping that culture going is my greatest task."
— Ron Vachris (interview context: post-CEO succession, 2024)

He attributed this conviction in part to Jim Sinegal, whose maxim had become deeply internalized:

> "Culture is not one priority among many but the only priority" — Jim Sinegal, as cited by Vachris; he noted the phrase was "etched into his mind."

---

## On Avoiding Complacency at Scale

> "We have to stay as focused as we were when we had 200 warehouses. We can't become arrogant. We can't become comfortable."
— Ron Vachris, [Fortune, 2024/05/29](https://fortune.com/2024/05/29/costco-ceo-ron-vachris-winning-strategy-formula-expansion-arrogant/)

**Context:** Said in reference to Costco's growth to ~890 warehouses globally. Vachris framed this as the central risk to the Costco model — success breeding the very complacency that its low-margin, high-value-to-member model is designed to prevent.

From the same period, on Costco's most strategic asset:

> "The most important item we sell is the membership card."
— Ron Vachris, [Fortune, 2024/05/29](https://fortune.com/2024/05/29/costco-ceo-ron-vachris-winning-strategy-formula-expansion-arrogant/)

---

## On Leadership Style: Trust, Empowerment, Teaching

> "You've got to give people room to try new things."
— Ron Vachris, [Fortune, 2024/05/28](https://fortune.com/2024/05/28/costco-ceo-ron-vachris-lifer-forklift-driver/)

> "90% of any leader's time should be spent teaching."
— Ron Vachris, [South Sound Biz, 2024](https://www.southsoundbiz.com/profiles/ron-vachris-costco-interview/article_7c03e854-37d9-11ef-b403-1fab84c884aa.html)

**Context:** Vachris frames leadership in terms of the "mentor-apprentice" model he experienced himself — Sol Price to Sinegal, Sinegal/Brotman to Vachris, and now Vachris to the next generation. The 90% teaching figure is a deliberate articulation of where leaders should allocate attention, reflecting Costco's longstanding practice of promoting entirely from within.

On employees as competitive advantage:

> "Our employees are our number one competitive advantage."
— Ron Vachris, [Cosmos Philly, 2024](https://cosmosphilly.com/ron-vachris-costco-greek-american-ceo/)

---

## On Accountability After a 2024 Union Incident

When asked about employee dissatisfaction that surfaced during a 2024 labor episode, Vachris's response was notably self-directed rather than defensive:

> "We're not disappointed in our employees. We're disappointed in ourselves as managers."
— Ron Vachris, [Cosmos Philly, 2024](https://cosmosphilly.com/ron-vachris-costco-greek-american-ceo/)

**Context:** This framing — accountability flowing upward, not downward — is consistent with Costco's historical stance on labor relations and its above-market wage philosophy.

---

## On Personal Priorities

> "I'm pretty transparent about my priorities. It's my family and my career."
— Ron Vachris, [Cosmos Philly, 2024](https://cosmosphilly.com/ron-vachris-costco-greek-american-ceo/)

**Context:** Vachris lives in Sammamish, WA, near Costco's Issaquah headquarters, with wife Kim and three adult children.
