## Morris Chang on Talent as TSMC's Strategic Moat

Morris Chang consistently identified talent — not equipment, not capital — as the primary source of TSMC's competitive advantage. The following quotes span his Brookings Institution speech, Acquired Podcast appearance, and biographical materials, revealing a coherent and deeply held philosophy about people.

---

### On Taiwan's Engineering Advantage

**Quote:** "Dedicated engineers are Taiwan's advantage."

**Context:** Chang made this observation during his April 2022 speech at the Brookings Institution, where he argued that Taiwan's geographic compactness created a structural talent deployment advantage that the United States could not replicate. He elaborated:

**Quote:** "TSMC's three manufacturing centers often have thousands of engineers whose location assignment changes but who do not have to move their family."

**Source:** [Brookings Institution, 2022-04](https://www.brookings.edu/wp-content/uploads/2022/04/Vying-for-Talent-Morris-Chang-20220414.pdf); [transcripts/Morris_Chang_2022_Brookings_CSIS.md](transcripts/Morris_Chang_2022_Brookings_CSIS.md)

**Investment significance:** This quote is often cited to explain why TSMC's Arizona fabs face a structural cost disadvantage: U.S. engineers cannot be reassigned between facilities without relocating their families, reducing operational flexibility and increasing overhead. TSMC's Taiwan operations allow thousands of engineers to rotate between Hsinchu, Taichung, and Tainan fabs without household disruption — a compounding organizational advantage over decades.

---

### On Layoffs and Human Capital Preservation

**Quote:** "People will not respect us if we lay off by performance ratings. Because it's very subjective."

**Quote:** "If you need the people back within a year, you shouldn't lay off."

**Context:** Chang shared these principles during his January 2025 Acquired Podcast appearance. He explained that during the 2008–2009 global financial crisis, TSMC deliberately chose not to conduct mass layoffs despite severe demand destruction across the semiconductor industry. His reasoning was twofold: performance ratings are subjective and create perceived injustice that damages organizational trust; and talent is not fungible — experienced process engineers take years to develop and cannot simply be rehired once lost.

**Source:** [Acquired Podcast, 2025-01](https://www.acquired.fm/episodes/tsmc-founder-morris-chang)

**Investment significance:** TSMC's annual employee turnover rate has been maintained at 4–5%, far below the semiconductor industry average of approximately 10–15%. Even during the 2021 global tech talent war, TSMC's overall turnover reached only 6.8% — though new employee turnover was higher at 17.6%, reflecting adaptation challenges in high-intensity fab shift work. ([Taiwan News, 2022-07-02](https://www.taiwannews.com.tw/news/4586957); [TSMC ESG Report](https://esg.tsmc.com/en-US/sustainability-roles/admired-employer)). The preservation of institutional knowledge through downturns is a key reason TSMC emerged from every cyclical trough with its technology lead intact.

---

### On R&D Spending Discipline

**Quote:** "8%, regardless of whether there's a recession or not. I wanted to stop that negotiation every year."

**Context:** Chang revealed in his January 2025 Acquired Podcast appearance that he established a fixed R&D spending ratio of 8% of revenue — immovable across business cycles. The motivation was organizational: annual budget negotiations around R&D allocations consumed management attention and created short-term thinking. By making the 8% floor non-negotiable, he protected long-term technology investment from cyclical revenue pressures.

**Source:** [Acquired Podcast, 2025-01](https://www.acquired.fm/episodes/tsmc-founder-morris-chang)

**Investment significance:** This R&D discipline compounds over decades. Competitor fabs that cut R&D during downturns (2001, 2009, 2015–16) fell behind on process node transitions and required expensive catch-up investments. TSMC's unbroken R&D cadence is a structural contributor to its consistent 1–2 node lead over Samsung Foundry and Intel Foundry.
