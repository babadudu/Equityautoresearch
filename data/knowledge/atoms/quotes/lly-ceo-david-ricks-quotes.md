## David A. Ricks — Direct Quotes

All quotes are from David A. Ricks, Chairman and CEO of Eli Lilly and Company, with source URLs and contextual notes.

---

### On Mission & Purpose

> "Lilly, with its massive scale, is not being built to make niche drugs for niche diseases. Lots of companies can do that — and do do that. We start with: What's human suffering? And work backwards."
— [Chief Executive, 2025](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/). Context: Explaining Lilly's portfolio strategy and why the company targets only the largest unmet medical needs.

> "I'm a capitalist. I like customers."
— [IBJ, 2018](https://www.ibj.com/articles/60488-the-interview-issue-dave-ricks). Context: Rejecting the framing that commercial motives and patient benefit are in tension.

> "If it's going to be a great drug, let's make sure it says Lilly on it."
— [TIME100 Health, 2025](https://time.com/collections/time100-health-2025/7279659/david-ricks/). Context: On competitive urgency to develop the best medicines in each target class.

> "What really rings the bell for most people in our company... is the feeling that I moved the needle on human health."
— [T. Rowe Price, 2025](https://www.troweprice.com/en/us/insights/the-long-view-eli-lilly). Context: On what actually motivates Lilly employees beyond compensation.

> "I was like, okay, click, I get it, this matters. And I will do this until I can't anymore."
— [T. Rowe Price, 2025](https://www.troweprice.com/en/us/insights/the-long-view-eli-lilly). Context: Describing the personal inflection point early in his Lilly career when he internalized the mission.

### On the Nature of Pharmaceutical Discovery

> "Our business is one of the few businesses that you do actual discovery. It's not development purely. You really don't know the answer. That's about the risk we take, but it's also about the utility when it works."
— [Chief Executive, 2025](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/). Context: Articulating why pharma's risk-reward profile differs fundamentally from other industries.

> "It's literally like the card flipping over and you win the hand or you don't."
— [Chief Executive, 2025](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/). Context: Describing the binary nature of Phase 3 clinical trial outcomes.

> "A lot of pharma innovation is iterative. So the other problem is people quit too soon."
— [All-In/Cheeky Pint, 2024](https://cheekypint.substack.com/p/dave-ricks-ceo-of-eli-lilly-on-glp). Context: Defending the multi-decade Alzheimer's research commitment after multiple Phase 3 failures.

> "It's rarely the case in our sector that the first thing is the best thing."
— [T. Rowe Price, 2025](https://www.troweprice.com/en/us/insights/the-long-view-eli-lilly). Context: On the incretin class progression from Byetta to Trulicity to tirzepatide, and the expectation that the next generation will surpass the current.

> "Often you change the standard of care forever. Because medicines don't go away... you just move the ratchet of what's possible."
— [T. Rowe Price, 2025](https://www.troweprice.com/en/us/insights/the-long-view-eli-lilly). Context: On why even failed clinical programs advance the field and justify continued investment.

### On R&D Process & Decision-Making

> "We actually have a rule to never decide in one meeting."
— [All-In/Cheeky Pint, 2024](https://cheekypint.substack.com/p/dave-ricks-ceo-of-eli-lilly-on-glp). Context: Describing Lilly's mandatory two-meeting rule for all major R&D decisions.

> "We have probably the highest return on investment in R&D in the industry because... the process by which we run it."
— [All-In/Cheeky Pint, 2024](https://cheekypint.substack.com/p/dave-ricks-ceo-of-eli-lilly-on-glp). Context: Claiming process superiority — not just scientific talent — as the source of R&D productivity.

> "I have at least one or two AIs running every minute of every meeting I'm in, and I just am asking it science questions. Either Claude or the xAI one. I find it more terse and the references actually check out more often."
— [All-In/Cheeky Pint, 2024](https://cheekypint.substack.com/p/dave-ricks-ceo-of-eli-lilly-on-glp). Context: Describing real-time AI use during scientific review meetings to supplement his own domain knowledge.

> "We can go faster and we've cut our times in roughly half. So we're failing a lot faster than others and that's an advantage."
— [Fortune, 2024-11](https://fortune.com/2024/11/13/eli-lilly-ceo-dave-ricks/). Context: On development cycle compression as a competitive moat.

> "I guarantee you that obesity was not on our corporate strategy document 10 years ago. But tirzepatide was first formulated in 2011. So, someone was off strategy, and it may be the biggest drug in the history of our industry."
— [Chief Executive, 2025](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/). Context: On the importance of preserving "allowable variation" and not over-constraining researchers to the current corporate strategy.

### On Scale, Investment & Capital Allocation

> "This year, Lilly will spend $14.5 billion. That's above Germany and Japan as a nation and headed closer to the NIH."
— [Chief Executive, 2025](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/). Context: Positioning Lilly's R&D investment as a national-scale commitment.

> "We have more than 4,000 PhDs doing research. That's more than MIT or Harvard. We do real science. We do it at scale."
— [Bell Pioneer, 2025](https://www.bellpioneer.com/insights/the-better-version-of-nice-inside-ceo-david-ricks-leadership-philosophy-at-eli-lilly). Context: Emphasizing that Lilly's scientific depth is a structural competitive advantage.

> "I haven't a shred of evidence that any of those big mergers have created value for shareholders or other constituents. They've probably destroyed value."
— [IBJ, 2018](https://www.ibj.com/articles/60488-the-interview-issue-dave-ricks). Context: Explaining Lilly's preference for organic growth over mega-M&A.

> "I think of [market cap] as more like an outcome that is derived from focusing on the things that create the value, not a goal by itself."
— [Fortune, 2024-11](https://fortune.com/2024/11/13/eli-lilly-ceo-dave-ricks/). Context: Deflecting a question about the significance of Lilly's $700B+ market capitalization.

> "There won't be innovation if people don't have an incentive to invest at risk."
— [Fortune, 2024-11](https://fortune.com/2024/11/13/eli-lilly-ceo-dave-ricks/). Context: Defending the pharmaceutical profit model as a necessary precondition for medical innovation.

### On Culture & Leadership

> "What you model doing — the shadow you cast — matters, because that's what people think you're doing."
— [Posse Foundation, 2019](https://www.possefoundation.org/news-and-events/ubben-posse-fellow-interviews-david-ricks). Context: On why CEO behavior, not words, is the actual cultural signal.

> "The words matter, but not that much. What matters is what you do. What and who you pay attention to."
— [Bell Pioneer, 2025](https://www.bellpioneer.com/insights/the-better-version-of-nice-inside-ceo-david-ricks-leadership-philosophy-at-eli-lilly). Context: Distilling his view on organizational culture change.

> "Almost everything I tried to do explicitly didn't work. But everything I did implicitly stuck."
— [Chief Executive, 2025](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/). Context: Reflecting on nine years of culture-building at Lilly.

> "Tough on problems, soft on people."
— [Chief Executive, 2025](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/). Context: His five-word summary of Lilly's leadership culture.

> "The company grew 45 percent in Q1. Did you grow 45 percent in Q1? As a leader, the implications are that if not, you're actually slowing us down."
— [Chief Executive, 2025](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/). Context: Setting explicit performance expectations for Lilly's executive team.

> "If I have to tell people how to organize and solve problems, we're losing."
— [Chief Executive, 2025](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/). Context: On the CEO's role — setting direction and removing obstacles, not micromanaging execution.

> "To be a great plant manager you have to love the plant enough to break it and put it back together." [quoting a Chinese plant manager he got to know]
— [Bell Pioneer, 2025](https://www.bellpioneer.com/insights/the-better-version-of-nice-inside-ceo-david-ricks-leadership-philosophy-at-eli-lilly). Context: Introducing his principle that genuine love for an organization requires willingness to disrupt it.

> "It is an honor to have a Lilly badge. You've got to uphold that."
— [Chief Executive, 2025](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/). Context: On the flip side of "soft on people" — that belonging to Lilly carries obligations.

### On Insulin Pricing & Industry Ethics

> "Our actual net on insulin really hasn't changed. It's like $30-$40, but the list price got up to 275. We competed on this spread... We kept raising the list price."
— [All-In/Cheeky Pint, 2024-10-08](https://cheekypint.substack.com/p/dave-ricks-ceo-of-eli-lilly-on-glp). Context: Acknowledging the pharmaceutical industry's — including Lilly's — role in insulin pricing dysfunction.

> "Can you imagine this? We go to a restaurant tonight and someone gives us the wine list and a bottle of like a hundred dollar Napa cabernet is like $14,000."
— [All-In/Cheeky Pint, 2024-10-08](https://cheekypint.substack.com/p/dave-ricks-ceo-of-eli-lilly-on-glp). Context: Using an analogy to expose the absurdity of pharmaceutical list-price inflation.

> "It shouldn't have happened... we're taking steps, we're learning, we're improving, and no one should have to ration their insulin."
— [NPR, 2023-03-01](https://www.npr.org/2023/03/01/1160457074/eli-lilly-ceo-david-ricks-comments-on-the-companys-new-insulin-price-cuts). Context: Responding to reports of patients rationing insulin due to unaffordability.

> "Last year, about 80 percent of our list prices went to pay ever-increasing fees and rebates to companies who don't invent, didn't develop nor manufacture the medicine."
— [CNBC, 2023-05-10](https://www.cnbc.com/2023/05/10/eli-lilly-novo-nordisk-sanofi-ceos-on-insulin-prices.html). Context: Senate HELP Committee testimony, exposing PBM economics as a driver of list-price inflation.

> "We'll leave our prices as they are for the insulins on the market today. In fact, we've been cutting them."
— [CNBC, 2023-05-10](https://www.cnbc.com/2023/05/10/eli-lilly-novo-nordisk-sanofi-ceos-on-insulin-prices.html). Context: Senate testimony; became the only major pharma CEO to make this explicit commitment.

### On Alzheimer's & Long-Term Conviction

> "Right, get on with it. We don't need to talk about it anymore. It's a good strategy. Full speed ahead."
— [Endpoints News, 2024](https://endpoints.news/failure-faith-and-8-billion-how-lillys-amyloid-conviction-brought-it-to-the-brink-of-alzheimers-approval/). Context: Response to Chief Scientific Officer Skovronsky after the 2016 solanezumab Phase 3 failure, when some questioned whether to continue Alzheimer's investment.

> "I've personally sat in the readouts of like five or six failed studies."
— [T. Rowe Price, 2025](https://www.troweprice.com/en/us/insights/the-long-view-eli-lilly). Context: Reflecting on the personal experience of serial failure in Alzheimer's research before the eventual approval of Kisunla.

### On GLP-1 & Obesity

> "We had to stop the study because people were losing too much weight to stay in it."
— [Chief Executive, 2025](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/). Context: Describing the 2016 tirzepatide Phase 2 data readout that defined his entire CEO tenure.

> "Since that day, it's been all execution. We need to make more, we need to do more trials, we need to make this an important global product. It wasn't difficult to see that... What was difficult was doing it."
— [Chief Executive, 2025](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/). Context: On the commercial and operational execution required once tirzepatide's potential was clear.

> "These medicines can be useful for other things we don't think about connected to weight... They're reducing that desire cycle. So next year, you'll see Lilly start large studies in alcohol abuse and nicotine use, even in drug abuse."
— [Economic Club DC, 2024-12-10](https://www.economicclub.org/events/david-ricks). Context: Announcing expansion of GLP-1 research into addiction behaviors.

> "One thing as we look back at the obesity market, it's probably surprised us at every turn, so I'm reluctant to give out very specific numbers or anything like that."
— [BioSpace/JPM26, 2026-01](https://www.biospace.com/business/jpm26-lilly-plots-path-to-dominance-as-obesity-market-enters-a-new-era). Context: Declining to provide specific long-term forecasts for the GLP-1 market at J.P. Morgan Healthcare Conference 2026.

> "There's turbulence. I'll own that... this is a market with hundreds of millions of people globally. When we run out, which we did last year, they get very angry with us."
— [Q4 2024 Earnings Call, 2025-02-06](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/02/06/eli-lilly-lly-q4-2024-earnings-call-transcript/). Context: Acknowledging supply shortages of Mounjaro/Zepbound during the Q4 2024 earnings call.

> "I think we're — first of all, we think we're in the early innings of this... the incretin story, tirzepatide story is going to be about unlocking new categories over and over again."
— [Quartr/JPM25, 2025](https://quartr.com/insights/business-philosophy/david-a-ricks-eli-lillys-chairman-and-ceo). Context: Long-term conviction statement on the GLP-1 franchise at J.P. Morgan Healthcare Conference 2025.

### On Legacy & Reflection

> "It feels like the kind of question where someone with a good answer would have a foot out the door."
— [Chief Executive, 2025](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/). Context: Asked about his legacy; deflecting toward present-tense focus.

> "I'm the 11th CEO of the company in 150 years."
— [T. Rowe Price, 2025](https://www.troweprice.com/en/us/insights/the-long-view-eli-lilly). Context: Framing his own tenure within the long institutional arc of Lilly's history.

> "I'll know I did a good job because we found a successor who can do it better."
— [Posse Foundation, 2019](https://www.possefoundation.org/news-and-events/ubben-posse-fellow-interviews-david-ricks). Context: On his own eventual succession — defining success as institutional perpetuation rather than personal achievement.

> "It's an important year for Lilly... since it's our 150th year in existence, which I think makes us the most valuable old company in America."
— [PharmExec/JPM26, 2026-01](https://www.pharmexec.com/view/jp-morgan-2026-eli-lilly-continuing-glp1-success). Context: Opening remarks at J.P. Morgan Healthcare Conference 2026.
