## Amazon Executives Supporting Quotes — Financial Performance (Part 3)

### Brian Olsavsky on Advertising Revenue Growth (Q4 2025)

Amazon CFO Brian Olsavsky provided detail on the advertising segment's momentum during the Q4 2025 earnings call:

> "Advertising revenue grew 22% in the fourth quarter and we added over $12 billion of incremental revenue in 2025 alone."
> — Brian Olsavsky, CFO, [Q4 2025 Earnings Call](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/05/amazon-amzn-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)

**Context:** Amazon's advertising business — primarily sponsored product placements on Amazon.com and Prime Video ads — reached an estimated ~$55B+ in annual revenue by end of 2025. The $12B incremental figure in a single year positions Amazon Advertising as one of the fastest-growing large-scale ad businesses globally, now competing directly with Google Search and Meta for digital ad budgets. The near-zero marginal cost of serving additional ad impressions means advertising revenue flows almost entirely to operating income, making it a critical lever in Amazon's overall margin expansion story.

### Andy Jassy on AWS Growth Acceleration (Q4 2025)

> "AWS growth continued to accelerate to 24%, the fastest we've seen in thirteen quarters."
> — Andy Jassy, CEO, [Q4 2025 Earnings Call](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/05/amazon-amzn-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)

**Context:** AWS had been growing at ~12–17% during 2022–2023 as enterprise customers engaged in "cost optimization" — reducing cloud spend after over-provisioning during the pandemic. The reacceleration to 24% by Q4 2025 signals that the cost optimization cycle has ended and AI-driven workloads have created a new demand cycle. At AWS's scale (~$110B+ annualized run rate), 24% growth is an exceptionally large absolute dollar addition per quarter. The "thirteen quarters" reference means this is the fastest growth since mid-2022 — pre-cost-optimization — and implies that AI tailwinds have pushed AWS into a new growth regime rather than merely recovering to prior levels.

### Andy Jassy on the $200B 2026 CapEx Rationale

> "The demand signals we're seeing for AI are really strong... we've been supply constrained in some areas of AI."
> — Andy Jassy, CEO, [Q4 2025 Earnings Call](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/05/amazon-amzn-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)

> "I expect our total capex in 2026 to be about $200 billion, with the majority devoted to AWS."
> — Andy Jassy, CEO, [Q4 2025 Earnings Call](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/05/amazon-amzn-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)

**Context:** These two statements — supply constraint followed by the $200B commitment — form a logical sequence: Amazon is not speculating on future demand but trying to serve demand that already exists and is currently unmet. At ~25% of trailing-twelve-month revenue, this level of capital intensity is unprecedented for Amazon and comparable only to Amazon's logistics build-out at its peak (2020–2022), which was also supply-constrained-driven. Historical precedent from that logistics cycle: capex intensity peaked and then normalized over 2–3 years as the infrastructure caught up to demand. Analysts modeling AWS margins should account for the near-term drag from this investment cycle before the operating leverage inflects.
