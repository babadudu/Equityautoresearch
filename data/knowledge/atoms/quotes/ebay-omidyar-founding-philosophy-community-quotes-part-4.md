## Pierre Omidyar — eBay Founding Philosophy, Community Trust & Philanthropy Quotes

### Technology Motivation — Not a Business Plan (undated)

On the absence of a grand commercial vision at founding — the real driver was intellectual curiosity:

> "I never had it in mind that I would start a company one day and it would really be successful. I have just been motivated by working on interesting technology"

— Pierre Omidyar, eBay Founder ([Entrepreneur](https://www.entrepreneur.com/growing-a-business/pierre-omidyar/197554))

---

### The Real Founding Mission — Economic Democracy (1995)

Omidyar has repeatedly corrected the widely circulated "Pez dispenser" origin story. The actual motivation was leveling the economic playing field:

> "The business success was, absolutely, but the birth of the idea is definitely a media-enhanced story"

— Pierre Omidyar ([Academy of Achievement](https://achievement.org/achiever/pierre-omidyar/))

> "I wanted to create an efficient market where individuals could benefit from participating in an efficient market, kind of level the playing field"

— Pierre Omidyar, eBay Founder ([Academy of Achievement](https://achievement.org/achiever/pierre-omidyar/))

---

### The First Transaction — Learning That Trust Precedes Commerce (1995)

The broken laser pointer sold for $14.83 to a self-described "collector of broken laser pointers." Omidyar's takeaway became the philosophical foundation for eBay's community model:

> "It became apparent very quickly... in order to do a trade you actually have to get to know that person and build a trusting relationship first"

— Pierre Omidyar, eBay Founder ([Academy of Achievement](https://achievement.org/achiever/pierre-omidyar/))

---

### Human Nature as Infrastructure (founding era)

On the foundational belief that shaped eBay's governance model — trusting users as the default rather than policing them:

> "I founded the company on the notion that people were basically good, and that if you give them the benefit of the doubt you're rarely disappointed"

— Pierre Omidyar, eBay Founder ([Academy of Achievement](https://achievement.org/achiever/pierre-omidyar/))

On the consequence of this belief — a self-regulating community without top-down control:

> [eBay is] "a self-policing community"

— Pierre Omidyar, eBay Founder ([Academy of Achievement](https://achievement.org/achiever/pierre-omidyar/))

---

### The Feedback Forum — Origin of the Reputation Economy (1996)

On the Feedback Forum, launched in 1996, which became the prototype for every online marketplace reputation system:

> "The feedback forum... allows people to kind of rate each other and give feedback on their transaction"

— Pierre Omidyar, eBay Founder ([Academy of Achievement](https://achievement.org/achiever/pierre-omidyar/))

---

### Commerce as the Base Layer of Human Activity (undated)

On why eBay's mission connects to something more fundamental than retail:

> "Commerce and trade is at the base of all human activity... fundamentally everything we do in human activities is related to trade"

— Pierre Omidyar, eBay Founder ([Academy of Achievement](https://achievement.org/achiever/pierre-omidyar/))

---

### Viral Growth Forcing Monetization (1996)

On the moment Omidyar realized AuctionWeb had become a real business — revenue arrived faster than he could open envelopes:

> "The biggest clue was that so many checks were piling up at my door that I had to hire part-time help to open them all"

— Pierre Omidyar, eBay Founder ([Entrepreneur](https://www.entrepreneur.com/growing-a-business/pierre-omidyar/197554))

---

### Accidental Entrepreneur — Self-Reflection (1997)

On bringing in Meg Whitman and accepting institutional capital — not part of the original plan:

> "I didn't set out to create a huge business on eBay. When it happened, I just took advantage of it"

— Pierre Omidyar, eBay Founder ([Entrepreneur](https://www.entrepreneur.com/growing-a-business/pierre-omidyar/197554))

---

### Resilience During the 1999 Outage Crisis

On maintaining conviction during the severe system downtime that threatened eBay's early reputation:

> "I've always had... unshakable faith that it's going to endure"

— Pierre Omidyar, eBay Founder ([Academy of Achievement](https://achievement.org/achiever/pierre-omidyar/))

---

### Philanthropy — Fundamental Equality of Capability (Omidyar Network)

On the mission animating the Omidyar Network, formed in 2004 with a commitment to give away "all but one percent of their fortune":

> "Every human being is born equally capable. What people lack is equal opportunity"

— Pierre Omidyar, eBay Founder ([Academy of Achievement](https://achievement.org/achiever/pierre-omidyar/))
