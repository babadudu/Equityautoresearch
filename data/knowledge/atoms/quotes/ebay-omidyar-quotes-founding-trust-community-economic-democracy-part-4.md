## Pierre Omidyar — Founding Vision, Trust, Community, and Economic Democracy

Pierre Omidyar founded eBay (originally AuctionWeb) on Labor Day 1995, driven by a belief in economic democratization and human goodness. The following quotes span his motivations, key milestones, and enduring philosophy.

---

### On Motivation and Entrepreneurship

**On not planning to build a big company:**
> "I never had it in mind that I would start a company one day and it would really be successful. I have just been motivated by working on interesting technology."
— [Entrepreneur](https://www.entrepreneur.com/growing-a-business/pierre-omidyar/197554)

**On seizing the opportunity once it emerged:**
> "I didn't set out to create a huge business on eBay. When it happened, I just took advantage of it."
— [Entrepreneur](https://www.entrepreneur.com/growing-a-business/pierre-omidyar/197554)

---

### On the Real Founding Story (vs. the Pez Myth)

The widely circulated story that eBay was created to help Omidyar's fiancée trade Pez dispensers is a media embellishment. Omidyar clarified:

> "The business success was, absolutely, but the birth of the idea is definitely a media-enhanced story."
— [Academy of Achievement](https://achievement.org/achiever/pierre-omidyar/)

His actual motivation was economic democratization:

> "I wanted to create an efficient market where individuals could benefit from participating in an efficient market, kind of level the playing field."
— [Academy of Achievement](https://achievement.org/achiever/pierre-omidyar/)

---

### On the Discovery of Trust as the Foundation

The first item sold on AuctionWeb was a broken laser pointer for $14.83. When Omidyar contacted the buyer to confirm the transaction:

> *The buyer replied: "I'm a collector of broken laser pointers."*

This exchange revealed a foundational insight:

> "It became apparent very quickly... in order to do a trade you actually have to get to know that person and build a trusting relationship first."
— [Academy of Achievement](https://achievement.org/achiever/pierre-omidyar/)

---

### On Community Self-Governance

Omidyar designed eBay as a self-regulating community from the outset, anchored in his belief in human goodness:

> "I founded the company on the notion that people were basically good, and that if you give them the benefit of the doubt you're rarely disappointed."
— [Academy of Achievement](https://achievement.org/achiever/pierre-omidyar/)

> "It was a self-policing community and the community would decide for itself what was appropriate and what was not appropriate."
— [Academy of Achievement](https://achievement.org/achiever/pierre-omidyar/)

---

### On the Feedback Forum (1996)

Omidyar introduced the Feedback Forum in 1996 — the first systematic online reputation system for peer-to-peer commerce:

> "The feedback forum... allows people to kind of rate each other and give feedback on their transaction."
— [Academy of Achievement](https://achievement.org/achiever/pierre-omidyar/)

This system became the prototype for all subsequent marketplace trust infrastructure.

---

### On the Surprise of Organic Growth (1996)

When traffic overwhelmed the free platform, Omidyar began charging listing fees. The scale of the response surprised even him:

> "The biggest clue was that so many checks were piling up at my door that I had to hire part-time help to open them all."
— [Entrepreneur](https://www.entrepreneur.com/growing-a-business/pierre-omidyar/197554)

---

### On Resilience During the 1999 System Outages

Facing severe server crashes in 1999 that threatened user trust, Omidyar remained steadfast:

> "I've always had... unshakable faith that it's going to endure."
— [Academy of Achievement](https://achievement.org/achiever/pierre-omidyar/)

---

### On the Deeper Purpose of Commerce

Omidyar viewed trade as a fundamental human activity, not merely a business function:

> "Commerce and trade is at the base of all human activity... fundamentally everything we do in human activities is related to trade."
— [Academy of Achievement](https://achievement.org/achiever/pierre-omidyar/)

He also discovered an unexpected dimension of the platform — community connection:

> "I thought people would simply use the service to buy and sell things. But what they really enjoyed was meeting people."
— [Entrepreneur](https://www.entrepreneur.com/growing-a-business/pierre-omidyar/197554)

---

### On Philanthropy and Human Equality

Omidyar and his wife Pamela pledged to donate all but one percent of their fortune within 20 years. His philosophy ties his philanthropic mission to his founding vision:

> "Every human being is born equally capable. What people lack is equal opportunity."
— [Academy of Achievement](https://achievement.org/achiever/pierre-omidyar/)

---

### Contextual Note

These quotes span Omidyar's founding of AuctionWeb in 1995, the early growth milestones (first Feedback Forum in 1996, Benchmark Capital investment in 1997, IPO in 1998), and his subsequent philanthropic focus. The through-line across all periods is a belief that markets, when designed around trust and community, can be instruments of economic empowerment — a philosophy that continues to inform eBay's brand positioning under CEO Jamie Iannone three decades later.
