## David Ricks on Manufacturing Capacity & Direct-to-Consumer Strategy

### Manufacturing at the Physical Limit

At the December 2024 Economic Club of Washington DC, Ricks described being constrained by the physical limits of industrial construction capacity:

> "We have announced investments of $23 billion in new capital in the United States for factories. We can't go faster. There's no more vendors to build plants faster than we're building right now."

*Source: [Economic Club DC, 2024-12-10](https://www.economicclub.org/events/david-ricks)*

On the overall five-year CapEx commitment:

> "$50 billion CapEx agenda... Over five years."

*Source: [T. Rowe Price, 2025-11](https://www.troweprice.com/en/us/insights/the-long-view-eli-lilly)*

**Context:** Lilly announced a $55 billion manufacturing build-out — the largest in company history — to address chronic supply shortages for Mounjaro and Zepbound. The constraint was not capital but industrial execution capacity: the company claims it is already absorbing essentially all available large-scale pharmaceutical manufacturing vendors.

---

### Supply Resolution — Progress Report

At the January 2026 JPMorgan Healthcare Conference, Ricks confirmed supply constraints had been substantially resolved:

> "We brought a lot of capacity online and solved the supply issues. This gave prescribers and consumers a lot of confidence in starting therapy."

*Source: [PharmExec/JPM26, 2026-01](https://www.pharmexec.com/view/jp-morgan-2026-eli-lilly-continuing-glp1-success)*

From the Q4 2025 Earnings Call:

> "We exceeded our goal to produce 1.8 times the number of incretin doses in 2025 compared to 2024."

> "We've committed over $55 billion, the largest manufacturing build-out in company history."

*Source: [Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-04](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/04/eli-lilly-lly-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)*

From a mid-2025 CNBC/S&P Global report, Ricks cited:

> "Produced more than 1.6 times the amount of salable incretin doses during the first half of 2025 when compared to the first half of 2024."

*Source: [CNBC/S&P Global, 2025](https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/research/2025/08/eli-lilly-set-to-leapfrog-novo-nordisk-in-weight-loss-drug-race)*

### LillyDirect — Direct-to-Consumer Breakthrough

On the patient experience driving adoption of LillyDirect, Lilly's direct purchasing platform:

> "People skip all this other morass and getting a 'this is not a bill' piece of paper. They're just like, 'Here's my Visa card number.'"

*Source: [All-In/Cheeky Pint, 2024](https://cheekypint.substack.com/p/dave-ricks-ceo-of-eli-lilly-on-glp)*

At the January 2026 JPMorgan Conference, Ricks revealed the DTC platform's scale:

> "Basically a million people a month go online and buy GLP-1 medicine directly from a drug manufacturer."

> "Vials on Lilly Direct are basically the second top-selling obesity drug next to Lilly Zepbound in an auto injector, and that's a profound achievement in one year."

*Source: [PharmExec/JPM26, 2026-01](https://www.pharmexec.com/view/jp-morgan-2026-eli-lilly-continuing-glp1-success)*

**Context:** LillyDirect launched in 2024 as a D2C platform enabling patients to purchase Zepbound vials directly, bypassing PBMs and insurance intermediaries. Within one year it became the second-largest obesity drug by volume on the platform. The vial format (vs. auto-injector pens) is priced lower, making it accessible to cash-pay patients. This channel also establishes a ready distribution infrastructure for orforglipron (oral GLP-1) at launch.

### Oral GLP-1 Pricing Strategy — Reaching the Masses

On orforglipron's mass-market pricing intent:

> "Our goal will be to reach the masses with that product, and so we need a pricing strategy in concert with that."

On the manufacturing advantage of oral over injectable:

> "We all know the production systems for injectables have really been stretched — and we continue to build sites. With oral technology we can make more and get this medicine to more people around the world."

*Source: [Fortune Well, 2025-01](https://fortune.com/well/article/eli-lilly-weight-loss-pill-orforglipron-glp1-2026/)*

On the core pricing philosophy:

> "It's all about price at the pump. When people go to the pharmacy counter, how can they have a consistent and low cost for benefits that are helping their health?"

*Source: [Bloomberg/JPM, 2025-01-13](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/videos/2025-01-13/lilly-ceo-on-fighting-cancer-and-obesity-drug-pricing-video)*

On Medicare coverage expansion for oral GLP-1:

> "That opens up things pretty wide, and we'll see where we can go from there."

*Source: [CNBC, 2026-01-30](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/30/eli-lilly-ceo-david-ricks-medicare-coverage-obesity-pills.html)*

**Context:** Orforglipron is a once-daily oral GLP-1 receptor agonist (small molecule, no food restriction unlike oral semaglutide). Ricks has consistently signaled that Lilly intends to price orforglipron more aggressively than injectable tirzepatide to maximize reach — representing a deliberate trade of margin for volume and TAM expansion.
