## CC Wei on Capex Discipline, Hyperscaler Validation, and AI Conviction (2024–2026)

### The AI Super-Cycle and TSMC's Revenue Inflection

The 2024–2025 period represented TSMC's most significant revenue inflection since its founding. Revenue grew 34% in 2024 and 32% in 2025, with ROIC reaching an all-time high of 41.5% in 2025. Wei articulated his conviction at the Q4 2025 earnings call:

> "We believe the AI is real. Not only real, it is starting to grow into our daily life."

([Source: The Next Platform, 2026-01-16](https://www.nextplatform.com/compute/2026/01/16/tsmc-has-no-choice-but-to-trust-the-sunny-ai-forecasts-of-its-customers/4092173))

### Capex Nervousness and Validation Process

Facing a 2025 capex guidance of $52–56B—the largest annual capital program in TSMC's history—Wei was unusually candid about his own anxiety and the discipline behind the decision:

> "I'm also very nervous about it. You bet, because we have to invest about $52 billion to $56 billion for the capex. If we didn't do it carefully, that would be a big disaster to TSMC for sure."

He then described his validation methodology:

> "I want to make sure that my customers' demand is real. So I talked to those cloud service providers — all of them. The answer is that I'm quite satisfied with the answer. They showed me the evidence that AI really helps their businesses."

([Source: The Next Platform, 2026-01-16](https://www.nextplatform.com/compute/2026/01/16/tsmc-has-no-choice-but-to-trust-the-sunny-ai-forecasts-of-its-customers/4092173))

### Hyperscaler Financial Health Check

In the same Q4 2025 call, Wei added a characteristically pragmatic validation step that received wide attention:

> "I also double checked their financial status — they are very rich."

([Source: The Next Platform, 2026-01-16](https://www.nextplatform.com/compute/2026/01/16/tsmc-has-no-choice-but-to-trust-the-sunny-ai-forecasts-of-its-customers/4092173))

Hyperscaler 2026 capex was estimated at ~$600B (36% YoY growth), with an upside scenario of ~$700B. The GPU/infrastructure investment ratio was shifting from 70:30 (2024) toward 50:50 (2026), reflecting rapid growth in AI inference infrastructure. ([Source: Institutional Investor](https://www.institutionalinvestor.com/article/market-intelligence/physical-world-upgrade-2026-outlook-when-ai-spreads-cloud-edge))

### N2 Adoption Velocity

N2 (TSMC's first GAA node) achieved customer tape-out volumes 2x that of N5 in its first year of production, and approximately 4x in its second year—TSMC's fastest-ever customer adoption for a new process node. This reflects the urgency of AI/HPC customers to access Gate-All-Around architecture performance benefits.

Wei on N2's strategic positioning (Q3 2025):

> "we believe N2, N2P, A16 and its derivatives will propel our N2 family to be another large and long-lasting node."

([Source: TSMC Q3 2025 Transcript](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/17/tsmc-tsm-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/))

### Demand Verification: Talking to the End User

Wei's demand validation process goes two levels deep in the supply chain—not just first-tier customers (NVIDIA, AMD, Apple) but the cloud service providers and enterprise AI operators who are the customers of those customers:

> "Right now, we pay a lot of attention to our customers' customer. We talk with them and see how they view the AI application, and then we make a judgment about how AI is going to grow."

([Source: App Economy Insights, 2025](https://www.appeconomyinsights.com/p/tsmc-ai-megatrend-engine))

This two-tier demand validation methodology helps TSMC distinguish genuine secular demand from inventory-driven cycles—a critical capability given the company's multi-year capex commitments.

### On the AI Cycle Being "Early Stage"

Across multiple venues in 2025, Wei consistently characterized AI as early in its adoption S-curve:

- Digitimes (November 2025): AI development is "still in early stage" ([Source: Digitimes, 2025-11-24](https://www.digitimes.com/news/a20251124VL206/taiwan-tsmc-chairman-intel-arizona.html))
- Q4 2025 earnings: "AI is real. Not only real, it is starting to grow into our daily life."
- 2025 Fortune interview: "The AI boom has sent TSMC's importance to an entirely new level." ([Source: Fortune Asia, 2025-07-30](https://fortune.com/asia/2025/07/30/tsmc-ai-boom-cc-wei-wendell-huang-global-500/))

The implication: TSMC's management does not view AI demand as a peak-cycle phenomenon, but as a multi-year secular growth driver that has not yet approached saturation.
