## eBay Executive Succession Bench — Key Appointments and Leadership Signals (2025–2026)

### Overview

As of early 2026, eBay's C-suite has undergone significant renewal under CEO Jamie Iannone. Three executives represent the core of eBay's next-generation leadership bench, each with distinct strategic responsibilities and succession relevance.

---

### Peggy Alford — Chief Financial Officer (CFO)

**Appointment:** May 2025, succeeding Steve Priest.

**Background:**
- Former Executive Vice President at PayPal
- Former Vice President of Core Payments at Facebook/Meta
- Deep experience in large-scale commerce and fintech financial leadership

**Strategic Focus:**

Alford joined eBay with a mandate centered on **"disciplined capital allocation"** — a phrase that signals a continued emphasis on shareholder returns (buybacks, dividends) over growth-at-all-costs investment. This posture is consistent with eBay's positioning as a mature, cash-generative business rather than a high-growth platform.

> Alford's arrival was framed in the context of eBay's capital return program and financial rigor under the transformation strategy.

— ([FinancialContent Analysis, 2026-02-20](https://markets.financialcontent.com/stocks/article/finterra-2026-2-20-resilience-in-re-commerce-an-in-depth-analysis-of-ebay-inc-ebay))

**Succession Relevance:** Alford's PayPal and Meta/Facebook pedigree, combined with her CFO role at a major public company, positions her as a credible CEO candidate in any future succession scenario. The CFO-to-CEO pathway is well-established in technology companies, and Alford's commerce and payments background aligns directly with eBay's core business.

---

### Mazen Rawashdeh — Chief Technology Officer (CTO)

**Appointment:** 2025.

**Strategic Focus:**

Rawashdeh was brought in to lead eBay's AI integration and technology platform build-out, with specific responsibility for:
- **In-house large language model (LLM)** development tailored to eBay's commerce data
- **Agentic commerce capabilities** — autonomous buying and selling agents
- Core platform infrastructure modernization

**Context:** Rawashdeh's hire signals that Iannone views AI not merely as a feature layer but as a fundamental platform rebuild. The decision to develop in-house LLM capabilities (rather than relying exclusively on third-party models like OpenAI or Anthropic) reflects eBay's belief that its 30-year transaction dataset is a proprietary training asset that warrants a custom AI stack.

— ([FinancialContent Analysis, 2026-02-20](https://markets.financialcontent.com/stocks/article/finterra-2026-2-20-resilience-in-re-commerce-an-in-depth-analysis-of-ebay-inc-ebay))

---

### Jordan Sweetnam — Chief Commercial Officer (CCO)

**Role:** Responsible for product development and marketplace integration — the intersection of buyer experience, seller tools, and category strategy.

**Strategic Focus:**

Sweetnam's CCO role bridges the commercial and product dimensions of eBay's transformation. His mandate encompasses:
- Focus category growth (Trading Cards, Sneakers, Luxury, Parts & Accessories, Refurbished)
- Buyer and seller product experience
- Marketplace integrity and trust features

— ([FinancialContent Analysis, 2026-02-20](https://markets.financialcontent.com/stocks/article/finterra-2026-2-20-resilience-in-re-commerce-an-in-depth-analysis-of-ebay-inc-ebay))

### Synthesis: What the Bench Signals

The composition of eBay's leadership bench as of 2025–2026 reflects three strategic priorities:

1. **Financial discipline over growth investment** (Alford's "disciplined capital allocation" mandate)
2. **AI as platform infrastructure** (Rawashdeh's in-house LLM and agentic commerce focus)
3. **Commercial depth in focus categories** (Sweetnam's product-marketplace integration role)

The absence of a publicly disclosed succession plan for Iannone is notable given his six-year tenure. Alford's background makes her the most visible internal succession candidate, but eBay has historically looked externally for CEO transitions (Iannone himself was a re-hire from Walmart eCommerce). The new CTO hire suggests the board and CEO view the AI platform build as a multi-year commitment that will extend well beyond the current transformation phase.
