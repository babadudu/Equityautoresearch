## CC Wei on AI Demand, CoWoS Capacity, and TSMC's Indispensability

### TSMC's Universal AI Role

Wei at the Q2 2024 earnings call articulated TSMC's platform-agnostic position in the AI supply chain:

> "Whether AI applications use ASICs or graphics processors, they all require very leading-edge technology and are working with TSMC."

([Source: TSMC Q2 2024 Transcript](https://investor.tsmc.com/english/encrypt/files/encrypt_file/reports/2024-08/5122725a56670882d777a8e8bfe0ed247cc55330/TSMC%202Q24%20Transcript.pdf))

This framing—GPU vs. custom ASIC is irrelevant, TSMC wins either way—became a recurring theme in Wei's investor communications through 2024–2025.

In the 2025 Fortune Global 500 interview, Wei described a step-change in TSMC's strategic importance:

> "The AI boom has sent TSMC's importance to an entirely new level."

([Source: Fortune Asia, 2025-07-30](https://fortune.com/asia/2025/07/30/tsmc-ai-boom-cc-wei-wendell-huang-global-500/))

### Exponential Token Growth as the Demand Engine

Wei in 2025 earnings calls revealed quantitative insight into why AI demand persistently exceeds TSMC's own forecasts:

> "AI demand actually continue to be very strong, stronger than we thought 3 months ago. The numbers are insane."

He then identified the root mechanism:

> "The number of tokens increase is exponential. Almost every 3 months, it will exponentially increase."

([Source: App Economy Insights, 2025](https://www.appeconomyinsights.com/p/tsmc-ai-megatrend-engine))

This exponential token growth—AI inference compute roughly doubling every quarter—is TSMC's fundamental demand thesis for sustained leading-edge wafer demand.

### Demand Validation Methodology: Customers' Customers

Wei explained how TSMC validates AI demand authenticity, going beyond tier-1 customers (NVIDIA, AMD, Apple) to their end users:

> "Right now, we pay a lot of attention to our customers' customer. We talk with them and see how they view the AI application, and then we make a judgment about how AI is going to grow."

([Source: App Economy Insights, 2025](https://www.appeconomyinsights.com/p/tsmc-ai-megatrend-engine))

At a TrendForce event (October 2024), Wei relayed a customer's description of current demand:

> "That's why we are still very comfortable that the demand on leading edge semiconductor is real."

He noted one key customer characterized demand as "crazy," describing the trend as "is only starting" and will "continue for several years." ([Source: TrendForce, 2024-10-17](https://www.trendforce.com/news/2024/10/17/news-tsmcs-c-c-wei-declares-no-interest-in-acquiring-intel-or-samsung-fabs-emphasizes-ai-is-real/))

In Digitimes (November 2025), Wei was even more explicit about the demand cycle's position on the S-curve:

> "[AI is] still in early stage."

([Source: Digitimes, 2025-11-24](https://www.digitimes.com/news/a20251124VL206/taiwan-tsmc-chairman-intel-arizona.html))

### CoWoS Capacity: Sold Out Through 2026

Wei repeatedly flagged advanced packaging as the tightest bottleneck in the AI supply chain:

> "Our CoWoS capacity is very tight and remains sold out through 2025 and into 2026."

([Source: Fusion Worldwide, 2025](https://info.fusionww.com/blog/inside-the-ai-bottleneck-cowos-hbm-and-2-3nm-capacity-constraints-through-2027))

On front-end wafer supply:

> "Advanced-node wafer demand is currently about three times short of available capacity."

([Source: Fusion Worldwide](https://info.fusionww.com/blog/inside-the-ai-bottleneck-cowos-hbm-and-2-3nm-capacity-constraints-through-2027))

His timeline for supply-demand balance:

> "I hope in 2025 or 2026 we can reach the balance."

([Source: The Register, 2024-07-18](https://www.theregister.com/2024/07/18/tsmc_ceo_predicts_ai_chip/))

On combined front-end and back-end tightness:

> "Everything related like front-end and back-end capacity is very tight. We are working very hard to make sure that the gap will be narrow."

([Source: App Economy Insights, 2025](https://www.appeconomyinsights.com/p/tsmc-ai-megatrend-engine))

### N2 Node Confidence

Wei on the N2 family's strategic longevity (Q3 2025 earnings call):

> "we believe N2, N2P, A16 and its derivatives will propel our N2 family to be another large and long-lasting node."

([Source: TSMC Q3 2025 Transcript](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/17/tsmc-tsm-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/))

CFO Wendell Huang confirmed N2's profitability profile is superior to N3:

> "N2 is well on track for volume production later this quarter. N2's structural profitability is better than the N3."

([Source: App Economy Insights, 2025](https://www.appeconomyinsights.com/p/tsmc-ai-megatrend-engine))

The GAA (Gate-All-Around) architecture in N2 enables higher wafer ASPs (estimated >$30K) while maintaining or improving gross margins, a combination that underpins TSMC's revenue-per-wafer expansion thesis.
