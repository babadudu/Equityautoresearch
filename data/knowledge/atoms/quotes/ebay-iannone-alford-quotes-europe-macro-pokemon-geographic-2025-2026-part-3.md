## eBay Executive Quotes: Geographic Divergence and 2026 Outlook

This atom collects direct quotes from eBay CEO Jamie Iannone, CFO Steve Alford, and VP John Egbert on the diverging performance between US and international markets in FY2025, the macro headwinds in Europe, cross-border trade policy pressures, and the outlook for Pokémon and trading cards in 2026.

---

### US Acceleration vs. International Deceleration in FY2025

eBay's US GMV accelerated sharply through FY2025: near 13% growth in Q3 2025 ([eBay Q3 2025 Earnings Call, 2025-10-29](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/29/ebay-ebay-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)) and approximately **19%** in Q4 2025 ([eBay Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-18](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/18/ebay-ebay-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)). International performance diverged: Q3 FX-neutral GMV grew ~4%, but Q4 international GMV turned negative on an organic FX-neutral basis (~-1%).

---

### Iannone on Europe's Consumer Confidence Challenge

On the Q4 2025 earnings call, Iannone offered a candid assessment of the European slowdown:

> "Europe has been more challenged as consumer confidence remains low and retail sales trends are subdued there."
> — Jamie Iannone, [eBay Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-18](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/18/ebay-ebay-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)

---

### Alford on UK, Germany, and Cross-Border Deceleration

CFO Steve Alford provided the specific geographic breakdown of international pressure, citing both macro weakness and US trade policy effects:

> "Challenging macroeconomic conditions in the UK and Germany and a deceleration in our cross-border volume growth due to U.S. trade policies."
> — Steve Alford, [eBay Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-18](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/18/ebay-ebay-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)

The UK and Germany are eBay's two largest European markets. The cross-border deceleration referenced here is distinct from the de minimis issue (which primarily impacts inbound US shipments); Alford's comment points to a broader chilling effect on international trade flows.

---

### Iannone on Cross-Border Impact Beyond China — Japan and Canada Sellers

On the Q3 2025 call, Iannone disclosed that the cross-border disruption from de minimis removal affects seller populations beyond China:

> "Sellers in Japan and Canada being amongst the most impacted [by de minimis removal]."
> — Jamie Iannone, [eBay Q3 2025 Earnings Call, 2025-10-29](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/29/ebay-ebay-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)

This is a notable clarification, as de minimis policy coverage in analyst models frequently focuses only on Chinese sellers.

---

### VP John Egbert on China's GMV Footprint and Forward Deployment

On the Q4 2024 earnings call (preceding these Q3/Q4 2025 calls), eBay VP John Egbert provided quantification of China's GMV exposure:

- Chinese-origin goods represent approximately **5% of total eBay GMV**
- Of that ~5%, approximately **75% has already been "forward deployed"** to US warehouses

Source: [Digital Commerce 360, 2025-03-10](https://www.digitalcommerce360.com/2025/03/10/ebay-sales-revenue-gmv-q4-2024/)

This forward-deployment figure is significant because it implies that the de minimis impact on eBay is substantially smaller than the headline China GMV exposure would suggest — most Chinese-seller inventory is already domestically warehoused in the US at time of sale.

---

### Alford on Pokémon Growth Deceleration in 2026

CFO Alford flagged a base-effect headwind for one of eBay's high-profile focus categories:

> "We are expecting a deceleration in Pokémon growth in 2026, just given the triple-digit growth that we saw in 2025. Although, I will point out that we expect healthy overall growth in trading cards."
> — Steve Alford, [eBay Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-18](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/18/ebay-ebay-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)

This quote is important for modeling: Pokémon achieved triple-digit GMV growth in 2025 (driven by a major card set release cycle and renewed collector demand), creating a very high comparable base for 2026. Alford's distinction between Pokémon-specific deceleration vs. overall trading card health suggests the broader category (Magic: The Gathering, sports cards, etc.) remains on a growth trajectory.

---

### Context and Analytical Implications

The geographic divergence in FY2025 (US ~19% Q4 GMV growth vs. international ~-1% organic FX-neutral) reflects two distinct forces:

1. **US strength**: Focus category execution (motors, trading cards, luxury) and AI-driven buyer conversion improvements
2. **International weakness**: European macro softness concentrated in UK and Germany, compounded by cross-border trade policy chilling effects (de minimis removal impacts inbound US, US trade uncertainty impacts outbound cross-border)

For modeling purposes:
- China's direct GMV exposure is bounded (~5% of total GMV), and 75% is already domestically warehoused
- Cross-border sensitivity is broader than China alone (Japan, Canada sellers also impacted)
- Pokémon provides a near-term 2026 headwind in trading cards specifically, not the broader collectibles category
- Europe (UK + Germany) remains the primary international drag, not resolved by eBay's focus-category strategy, which is primarily US-centric
