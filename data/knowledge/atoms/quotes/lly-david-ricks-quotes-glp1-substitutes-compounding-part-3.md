## David Ricks — GLP-1 Substitutes, Compounding Threat, and Buyer Power

### On why GLP-1 drugs are biologically irreplaceable

Ricks grounds the case against lifestyle-based substitutes in evolutionary biology:

> "Our ancestors roaming the planes... We're in a background of starvation."

He noted that human genetics were optimized for caloric scarcity, making modern environments — where average American intake reaches "3,600 calories" per day — a biological mismatch that diet and exercise alone cannot correct. — [All-In/Cheeky Pint, 2024](https://cheekypint.substack.com/p/dave-ricks-ceo-of-eli-lilly-on-glp)

> "Once you develop overweight or obesity, it's pathologic, it's difficult to very difficult to use traditional tools like diet and exercise."

— [Fortune, Nov 2024](https://fortune.com/2024/11/13/eli-lilly-ceo-dave-ricks/)

In the Washington Reporter interview, Ricks positioned pharmacotherapy as a national health infrastructure necessity, not merely a consumer product:

> "If people have been overweight or obese for most of their adult life, reducing it without medicine is really difficult."

> "Through lifestyle intervention and medicine, we could make this national crisis a distant memory."

— [Washington Reporter, Mar 2025](https://washingtonreporter.news/p/interview-eli-lilly-ceo-david-ricks)

### On discontinuation rates

On the Plain English podcast with Derek Thompson, Ricks directly rebutted the common bear argument that high GLP-1 discontinuation rates undermine the chronic treatment thesis:

> "Not unusual for chronic therapies."

He compared GLP-1 adherence profiles to those of hypertension and diabetes medications — chronic conditions where discontinuation is similarly common but does not diminish the pharmaceutical market's size or durability. — [Derek Thompson/Plain English, Feb 24 2026](https://www.theringer.com/podcasts/plain-english-with-derek-thompson/2026/02/24/the-future-of-glp-1-drugs-and-ai-medicine-with-eli-lilly-ceo-david-ricks)

**Context:** This rebuttal is important for investors: the discontinuation argument is used to argue GLP-1 drugs function more like acute treatments than chronic ones, which would compress the addressable lifetime value per patient.

### On the compounding/gray market threat

Ricks has been notably combative on the compounding pharmacy issue, framing it as a systemic market distortion and patent violation:

> "There's just too much opaque pricing and profit taking."

> "You've created basically a shadow generic industry undermining patents itself."

— [All-In/Cheeky Pint, 2024](https://cheekypint.substack.com/p/dave-ricks-ceo-of-eli-lilly-on-glp)

On the origin of counterfeit supply chains:

> "Most of them are sourced out of Chinese labs that aren't even approved in China."

— [Washington Reporter, Mar 2025](https://washingtonreporter.news/p/interview-eli-lilly-ceo-david-ricks)

**Context:** Ricks estimated 1–2 million Americans were using unverified compounded GLP-1 products. The FDA removed GLP-1 from its drug shortage list in April 2025, ending the regulatory grace period for compounding pharmacies — a development Ricks had been advocating for.

### On Medicare coverage and buyer power

Following negotiated Medicare GLP-1 pricing (monthly cost reduced to $245 for patients at $50 copay, ~75% below list price), Ricks framed the outcome as a volume expansion opportunity:

> "That opens up things pretty wide, and we'll see where we can go from there."

— [CNBC, Jan 30 2026](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/30/eli-lilly-ceo-david-ricks-medicare-coverage-obesity-pills.html)

On the Medicare implementation timeline:

> "We're excited to get going. We expect this to be effective by July 1."

— [Q4 2025 Earnings Call, Feb 4 2026](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/04/eli-lilly-lly-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)

### On consumer-facing pricing philosophy

Ricks frames Lilly's pricing strategy around what patients actually pay at the pharmacy counter, not list prices:

> "It's all about price at the pump. When people go to the pharmacy counter, how can they have a consistent and low cost for benefits that are helping their health?"

— [Bloomberg/JPM, Jan 13 2025](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/videos/2025-01-13/lilly-ceo-on-fighting-cancer-and-obesity-drug-pricing-video)

**Context:** LillyDirect, Lilly's DTC platform, surpassed 1 million monthly users by January 2026, making LillyDirect-packaged product the second-best-selling obesity medication in the US. The oral GLP-1 (orforglipron) is expected to have significantly lower manufacturing costs than injectable peptides, further reducing the cost-to-patient floor and weakening the compounding pharmacy value proposition.
