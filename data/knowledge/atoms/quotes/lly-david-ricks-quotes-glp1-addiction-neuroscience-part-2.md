## David Ricks on GLP-1 Addiction Indications & Neuroscience

### GLP-1 as "Anti-Hedonics" — Addiction Applications

In December 2024, Ricks made his most expansive public statement on behavioral health applications of GLP-1 at the Economic Club of Washington DC:

> "These medicines can be useful for other things we don't think about connected to weight."

He described tirzepatide and related GLP-1 compounds as "anti-hedonics":

> "They're reducing that desire cycle. So next year, you'll see Lilly start large studies in alcohol abuse and nicotine use, even in drug abuse."

*Source: [Economic Club DC, 2024-12-10](https://www.economicclub.org/events/david-ricks); [IBJ, 2024-12](https://www.ibj.com/articles/lilly-approves-15-billion-in-share-buybacks-raises-dividend)*

**Context:** This statement was significant because it publicly confirmed Lilly's intention to pursue addiction as a formal clinical indication for GLP-1 therapy. If validated, addiction indications would dramatically expand the GLP-1 TAM — the US alone has approximately 29 million people with alcohol use disorder and 31 million tobacco users. The "anti-hedonics" framing suggests the mechanism works by blunting reward-seeking behavior broadly, not just appetite.

### Alzheimer's Disease — 30 Years of Research Bearing Fruit

On Lilly's decades-long commitment to Alzheimer's research, culminating in Kisunla (donanemab) receiving FDA approval in 2024:

> "We've been studying... Alzheimer's for basically 30 years at Lilly."

On the clinical efficacy data for amyloid plaque clearance:

> "More than 80% reduction in like a year. Even to undetectable levels."

*Source: [T. Rowe Price, 2025-11](https://www.troweprice.com/en/us/insights/the-long-view-eli-lilly)*

On the broader neuroscience opportunity — framing the next decade:

> "The decade of the brain... brain health is probably the biggest category."

*Source: [Fortune, 2024-11](https://fortune.com/2024/11/13/eli-lilly-ceo-dave-ricks/)*

**Context:** Kisunla (donanemab) received FDA approval in 2024 for early symptomatic Alzheimer's disease. Ricks has positioned neuroscience as Lilly's third major growth pillar alongside cardiometabolic and oncology. The "decade of the brain" framing mirrors how he previously described the obesity opportunity — large unmet need, structural underpenetration, early in the S-curve.
