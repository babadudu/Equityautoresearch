## Anat Ashkenazi (CFO, Alphabet) — Capital Allocation, CapEx Discipline & Operational Efficiency

### On the Rigorous Investment Framework for Long-Term CapEx

**Quote:** "We have a rigorous framework and approach by which we evaluate these long-term investments."
**Source:** [Q3 2025 Earnings Call, Nov 2025](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/11/27/alphabet-googl-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)
**Context:** Ashkenazi defending Alphabet's 2025 CapEx of approximately $85-93B (roughly 22% of revenue) against analyst skepticism. The quote signals that the AI infrastructure buildout is not speculative — it follows a structured evaluation framework tied to projected returns on data center and compute investments.

### On Margin Performance Excluding Non-Recurring Charges

**Quote:** "Excluding the EC fine, operating income increased 22%, and operating margin was 33.9%."
**Source:** [Q3 2025 Earnings Call, Nov 2025](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/11/27/alphabet-googl-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)
**Context:** Ashkenazi clarifying Alphabet's Q3 2025 underlying margin performance by stripping out an EU regulatory fine. The 33.9% operating margin, achieved during a period of heavy CapEx investment, demonstrates that the infrastructure buildout has not yet structurally compressed profitability.

### On Productivity and Efficiency Commitments

**Quote:** "We continue to focus on driving improvements in productivity, efficiency, and utilization."
**Source:** [Q1 2025 Earnings Call, Apr 2025](https://www.rev.com/transcripts/alphabet-q1-2025-earnings-call)
**Context:** Ashkenazi reaffirming the efficiency-first posture adopted since the 2023 restructuring. This commitment is operationalized through AI-assisted coding (25%+ of Google's code now AI-generated per Pichai), head count discipline, and data center utilization optimization.

**Quote:** "We're focusing on continue to moderate the pace of compensation growth."
**Source:** [Q1 2025 Earnings Call, Apr 2025](https://www.rev.com/transcripts/alphabet-q1-2025-earnings-call)
**Context:** Ashkenazi communicating a deliberate deceleration in compensation growth — a meaningful signal given that Google's employee compensation historically represented one of the largest operating expense categories. This policy reinforces operating leverage as revenue scales.

### On Dynamic Capital Reallocation

**Quote:** "I plan to build on these efforts but also evaluate where we might be able to accelerate work and where we might need to pivot to free up capital for more attractive opportunities."
**Source:** [Q2 2025 Earnings Call, Jul 2025](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/07/23/alphabet-googl-q2-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)
**Context:** Ashkenazi framing the CapEx strategy as dynamic rather than a fixed commitment — acknowledging that some AI infrastructure investments may be reallocated if more attractive return opportunities emerge. This is a CFO-level hedge against overinvestment narratives, signaling flexibility within the overall AI buildout.

### Summary Context: ROIC and Operating Efficiency

These quotes come against the backdrop of Alphabet's sustained ROIC of 25-38% vs. WACC of approximately 9.4% — evidence of persistent excess value creation. The 2024 operating margin reached 32%, a historical peak, driven by post-2023 restructuring cost discipline. The 2025 CapEx spike (to ~$85-93B) temporarily compresses ROIC but management's stated framework (rigorous evaluation, productivity focus, compensation moderation, dynamic reallocation) is designed to defend long-run returns.
