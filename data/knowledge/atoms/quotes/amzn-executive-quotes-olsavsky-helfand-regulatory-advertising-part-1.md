# Amazon Executive Quotes: CFO Brian Olsavsky on Regulatory Defense; VP Jeremy Helfand on Prime Video Advertising

This atom collects direct quotes from Amazon's Chief Financial Officer Brian Olsavsky and Prime Video Advertising VP Jeremy Helfand. Topics covered: Amazon's response to antitrust scrutiny, competitive retail market framing, and the strategic scale of Prime Video's advertising tier.

---

## Brian Olsavsky — CFO

### On Amazon's Low-Price Focus and Consumer Surplus Defense Against FTC Claims (Feb 2025)

**Quote:**

> "Our focus on low prices has driven significant consumer surplus. We compete in a highly competitive retail market where customers have many choices."

— Brian Olsavsky, CFO, [AMZN Q4 2024 Earnings Call, Feb 2025](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1018724/000101872425000002/amzn-20241231xex991.htm)

**Context:** Olsavsky made this statement on Amazon's Q4 2024 earnings call in direct response to analyst questions about the FTC's antitrust lawsuit (Case 2:23-cv-01495, filed September 2023). The FTC complaint alleged that Amazon maintains monopoly power by penalizing sellers who offer lower prices on competing platforms (the "anti-discounting" allegation) and by tying Prime eligibility to use of Amazon's Fulfillment by Amazon (FBA) service.

Olsavsky's response deploys two classic antitrust defense arguments:
1. **Consumer welfare standard**: "Significant consumer surplus" — Amazon's pricing practices benefit consumers, not harm them, which is the central test under U.S. antitrust law.
2. **Market definition rebuttal**: "Highly competitive retail market where customers have many choices" — Amazon contests the FTC's market definition, arguing that the relevant market is total retail (where Amazon's share is ~5–6%), not online retail (where Amazon holds ~37–40% U.S. share).

**Analytical note:** The FTC's antitrust case (Case 2:23-cv-01495) carries an estimated 35% probability of adverse outcome in current analyst models (including approximately 20% probability FTC prevails and 15% probability of a structural remedy settlement). If structural remedies are imposed — such as mandatory FBA decoupling from Prime eligibility — analysts estimate a 5–10% revenue impact. Olsavsky's Q4 2024 framing suggests Amazon's legal strategy centers on market definition rather than behavioral concessions.

## Jeremy Helfand — VP, Prime Video Advertising

### On Prime Video's Advertising Scale at 315 Million Monthly Viewers (Nov 2025)

**Quote:**

> "We're just beginning to see what's possible when premium entertainment, engaged viewers, and innovative ad-technology converge with relevant and performant advertising at this unprecedented scale."

— Jeremy Helfand, VP Prime Video Advertising, [MediaPost, Nov 2025](https://www.mediapost.com/publications/article/410592/amazon-prime-video-ad-reach-jumps-nearly-60-to-31.html)

**Context:** Helfand made this statement in November 2025, when Amazon reported that Prime Video's ad-supported tier had reached **315 million average monthly viewers globally** — a 58% increase from 200 million reported in April 2024. Amazon introduced advertising to Prime Video in January 2024 (U.S., UK, Germany, Canada), with subsequent rollouts across 16 total markets by 2025.

**Strategic significance:**
- Prime Video's 315M monthly viewers exceeds Netflix's ~190M ad-supported tier viewers by approximately 125 million — giving Amazon a first-mover scale advantage in streaming advertising.
- MoffettNathanson Research estimated Prime Video advertising revenue at approximately $3.3B in 2024, $5.2B in 2025, and a trajectory toward $7.1B by 2026 ([Variety, Jan 2024](https://variety.com/2024/tv/news/amazon-prime-video-advertising-revenue-forecast-1235888805/)).
- The differentiation Helfand points to — "relevant and performant advertising" — refers to Amazon's unique first-party shopper intent data. Unlike social media or linear TV advertising, Amazon can connect a Prime Video ad impression directly to a purchase event on Amazon.com, providing measurable ROAS (return on ad spend) unavailable to competing streaming platforms.

**Context on Amazon's broader advertising position:** Amazon Advertising generated $68.6B in 2025 revenue, establishing Amazon as the world's third-largest digital advertising platform behind Google (~28% global share) and Meta (~20% global share), with Amazon at approximately 8–10% and growing. Prime Video's advertising expansion represents the incremental inventory layer that enables continued share gains — particularly in the high-CPM premium video category where Google (YouTube) and Meta (Instagram Reels) have historically dominated.

## Analytical Summary

These two executives address distinct but structurally related aspects of Amazon's business:

**Olsavsky** represents Amazon's defensive posture in the regulatory environment — framing the FTC's antitrust case as a mischaracterization of a competitively functioning market, while grounding the defense in consumer welfare economics. The "many choices" framing is central to Amazon's legal strategy and shapes how investors should think about downside scenarios from antitrust outcomes.

**Helfand** represents Amazon's forward-looking monetization expansion — the Prime Video advertising tier is the clearest current example of Amazon's "flywheel plus advertising" strategy, where existing Prime subscriber scale (a product of the e-commerce flywheel) is converted into a premium advertising inventory asset that generates incremental high-margin revenue without additional subscriber acquisition costs.
