### Ethics, Integrity & Value-Creation Drive (Score: 4.5 / 5)

**Morris Chang — Three Integrity Examples (source: 2005 遠見/Vision Magazine interview):**
1. **Top-down integrity**: Chang established that integrity must flow from the CEO downward — the leader's personal conduct sets the ethical ceiling for the entire organization
2. **Personally demanded resignations**: Chang directly required executives to resign when integrity was compromised, demonstrating zero-tolerance enforcement rather than delegating consequences
3. **TSMC as "淨土" (Pure Land / Clean Soil)**: Chang used this metaphor to describe TSMC as an island of ethical conduct in an industry context where corruption and kickbacks are common — an aspirational identity claim

**CC Wei — Integrity Commitment (2024):**
- Wei publicly reaffirmed the integrity-first culture as a standing commitment, signaling continuity beyond Chang's tenure

**Institutional governance evidence:**
- Corporate governance ranking: top 5% for 11 consecutive years (Taiwan corporate governance assessment)
- 77,000 employees subject to compliance program
- Ombudsmen system: internal whistleblower infrastructure allowing anonymous reporting

The 4.5/5 score reflects comprehensive multi-source documentation of integrity culture, with the minor deduction for limited third-party adversarial testing of actual compliance outcomes vs. stated policies.

### Key Thematic Implications

The leadership scoring pattern reveals TSMC's institutional design philosophy: ethical conduct is treated as a **competitive moat**, not merely a compliance requirement. Chang's framing of TSMC as a "pure land" positions integrity as a differentiator in talent attraction (engineers prefer employers with reputational stability) and customer trust (foundry clients share IP and roadmap data — trust is the product). Wei's 2024 reaffirmation signals the culture has been successfully institutionalized beyond its founder.
