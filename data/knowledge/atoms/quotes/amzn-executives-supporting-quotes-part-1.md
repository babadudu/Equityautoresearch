## Amazon Supporting Executives — Direct Quotes

This atom collects direct quotes from Amazon executives other than Bezos and Jassy, including CFO Brian Olsavsky and Prime Video Advertising VP Jeremy Helfand. Also includes a notable anonymous internal quote from FTC litigation documents.

---

### Brian Olsavsky (CFO) — On Antitrust and Consumer Welfare

> "Our focus on low prices has driven significant consumer surplus. We compete in a highly competitive retail market where customers have many choices."

**Source:** [AMZN Q4 2024 Earnings Call, Feb 2025](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1018724/000101872425000002/amzn-20241231xex991.htm)

**Person:** Brian Olsavsky, Amazon CFO

**Context:** Said during the Q4 2024 earnings call in response to questions about the FTC antitrust case (Case 2:23-cv-01495). The FTC's complaint alleges that Amazon's anti-discounting measures and FBA bundling harm both sellers and consumers. Olsavsky's response is Amazon's standard legal defense framing: that Amazon's platform drives price competition across the entire retail ecosystem and that customers benefit from the competition Amazon enables. The tension between this statement and FTC allegations of price-fixing via "Project Nessie" (a secret pricing algorithm) was a live litigation question at the time of the earnings call.

### Jeremy Helfand (Prime Video Advertising VP) — On Streaming Ad Scale

> "We're just beginning to see what's possible when premium entertainment, engaged viewers, and innovative ad-technology converge with relevant and performant advertising at this unprecedented scale."

**Source:** [MediaPost, Nov 2025](https://www.mediapost.com/publications/article/410592/amazon-prime-video-ad-reach-jumps-nearly-60-to-31.html)

**Person:** Jeremy Helfand, VP of Prime Video Advertising

**Context:** Said in November 2025 as Amazon reported Prime Video's ad-supported tier had reached 315 million average monthly viewers globally — up 58% from 200 million in April 2024. Helfand was emphasizing the uniqueness of Amazon's position: Prime Video combines premium streaming content (comparable to Netflix) with Amazon's first-party shopper intent data (unmatched in digital advertising). MoffettNathanson Research estimated Prime Video advertising revenue at approximately $3.3B in 2024, $5.2B in 2025, and projected $7.1B in 2026 ([Variety, Jan 2024](https://variety.com/2024/tv/news/amazon-prime-video-advertising-revenue-forecast-1235888805/)). For context, Netflix's ad-supported tier had approximately 190 million monthly viewers as of the same period — meaning Prime Video's addressable advertising audience was approximately 125 million larger.

### Anonymous Amazon Executive — Internal Prime Subscription Characterization

> "An unspoken cancer."

**Source:** FTC v. Amazon Prime Dark Patterns, Case No. 2:23-cv-0932-JHC; internal documents cited in FTC complaint

**Person:** Unnamed Amazon executive (identity protected in litigation documents)

**Context:** Internal Amazon documents produced in discovery for the FTC's dark patterns case revealed that at least one senior executive had privately characterized Amazon's Prime subscription mechanism using this phrase. The FTC alleged that Amazon used dark patterns to enroll users in Prime subscriptions without informed consent and deliberately complicated the cancellation process. In October 2025, Amazon agreed to a $2.5B settlement ($1B civil penalty + $1.5B consumer refunds), described as one of the largest FTC civil penalties in history ([Top Class Actions, Oct 2025](https://topclassactions.com/lawsuit-settlements/lawsuit-news/amazon-agrees-to-historic-2-5b-settlement-over-deceptive-prime-practices/)). The settlement required Amazon to provide clear opt-out mechanisms, transparent Prime disclosures, and a simplified cancellation flow. The anonymous internal quote is significant because it demonstrates that Amazon's own leadership recognized the problematic nature of the enrollment practices while they remained in place.
