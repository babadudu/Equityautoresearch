## Tim Cook — Selected Executive Quotes

All quotes are direct attributions to Tim Cook, Apple CEO. Organized thematically.

---

### On Joining Apple & Apple's Culture

**Context:** Cook reflecting on his 1998 decision to leave Compaq for a near-bankrupt Apple, speaking at Auburn University's commencement.

> "No more than five minutes into my initial interview with Steve, I wanted to throw caution and logic to the wind and join Apple."

— [Auburn University Commencement, 2010](https://humsci.auburn.edu/iqla/cook.php)

**Context:** Cook on Apple's philosophy of collaborative idea development, quoted in a 2026 Fortune retrospective on Steve Jobs.

> "That one plus one is equal to three, not two. That if you share an idea and debate it, it gets bigger and better."

— [Fortune, 2026-03-11](https://fortune.com/2026/03/11/apple-ceo-tim-cook-late-predecessor-steve-jobs-unusual-advice-never-ask-what-i-would-do/)

### On iPhone & Products as Personal Expression

**Context:** Cook on Apple's 50th anniversary, discussing the emotional significance of iPhone, in a GMA interview.

> "Oh, it brings me such joy because we make products that empower other people to express themselves."

— [GMA, 2026-03-17](https://abcnews.com/GMA/Culture/tim-cook-talks-apples-50th-anniversary-future-ai/story?id=131137939)

**Context:** Cook discussing iPhone Air's ultra-thin design, positioning iPhone as a deeply personal object in a WSJ interview.

> "When something becomes so much a part of you, it needs to reflect your style... We're saying this product is so personal that it needs to reflect you."

— [WSJ / 9to5Mac, 2025-09-10](https://9to5mac.com/2025/09/10/tim-cook-iphone-air-interview/)

### On Screen Time & Responsible Technology Use

**Context:** Cook in a WIRED deep-dive interview on technology and personal relationships.

> "My fundamental belief is, if you're looking at your phone more than you're looking in somebody's eyes, that's a problem."

> "I worry about people endlessly scrolling. That's the reason we do things like Screen Time, to try to guide people."

— [WIRED, 2024-12](https://www.macrumors.com/2024/12/04/apple-ceo-tim-cook-opens-up-in-new-interview/)

### On AI and Apple Intelligence

**Context:** Cook at Apple's 50th anniversary GMA interview, articulating Apple's values-driven approach to AI.

> "I think AI is so profound and can be so positive, but technology doesn't wanna be good. You have to make it good, and that requires it being built with certain values."

— [GMA, 2026-03-17](https://abcnews.com/GMA/Culture/tim-cook-talks-apples-50th-anniversary-future-ai/story?id=131137939)

**Context:** Cook on Apple Q3 FY2024 earnings call introducing Apple Intelligence following WWDC 2024.

> "Apple Intelligence builds on years of innovation and investment in AI and machine learning. It will transform how users interact with technology."

> "Apple Intelligence is built on a foundation of privacy, both through on-device processing and through Private Cloud Compute."

— [Apple Q3 FY2024 Earnings Call / Six Colors, 2024-08](https://sixcolors.com/post/2024/08/this-is-tim-q3-2024-analyst-call-transcript/)

**Context:** Cook at Q3 FY2024 earnings positioning Apple Intelligence as an upgrade cycle catalyst.

> "With Apple Intelligence, we are very excited about the level of value that we're going to provide to users, and we believe that presents another compelling upgrade reason."

— [Apple Q3 FY2024 Earnings Call / Six Colors, 2024-08](https://sixcolors.com/post/2024/08/this-is-tim-q3-2024-analyst-call-transcript/)

**Context:** Cook in WIRED interview on the current state of AI versus AGI timelines.

> "Right now the technology is good enough where we can deliver it to people and change their lives, and that's what we're focused on. We'll keep pulling the string and see where it takes us."

> "AGI itself is a ways away, at a minimum. We'll sort out along the way what the guardrails need to be in such an environment."

> "There's so much extraordinary benefit for humanity. Are there some things you have to have guardrails on? Of course."

— [WIRED / MacRumors, 2024-12-04](https://www.macrumors.com/2024/12/04/apple-ceo-tim-cook-opens-up-in-new-interview/)

### On Privacy, Regulation, and Government

**Context:** Cook during the 2016 FBI encryption standoff, when the U.S. government sought a backdoor into the San Bernardino shooter's iPhone. Cook refused, establishing a landmark privacy precedent.

> "We're in this bizarre position where we're defending the civil liberties of the country against the government."

> "No one should have a key that turns a billion locks."

> "We would have felt we had sold out."

— [TIME, 2016-03](https://time.com/4261796/tim-cook-transcript/)

**Context:** Cook on Apple's compliance with the EU Digital Markets Act (DMA), while expressing concerns about security implications.

> "We will comply with the DMA, but we have concerns about the impact on user security and privacy."

— [Apple Q2 FY2024 Earnings Call](https://investor.apple.com/investor-relations/default.aspx), 2024-05-02

**Context:** Cook on navigating China policy tensions, emphasizing Apple's non-partisan stance.

> "What I do is I interact on policy, not politics. I'm not a political person."

— [GMA, 2026-03-17](https://abcnews.com/GMA/Culture/tim-cook-talks-apples-50th-anniversary-future-ai/story?id=131137939)

**Context:** Cook announcing a major U.S. investment commitment at Apple's 50th anniversary, as part of a dual-track regulatory strategy.

> "We're putting $600 billion into the U.S. over the next four years."

— [GMA, 2026-03-17](https://abcnews.com/GMA/Culture/tim-cook-talks-apples-50th-anniversary-future-ai/story?id=131137939)
