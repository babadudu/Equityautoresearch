# eBay — Iannone and Alford on Geographic Performance, Macro Headwinds, and Trade Policy

## Context
eBay operates across 190 markets, with revenue roughly split between US and International segments. FY2025 saw a marked divergence: US GMV accelerated sharply while international, particularly Europe, faced compounding headwinds from consumer confidence weakness and US trade policy changes (de minimis removal). The following quotes from CEO Jamie Iannone, CFO Steve Alford, and VP John Egbert document this bifurcation in real time.

---

## US GMV Acceleration

**Source:** [eBay Q3 2025 Earnings Call, 2025-10-29](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/29/ebay-ebay-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/); [eBay Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-18](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/18/ebay-ebay-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)

**Context:** US GMV accelerated from approximately 13% growth in Q3 2025 to approximately 19% growth in Q4 2025 — a meaningful reacceleration that Iannone and Alford attributed to focus category execution (collectibles, motors, luxury), AI-driven listing and search improvements, and the structural shift toward recommerce in an inflationary consumer environment.

---

## Europe Challenged: Consumer Confidence and Retail Sales

**Source:** [eBay Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-18](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/18/ebay-ebay-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)

**Jamie Iannone:**
> "Europe has been more challenged as consumer confidence remains low and retail sales trends are subdued there."

**Context:** International GMV grew approximately 4% on an FX-neutral basis in Q3 2025 but declined approximately 1% on an organic FX-neutral basis in Q4 2025. Iannone's candid acknowledgment of European weakness reflects the macro divergence between the resilient US consumer and a Europe dealing with post-energy-crisis demand softness and elevated cost of living.

---

## CFO Alford: UK, Germany, and Cross-Border Deceleration

**Source:** [eBay Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-18](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/18/ebay-ebay-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)

**Steve Alford (CFO):**
> "Challenging macroeconomic conditions in the UK and Germany and a deceleration in our cross-border volume growth due to U.S. trade policies."

**Context:** Alford was more specific than Iannone in identifying the pressure sources: (1) UK and Germany as the two most affected European markets for domestic eBay activity; (2) US trade policies — specifically the removal of the de minimis exemption — as a structural deceleration in cross-border trade volume. This is significant because cross-border trade has historically been a competitive advantage for eBay's international marketplace.

---

## De Minimis Impact: Japan and Canada Also Affected

**Source:** [eBay Q3 2025 Earnings Call, 2025-10-29](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/29/ebay-ebay-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)

**Jamie Iannone:**
> "Sellers in Japan and Canada being amongst the most impacted" by de minimis removal.

**Context:** Iannone disclosed that the impact of US de minimis policy changes extended well beyond China-origin sellers. Japanese and Canadian sellers — who ship lower-value specialty items and collectibles to US buyers — experienced disproportionate disruption as the customs exemption was removed. This is notable because it indicates eBay's cross-border trade exposure is more geographically diverse than the China-to-US narrative that dominated headlines.

---

## China GMV Structure: Forward Deployed to US Warehouses

**Source:** [Digital Commerce 360, 2025-03-10](https://www.digitalcommerce360.com/2025/03/10/ebay-sales-revenue-gmv-q4-2024/)

**John Egbert (VP):**
> Goods from China account for approximately 5% of total GMV, of which approximately 75% have already been "forward deployed" to US warehouses.

**Context:** Egbert disclosed this figure during the Q4 2024 earnings call in response to analyst questions about eBay's China exposure. The 75% forward-deployment figure is significant — it means that the majority of Chinese-origin GMV on eBay is already physically located in the US before sale, substantially mitigating tariff and de minimis risks for that segment. The remaining ~25% is shipped directly from China to US buyers, which is the portion most exposed to trade policy changes.

---

## Enthusiast Buyer Resilience in Macro Weakness

**Source:** [eBay Q3 2025 Earnings Call, 2025-10-29](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/29/ebay-ebay-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)

**Jamie Iannone:**
> Even "in a challenging environment, macro environment in Europe," enthusiast buyers "don't churn on the marketplace."

**Context:** Iannone used this observation to reinforce eBay's focus category strategy: enthusiast buyers — those who purchase sports cards, vintage electronics, rare auto parts, or luxury collectibles — demonstrate lower price sensitivity and higher platform stickiness than general e-commerce shoppers. This behavioral durability supports eBay's thesis that deepening engagement in high-frequency, passion-driven categories insulates it from macro cyclicality.

---

## Global Marketplace Scale: 190 Markets

**Source:** [LinkedIn, 2021](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/empowering-economic-opportunity-all-jamie-iannone)

**Jamie Iannone:**
> "Over 147 million active buyers and 17 million sellers across 190 markets."

**Context:** Quoted from eBay's 2021 annual report. By 2025, active buyers had declined to approximately 132-134 million as eBay deliberately pruned low-engagement buyers through changes to the active buyer definition and de-emphasis of commoditized categories. However, the 190-market presence remains one of eBay's structural advantages over US-centric competitors.

## CFO Alford: Pokémon Growth Deceleration Warning

**Source:** [eBay Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-18](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/18/ebay-ebay-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)

**Steve Alford (CFO):**
> "We are expecting a deceleration in Pokémon growth in 2026, just given the triple-digit growth that we saw in 2025. Although, I will point out that we expect healthy overall growth in trading cards."

**Context:** Alford issued a rare product-specific forward guidance caution: Pokémon cards drove triple-digit GMV growth in 2025, creating a demanding comparison base that would mechanically suppress 2026 growth rates. Crucially, he separated the Pokémon-specific comparison effect from the broader trading card category, which he expected to grow healthily — signaling that the structural tailwind for collectibles remained intact even as one hypergrowth subcategory normalized.
