## C.C. Wei (魏哲家) — Selected Quotes

**Person:** C.C. Wei (魏哲家), Chairman and CEO of TSMC. Born 1953 in Lugu Township, Nantou County, Taiwan. Holds a B.S. and M.S. in Electrical Engineering from National Chiao Tung University, and a Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering from Yale University (1985), with a dissertation titled "RF Plasma Damage in MOS Structures" under advisor Prof. Tso-Ping Ma. ([Source: Wikipedia - Che-Chia Wei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Che-Chia_Wei))

---

### On AI Demand Being Structural and Persistent

**Quote (Q3 2025 Earnings Call):**
> "AI demand has continued to be very strong and stronger than expected three months prior."

**Context:** Spoken during TSMC's Q3 2025 earnings call, reinforcing the pattern that each successive quarter's AI demand outlook has exceeded the prior quarter's forecast — signaling that AI is not a temporary cyclical uplift but a structural demand shift. ([Source: TSMC Q3 2025 Transcript](https://investor.tsmc.com/english/encrypt/files/encrypt_file/reports/2025-10/6860312f04fd291d0f26b46c1234f84e6332717e/TSMC%203Q25%20Transcript.pdf))

---

**Quote (Q2 2024 Earnings Call):**
> "Whether AI applications use ASICs or graphics processors, they all require very leading-edge technology and are working with TSMC."

**Context:** Wei's response to questions about whether AI demand could shift away from GPU-centric architectures toward custom ASICs (e.g., Google TPUs, custom inference chips). His point: TSMC wins regardless of the AI chip architecture, because both ASICs and GPUs at leading-edge nodes are manufactured exclusively at TSMC. This underscores TSMC's platform-agnostic moat in the AI supply chain. ([Source: TSMC Q2 2024 Transcript](https://investor.tsmc.com/english/encrypt/files/encrypt_file/reports/2024-08/5122725a56670882d777a8e8bfe0ed247cc55330/TSMC%202Q24%20Transcript.pdf))

### On CoWoS Advanced Packaging Capacity

**Quote (Q2 2024 Earnings Call):**
> "From last year to this year, we have more than doubled [CoWoS capacity], and I expect next year we can double it again."

**Context:** Said in the context of TSMC aggressively ramping CoWoS (Chip-on-Wafer-on-Substrate) advanced packaging capacity to meet AI accelerator demand — primarily for NVIDIA HGX/GB200 systems. CoWoS integrates HBM memory directly with compute dies, and has become the critical bottleneck in AI server supply chains. ([Source: The Register, 2024-07-18](https://www.theregister.com/2024/07/18/tsmc_ceo_predicts_ai_chip/))

---

**Quote (2025):**
> "Our CoWoS capacity is very tight and remains sold out through 2025 and into 2026."

**Context:** Reaffirming that even after the capacity doublings referenced above, demand continues to outstrip supply. This supply tightness is the source of TSMC's pricing power in advanced packaging. ([Source: Fusion Worldwide, 2025](https://info.fusionww.com/blog/inside-the-ai-bottleneck-cowos-hbm-and-2-3nm-capacity-constraints-through-2027))

---

**Quote (Q2 2024 Earnings Call — on supply/demand balance):**
> "I hope in 2025 or 2026 we can reach the balance."

**Context:** Wei's candid acknowledgment that CoWoS supply will remain structurally short relative to AI chip demand for at least two years. The word "hope" is notable — it signals that even TSMC's aggressive ramp cannot be guaranteed to close the gap. ([Source: The Register, 2024-07-18](https://www.theregister.com/2024/07/18/tsmc_ceo_predicts_ai_chip/))

---

### On Competitive Position vs. Samsung

**Quote (Q3 2024 Earnings Call — when asked about Samsung's yield problems):**
> "AI is real."

**Context:** Wei's two-word answer when analysts pressed him on Samsung Foundry's well-publicized yield issues at 3nm and whether this created opportunity for TSMC. The brevity was deliberate: AI demand is so urgent and real that customers cannot afford the risk of poor yields, which structurally drives them to TSMC. Qualcomm and MediaTek had already shifted advanced-node orders away from Samsung to TSMC around this period. ([Source: TrendForce, 2024-10-17](https://www.trendforce.com/news/2024/10/17/news-tsmcs-c-c-wei-declares-no-interest-in-acquiring-intel-or-samsung-fabs-emphasizes-ai-is-real/))

### On Globalization and Organizational Culture

**Quote (CommonWealth Magazine interview, 2025):**
> "Turning Taiwan's TSMC into the World's TSMC."

**Context:** Wei's articulation of TSMC's long-term globalization vision as it accelerates construction of fabs in the United States (Arizona), Japan (Kumamoto), Germany (Dresden), and Singapore. The quote signals that globalization is not merely a geopolitical concession — it is a strategic identity shift for the company. ([Source: CommonWealth Magazine, 2025-07-10](https://english.cw.com.tw/article/article.action?id=4215))

---

**Quote (Fortune Asia interview, 2025):**
> "The semiconductor industry has never fought alone. Our success is built on the joint efforts of our colleagues, our customers, and our suppliers."

**Context:** Spoken in the context of Fortune's Global 500 recognition. Wei frames TSMC's dominance not as a solo achievement but as an ecosystem outcome — reflecting the company's open-platform foundry philosophy (serving all customers, no internal products competing with theirs) that Morris Chang designed from the start. ([Source: Fortune Asia, 2025-07-30](https://fortune.com/asia/2025/07/30/tsmc-ai-boom-cc-wei-wendell-huang-global-500/))
