## Steve Jobs — Founding Era and Resilience Quotes

All quotes are direct attributions to Steve Jobs, Apple co-founder and former CEO.

---

### On Being Fired from Apple (1985) and Resilience

**Context:** Jobs delivered the 2005 Stanford University commencement address, reflecting on his firing from Apple in 1985 by the board he helped create. His exile lasted until 1997, during which he founded NeXT and acquired Pixar. Upon returning to Apple he led the iMac (1998), iPod (2001), and eventual iPhone (2007) revolutions.

> "I didn't see it then, but it turned out that getting fired from Apple was the best thing that could have ever happened to me."

— [Stanford Commencement, 2005-06-12](https://news.stanford.edu/stories/2005/06/youve-got-find-love-jobs-says)

**Editorial note:** Jobs framed the firing as liberating him from the inertia of success, allowing him to re-enter Apple as a creative outsider. This perspective became foundational to Apple's culture of treating setbacks as strategic pivots rather than terminal failures.

### On the iPhone Launch (2007)

**Context:** Jobs introduced the iPhone on January 9, 2007 at Macworld Conference & Expo in San Francisco. The product combined three previously separate device categories — music player, phone, and internet device — with a full multi-touch interface and no physical keyboard. The announcement is widely considered one of the most consequential product launches in consumer electronics history.

> "A revolutionary mobile phone, a widescreen iPod with touch controls, and a breakthrough Internet communications device."

— [Apple Newsroom, 2007-01-09](https://www.apple.com/newsroom/2007/01/09Apple-Reinvents-the-Phone-with-iPhone/)

**Market impact:** iPhone sold 1.4 million units in its first year (2007), scaling to 11.6 million in 2008. Nokia and BlackBerry — dominant players at the time — lost their leadership positions within four years. The App Store launch in 2008 transformed iPhone into a platform, giving birth to the modern mobile software economy. The iPhone industry now generates approximately $587 billion in annual revenue and employs roughly 19 million people globally ([History Cooperative](https://historycooperative.org/the-history-of-the-iphone/)).
