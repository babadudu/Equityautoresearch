## David Ricks — Career Trajectory Quotes

Direct quotes and attributed context tracing David Ricks' path from IBM to Eli Lilly CEO.

### On joining Lilly and early career

Ricks joined Eli Lilly in 1996 as a business development associate focused on M&A — an unusual entry point that gave him early exposure to pharma's global complexity before most peers had left commercial roles. The reason he ended up in Indianapolis:

> His girlfriend (now wife) was in medical school.

— [Chief Executive, 2025](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/)

Sources on early career arc: [Quartr, 2025](https://quartr.com/insights/business-philosophy/david-a-ricks-eli-lillys-chairman-and-ceo); [Wikipedia — David A. Ricks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_A._Ricks)

### On his place in Lilly's history

> "I'm the 11th CEO of the company in 150 years."

— [T. Rowe Price, 2025](https://www.troweprice.com/en/us/insights/the-long-view-eli-lilly)

**Context:** Ricks became CEO in January 2017 when Lilly's market cap was approximately $70B. By 2024, it had grown to over $700B — a ~10x expansion driven primarily by tirzepatide (Mounjaro/Zepbound) adoption.

### On the China assignment (2008–2010) and its impact

Ricks served as President and General Manager of Eli Lilly China from approximately 2008, managing Lilly's operations during China's high-growth pharmaceutical market expansion. This experience directly informed his later hardline stance on compounded GLP-1 products:

> "Most of them are sourced out of Chinese labs that aren't even approved in China."

— [Washington Reporter, Mar 2025](https://washingtonreporter.news/p/interview-eli-lilly-ceo-david-ricks)

### On the tirzepatide discovery and what it meant for his CEO tenure

In late 2016, just before assuming the CEO role, Ricks received clinical data that redefined everything:

> "We had to stop the study because people were losing too much weight to stay in it."

This led to his self-description of the subsequent nine years:

> "It's been all execution."

— [Chief Executive, 2025](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/)

### On Lilly's institutional continuity as competitive advantage

> "I'm the 11th CEO of the company in 150 years."

Ricks references Lilly's low CEO turnover (averaging ~14 years per tenure) as structural to its long-cycle R&D bets — commitments like the 30-year Alzheimer's program require institutional patience that companies with frequent leadership changes cannot sustain. — [T. Rowe Price, 2025](https://www.troweprice.com/en/us/insights/the-long-view-eli-lilly)

### Career timeline (sourced)

- **1990**: BS from Purdue University; joins IBM as business analyst
- **1996**: MBA from Indiana University Kelley School; joins Eli Lilly as business development associate (M&A focus)
- **1998–2001**: Marketing manager, then regional sales manager
- **2002–2005**: Marketing Director then General Manager, Eli Lilly Canada — first P&L ownership
- **2008–2010**: President & General Manager, Eli Lilly China (based in Shanghai) — [Quartr, 2025](https://quartr.com/insights/business-philosophy/david-a-ricks-eli-lillys-chairman-and-ceo)
- **2010**: Returns as President, Eli Lilly USA
- **2012**: President, Bio-Medicines division (neuroscience, pain, immunology)
- **2016**: Receives tirzepatide Phase 2 data; inherits an execution mandate
- **Jan 2017**: Becomes CEO (11th in company's 150-year history); market cap ~$70B
- **2017–2022**: Divests Elanco (animal health), exits non-core assets, concentrates portfolio
- **2023–2026**: GLP-1 commercial scale-up, $55B manufacturing expansion, LillyDirect DTC launch
