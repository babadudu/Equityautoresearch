## CC Wei on AI Demand: Customer Visits and Financial Verification

**Context:** In late 2025, questions arose about whether AI hyperscaler CapEx spending — projected at ~$600 billion for 2026 — was sustainable or approaching bubble territory. Wei addressed investor skepticism directly after personally visiting all major cloud service provider (CSP) customers.

> "I also double checked their financial status—they are very rich."
— CC Wei, Chairman & CEO, TSMC; [Digitimes, 2025-11-24](https://www.digitimes.com/news/a20251124VL206/taiwan-tsmc-chairman-intel-arizona.html)

**Context:** Wei's remark was made during a trip to Arizona where he met with major US technology customers. The quote reflects TSMC's unusual position: as the sole advanced-node supplier, Wei is one of the few people outside hyperscalers who can triangulate actual AI infrastructure demand across all major players simultaneously.

## CC Wei on AI Demand: "Still in Early Stage"

**Context:** Amid investor debate over whether the AI infrastructure buildout was a supercycle or a short-term pull-forward, Wei articulated his conviction that AI adoption remains in its infancy.

> "[AI development is] still in early stage."
— CC Wei, Chairman & CEO, TSMC; [Digitimes, 2025-11-24](https://www.digitimes.com/news/a20251124VL206/taiwan-tsmc-chairman-intel-arizona.html)

**Context:** This framing aligns with TSMC's internal demand forecasting, which projects AI accelerator revenue CAGR of 54–56% from 2024 to 2029E. Wei's view is that each successive wave of AI inference scaling — where token volumes grow exponentially every three months — creates compounding demand that will persist for years.

## CC Wei on Customer Engagement Lead Times: Industry-Wide Visibility

**Context:** At the Q3 2025 earnings call, an analyst asked how TSMC can be confident in multi-year demand forecasts given the semiconductor industry's historically cyclical nature. Wei described an underappreciated structural advantage.

> "As process technology complexity increases, the engagement lead time with customer is now at least 2 to 3 years in advance. Therefore, we probably get the deepest and widest look possible in the industry."
— CC Wei, Chairman & CEO, TSMC; [TSMC Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/17/tsmc-tsm-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)

**Context:** This quote reveals a structural information advantage: because chip design cycles require customers to engage TSMC 2–3 years before tape-out, TSMC has de facto demand visibility extending further forward than any analyst, customer, or competitor. At N2 and below, the design-to-production cycle is so complex that customers must commit design architecture and volume forecasts years in advance, giving TSMC a proprietary leading indicator of global AI and consumer electronics demand.

## CC Wei on AI CapEx Risk: Personal Anxiety and Discipline

**Context:** Despite publicly validating AI demand, Wei also acknowledged the execution risk of committing to $52–56 billion in annual CapEx against uncertain AI commercialization timelines. This quote emerged in the context of the bull/bear debate around whether TSMC's investment cycle could result in overcapacity if AI adoption disappointed.

> "I'm also very nervous about it...If we didn't do it carefully, that would be a big disaster to TSMC."
— CC Wei, Chairman & CEO, TSMC; [The Next Platform, 2026-01-16](https://www.nextplatform.com/compute/2026/01/16/tsmc-has-no-choice-but-to-trust-the-sunny-ai-forecasts-of-its-customers/4092173)

**Context:** The admission is rare for a CEO of TSMC's stature. Wei is signaling that while he believes AI demand is real, TSMC's capital deployment is not unconditional — the company actively monitors demand signals and customer commitment levels before authorizing capacity additions. Historically, TSMC has maintained ~50% gross margins even through downturns by pacing capacity to demand; Wei's nervousness reflects awareness that the $52–56B CapEx level is unprecedented and leaves less room for error than prior cycles.
