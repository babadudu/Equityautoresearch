## CC Wei — AI Demand Conviction, CapEx Confidence, and Intel Competition

C.C. Wei (魏哲家) became TSMC's CEO in 2018 and assumed the additional role of Chairman in 2024. The following quotes span 2023–2026 and reflect his strategic thinking on AI demand, capital allocation, and competitive positioning.

---

### On AI Demand Strength

**Context:** Q3 2025 earnings call (October 2025), responding to analyst questions about whether AI demand was sustainable after a period of explosive growth.

> "AI demand actually continue to be very strong, it's more — more stronger than we thought 3 months ago."

> "The demand on leading edge semiconductor is real."

**Source:** [TSMC Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/17/tsmc-tsm-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)

**Significance:** These statements came after TSMC had already significantly raised its AI-related revenue forecasts. Wei's willingness to reiterate and strengthen the bullish demand view at a high base reflects genuine visibility from customer order books rather than promotional commentary.

---

### On Verifying Hyperscaler Financial Capacity

**Context:** Q4 2025 earnings call (January 2026). Wei was asked how TSMC could have confidence in $52–56B CapEx guidance when customer AI demand forecasts had repeatedly shifted. He described personally visiting all major cloud service provider (CSP) customers.

> "I also double checked their financial status — they are very rich."

**Source:** [The Next Platform, 2026-01-16](https://www.nextplatform.com/compute/2026/01/16/tsmc-has-no-choice-but-to-trust-the-sunny-ai-forecasts-of-its-customers/4092173)

**Significance:** A rare moment of candor from a typically measured executive. The quote reveals that TSMC's capacity commitments are backstopped not only by demand signals but by direct assessments of customer balance sheet strength — a supplier due-diligence check running in reverse.

---

### On Whether All AI Compute Converges on TSMC

**Context:** Q2 2024 earnings call, responding to a question about whether the shift toward custom ASICs (from hyperscalers like Google, Amazon, Microsoft) would reduce TSMC's exposure to NVIDIA.

> "Whether AI applications use ASICs or graphics processors, they all require very leading-edge technology and are working with TSMC."

**Source:** [TSMC Q2 2024 Earnings Call Transcript](https://investor.tsmc.com/english/encrypt/files/encrypt_file/reports/2024-08/5122725a56670882d777a8e8bfe0ed247cc55330/TSMC%202Q24%20Transcript.pdf)

**Significance:** This statement encapsulates TSMC's structural position in the AI era — it is the common infrastructure layer beneath both GPU and ASIC compute paradigms. The insight reframes the NVIDIA vs. ASIC debate as irrelevant to TSMC's revenue trajectory.

---

### On Intel Foundry's Competitiveness

**Context:** January 2026 earnings call. Analysts pressed Wei on whether Intel Foundry Services' aggressive investment program posed a competitive threat to TSMC.

> "In the development of semiconductor technology to this point, receiving investment has not helped to improve competitiveness, right?"

When directly asked whether he was worried about Intel's progress:

> "Not worried."

**Source:** [WCCFTech, 2026-01](https://wccftech.com/tsmcs-ceo-says-intel-foundry-wont-be-competitive-by-just-throwing-money/)

**Significance:** Wei's argument is that semiconductor manufacturing leadership is not purchasable — it requires decades of accumulated process knowledge, engineering talent, and organizational culture. Capital is necessary but not sufficient. The brevity of "Not worried" is itself notable for a CEO typically careful with public statements about competitors.

---

### On Geopolitical Disruptions Not Changing Strategy

**Context:** July 2024, responding to political commentary from Donald Trump suggesting Taiwan should pay for its own defense and implying the U.S. should not commit to defending Taiwan.

> "We did not change any of our original plan of expansion of our overseas fab. We continue to expand in Arizona, in Kumamoto and maybe in future in Europe. No change to our strategy."

**Source:** [The Register, 2024-07-18](https://www.theregister.com/2024/07/18/tsmc_ceo_predicts_ai_chip/)

**Significance:** Wei's posture — acknowledge the geopolitical noise, reaffirm the operational plan — reflects TSMC's strategic discipline. The company treats overseas fab expansion as economically driven (customer proximity, government incentives, risk diversification) rather than as a political response to any particular administration.

---

### On U.S. Talent and Arizona Site Selection Logic

**Context:** October 2023, Wei returned to Yale University (his alma mater) to speak at the Yale Science Building. He addressed the audience of students and faculty about TSMC's U.S. expansion.

> "The U.S. is the country with the most talented and innovative people, especially for the Ivy League student."

He also noted a practical constraint: TSMC's Arizona site selection was partly driven by the need for a large local pool of on-site semiconductor engineers — a resource that remains scarce in the U.S. relative to Taiwan.

**Source:** [Yale Daily News, 2023-10-05](https://yaledailynews.com/blog/2023/10/05/taiwan-semiconductor-ceo-c-c-wei-speaks-at-the-yale-science-building/)

**Significance:** The quote serves dual purposes — genuine flattery of the audience and a substantive acknowledgment that human capital is TSMC's binding constraint in U.S. expansion, more so than capital or technology.

---

### On Globalization Vision

**Context:** CommonWealth Magazine interview, July 2025, discussing how Wei thinks about TSMC's identity after the $165B U.S. investment announcement.

> "Turning Taiwan's TSMC into the World's TSMC."

**Source:** [CommonWealth Magazine, 2025-07-10](https://english.cw.com.tw/article/article.action?id=4215)

**Significance:** This framing represents a deliberate rebranding of TSMC's identity under geopolitical pressure. Rather than defending Taiwan-centric positioning, Wei is reframing TSMC's global footprint as a feature — making the company harder to nationalize, sanction, or exclude from any regional market.

---

### On Governance and Integrity (Post-Chang Succession)

**Context:** 2024, following Wei's assumption of the Chairman role previously held by Morris Chang and then Mark Liu.

> "No matter where TSMC conducts business in the world, it will abide by the highest standards of corporate governance, stick to the bottom line of integrity."

**Source:** [Ventron Chip, 2024](https://www.ventronchip.com/news/tsmc-completes-leadership-handover-c.-c.-wei-takes-over-as-chairman.html)

**Significance:** This statement directly addresses the concern of global customers — particularly fabless semiconductor companies — that TSMC's pure-play foundry model is only trustworthy if IP confidentiality and process integrity are guaranteed regardless of geography. The explicit commitment to consistent governance standards across all fab locations (Arizona, Kumamoto, Dresden) is central to TSMC's customer retention argument.
