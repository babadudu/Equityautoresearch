## David Ricks — Mission, Culture & Pharmaceutical Strategy

**David Ricks** is Chairman and CEO of Eli Lilly and Company. The following quotes are drawn from multiple interviews (2023–2026) and reflect his philosophy on corporate mission, pharmaceutical development culture, and long-term strategic orientation.

---

### On Eli Lilly's Core Mission

> **"We start with: What's human suffering? And work backwards."**
> — David Ricks, *Chief Executive*, 2025 ([source](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/))

Context: Ricks uses this framing to explain why Lilly targets only the largest disease categories — obesity, diabetes, Alzheimer's, cancer — rather than rare or niche indications. The mission is explicitly not to optimize for return on niche pricing but to solve the highest-burden diseases globally.

---

### On Pharmaceutical Development as a Team Sport

> **"Pharmaceutical development is the ultimate team sport."**
> — David Ricks, *T. Rowe Price*, 2025 ([source](https://www.troweprice.com/en/us/insights/the-long-view-eli-lilly))

Context: Ricks made this comment when discussing Lilly's organizational culture and how credit is distributed across research, clinical, manufacturing, and commercial teams. He attributes Lilly's success not to individual genius but to sustained cross-functional execution over multi-decade development cycles. The company runs on a 138-person lean executive team despite $14.5B annual R&D spend.

---

### On Lilly as GLP-1 Pioneer

> **"We're the first innovator in the space, having launched the first GLP-1 in the world in mid-2000s."**
> — David Ricks, *T. Rowe Price*, November 2025 ([source](https://www.troweprice.com/en/us/insights/the-long-view-eli-lilly))

Context: Ricks corrects the common narrative that Novo Nordisk is the GLP-1 category inventor. Lilly launched Byetta (exenatide, co-developed with Amylin) as the first approved GLP-1 receptor agonist in 2005. He uses this to reinforce Lilly's institutional depth in GLP-1 biology relative to newer entrants.

---

### On the Scale of the Remaining Work

> **"The work has just begun."**
> — David Ricks, *Chief Executive*, 2025 ([source](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/))

Context: Said in reference to the GLP-1 penetration gap — of ~110 million obese Americans, only 6–7 million are currently on medication. Ricks frames this not as a market saturation concern but as evidence that the commercial opportunity is still in its earliest innings despite Lilly's rapid growth.

---

### On Obesity as a National Priority

> **"All of the above strategy"** — Ricks's phrase for positioning GLP-1 drugs as complementary to — not a replacement for — diet and lifestyle interventions.
> — David Ricks, *Washington Reporter*, March 2025 ([source](https://washingtonreporter.news/p/interview-eli-lilly-ceo-david-ricks))

Context: Responding to RFK Jr.-aligned MAHA (Make America Healthy Again) rhetoric that treats pharmaceutical interventions as opposed to healthy lifestyle choices. Ricks explicitly embraces the dual approach: medicine AND behavior change.

---

### On Alignment with MAHA Health Policy

> **"At the top level, we have a huge amount of alignment. Everyone in our company wakes up every day trying to make people healthy."**
> — David Ricks, *Washington Reporter*, March 2025 ([source](https://washingtonreporter.news/p/interview-eli-lilly-ceo-david-ricks))

> **"Medicine isn't always the answer. Science tells us that already, so that's easy to embrace."**
> — David Ricks, *Washington Reporter*, March 2025 ([source](https://washingtonreporter.news/p/interview-eli-lilly-ceo-david-ricks))

Context: Ricks is navigating the politically charged environment created by the Trump administration's MAHA agenda. Rather than positioning pharma as adversarial to the wellness movement, he frames Lilly's GLP-1 program as deeply aligned with it — reducing chronic disease burden, not just selling pills.

---

### On the Golden Era for Medical Discovery

> **"A kind of a golden era... for medical discovery."**
> — David Ricks, *Washington Reporter*, March 2025 ([source](https://washingtonreporter.news/p/interview-eli-lilly-ceo-david-ricks))

Context: Ricks sees the convergence of GLP-1 biology, AI-assisted drug discovery, and advances in neuroscience as creating an unusually productive scientific environment. He made this comment in the context of discussing Lilly's pipeline breadth — from obesity to Alzheimer's to cancer — and the unprecedented productivity of modern drug discovery platforms.
