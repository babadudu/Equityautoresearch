## TSMC Leadership Quotes

### Morris Chang（張忠謀）— Founder

---

**On his early life and character formation:**
> "The early part of my life between birth and 18, the background was war, poverty, injustice."

*Context: Chang reflecting on his childhood in wartime China, which he credits with shaping his resilience and discipline.* ([Source: Synergy Startup Substack](https://synergystartup.substack.com/p/tsmc-the-untold-story-of-morris-chang))

---

**On leaving Sylvania:**
> "Sylvania wasn't going anywhere in semiconductors."

*Context: Chang explaining his 1958 decision to leave Sylvania Semiconductor and join Texas Instruments.* ([Source: Synergy Startup Substack](https://synergystartup.substack.com/p/tsmc-the-untold-story-of-morris-chang))

---

**On leaving Texas Instruments (1983):**
> "I left TI without any job offer, and I left because I felt that essentially I had been put out to pasture at TI."

*Context: Chang on the frustration of being sidelined at TI after 25 years, which ultimately led him toward founding TSMC.* ([Source: Synergy Startup Substack](https://synergystartup.substack.com/p/tsmc-the-untold-story-of-morris-chang))

---

**On General Instrument (brief tenure, early 1980s):**
> "General Instrument was mainly about acquisitions, mergers...it was just very different from what I was used to at Texas Instruments."

*Context: Chang explaining why General Instrument was not a fit after TI, reinforcing his preference for deep operational and engineering-focused organizations.* ([Source: Synergy Startup Substack](https://synergystartup.substack.com/p/tsmc-the-untold-story-of-morris-chang))

---

**On founding the foundry model:**
> "I wanted to establish a company that would be the foundry for the world's semiconductor industry. At that time, there was no such company."

*Context: Chang articulating the market gap he saw in 1987 — that the emerging fabless ecosystem would need a dedicated, neutral foundry partner.* ([Source: CommonWealth Publishing, 2024-12-02](https://english.cw.com.tw/article/article.action?id=3849))

---

**On TSMC's ambition at founding:**
> "When I founded a semiconductor company, there was only one path: to make it a world-class one."

*Context: From Chang's autobiography, reflecting on the single-minded ambition that drove TSMC from its founding.* ([Source: CommonWealth Publishing, 2024-12-02](https://english.cw.com.tw/article/article.action?id=3849))

---

**On TSMC's sources of competitive advantage:**
> "A pioneering business model executed to perfection and relentless innovation through proprietary technologies."

*Context: Chang's own summary of what made TSMC successful, offered in retrospect in his autobiography.* ([Source: CommonWealth Publishing, 2024-12-02](https://english.cw.com.tw/article/article.action?id=3849))

---

**On TSMC's foundry pricing philosophy (annotating the Apple/Intel comparison):**
> "Unwilling to take the price that customers can afford means the same that they were not good at doing dedicated foundry service. What TSMC is good at is making reasonable profits out of the price that is acceptable to the customer."

*Context: From Chang's autobiography describing the 2011 Apple negotiations; Chang was annotating Tim Cook's remark that Intel wasn't good at dedicated foundry — Chang explained TSMC's customer-centric pricing discipline as the distinguishing factor.* ([Source: TechSoda, Morris Chang autobiography analysis](https://techsoda.substack.com/p/tsmc-founder-morris-changs-autobiography))

---

**On refusing Apple's demand to eliminate dividends for capacity:**
> "I decided that I would take half of what Apple asked for... He said, well, I think you can eliminate your dividend. Your shareholders will understand that. I said, well, no."

*Context: Chang describing his negotiations with Apple over capacity allocation; even facing a massive order from the world's most important customer, he refused to sacrifice shareholder returns for short-term growth.* ([Source: Acquired Briefing, 2025-01](https://www.acquiredbriefing.com/p/the-morris-chang-interview))

---

**On declining IBM's partnership proposal:**
> "IBM still consider themselves to be the senior partner in any partnership they established... We declined without having to think about it at all."

*Context: Chang on rejecting IBM's joint R&D offer; TSMC needed to protect its independent technology roadmap and avoid being constrained by IBM's partnership terms.* ([Source: Acquired Briefing, 2025-01](https://www.acquiredbriefing.com/p/the-morris-chang-interview))

---

**On never dismissing small customers (about early NVIDIA):**
> "We should never be negligent in talking to future customers, even if the customer seems very small."

*Context: Chang reflecting on TSMC's 1997 decision to accept and nurture NVIDIA when it was still a small company; NVIDIA's 1997 letter to Hsinchu initiated what became one of the most important customer relationships in semiconductor history.* ([Source: TechSoda, Morris Chang autobiography analysis](https://techsoda.substack.com/p/tsmc-founder-morris-changs-autobiography))

---

**On considering Jensen Huang as CEO successor:**
> "Although I was quite familiar with the talent within the company, there was one person outside the company who, in terms of character, vision, professional experience, and knowledge in semiconductors—across all criteria—was worth considering as a successor for TSMC. I knew I should speak with him, and that person was Jensen Huang."

*Context: Revealed in Chang's autobiography — he had seriously evaluated Huang as the most qualified person to lead TSMC, underscoring the depth of his regard for Huang's capabilities.* ([Source: TechSoda, Morris Chang autobiography analysis](https://techsoda.substack.com/p/tsmc-founder-morris-changs-autobiography))

---

**Jensen Huang's response to the CEO offer:**
> "I spent 10 minutes explaining my deep aspirations of TSMC and how TSMC's package for CEO would be much higher than his income at that time, but he had no question and did not refuse me directly, but simply said, 'I already have a job'."

*Context: Chang recounting Huang's polite but firm refusal of the TSMC CEO role — a reminder that Huang's commitment to NVIDIA was unshakeable even when offered what would have been a higher-paying role.* ([Source: TechSoda, Morris Chang autobiography analysis](https://techsoda.substack.com/p/tsmc-founder-morris-changs-autobiography))

---

**On layoff philosophy during the 2008–2009 financial crisis:**
> "People will not respect us if we lay off by performance ratings. Because it's very subjective."

> "If you need the people back within a year, you shouldn't lay off."

*Context: Chang sharing TSMC's approach to the global financial crisis — preserving core talent even under cost pressure, which proved critical for the subsequent technology surge into 28nm and EUV nodes.* ([Source: Acquired Podcast, 2025-01](https://podscripts.co/podcasts/acquired/tsmc-founder-morris-chang))

---

**On globalization:**
> "Globalization is almost dead. Free trade is almost dead."

*Context: Chang at the Brookings Institution, April 2022 — a blunt assessment that directly underpinned TSMC's strategic decision to diversify manufacturing geographically into the U.S., Japan, and Europe.* ([Source: Brookings Institution, 2022-04](https://www.brookings.edu/wp-content/uploads/2022/04/Vying-for-Talent-Morris-Chang-20220414.pdf))

---

**On integrity governance:**
> "TSMC's executives are refrained from being anxious for quick success… I try my best to trickle down core company value — Integrity — from the top down."

> "If there is any executive violating this very principle, with a very heavy heart I must personally handle the situation and ask him/her to resign."

> "It is my goal to make TSMC the pure land of maintaining the core value of the society."

*Context: Chang speaking to Global Views Monthly (遠見雜誌), 2005, on how he personally enforced TSMC's integrity culture — treating it not as a slogan but as a personal accountability he would enforce even against senior executives.* ([Source: Global Views Monthly / Culc Hsin Hsin Blog, 2005](https://exampleof2015.wordpress.com/2016/03/03/integrity-we-should-carry-on-throughout-all-of-our-life/))

---

### C.C. Wei（魏哲家）— Chairman & CEO

---

**On choosing TSMC as a career:**
> "When looking for a job, you have to choose your boss first, and I had a great boss."

*Context: Wei explaining why he built his career at TSMC — a direct tribute to Morris Chang's mentorship and leadership.* ([Source: CommonWealth Magazine, 2024-06-03](https://english.cw.com.tw/article/article.action?id=3710))

---

**On TSMC's global strategic identity:**
> "Turning Taiwan's TSMC into the World's TSMC."

*Context: Wei articulating the central strategic shift of his tenure — from a Taiwan-headquartered company to a globally distributed institution, in response to geopolitical pressure and customer demand for supply chain resilience.* ([Source: CommonWealth Magazine, 2025-07-10](https://english.cw.com.tw/article/article.action?id=4215))

---

**On geopolitical stability of TSMC's strategy (2024, on Trump's Taiwan remarks):**
> "We did not change any of our original plan of expansion of our overseas fab. We continue to expand in Arizona, in Kumamoto and maybe in future in Europe. No change to our strategy."

*Context: Wei responding to political volatility around Taiwan; demonstrating TSMC's strategic composure and commitment to its multi-geography plan regardless of political noise.* ([Source: The Register, 2024-07-18](https://www.theregister.com/2024/07/18/tsmc_ceo_predicts_ai_chip/))

---

**On TSMC's position in the AI era:**
> "Whether AI applications use ASICs or graphics processors, they all require very leading-edge technology and are working with TSMC."

*Context: From TSMC Q2 2024 earnings call — Wei's articulation of TSMC's platform-agnostic indispensability in the AI compute stack, whether the winning architecture is GPU, custom ASIC, or otherwise.* ([Source: TSMC Q2 2024 Earnings Transcript](https://investor.tsmc.com/english/encrypt/files/encrypt_file/reports/2024-08/5122725a56670882d777a8e8bfe0ed247cc55330/TSMC%202Q24%20Transcript.pdf))

---

**On Intel Foundry's ability to compete through capital investment (January 2026):**
> "In the development of semiconductor technology to this point, receiving investment has not helped to improve competitiveness, right?"

> [When asked if he was worried about Intel Foundry's progress]: "Not worried."

*Context: Wei at the January 2026 earnings call, directly addressing Intel's multi-billion dollar foundry investments — arguing that capital cannot substitute for TSMC's accumulated decades of process knowledge, talent, and culture.* ([Source: WCCFTech, 2026-01](https://wccftech.com/tsmcs-ceo-says-intel-foundry-wont-be-competitive-by-just-throwing-money/))

---

**On governance continuity after taking Chairmanship:**
> "No matter where TSMC conducts business in the world, it will abide by the highest standards of corporate governance, stick to the bottom line of integrity."

*Context: Wei upon completing the leadership transition in June 2024, signaling to global customers that TSMC's integrity-first pure-play foundry model would continue unchanged under his combined Chairman/CEO role.* ([Source: Ventron Chip, 2024](https://www.ventronchip.com/news/tsmc-completes-leadership-handover-c.-c.-wei-takes-over-as-chairman.html))
