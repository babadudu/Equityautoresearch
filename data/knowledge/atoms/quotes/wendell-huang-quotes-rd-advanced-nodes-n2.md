## Wendell Huang — Current R&D Focus: 14Å, 16Å, and 2nm

TSMC's CFO Wendell Huang confirmed the company's leading-edge R&D priorities in a 2025 interview, providing a rare explicit statement from the finance side about technology roadmap bets:

> "We are focusing on 14-angstrom, 16-angstrom, and 2-nanometer process technologies."

([Source: TechSoda, TSMC 2024 Annual Report Analysis](https://techsoda.substack.com/p/explainer-tsmcs-2024-annual-report))

The mention of both angstrom-scale nodes (A14, A16) and 2nm (N2) in a single statement reflects the overlapping nature of TSMC's roadmap execution. N2 was in the final ramp stages in early 2025, while A16 and A14 represent the next generation — introducing backside power delivery (BSPDN) that enables significant density and performance gains. The fact that a CFO (rather than a CTO) is citing specific node names signals how central technology execution is to TSMC's financial narrative and investor communications.

## Wendell Huang — N2 on Track, with Better Structural Profitability Than N3

Huang provided a critical financial data point about the N2 ramp that goes beyond typical production timeline updates:

> "N2 is well on track for volume production later this quarter. N2's structural profitability is better than the N3."

([Source: App Economy Insights, 2025](https://www.appeconomyinsights.com/p/tsmc-ai-megatrend-engine))

This statement is significant for several reasons:

**1. Production timeline confidence**: "Well on track" from a CFO — who is accountable to investors for guidance accuracy — carries more weight than engineering milestone claims. It signals that yield ramp, equipment delivery, and customer qualification are all proceeding without material surprises.

**2. Structural profitability improvement**: The claim that N2 has better structural profitability than N3 is a counter-intuitive but important insight. More advanced nodes typically require more EUV mask layers, more process steps, and more expensive equipment — all of which increase cost per wafer. However, TSMC's experience curve effects, improved tooling efficiency, and the substantially higher wafer ASPs it can command for N2 (estimated at $30,000+ vs. $18,000–$20,000 for N3) more than offset the cost increases. This means TSMC's gross margin expansion is not just cyclical (AI demand) but structural (each new node generation contributes higher absolute margin).

**3. Implications for the long-term margin floor**: If N2 is more profitable than N3, and N3 has been more profitable than N5, the implication is that TSMC's 53% long-term gross margin floor (also cited by Huang) is a conservative baseline that will be revised upward as the revenue mix shifts toward newer nodes. As of 2025, 3nm revenue represented approximately 26% of total wafer revenue, with 5nm adding another 34% — meaning over 60% of revenue was already on sub-7nm nodes.

## Context: Morris Chang's 8% R&D Rule as Foundation

Huang's R&D focus statements sit on top of a structural commitment to research spending that predates his tenure. TSMC founder Morris Chang established a rule that TSMC would spend 8% of revenue on R&D regardless of business conditions:

> "8%, regardless of whether there's a recession or not. And it's just 8% of revenue."

Chang explained the design intent:

> "I wanted to make him at ease, you know what I mean? He doesn't have to argue, he doesn't have to request every year."

([Source: Acquired Podcast, January 2025](https://podscripts.co/podcasts/acquired/tsmc-founder-morris-chang))

The 8% rule removed R&D budget from the annual negotiation cycle and made it a structural commitment. For Huang, as CFO, this means the research programs targeting A14, A16, and N2 are funded through cycles — they do not compete with near-term profitability targets. This structural funding continuity is a key reason TSMC has maintained its process technology lead through multiple semiconductor downturns while competitors (Intel, Samsung Foundry) have experienced execution setbacks.

## Summary: Huang's Technology-Finance Bridge

Wendell Huang occupies an unusual position as a CFO who routinely discusses specific process node names, yield trajectories, and R&D allocations. This reflects TSMC's culture of treating technology leadership as the primary financial asset. Key takeaways from his statements on advanced nodes:

- **N2 ramp is on track** and structurally more profitable than N3 — each new node generation improves margin structure, not just revenue
- **14Å and 16Å** are the active frontier of R&D investment, with backside power delivery as the key architectural advancement
- **8% R&D rule** (Chang-era policy) provides structural funding continuity that insulates technology programs from economic cycles
- **Capex and R&D together** create a compounding technology moat: consistent R&D produces leadership nodes; consistent capex builds the fabs to monetize them at scale
