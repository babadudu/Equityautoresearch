# Jamie Iannone & Peggy Alford — eBay De Minimis Tariff Impact Quotes (2025)

On August 29, 2025, the U.S. fully eliminated the de minimis exemption for goods under $800, replacing it with a flat per-item tariff of $80-$200 during a transition period before conversion to full ad valorem rates. This policy shift was the most significant regulatory change affecting global e-commerce in 2025, disproportionately impacting Chinese direct-ship platforms (Temu, SHEIN) while creating complex cross-currents for eBay's cross-border marketplace.

---

## Jamie Iannone — On the Immediate Impact (Q3 2025)

> "The recent elimination of the de minimis exemption for imports under $800 has created incremental cost."

— Jamie Iannone, CEO, eBay ([eBay Q3 2025 Earnings Call, 2025-10-29](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/29/ebay-ebay-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/))

**Context:** First public acknowledgment of the de minimis impact on eBay's GMV trajectory. The elimination affected cross-border sellers—particularly Japanese and Canadian sellers shipping to U.S. buyers—who faced new cost structures that compressed their margins or made certain low-price-point transactions uneconomical.

---

## Iannone — On Volume Deceleration from De Minimis Removal

> "We saw a deceleration in year-over-year volume growth starting in September in key markets importing into the U.S. after the removal of the de minimis exemption."

— Jamie Iannone, CEO, eBay ([eBay Q3 2025 Earnings Call, 2025-10-29](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/29/ebay-ebay-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/))

**Context:** September 2025 was the first full month under the new tariff regime (effective August 29). The deceleration was concentrated in international-origin listings targeting U.S. buyers, particularly in categories like trading cards, collectibles, and electronics where Japan and other Asian markets have historically been strong supply sources.

---

## Iannone — On Q4 2025 Headwind Duration

> "We expect to face... a full quarter of impact from the removal of global de minimis exemption, versus a single month."

— Jamie Iannone, CEO, eBay ([eBay Q3 2025 Earnings Call, 2025-10-29](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/29/ebay-ebay-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/))

**Context:** Forward guidance given during Q3 2025 earnings (reported October 29, 2025), warning that Q4 2025 would absorb a full three months of de minimis impact vs. only one month (September) in Q3. This was a key factor in eBay's cautious Q4 guidance, despite the underlying U.S. domestic GMV accelerating sharply.

---

## Peggy Alford (CFO) — On Annualizing De Minimis Headwinds

> "We expect to face incremental headwinds from annualizing breakage associated with the global de minimis changes."

— Peggy Alford, CFO, eBay ([eBay Q3 2025 Earnings Call, 2025-10-29](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/29/ebay-ebay-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/))

**Context:** Alford's framing of "annualizing breakage" refers to the one-time step-down in cross-border GMV caused by the tariff change. Once lapped (i.e., after the 12-month anniversary of the August 2025 implementation), this headwind becomes a comp-period baseline rather than an ongoing deceleration. This provided reassurance that the de minimis impact was a transition headwind, not a permanent structural impairment.

## Analytical Context: Competitive Asymmetry of De Minimis Removal

While de minimis removal created short-term volume headwinds for eBay's cross-border sellers, industry analysts noted a structural competitive benefit: platforms like Temu and SHEIN, which relied heavily on sub-$800 direct-ship fulfillment from Chinese warehouses, faced far more severe cost structure disruptions. 

As noted in a [FinancialContent analysis (2026-02-20)](https://markets.financialcontent.com/stocks/article/finterra-2026-2-20-resilience-in-re-commerce-an-in-depth-analysis-of-ebay-inc-ebay), the new tariffs "inadvertently benefited eBay by leveling the playing field against ultra-low-cost importers." eBay's 90% non-new, secondhand inventory composition means the majority of its GMV involves used goods exchanged between U.S.-based buyers and sellers—largely insulated from import tariffs. The cross-border sellers most affected (Japan, Canada, Europe) represent a minority of total platform GMV.
