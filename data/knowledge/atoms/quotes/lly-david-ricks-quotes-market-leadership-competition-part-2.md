## David Ricks on GLP-1 Market Leadership & Competition

### Claiming the Top Position

At the February 2026 CNBC Squawk Box interview following Q4 2025 earnings:

> "We're the market leader in both diabetes and obesity now."

*Source: [CNBC Squawk Box, 2026-02-04](https://www.cnbc.com/video/2026/02/04/watch-cnbcs-full-interview-with-eli-lilly-ceo-david-ricks.html)*

In the Q4 2025 Earnings Call, Ricks quantified the US diabetes market position:

> "Exiting the quarter with over 55% of new prescriptions."

*Source: [Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-04](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/04/eli-lilly-lly-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)*

On the historical sweep of the moment:

> "We're probably having our best moment in 150 years."

*Source: [Chief Executive, 2025](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/)*

At JPMorgan 2026 on the molecule itself:

> "Tirzepatide is an incredible molecule that's changing the lives of millions of people around the world."

*Source: [PharmExec/JPM26, 2026-01](https://www.pharmexec.com/view/jp-morgan-2026-eli-lilly-continuing-glp1-success)*

### On Oral GLP-1 Competition (vs. Novo Nordisk's Oral Semaglutide)

On orforglipron's clinical positioning against semaglutide:

> "Is it as good as Ozempic, essentially, or semaglutide? That's the goal for us."

*Source: [Fortune Well, 2025-01](https://fortune.com/well/article/eli-lilly-weight-loss-pill-orforglipron-glp1-2026/)*

On whether oral GLP-1 will cannibalize injectable tirzepatide:

> "Not what we're seeing right now nor is it what we really expect."

*Source: [Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-04](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/04/eli-lilly-lly-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)*

**Context:** Novo Nordisk's oral semaglutide (Wegovy oral) received FDA approval in December 2025, opening the oral obesity treatment segment. Lilly's orforglipron was in Phase 3 as of early 2026 with launch expected first half 2026. Ricks' positioning: oral GLP-1 expands the market by reaching injection-averse patients, rather than converting existing injectable users — i.e., it is a TAM expansion play, not cannibalization.

### On Competitive Entrants and the Obesity Ecosystem

At JPMorgan 2026, on how future entrants will realistically compete in the obesity space:

> "If there's 200 chronic diseases that are downstream affected by obesity, surely Novo and Lilly will have left one or two behind... I think some companies will choose to niche into one of those, try to get pricing power [and] own a narrow but focused segment. That's viable."

*Source: [BioSpace/JPM26, 2026-01](https://www.biospace.com/business/jpm26-lilly-plots-path-to-dominance-as-obesity-market-enters-a-new-era)*

On welcoming more players to grow the overall market:

> "Someone has to do this work" — more participants expanding the market matters more than protecting share.

*Source: [T. Rowe Price, 2025-11](https://www.troweprice.com/en/us/insights/the-long-view-eli-lilly)*

On supply constraints acting as a competitive moat during the market build phase:

> "We're still gating promotion and gating launches globally... we're building facilities as fast as we can."

> "I'm pretty confident... we'll need to do more [capacity building]."

*Source: [Q4 2024 Earnings Call, 2025-02-06](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/02/06/eli-lilly-lly-q4-2024-earnings-call-transcript/)*

**Context:** As of early 2026, 193 obesity candidate drugs were in development globally (IQVIA, 2026-01), but Ricks' competitive framework is nuanced: he acknowledges the duopoly will be challenged at the margin by niche players focusing on specific downstream comorbidities. The real competitive moat during 2024-2026 was manufacturing scale and supply — Lilly and Novo's production infrastructure is measured in years to replicate. Ricks' framing suggests he is less concerned about head-to-head competition and more focused on ensuring Lilly captures the core diabetes/obesity market while competitors fight for the periphery.

### On China Market and Counterfeit GLP-1s

On the quality risk from Chinese-sourced GLP-1 copies:

> "Most of them are sourced out of Chinese labs that aren't even approved in China."

*Source: [Washington Reporter, 2025-03](https://washingtonreporter.news/p/interview-eli-lilly-ceo-david-ricks)*

**Context:** Ricks made this remark in the context of the growing compounded/counterfeit GLP-1 market. With semaglutide's core patent set to expire in China in 2026, and local GLP-1 competitors (e.g., Innovent's mazdutide) advancing, Ricks was drawing a sharp quality distinction between Lilly-manufactured tirzepatide and unregulated copies entering the market.
