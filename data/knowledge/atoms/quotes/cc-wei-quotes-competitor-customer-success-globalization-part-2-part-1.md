## CC Wei on Competitors, Customer Success Philosophy, and Globalization Surprise

### No Acquisitions: Organic Growth Only

At the Q3 2024 earnings call, Wei was asked whether TSMC would consider acquiring Intel or Samsung fabrication facilities. He answered twice, both times with a single word:

> "No."

Wei made clear TSMC's strategy is self-built capacity expansion rather than inorganic acquisition. ([Source: TrendForce, 2024-10-17](https://www.trendforce.com/news/2024/10/17/news-tsmcs-c-c-wei-declares-no-interest-in-acquiring-intel-or-samsung-fabs-emphasizes-ai-is-real/))

### Geographic Flexibility as Customer Value

Wei framed TSMC's global fab expansion not as political compliance but as a customer requirement:

> "Many customers stress the importance of TSMC's manufacturing expertise with geographic flexibility."

([Source: TSMC PR, 2025](https://pr.tsmc.com/english/news/3210))

This positions overseas fabs (Arizona, Kumamoto, Dresden) as a customer-driven service differentiation rather than a cost or political concession.

### Geopolitical Steadiness: No Strategy Change

Despite sustained pressure from US, EU, and Japanese industrial policies, Wei consistently communicated strategic continuity:

> "So far, we did not change any of our original plan of expansion of our overseas fab. We continue to expand in Arizona, in Kumamoto and maybe in future in Europe. No change to our strategy."

([Source: The Register, 2024-07-18](https://www.theregister.com/2024/07/18/tsmc_ceo_predicts_ai_chip/))

### The Globalization Surprise

In the 2025 Fortune Asia interview, Wei reflected on the unprecedented scale and speed of TSMC's geopolitical entanglement:

> "If someone had told me 10 years ago that I'd meet two presidents in one week to discuss hundreds of billions of dollars in investments to expand our global footprint, I would have thought they were joking."

([Source: Fortune Asia, 2025-07-30](https://fortune.com/asia/2025/07/30/tsmc-ai-boom-cc-wei-wendell-huang-global-500/))

This quote—made when TSMC had just announced $165B in US investment commitments alongside President Trump—illustrates how radically the geopolitical context of TSMC's business had shifted from the company's Taiwan-centric origins.

### US Investment Announcement and AI Foundation

At the White House announcement of TSMC's expanded US investment (March 2025), Wei stated:

> "AI is reshaping our daily lives, and semiconductor technology is the foundation for new capabilities and applications."

([Source: TIME, 2025](https://time.com/collections/time100-ai-2025/7305839/cc-wei-ai/))

TSMC received approximately $6.6B in direct CHIPS Act subsidies for its Arizona fab, as part of the broader $52.7B CHIPS and Science Act (2022). ([Source: TSMC PR, 2025-03](https://pr.tsmc.com/english/news/3210))

### Government Communications: Frank but Guarded

Wei on TSMC's dialogue with the incoming and outgoing US administrations (Q4 2024 earnings call):

> "We have a very frank and open communication with the current government and with the future one also. I cannot say anything more than that."

([Source: TSMC Q4 2024 Transcript](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/01/16/taiwan-semiconductor-manufacturing-tsm-q4-2024-ear/))

### China: Legal Guardrails on Public Comment

Wei revealed the procedural constraints he operates under when discussing China, in a TIME interview (2024):

> "I was not allowed to give a speech without going through my legal department…I don't make a public comment on China."

([Source: TIME, 2024-09](https://time.com/collections/time100-ai-2024/7012769/cc-wei/))

This reflects TSMC's exposure to US export control enforcement—a November 2024 BIS notification required TSMC to restrict exports of sub-7nm AI chips to China after TSMC-manufactured chips were found in Huawei AI processors. TSMC's China revenue share fell from ~22% (2020) to ~9% (2024). ([Source: Motley Fool](https://www.fool.com/research/tsmc-revenue/))

Despite the China revenue compression, Wei remained bullish on AI's overall trajectory (Q3 2025 earnings):

> "if the China market is not available, but I still think the AI's growth will be very dramatically."

([Source: TSMC Q3 2025 Transcript](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/17/tsmc-tsm-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/))

### Tariff Risk: From Dismissal to Formal Acknowledgment

Wei's tone on tariffs evolved materially between 2024 and 2025. At the Q2 2024 call, he was nonchalant:

> "On the tariff, not that we know of. Normally, if there's an import tariff, the customers will be responsible for that, but no discussion, nothing."

([Source: The Register, 2024-07-18](https://www.theregister.com/2024/07/18/tsmc_ceo_predicts_ai_chip/))

By Q3 2025, tariffs had entered TSMC's formal forward guidance language:

> "we understand there are uncertainties and risk from the potential impact of tariff policies, especially in consumer-related and price-sensitive end market segment."

([Source: TSMC Q3 2025 Transcript](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/17/tsmc-tsm-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/))

The shift from informal dismissal to formal risk disclosure marks a meaningful evolution in how TSMC communicates macro policy risk to investors.
