## David Ricks on Strategic History & CEO Tenure

### 2017 — Turbulence at the Start

On the early challenges he inherited upon becoming CEO in January 2017:

> "There's turbulence. I'll own that... this is a market with hundreds of millions of people globally."

*Source: [Q4 2024 Earnings Call, 2025-02-06](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/02/06/eli-lilly-lly-q4-2024-earnings-call-transcript/)*

**Context:** When Ricks took over as CEO, Lilly was still working through the tail effects of the Zyprexa (olanzapine) patent cliff — a ~$7B revenue gap created when its blockbuster psychiatric drug lost exclusivity in 2011. The company's EPS for 2017 was -$0.26 (the only loss year), partly reflecting a one-time tax reform charge but also the genuine profitability pressure of the transition period. Ricks' immediate strategic moves: streamline the executive team to 138 members, divest animal health (Elanco), and concentrate R&D on diabetes/obesity, oncology, and neuroscience.

---

### 2019 — Elanco Divestiture and Pure-Play Focus

On the strategic rationale for spinning off Elanco:

> "That's what Lilly's for. Doing things at scale, but also focusing on where the most suffering is."

*Source: [Fortune, 2024-11](https://fortune.com/2024/11/13/eli-lilly-ceo-dave-ricks/)*

**Context:** Elanco Animal Health IPO'd in September 2018 and was fully separated from Lilly in 2019. The divestiture transformed Lilly into a pure-play human pharmaceutical company. In retrospect, the timing was strategically crucial — all capital and management attention was redirected to tirzepatide's Phase 3 development and manufacturing scale-up, which would require $55B+ in CapEx by 2026.

### 2021-2022 — The Tirzepatide Revelation

On the moment Phase 3 SURPASS trial data revealed tirzepatide's extraordinary weight-loss efficacy:

> "We had to stop the study because people were losing too much weight to stay in it."

*Source: [Chief Executive, 2025](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/)*

**Context:** The SURPASS-2 trial in 2021 was the inflection point. Tirzepatide's dual GIP+GLP-1 agonism produced ~20% average body weight reduction — roughly double what semaglutide had achieved. The quote reflects both the clinical drama (patients losing weight so rapidly that trial protocols were strained) and the strategic clarity it provided: Lilly had a molecule categorically superior to anything else in development.

### 2025-2026 — Market Dominance Achieved

On supply expansion results in 2025:

> "We exceeded our goal to produce 1.8 times the number of incretin doses in 2025 compared to 2024."

> "We've committed over $55 billion, the largest manufacturing build-out in company history."

*Source: [Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-04](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/04/eli-lilly-lly-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)*

On the historical significance of this moment in Lilly's 150-year history:

> "We're probably having our best moment in 150 years."

*Source: [Chief Executive, 2025](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/)*

### Long-Term Confidence in the Obesity Opportunity

On the multi-decade trajectory of GLP-1 adoption:

> "We believe both products are still very early in their launch cycle."

> "By the time we get to the end of the decade, it's hard to imagine we are not using obesity medications."

*Source: [Q4 2024 Earnings Call, 2025-02-06](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/02/06/eli-lilly-lly-q4-2024-earnings-call-transcript/)*

**Context:** Despite tirzepatide generating $36.5B in 2025 revenue, Ricks' long-term framing positions this as early innings. Current US obesity drug penetration is estimated at 3–5% of the eligible population. His decade-end statement implies continued compound growth through the 2020s as access expands (Medicare coverage, DTC channels, oral formats), international markets scale, and clinical indications multiply (cardiovascular, addiction, NASH, sleep apnea, Alzheimer's).
