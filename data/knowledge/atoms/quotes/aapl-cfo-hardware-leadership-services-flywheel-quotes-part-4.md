# AAPL Executive Quotes: CFO Parekh, CFO Maestri & SVP Ternus on Services Flywheel and Hardware Transformation

This atom collects direct quotes from Apple's current CFO Kevan Parekh, former CFO Luca Maestri, and SVP of Hardware Engineering John Ternus. Topics covered include the installed base as a services growth engine, paid subscription trajectory, geographic records, margin accretion from services, and Apple Silicon's transformation of the Mac. These quotes provide the financial and technical leadership perspective on the two structural shifts reshaping Apple's business: the Services flywheel and the Apple Silicon platform transition.

---

## Kevan Parekh — CFO (2025–present)

### Services: Accretion to Company Margins

> "Services business in general and aggregate is accretive to the overall company margin."

— Kevan Parekh, [Apple Q1 FY2025 Earnings Call / Six Colors](https://sixcolors.com/post/2025/01/this-is-tim-and-kevan-apples-q1-results-call-transcribed/), 2025-01-30

**Context:** Parekh's first major earnings call as CFO (succeeding Luca Maestri) included this explicit confirmation that the Services segment (~70% gross margin) is structurally improving Apple's overall margin profile (which reached 46.9% in FY2025, up from 37.8% in FY2019).

> "We have a very broad services portfolio and those businesses have very different margin profiles."

— Kevan Parekh, [Apple Q1 FY2025 Earnings Call / Six Colors](https://sixcolors.com/post/2025/01/this-is-tim-and-kevan-apples-q1-results-call-transcribed/), 2025-01-30

**Context:** Parekh added nuance — not all services carry the same margin. The Google search revenue sharing deal (estimated ~$20B/year) and App Store commission revenue carry very different margin structures than Apple TV+ (content-cost-intensive) or AppleCare (claims-exposure). The "very different margin profiles" comment signals that Services mix matters, not just Services aggregate.

### Installed Base as Growth Opportunity

> "Services continues to see strong momentum, and the growth of our installed base of active devices gives us great opportunities."

— Kevan Parekh, [Apple Q1 FY2025 Earnings Call / Six Colors](https://sixcolors.com/post/2025/01/this-is-tim-and-kevan-apples-q1-results-call-transcribed/), 2025-01-30

**Context:** Parekh articulated the flywheel mechanic directly — installed base growth is not just a hardware story but a services opportunity expansion. Each new device added to the 2.35B active base is a potential incremental subscriber.

### Geographic Records and Global Momentum

> "We set September quarter records in the Americas, Europe, Japan, and the rest of Asia-Pacific and grew in the vast majority of markets we track."

— Kevan Parekh, [Apple Q4 FY2025 Earnings Call / Six Colors](https://sixcolors.com/post/2025/10/this-is-tim-transcript-of-apples-q4-2025-financial-call/), 2025-10

> "We grew in every geographic segment and saw double-digit growth in Europe, Greater China, and the rest of Asia-Pacific."

— Kevan Parekh, [Apple Q3 FY2025 Earnings Call / Six Colors](https://sixcolors.com/post/2025/07/this-is-tim-transcript-of-apples-q3-2025-financial-call/), 2025-07

> "We achieved all-time revenue records in the Americas, Europe, Japan, and Rest of Asia Pacific."

— Kevan Parekh, [Apple Q4 FY2025 Earnings Call / Six Colors](https://sixcolors.com/post/2025/10/this-is-tim-transcript-of-apples-q4-2025-financial-call/), 2025-10

> "We set an all-time revenue record in India."

— Kevan Parekh, [Apple Q4 FY2025 Earnings Call / Six Colors](https://sixcolors.com/post/2025/10/this-is-tim-transcript-of-apples-q4-2025-financial-call/), 2025-10

### Services Crossing $100 Billion

> "We surpassed $100 billion, so the best year ever at 14% year-on-year."

— Kevan Parekh, [Apple Q4 FY2025 Earnings Call / Six Colors](https://sixcolors.com/post/2025/10/this-is-tim-transcript-of-apples-q4-2025-financial-call/), 2025-10

**Context:** FY2025 marked the first year Apple's Services segment crossed $100B annually ($109.2B), representing 26.2% of total revenue. Parekh's use of "best year ever" and the 14% YoY growth rate frames this as acceleration, not maturation.

### Paid Subscriptions: Continued Double-Digit Growth

> "Both transacting and paid accounts reached new all-time highs, with paid accounts growing double digits year-over-year...well over a billion paid subscriptions."

— Kevan Parekh, [Apple Q4 FY2025 Earnings Call / Six Colors](https://sixcolors.com/post/2025/10/this-is-tim-transcript-of-apples-q4-2025-financial-call/), 2025-10

**Context:** Apple crossed 1 billion paid subscriptions in Q3 FY2024 and has continued expanding. At well over 1 billion paid subscriptions across the platform (including third-party subscriptions transacted through the App Store), Apple operates one of the world's largest subscription distribution networks.

## Luca Maestri — Former CFO (2014–2024)

### The Installed Base as Services Engine

> "The installed base is the engine for services growth, and the fact that the installed base is growing very nicely...that gives us a larger addressable pool."

— Luca Maestri, [Apple Q1 FY2023 Earnings Call / Six Colors](https://sixcolors.com/post/2023/02/this-is-tim-apple-q1-2023-analyst-call-transcript/), 2023-02

**Context:** Maestri provided the clearest single-sentence articulation of Apple's flywheel logic: the installed base is not a metric of past hardware sales but a forward indicator of services monetization potential. The phrase "larger addressable pool" directly links device accumulation to revenue opportunity.

### Paid Subscription Growth Trajectory (FY2022–FY2024)

Maestri documented the paid subscription escalation across three key milestones:

**860 million (Q3 FY2022):**
> "We now have more than 860 million paid subscriptions across the services on our platform, which is up more than 160 million during the last 12 months alone."

— Luca Maestri, [Apple Q3 FY2022 Earnings Call / Six Colors](https://sixcolors.com/post/2022/07/this-is-tim-q3-2022-analyst-call-transcript/), 2022-07

**935 million (Q1 FY2023):**
> "Apple now has more than 935 million paid subscriptions."

— Luca Maestri, [Apple Q1 FY2023 Earnings Call / Six Colors](https://sixcolors.com/post/2023/02/this-is-tim-apple-q1-2023-analyst-call-transcript/), 2023-02

**Over 1 billion (Q3 FY2024):**
> "We have well over one billion paid subscriptions across the services on our platform, more than double the number that we had only four years ago."

— Luca Maestri, [Apple Q3 FY2024 Earnings Call / Six Colors](https://sixcolors.com/post/2024/08/this-is-tim-q3-2024-analyst-call-transcript/), 2024-08

**Context:** The trajectory from 860M (mid-2022) → 935M (early 2023) → 1B+ (mid-2024) → "well over 1B" (end-2025) reflects both organic subscriber growth and Apple's expansion of its services portfolio. Maestri's note that subscriptions had "more than doubled in four years" frames the growth as structural, not episodic.

### Services Revenue Milestones Under Maestri

**Q2 FY2024 — $23.9B:**
> "Services revenue set an all-time record of $23.9 billion, up 14% year-over-year."

— Luca Maestri, [Apple Q2 FY2024 Earnings Call / Six Colors](https://sixcolors.com/post/2024/05/this-is-tim-transcript-of-apples-q2-2024-analyst-call/), 2024-05

**Q1 FY2023 — $20.8B:**
> "Set an all-time revenue record of $20.8 billion in services, which was better than what we had expected."

— Luca Maestri, [Apple Q1 FY2023 Earnings Call / Six Colors](https://sixcolors.com/post/2023/02/this-is-tim-apple-q1-2023-analyst-call-transcript/), 2023-02

## John Ternus — SVP Hardware Engineering

### Apple Silicon and the Mac Transformation

> "Apple silicon has completely transformed the Mac experience, and we're just getting started."

— John Ternus, [WWDC 2023 Keynote / Apple.com](https://www.apple.com/apple-events/), 2023-06-05

**Context:** Ternus delivered this at WWDC 2023, three years into the M-series transition (M1 launched November 2020). By the time of this statement, Mac had posted two consecutive years of revenue reacceleration. The phrase "we're just getting started" proved forward-looking: Mac FY2025 revenue reached $33.7B (+12.4% YoY), the second-fastest growing segment behind Services. The M3 generation (2023–2024) and M4 generation (2024–2025) continued to extend Mac's performance-per-watt advantage over Intel/AMD alternatives in professional creative and AI development workflows.

**Context for financial impact:** Apple Silicon reduced Mac's chip procurement costs (from Intel licensing to in-house TSMC fabrication shared across iPhone, iPad, and Mac), contributing to hardware gross margin expansion from ~31% (FY2019) to ~36% (FY2025). This margin improvement across the hardware base, combined with Services mix shift, accounts for Apple's total gross margin expansion of +910bps over the same period.

## Synthesis

The CFO and hardware leadership quotes collectively document the two structural transformations reshaping Apple's financial profile:

1. **Services flywheel (Maestri → Parekh continuity):** Both CFOs frame the installed base — now 2.35B active devices — as the primary forward driver of revenue quality. Subscription count (860M → 935M → 1B+ → well over 1B) and Services revenue ($20.8B quarterly → $28.8B quarterly) demonstrate that the flywheel is not merely theoretical but compounding measurably each year.

2. **Apple Silicon hardware transformation (Ternus):** "We're just getting started" (2023) has been validated by Mac's sustained growth reacceleration. The chip transition is simultaneously a margin story (lower component costs), a performance differentiation story (competitive moat in pro workflows), and an AI readiness story (on-device neural processing enabling Apple Intelligence across the Mac lineup).
