# Craig Jelinek Executive Quotes — Leadership Philosophy, Pricing Discipline, and Member Focus

Craig Jelinek served as President and CEO of Costco Wholesale from January 2012 through January 2024, succeeding co-founder Jim Sinegal. Jelinek joined Costco in 1984 — one year after its founding — and spent nearly three decades rising through the organization before taking the top role. His leadership style was widely described as a continuation of Sinegal's cultural framework: low margins, employee-first compensation, and an uncompromising focus on member value. The quotes below span his 12-year tenure.

---

## On the $1.50 Hot Dog — Continuing Sinegal's Commitment

> **"Trust me, that's never going to change. If I have to subsidize it personally, it's not going to change."**
> — Craig Jelinek, *Motley Fool*, August 1, 2013 ([source](https://www.fool.com/investing/general/2013/08/01/an-interview-with-costco-ceo-craig-jelinek.aspx))

**Context:** In his first major deep-dive interview as CEO, Jelinek was asked about the iconic $1.50 hot dog and soda combo that Sinegal had vowed would never increase in price. Jelinek did not simply affirm the commitment — he escalated it, pledging personal financial subsidy if necessary. The fact that both Sinegal and Jelinek made near-identical commitments to this specific price point (separated by four years) demonstrates how the $1.50 hot dog functions as a cultural anchor: it is a visible, verifiable test of whether Costco's stated values align with its actual pricing behavior. As of FY2025, the price remains $1.50 — over 40 years unchanged.

---

## On Membership Fee Increases — Restraint Under Inflationary Pressure

> **"We think about it every year. Right now, in terms of the membership fee, it is not on the table right at the moment."**
> — Craig Jelinek, *CNBC*, July 11, 2022 ([source](https://www.cnbc.com/2022/07/11/4-things-costco-ceo-craig-jelinek-said-in-a-rare-interview-on-the-economy-retail-trends-and-more.html))

**Context:** This statement came at the height of the 2022 inflation surge, when Costco faced significant input cost pressure and investors were widely expecting a membership fee increase as a lever to protect margins. Jelinek's response — acknowledging annual deliberation but deferring action — reflects the company's philosophy of absorbing cost shocks rather than immediately passing them to members. Costco did eventually raise membership fees in September 2024 (first increase since 2017): individual Gold Star from $60 to $65, Executive from $120 to $130. The 7-year gap between increases (2017–2024) illustrates the discipline Jelinek's quote foreshadowed.

---

## Continuity of Philosophy Across CEOs

Jelinek's public statements consistently reinforced the framework established by Sinegal. The through-line from Sinegal (CEO 1983–2012) to Jelinek (2012–2024) to Ron Vachris (2024–present) on the hot dog price commitment illustrates an organizational culture where pricing philosophy is institutionalized rather than personality-dependent. Three successive CEOs spanning four decades have made substantively identical public commitments to the same $1.50 price point — a degree of cultural continuity that is rare in large-cap retail. Jelinek often deflected personal credit for Costco's success, attributing results to the system Sinegal built: employee retention, limited SKU discipline, and the markup cap that prevents margin creep even when competitive pressures ease.
