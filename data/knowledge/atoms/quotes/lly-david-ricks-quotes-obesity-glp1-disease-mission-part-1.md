# David Ricks on Obesity as Disease, GLP-1 Mission, and Patient Biology

**David Ricks** is the CEO of Eli Lilly and Company. The quotes below span 2024–2025 and address Lilly's core mission, the biological basis for treating obesity with medication, and Ricks' framing of the GLP-1 opportunity as a generational public health intervention.

---

## On Lilly's Core Mission

**Source: [Chief Executive, 2025](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/)**

> "We start with: What's human suffering? And work backwards."

*Context: Ricks explaining Lilly's therapeutic area selection philosophy — the company deliberately targets the largest unmet medical needs (obesity, diabetes, Alzheimer's, cancer) rather than rare or niche diseases.*

---

**Source: [T. Rowe Price, 2025](https://www.troweprice.com/en/us/insights/the-long-view-eli-lilly)**

> "Pharmaceutical development is the ultimate team sport."

*Context: Ricks describing Lilly's organizational culture and why the company structures itself around cross-functional collaboration rather than individual brilliance.*

> "We're the first innovator in the space, having launched the first GLP-1 in the world in mid-2000s."

*Context: Ricks situating Lilly's historical claim to GLP-1 leadership, noting the company was a pioneer in incretin biology before tirzepatide became a blockbuster.*

> "The work has just begun."

*Context: Ricks responding to suggestions that Lilly has already achieved its GLP-1 ambitions — he emphasizes that with only 6–7 million Americans on GLP-1 therapy out of 110 million obese, the penetration story is in its earliest stages.*

---

## On the Biology of Obesity and Why Medication Is Necessary

**Source: [Cheeky Pint / All-In, 2024](https://cheekypint.substack.com/p/dave-ricks-ceo-of-eli-lilly-on-glp)**

> "Our ancestors roaming the planes... We're in a background of starvation."

*Context: Ricks explaining the evolutionary mismatch underlying the modern obesity epidemic — human genetics were optimized for caloric scarcity, not the average American diet of 3,600 calories per day. He uses this to argue that obesity is not a failure of willpower but a biological response to an environment humans did not evolve for.*

> "People lose the weight and they don't feel miserable."

*Context: Ricks on the patient experience of tirzepatide — distinguishing it from traditional caloric restriction diets, which patients find psychologically and physically punishing. GLP-1 agonists alter appetite signaling at the neurological level, making caloric reduction feel natural rather than forced.*

> "800 calories a day, it's 800, which is almost a meal."

*Context: Ricks citing the average daily caloric reduction achieved by tirzepatide patients — approximately one full meal's worth of calories per day — as the mechanism behind its 20% average body weight reduction.*

---

**Source: [Fortune, 2024-11](https://fortune.com/2024/11/13/eli-lilly-ceo-dave-ricks/)**

> "Once you develop overweight or obesity, it's pathologic, it's difficult to very difficult to use traditional tools like diet and exercise."

*Context: Ricks making the clinical case that obesity is a chronic disease requiring pharmacological intervention, not merely a behavioral problem solvable through lifestyle changes. This framing is central to Lilly's argument for insurance reimbursement.*

> "The drugs work and they add to the diet exercise regime for people who are already overweight."

*Context: Ricks responding to critics who argue that GLP-1 drugs replace rather than complement healthy behaviors — he positions them as additive, not substitutive.*

## On the Scale of the Opportunity

**Source: [Chief Executive, 2025](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/)**

> "There's a billion people globally with obesity within five years."

*Context: Ricks framing the global TAM for GLP-1 obesity treatment. With approximately 110 million obese Americans and only 6–7 million currently on medication, the penetration gap is enormous. Globally, the WHO projects obesity rates will continue rising across emerging markets.*

> "60+ cancer studies" and GLP-1 exploration in "addiction and behavioral health."

*Context: Ricks describing Lilly's pipeline diversification — the company is running over 60 oncology studies using GLP-1 biology and exploring applications in addiction (early data suggest GLP-1 reduces alcohol and drug cravings) and behavioral health. Each new indication expands the addressable patient population beyond the core obesity/diabetes market.*

## On the National Public Health Dimension

**Source: [Washington Reporter, 2025-03](https://washingtonreporter.news/p/interview-eli-lilly-ceo-david-ricks)**

> "To us, it's one of the most important things we could do. We need an all of the above strategy."

*Context: Ricks on the obesity epidemic as a national crisis requiring pharmaceutical intervention alongside lifestyle programs — he explicitly endorses a multi-pronged approach rather than positioning drugs as the only solution.*

> "If people have been overweight or obese for most of their adult life, reducing it without medicine is really difficult."

*Context: Ricks reinforcing the disease model of obesity and the medical necessity of pharmacological support for chronic, long-duration cases.*

> "Through lifestyle intervention and medicine, we could make this national crisis a distant memory."

*Context: Ricks' most optimistic public statement on the long-term potential of GLP-1 therapy — positioning it alongside statins and antibiotics as a category of medicine that fundamentally altered population-level health outcomes.*

## On Tirzepatide's Clinical Breakthrough Moment

**Source: [Chief Executive, 2025](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/)**

> "We had to stop the study because people were losing too much weight to stay in it."

*Context: Ricks recounting the clinical trial experience with tirzepatide — the SURMOUNT trials showed weight loss so dramatic that participants fell below the minimum BMI threshold for study inclusion, requiring protocol modification. Average weight loss of 20% of body weight was unprecedented in pharmacological obesity treatment.*

**Source: [Fortune, 2024-11](https://fortune.com/2024/11/13/eli-lilly-ceo-dave-ricks/)**

> "It's reducing weight on average, 20% of body weight."

*Context: Ricks citing the headline efficacy figure from SURMOUNT trials — tirzepatide's 20% average body weight reduction compared to 5–7% for older GLP-1 monotherapy agents and 2–3% for traditional anti-obesity medications.*
