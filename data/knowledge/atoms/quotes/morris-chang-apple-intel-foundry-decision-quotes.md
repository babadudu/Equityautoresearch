## Morris Chang — TSMC's Foundry Philosophy and the Apple-Intel Decision

### Context

In early 2011, Intel's CEO approached Apple with an offer to manufacture chips for the iPhone. Tim Cook met with TSMC founder Morris Chang and subsequently rejected Intel's overture. Cook's blunt assessment: **"Intel just does not know how to be a foundry"** ([MacRumors, 2025-01-30](https://www.macrumors.com/2025/01/30/tsmc-reveals-why-apple-snubbed-intel-chips/)).

This episode — recounted publicly by Morris Chang — reveals both TSMC's competitive differentiation and the foundational principle behind Apple's supplier selection philosophy.

### Morris Chang's Core Quote on Customer Responsiveness

> "When the customer asks a lot of things, we have learned to respond to every request. Some of them were crazy, some of them were irrational, but we respond to each request courteously. Intel has never done that."

**— Morris Chang, TSMC Founder**, recalling TSMC's pitch to Apple ([MacRumors, 2025-01-30](https://www.macrumors.com/2025/01/30/tsmc-reveals-why-apple-snubbed-intel-chips/))

### Analysis and Investment Significance

The Apple–TSMC relationship illustrates a structural moat that compounds over time. Apple became TSMC's largest customer, with TSMC manufacturing every Apple Silicon chip (A-series, M-series, S-series) exclusively. This relationship predates the public announcement of the Apple Silicon transition (WWDC 2020) by nearly a decade.

Morris Chang's framing highlights three dimensions of TSMC's competitive advantage:

1. **Service culture**: TSMC treats every customer request — even irrational ones — with courtesy and follow-through. This is a learned organizational behavior, not a natural instinct, and one that Intel's IDM (Integrated Device Manufacturer) culture historically found difficult to adopt.

2. **Foundry-first identity**: Intel's core identity was as a product company that designed and sold its own chips. Becoming a foundry for competitors required a cultural inversion Intel had not made by 2011 (and struggled with even after launching Intel Foundry Services in 2021).

3. **Strategic alignment**: For Apple, choosing TSMC meant betting on the world's most advanced process node partner without the conflict-of-interest risk of an IDM that competes in adjacent markets.
