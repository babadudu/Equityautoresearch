## Jamie Iannone — De Minimis Elimination & Cross-Border Trade Policy Quotes

### Context
The U.S. elimination of the de minimis exemption (previously covering imports under $800) created a material headwind for eBay's cross-border business in 2025. eBay CEO Jamie Iannone addressed the impact extensively across multiple earnings calls, describing both the near-term volume deceleration and eBay's strategic response.

---

### Q3 2025 Earnings Call — Immediate Impact (October 29, 2025)

**On the de minimis cost impact:**
> "The recent elimination of the de minimis exemption for imports under $800 has created incremental cost."

— Jamie Iannone, eBay CEO, [eBay Q3 2025 Earnings Call, 2025-10-29](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/29/ebay-ebay-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)

**On volume deceleration in September 2025:**
> "We saw a deceleration in year-over-year volume growth starting in September in key markets importing into the U.S. after the removal of the de minimis exemption."

— Jamie Iannone, eBay CEO, [eBay Q3 2025 Earnings Call, 2025-10-29](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/29/ebay-ebay-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)

**On Q4 2025 headwind magnitude:**
> "A full quarter of impact from the removal of global de minimis exemption, versus a single month."

— Jamie Iannone, eBay CEO, [eBay Q3 2025 Earnings Call, 2025-10-29](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/29/ebay-ebay-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)

*Note: This quote signals that Q3 2025 reflected only one month of de minimis impact (September), while Q4 2025 would bear the full-quarter brunt — a sequential escalation that management flagged as a forward-looking risk.*

---

### Q4 2025 Earnings Call — Strategic Response (February 18, 2026)

**On accelerating the product roadmap in response to tariffs:**
> "We accelerated our product roadmap for cross-border solutions to help our sellers navigate new tariffs and trade policy changes."

— Jamie Iannone, eBay CEO, [eBay Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-18](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/18/ebay-ebay-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)

*Note: This quote is notable for framing eBay's response as a product acceleration rather than a retreat — signaling that Iannone views trade disruption as a solvable seller tooling problem, consistent with eBay's positioning as a seller-centric platform.*

### Investment Analysis Context

The de minimis elimination primarily affected Chinese cross-border sellers shipping directly to U.S. buyers. eBay's exposure is partially mitigated because approximately 75% of eBay's China-origin GMV is already "forward deployed" — stored in U.S. warehouses before sale — and therefore not subject to the de minimis change (data from VP John Egbert, Q4 2024 earnings call). The direct de minimis exposure represents roughly 5% of total eBay GMV from Chinese sellers × the fraction shipped direct from China (~25%), so structural GMV exposure is estimated at ~1-2% of total GMV.

The longer-term bull case: de minimis elimination "levels the playing field" by removing the landed-cost advantage previously enjoyed by Temu and Shein versus domestic eBay sellers — potentially structural tailwinds for eBay's U.S.-based recommerce inventory.
