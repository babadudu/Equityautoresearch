## Morris Chang on NVIDIA and the Jensen Huang CEO Succession Offer

Perhaps the most striking revelation in Morris Chang's autobiography Volume 2 (December 2024) is that he formally considered Jensen Huang as a potential successor for the TSMC CEO role. The TSMC–NVIDIA relationship, which today accounts for a significant share of TSMC's CoWoS advanced packaging demand, began with a single letter in 1997.

---

### On the Origins of the TSMC–NVIDIA Relationship (1997)

**Chang's principle:** "We should never be negligent in talking to future customers, even if the customer seems very small."

**Context:** In 1997, Jensen Huang sent a letter to TSMC's headquarters in Hsinchu, introducing NVIDIA as a promising small company and requesting a foundry relationship. Chang's policy was to treat all prospective customers with equal seriousness regardless of current revenue. TSMC accepted the engagement and — unusually — dispatched two production planning engineers to be stationed at NVIDIA's offices for one month to ensure the relationship started correctly.

**Source:** [TechSoda, Morris Chang autobiography analysis](https://techsoda.substack.com/p/tsmc-founder-morris-changs-autobiography)

**Investment significance:** This early investment in the NVIDIA relationship is now worth tens of billions in annual wafer revenue. NVIDIA is TSMC's second-largest customer (after Apple), consuming leading-edge N4/N3 logic capacity for GPUs and driving the entire CoWoS advanced packaging buildout. The relationship illustrates Chang's principle that foundry relationships are long-duration partnerships where early trust-building has asymmetric returns.

---

### On Considering Jensen Huang as TSMC CEO Successor

**Quote:** "Although I was quite familiar with the talent within the company, there was one person outside the company who, in terms of character, vision, professional experience, and knowledge in semiconductors — across all criteria — was worth considering as a successor for TSMC. I knew I should speak with him, and that person was Jensen Huang."

**Chang's account of the conversation with Jensen:**

**Quote:** "I spent 10 minutes explaining my deep aspirations of TSMC and how TSMC's package for CEO would be much higher than his income at that time, but he had no question and did not refuse me directly, but simply said, 'I already have a job'."

**Context:** Chang revealed in his autobiography that he conducted a formal external succession search before ultimately selecting C.C. Wei. The timeline is not precisely specified, but it appears to have occurred in the 2000s–2010s when Jensen Huang's profile was rising but NVIDIA had not yet achieved its current $3T+ valuation. Huang's polite but firm deflection — "I already have a job" — is characteristic of the directness attributed to him in other biographical accounts.

**Source:** [TechSoda, Morris Chang autobiography analysis](https://techsoda.substack.com/p/tsmc-founder-morris-changs-autobiography)

**Investment significance:** That Chang considered Jensen Huang the most qualified external candidate for TSMC's CEO role — rating him on "character, vision, professional experience, and knowledge in semiconductors" above all internal candidates — validates two things simultaneously: the depth of trust TSMC's founder placed in Huang personally, and the strategic alignment between the two companies that made their partnership so durable. Today TSMC's fate and NVIDIA's fate are structurally intertwined through AI infrastructure demand.
