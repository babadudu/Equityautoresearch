## Morris Chang on Integrity as a Non-Negotiable Organizational Principle

Morris Chang consistently identified integrity as the foundational value of TSMC — not a cultural aspiration but an enforceable standard with real consequences. The following quotes, drawn from a 2005 Global Views Monthly interview and related biographical materials, reveal both the philosophy and the enforcement mechanism.

---

### On the CEO's Role in Propagating Integrity

**Quote:** "TSMC's executives are refrained from being anxious for quick success… I try my best to trickle down core company value — Integrity — from the top down."

**Context:** Chang gave this statement in a 2005 interview with Global Views Monthly (遠見雜誌). He characterized the CEO's primary responsibility not as strategy or capital allocation, but as the downward propagation of core values — specifically integrity. His use of "trickle down" is deliberate: values flow from the top, and if the CEO does not model them personally, they cannot be sustained organizationally.

**Source:** [Global Views Monthly / Culc Hsin Hsin Blog, 2005](https://exampleof2015.wordpress.com/2016/03/03/integrity-we-should-carry-on-throughout-all-of-our-life/)

---

### On the Consequence for Integrity Violations

**Quote:** "If there is any executive violating this very principle, with a very heavy heart I must personally handle the situation and ask him/her to resign."

**Context:** When the interviewer pressed Chang on what would happen if a senior executive violated TSMC's integrity standard, he answered without equivocation. The phrase "very heavy heart" (很沈重的心情) acknowledges the personal cost of the action, while "must personally handle" makes clear that consequences cannot be delegated. This combination — personal acknowledgment of difficulty plus personal accountability for enforcement — is what gives the standard its organizational credibility.

**Source:** [Global Views Monthly, 2005](https://exampleof2015.wordpress.com/2016/03/03/integrity-we-should-carry-on-throughout-all-of-our-life/)

**Investment significance:** TSMC's pure-play foundry model is architecturally dependent on customer trust. Fabless companies — Apple, NVIDIA, AMD, Qualcomm — share their most sensitive IP (chip designs) with TSMC. Any integrity breach involving customer IP would be an existential event for the foundry model. Chang's willingness to terminate senior executives for integrity violations was not merely ethical governance; it was a direct defense of TSMC's business model. This cultural infrastructure is a genuine competitive moat that cannot be replicated quickly by entrants.

---

### C.C. Wei Continuing Chang's Integrity Standard

**Quote (C.C. Wei):** "No matter where TSMC conducts business in the world, it will abide by the highest standards of corporate governance, stick to the bottom line of integrity."

**Context:** Upon assuming the Chairman & CEO role in June 2024, Wei explicitly committed to extending Chang's integrity standard to TSMC's global operations — including its Arizona, Japan, and Germany fabs. This statement is directed simultaneously at internal employees and at global customers: it is a reaffirmation that the foundry trust model transfers to non-Taiwan geographies.

**Source:** [Ventron Chip, 2024](https://www.ventronchip.com/news/tsmc-completes-leadership-handover-c.-c.-wei-takes-over-as-chairman.html)

---

### Morris Chang's Own Evaluation of C.C. Wei's Preparation

**Quote (Morris Chang on C.C. Wei):** "He went through all three departments — so I call him the best-prepared CEO."

**Context:** Chang made this remark publicly, noting that in 2009 he had deliberately rotated Wei from technical roles to business development — a non-obvious assignment — so that Wei would have deep experience in TSMC's technology, manufacturing, and customer-facing functions before assuming the top leadership role. Chang's deliberate succession planning over 15+ years reflects the same long-horizon thinking he applied to R&D investment and customer relationships.

**Source:** [Wikipedia, CommonWealth Magazine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Che-Chia_Wei)
