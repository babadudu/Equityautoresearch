# Andy Jassy CEO Interviews & Key Quotes (2022–2026)

This atom captures Andy Jassy's most significant public statements since becoming Amazon CEO in July 2021, tracking the evolution of his leadership priorities from succession through the generative AI era.

---

## 2022 TIME 100 Most Influential Companies Interview — First Deep CEO Profile
**Source:** [Andy Jassy — TIME 100 Most Influential Companies Interview](https://time.com/collections/time100-companies-2022/6159473/amazon-ceo-andy-jassy-interview/) (March 2022)

**Key Quotes:**
> "Speed is not preordained. It's very much a leadership decision."

> "We exist to make customers' lives better."

**Context:** This interview, conducted approximately eight months into Jassy's tenure as CEO, was his first in-depth profile as Amazon's chief executive. Key themes:

1. **Speed as a cultural choice:** Jassy explicitly rejected the notion that large companies inevitably slow down. Speed, in his framing, is a decision — not a function of company size. This prefigures the bureaucracy-reduction campaign he would escalate in subsequent years (culminating in the 375 policy changes announced in the 2024 shareholder letter).

2. **Project Kuiper:** Jassy outlined Amazon's satellite broadband initiative as a long-term infrastructure bet, framing it as consistent with Amazon's pattern of investing in infrastructure that customers need before competitors force the issue.

3. **Zoox:** Amazon's autonomous vehicle subsidiary was discussed as a patient, decade-scale investment — consistent with the Bezos-era capital allocation philosophy.

4. **Customer mission:** The "we exist to make customers' lives better" framing is a deliberate restatement of Bezos's customer-obsession principle, signaling continuity with Amazon's founding culture under new leadership.

## 2024 Letter to Shareholders — Speed, GenAI, and Bureaucracy Elimination
**Source:** [Andy Jassy 2024 Letter to Shareholders](https://www.aboutamazon.com/news/company-news/amazon-ceo-andy-jassy-2024-letter-to-shareholders) (published April 2025)

**Key Quotes:**
> "Speed disproportionately matters."

> "GenAI is going to reinvent every experience."

**Themes:**
1. **375 bureaucracy reforms:** Jassy announced that Amazon had eliminated or restructured 375 internal policies, processes, and approval layers as part of a deliberate effort to restore startup velocity at enterprise scale. This is among the most concrete quantifications of "Day 1" culture maintenance.

2. **Lowest-priced retailer:** The letter stated Amazon had maintained its position as the lowest-priced retailer for eight consecutive years — a direct continuation of Bezos's proactive price reduction philosophy.

3. **WhyQ culture:** Jassy articulated "WhyQ" — a cultural practice of questioning the rationale for existing processes before accepting them as fixed constraints. This is a formalized version of the Bezos-era principle that proxies (metrics, processes) should never substitute for the underlying goal they were meant to measure.

4. **Generative AI reinvention:** The letter characterized GenAI as the broadest platform shift Amazon has encountered, affecting every business line — retail, AWS, advertising, logistics, devices. Jassy framed Amazon as having unique advantages: proprietary data, Trainium chips, Bedrock platform, and Anthropic partnership.

## 2026 CNBC Squawk Box — OpenAI Partnership Announcement
**Source:** [Andy Jassy on CNBC Squawk Box with Sam Altman](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/27/first-on-cnbc-cnbc-transcript-amazon-ceo-andy-jassy-and-openai-ceo-sam-altman-speak-with-cnbcs-andrew-ross-sorkin-on-squawk-box-today.html) (February 2026)

**Key Quotes:**
> "OpenAI wouldn't use Trainium if it wasn't compelling for them."

**Context:** This appearance — with OpenAI CEO Sam Altman on CNBC — announced a strategic partnership in which OpenAI would run workloads on AWS, including on Amazon's custom Trainium AI chips. The significance:

1. **Trainium validation:** OpenAI's willingness to use Trainium — rather than exclusively Nvidia GPUs — was framed by Jassy as third-party validation of the chip's price-performance ratio. Jassy cited a 30–40% cost-performance advantage versus comparable GPU alternatives.

2. **Multi-model strategy:** Jassy emphasized that the OpenAI partnership does not affect Amazon's relationship with Anthropic (in which Amazon has invested approximately $4 billion). Amazon's stated strategy is to provide infrastructure for multiple frontier AI labs simultaneously — a "picks and shovels" positioning.

3. **Competitive context:** At the time of this announcement, Microsoft was OpenAI's primary cloud partner. The AWS deal represented a significant expansion of OpenAI's infrastructure relationships and validated AWS's competitiveness in AI infrastructure.

## 2025 CNBC Mad Money Interview — AI Returns and Capital Allocation
**Source:** [Andy Jassy on CNBC Mad Money with Jim Cramer](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/06/30/cnbc-exclusive-transcript-amazon-ceo-andy-jassy-speaks-with-cnbcs-jim-cramer-on-mad-money-today.html) (June 2025)

**Key Themes:**
- AI investment return outlook and the timeline for ROI on massive capex commitments
- AWS growth momentum and demand signals for cloud and AI infrastructure
- Retail margin expansion trajectory as fulfillment network efficiency improvements compound
- Capital expenditure strategy: Jassy's framing of infrastructure investment as demand-driven rather than speculative

**Investment Relevance:** This interview addressed the central investor concern of 2025: Amazon was spending tens of billions annually on AI and cloud infrastructure — when would returns materialize? Jassy's answers provided the clearest public articulation of management's ROI thesis for the capex cycle.
