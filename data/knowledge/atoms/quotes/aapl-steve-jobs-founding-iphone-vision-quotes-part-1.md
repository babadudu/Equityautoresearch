# Steve Jobs — Founding Vision, iPhone Launch, and Resilience Quotes

This atom collects direct quotes from Apple co-founder Steve Jobs covering his philosophy on the iPhone's invention, his firing and return to Apple, and the product vision that shaped the company's trajectory.

---

## On Getting Fired from Apple as a Gift

> "I didn't see it then, but it turned out that getting fired from Apple was the best thing that could have ever happened to me."

— Steve Jobs, Stanford University Commencement Address, 2005-06-12 ([Stanford News](https://news.stanford.edu/stories/2005/06/youve-got-find-love-jobs-says))

**Context:** Jobs was ousted from Apple by the board in 1985 following a power struggle with CEO John Sculley. During his absence (1985–1997), he founded NeXT (whose operating system became the foundation for macOS) and acquired Pixar, turning it into the world's leading animation studio. Apple acquired NeXT for $429M in 1997, bringing Jobs back as interim CEO. He then orchestrated one of the most dramatic corporate turnarounds in history: killing 70% of Apple's product lines, launching the iMac (1998), iPod + iTunes (2001), and ultimately the iPhone (2007). Jobs's framing — that failure was a hidden gift — became a defining element of Silicon Valley mythology and Apple's cultural identity around accepting risk and reinvention.

## On the iPhone as Three Devices in One (Launch Announcement)

> "A revolutionary mobile phone, a widescreen iPod with touch controls, and a breakthrough Internet communications device."

— Steve Jobs, Macworld Conference & Expo keynote, 2007-01-09 ([Apple Newsroom](https://www.apple.com/newsroom/2007/01/09Apple-Reinvents-the-Phone-with-iPhone/))

**Context:** Jobs delivered this line three times in succession during the original iPhone introduction, building theatrical suspense before revealing they were all the same device. The framing was strategically precise: it anchored iPhone's value proposition against three existing product categories (smartphones, portable media players, internet communicators), letting audiences mentally place the product while simultaneously suggesting it transcended each category. iPhone sold 1.4 million units in its first year (2007) and 11.6 million in 2008 ([Textedly](https://textedly.com/blog//smartphone-history-when-were-smartphones-invented)), triggering the collapse of Nokia's and BlackBerry's dominant positions within five years. The global smartphone industry now generates approximately $587B in annual revenue and employs ~19 million people ([History Cooperative](https://historycooperative.org/the-history-of-the-iphone/)).

## Background: Apple's Founding

For full context on Jobs's vision: Apple Computer Company was founded on April 1, 1976 by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne in a garage in Los Altos, California. The Apple I — a hand-assembled circuit board sold for $666.66 — was followed by the Apple II (1977), which became one of the most successful mass-produced personal computers in history ([PBS: Apple Timeline](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/science/a-timeline-of-apples-most-influential-product-announcements)). The 1984 Macintosh, with its graphical user interface and mouse-driven input, fundamentally changed human-computer interaction. The Super Bowl ad directed by Ridley Scott remains considered a pinnacle of technology marketing ([Britannica: Apple Inc.](https://www.britannica.com/money/Apple-Inc)).

Jobs's firing in 1985 and return in 1997 represent the two most consequential inflection points in Apple's history. The 2005 Stanford commencement speech — in which the "getting fired" quote appears — is the canonical primary source for Jobs's own interpretation of that arc.
