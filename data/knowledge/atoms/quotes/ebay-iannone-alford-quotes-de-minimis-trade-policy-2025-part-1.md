## Jamie Iannone and Peggy Alford — Quotes on De Minimis Exemption Removal and Trade Policy Impact

On August 29, 2025, the United States fully eliminated the de minimis import exemption for goods valued under $800, replacing it with a flat $80–$200 per-item tariff during the transition period, with full ad valorem tariffs to follow. This policy change — the most significant structural shift in U.S. cross-border e-commerce taxation in decades — directly impacted eBay's cross-border GMV. CEO Jamie Iannone and CFO Peggy Alford addressed its impact across Q3 2025 and Q4 2025 earnings calls.

Sources: [CedCommerce, 2025](https://cedcommerce.com/blog/de-minimis-rule-ends-what-sellers-must-know-and-do-before-august-29/); [Fortune, 2025-08-29](https://fortune.com/2025/08/29/etsy-ebay-shein-de-minimis-tariff-exemption-ends-adding-charges/)

---

### Q3 2025 Earnings — Immediate Impact Assessment

**On the cost burden created:**

> "The recent elimination of the de minimis exemption for imports under $800 has created incremental cost."

— Jamie Iannone, Q3 2025 Earnings Call ([eBay Q3 2025 Earnings Call, 2025-10-29](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/29/ebay-ebay-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/))

**Context:** Iannone's initial characterization was measured — "incremental cost" rather than structural disruption. The de minimis change particularly affected cross-border sellers in Japan and Canada who listed goods for U.S. buyers, as their per-item economics were immediately repriced.

---

**On the volume deceleration signal:**

> "We saw a deceleration in year-over-year volume growth starting in September in key markets importing into the U.S. after the removal of the de minimis exemption."

— Jamie Iannone, Q3 2025 Earnings Call ([eBay Q3 2025 Earnings Call, 2025-10-29](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/29/ebay-ebay-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/))

**Context:** The exemption was eliminated on August 29; deceleration began in September — a one-month lag consistent with order pipelines clearing. eBay's cross-border GMV, which includes significant Japan-to-U.S. and Canada-to-U.S. flows (trading cards, vintage goods, automotive parts), saw the most direct impact.

---

**On the forward Q4 headwind warning:**

> "A full quarter of impact from the removal of global de minimis exemption, versus a single month."

— Jamie Iannone, Q3 2025 Earnings Call ([eBay Q3 2025 Earnings Call, 2025-10-29](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/29/ebay-ebay-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/))

**Context:** Iannone explicitly pre-announced the Q4 headwind as a year-over-year comparison problem: Q3 2025 caught only one month of full impact (September), while Q4 2025 would carry a full three-month impact. This framing managed expectations for Q4 international GMV.

---

**CFO on annualization headwinds:**

> "We expect to face incremental headwinds from annualizing breakage associated with the global de minimis changes."

— Peggy Alford, Q3 2025 Earnings Call ([eBay Q3 2025 Earnings Call, 2025-10-29](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/29/ebay-ebay-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/))

**Context:** CFO Peggy Alford used "breakage" — a term typically applied to gift card non-redemption — to describe the structural volume loss caused by cross-border sellers exiting the platform as their economics deteriorated. "Annualizing breakage" signals that the year-over-year comparison would remain challenging through at least mid-2026, when the policy change fully anniversaries.

---

### Q4 2025 Earnings — Response and Residual Impact

**On eBay's product roadmap acceleration:**

> "We accelerated our product roadmap for cross-border solutions to help our sellers navigate new tariffs and trade policy changes."

— Jamie Iannone, Q4 2025 Earnings Call ([eBay Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-18](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/18/ebay-ebay-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/))

**Context:** By Q4 2025, eBay had pivoted from impact assessment to product response — building tariff calculation tools, duty estimation at checkout, and cross-border seller guidance into the platform. This response mirrors eBay's historical pattern of building infrastructure around regulatory change rather than lobbying against it.

---

**CFO on international GMV pressures:**

> "Challenging macroeconomic conditions in the UK and Germany and a deceleration in our cross-border volume growth due to U.S. trade policies."

— Peggy Alford, Q4 2025 Earnings Call ([eBay Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-18](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/18/ebay-ebay-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/))

**Context:** Alford identified two distinct vectors compressing international GMV in Q4 2025: (1) European macro weakness (UK and Germany consumer confidence at multi-year lows) affecting domestic-to-domestic transactions; and (2) U.S. trade policy (de minimis elimination) suppressing cross-border flows into the U.S. The combined effect pushed international GMV to approximately -1% YoY on an organic FX-neutral basis in Q4, contrasting sharply with domestic U.S. GMV growth of ~19%.

### Structural Note: Competitive Asymmetry

Third-party analysis noted that the de minimis elimination created asymmetric competitive impact. Per [FinancialContent analysis (2026-02-20)](https://markets.financialcontent.com/stocks/article/finterra-2026-2-20-resilience-in-re-commerce-an-in-depth-analysis-of-ebay-inc-ebay), the policy change "inadvertently benefited eBay by leveling the playing field against ultra-low-cost importers" — specifically Temu and SHEIN, whose business models were built around the de minimis exemption for new goods shipped directly from China. eBay's 90% non-new inventory profile means the majority of its catalog is sourced domestically or from established international sellers, not de minimis-dependent drop-shippers.

---

### Summary

The de minimis elimination created a temporary cross-border volume headwind for eBay concentrated in Q3–Q4 2025 and expected to persist through the first half of 2026 as it anniversaries. eBay's response was product-led (cross-border tariff tools) rather than reliant on policy reversal. The structural asymmetry — eBay's 90% non-new inventory versus competitors' new-goods import dependency — means the long-run competitive effect may be net positive for eBay's market position in recommerce, even as the short-run volume impact was negative.
