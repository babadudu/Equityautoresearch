## Wendell Huang — Gross Margin Floor and CapEx-Growth Philosophy

Wendell Huang (黃仁昭) serves as TSMC's Chief Financial Officer. The following quotes from 2025 earnings calls articulate TSMC's long-term financial framework: the gross margin floor and the relationship between capital expenditure and revenue growth.

---

### On the Long-Term Gross Margin Floor

**Context:** Q2 2025 earnings call. An analyst asked whether currency headwinds (TWD appreciation against USD) would compress TSMC's gross margin below its historical guidance range. Huang addressed the long-term structural margin outlook.

> "Thus, even with the unfavorable foreign exchange rate, we believe a long-term gross margin of 53% and higher remains well achievable."

**Source:** [TSMC Q2 2025 Earnings Call Transcript](https://investor.tsmc.com/chinese/encrypt/files/encrypt_file/reports/2025-07/1f4f86c935f1de837672a6973154e64b26bdae57/TSMC%202Q25%20Transcript.pdf)

**Significance:** The 53% figure is a floor, not a target. As of 2025, TSMC's actual gross margin ran at 59.9% — implying that even in an adverse macro scenario (currency headwinds, cost inflation from overseas fabs), the business retains substantial margin buffer. The statement also signals pricing power: TSMC believes advanced node pricing can absorb FX volatility without structural margin compression.

**Analytical context:** TSMC's gross margin trajectory 2021→2025: 51.6% → 59.9%, driven by a mix shift to advanced nodes (N3/N2) which carry structurally higher ASPs. Operating margin followed: 41.0% → 50.8%, demonstrating operating leverage as revenue growth outpaced expense growth.

---

### On CapEx and Growth Correlation

**Context:** Q3 2025 earnings call (October 2025). An analyst raised concerns about TSMC's 2026 CapEx guidance of $52–56B — a significant step-up from prior years — and asked whether this level of spending was sustainable given return expectations.

> "A higher level of CapEx is always correlated with higher growth opportunities. If we do our job right, the growth of our business should outpace the growth of the CapEx."

**Source:** [TSMC Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2025/10/17/tsmc-tsm-q3-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)

**Significance:** Huang is articulating a framework where CapEx intensity is a leading indicator of revenue, not a drag on returns. The implicit claim is that TSMC's ROIC — which recovered from a 2023 trough of 25.2% to 41.5% in 2025 — can be maintained even at $52–56B CapEx, provided the AI demand cycle sustains advanced node utilization. The phrase "if we do our job right" introduces operational accountability: the commitment is conditional on execution, not guaranteed by macro tailwinds alone.

**Analytical context:** TSMC's ROIC trajectory 2023→2025: 25.2% → 41.5%. The 2023 trough coincided with the post-COVID inventory correction. The rapid recovery to 41.5% in 2025 reflects AI-driven N3/N2 demand absorbing capacity that had been underutilized during the downturn. Huang's forward guidance implies ROIC in the 30%+ range even at elevated CapEx — a claim that assumes AI infrastructure spending by CSPs remains durable through 2026–2027.
