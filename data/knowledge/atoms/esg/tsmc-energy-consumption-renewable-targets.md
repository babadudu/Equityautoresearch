## TSMC Energy Consumption, Renewable Energy Targets & Taiwan Grid Dependency

TSMC is Taiwan's largest single electricity consumer and one of the largest in the global semiconductor industry.

### Absolute Consumption & Taiwan Grid Share

According to Statista (2024), TSMC's total electricity consumption in 2023 was approximately **24.78 TWh** (24,780 GWh), representing roughly **8.9% of Taiwan's total national electricity consumption**. ([Source: Statista — TSMC Energy Consumption 2023](https://www.statista.com/statistics/1312965/tsmc-energy-consumption-by-source/))

According to New Lines Institute (2025), TSMC accounts for **41.3% of Taiwan's industrial electricity consumption** (based on the industrial sector total of 63,242 GWh). ([Source: New Lines Institute](https://newlinesinstitute.org/geo-economics/taiwans-semiconductor-sustainability-and-global-implications/))

According to Global Taiwan Institute (2026), TSMC accounts for approximately **10% of Taiwan's total energy consumption**, while the semiconductor industry as a whole accounts for over half of industrial electricity, with advanced-process manufacturing consuming nearly half of the industry's energy supply. ([Source: Global Taiwan Institute, 2026-02](https://globaltaiwan.org/2026/02/taiwans-bumpy-road-to-energy-resilience/))

According to Greenpeace East Asia (2023), TSMC's electricity consumption is projected to reach **267% of 2021 levels by 2030** — the largest growth rate among all semiconductor manufacturers studied. By then, TSMC's electricity demand would be equivalent to the consumption of **5.8 million people** (approximately one-quarter of Taiwan's population). Greenpeace criticized that TSMC's 2021 renewable energy share of only 9% was "far below the renewable energy usage rate of its largest competitors," and called on TSMC to advance its RE100 target from 2040 to 2030 to match the climate commitments of major customers Apple and Microsoft. ([Source: Greenpeace East Asia, 2023-08](https://www.greenpeace.org/eastasia/press/7930/semiconductor-industry-electricity-consumption-to-more-than-double-by-2030-study/))

According to CommonWealth Magazine (2024), TSMC's projected 2030 electricity consumption is equivalent to the output of "three nuclear reactor units" — a framing that highlights Taiwan's energy supply pressure under its nuclear phase-out policy. ([Source: CommonWealth Magazine, 2024-08-28](https://english.cw.com.tw/article/article.action?id=3766))

### Renewable Energy Procurement Structure

TSMC's contracted renewable energy capacity reached **4.4 GW** in 2024, sourced primarily through:
1. Corporate Power Purchase Agreements (CPPAs) with Taiwan solar/offshore wind developers via long-term contracts
2. International Renewable Energy Certificates (I-RECs) procurement
3. A pioneering **20-year joint procurement agreement** — locking in stable pricing for 20,000 GWh of renewables for suppliers and TSMC subsidiaries

Global offices and overseas facilities have achieved **100% renewable energy**, but Taiwan fab sites remain constrained by the scale of their production capacity. ([Source: TSMC 2024 Sustainability Report](https://esg.tsmc.com/en-US/file/public/2024-TSMC-Sustainability-Report-e.pdf); [SemiWiki](https://semiwiki.com/semiconductor-manufacturers/tsmc/361249-tsmcs-tsmc-2024-sustainability-report-pioneering-a-greener-semiconductor-future/))

### Structural Grid Challenges

Taiwan's electricity generation is concentrated in the south while major demand is in the north, creating significant transmission efficiency losses. Taiwan's energy import dependence stands at **97%**, and Taiwan Power Company accumulated losses exceeding NT$350 billion ($11.08 billion USD) between 2022–2024 due to government price subsidies. As TSMC scales advanced processes (N2/A16) with higher per-wafer energy requirements, Taiwan's grid capacity faces increasing strain at an annual demand growth rate of approximately 1.7%. ([Source: Global Taiwan Institute, 2026-02](https://globaltaiwan.org/2026/02/taiwans-bumpy-road-to-energy-resilience/))

### Taiwan 2024 Energy Mix

Taiwan's 2024 energy structure remains heavily fossil-fuel dependent: petroleum 35.87%, coal 31.75%, natural gas 23.73%, solar 2.91%, nuclear 2.38%, wind and hydro combined ~3%. Nuclear's share is declining as Taiwan's last nuclear plant retires in 2025. ([Source: Global Taiwan Institute, 2026](https://globaltaiwan.org/2026/02/taiwans-bumpy-road-to-energy-resilience/))

This high-carbon energy structure means that even with large-scale renewable procurement, TSMC's Scope 2 (electricity indirect emissions) decarbonization effectiveness depends on the pace of Taiwan's overall grid greening. Taiwan's national 2030 renewable target is 20%, while TSMC's own target is 60% — the gap must be bridged through large-scale CPPA and I-RECs procurement, with both cost and supply feasibility remaining uncertain.
