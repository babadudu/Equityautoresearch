### Analyst Tension: High Ratings vs. Fossil Grid Dependency

TSMC's ESG rating performance is exceptional by industry standards — MSCI AAA is among the highest ratings in semiconductors, and 24 consecutive years on DJSI is an industry record. However, according to Greenpeace East Asia (2023), TSMC still lags behind major competitors and customers on renewable energy penetration, and has "largely escaped scrutiny in the climate debate despite their reliance on coal and other fossil fuels." This criticism highlights that ESG ratings may insufficiently reflect TSMC's actual dependence on Taiwan's high-carbon grid. ([Source: Greenpeace East Asia, 2023-08](https://www.greenpeace.org/eastasia/press/7930/semiconductor-industry-electricity-consumption-to-more-than-double-by-2030-study/))

### Global Manufacturing Footprint

TSMC operates **12 300mm wafer fabs** and **4 200mm fabs** in Taiwan, primarily in Hsinchu, Taichung, and Tainan Science Parks. Including fabs under construction in Arizona (USA), Kumamoto (Japan), and Dresden (Germany), TSMC currently has **24 semiconductor fabs** in operation or under construction globally. ([Source: TweakTown, 2024](https://www.tweaktown.com/news/104783/tsmc-has-24-semiconductor-fabs-under-construction-worldwide-83-825-employees-in-total/index.html)) The Arizona campus covers approximately **1,100 acres (~445 hectares)** and plans for 6 wafer fabs, 2 advanced packaging plants, and 1 R&D center. ([Source: CFR, 2025-03](https://www.cfr.org/articles/unpacking-tsmcs-100-billion-investment-united-states))
