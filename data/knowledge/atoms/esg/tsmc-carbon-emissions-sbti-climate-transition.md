## TSMC Carbon Emissions, SBTi Commitment & Climate Transition Roadmap

### Greenhouse Gas Emissions Overview

According to New Lines Institute (2025), TSMC's total carbon emissions in 2023 were approximately **11.42 million tonnes CO₂e** (Scope 1+2), equivalent to the annual emissions of four Taichung thermal power plant units. ([Source: New Lines Institute](https://newlinesinstitute.org/geo-economics/taiwans-semiconductor-sustainability-and-global-implications/))

### SBTi Commitment & Carbon Milestones

TSMC formally committed to the Science Based Targets initiative (SBTi) Corporate Net-Zero Standard in 2024, using **2025 as the baseline year** and setting absolute reduction targets for Scope 1/2/3 to be achieved within the following decade. ([Source: TSMC PR — SBTi, 2024](https://pr.tsmc.com/english/news/3227))

| Milestone | Target Year | 2024 Status | Source |
|-----------|------------|-------------|--------|
| **Peak carbon emissions** | 2025 | In progress — 2025 designated as SBTi baseline year | [TSMC ESG](https://esg.tsmc.com/en-US/file/public/e-APractitionerofGreenPower_1.pdf); [TSMC PR — SBTi](https://pr.tsmc.com/english/news/3227) |
| **Carbon down to 2020 levels** | 2030 | Planned — requires simultaneous RE60 achievement | [TSMC PR — SBTi](https://pr.tsmc.com/english/news/3227) |

### Scope 3 Supply Chain Decarbonization Innovations

TSMC launched two pioneering measures in 2024:
1. **Carbon reduction equipment upgrade subsidies** for Tier-1 raw material suppliers in Taiwan — projected to reduce 450,000 tonnes CO₂
2. **GREEN Agreement** — covering 90% of raw material emission sources, requiring suppliers to set and execute carbon reduction plans

TSMC also pioneered a **20-year renewable energy joint procurement agreement** (20,000 GWh), locking in stable green power pricing for suppliers and TSMC subsidiaries, lowering the barrier for smaller suppliers to procure renewables. ([Source: TSMC PR — SBTi](https://pr.tsmc.com/english/news/3227); [SemiWiki](https://semiwiki.com/semiconductor-manufacturers/tsmc/361249-tsmcs-tsmc-2024-sustainability-report-pioneering-a-greener-semiconductor-future/))

**Against TSMC's 2025 estimated net income of NT$1.72 trillion, total climate transition costs are estimated at no more than 1–2% of net income — financially manageable at current levels.**

### "Net Positive" Energy Benefit Narrative

TSMC's core ESG investor narrative is that the energy efficiency gains from its chips globally far exceed its own energy consumption. According to the TSMC 2024 Sustainability Report, TSMC's efficient chips saved **104.2 billion kWh globally** in 2024, equivalent to reducing **44 million tonnes CO₂** — compared to TSMC's own 2023 Scope 1+2 emissions of approximately 11.42 million tonnes CO₂e. By 2030, for every 1 kWh consumed in manufacturing, TSMC projects **6.39 kWh saved globally** — a "net positive" multiplier of 6.39×. ([Source: TSMC 2024 Sustainability Report](https://esg.tsmc.com/en-US/file/public/2024-TSMC-Sustainability-Report-e.pdf); [SemiWiki](https://semiwiki.com/semiconductor-manufacturers/tsmc/361249-tsmcs-tsmc-2024-sustainability-report-pioneering-a-greener-semiconductor-future/))

However, Greenpeace has reservations about this narrative, arguing that TSMC should not use downstream product energy savings to offset its actual emission responsibilities on Taiwan's high-carbon grid — particularly when TSMC's renewable energy share (13–14%) remains far below its RE100 target and customer expectations. ([Source: Greenpeace East Asia, 2023](https://www.greenpeace.org/eastasia/press/7930/semiconductor-industry-electricity-consumption-to-more-than-double-by-2030-study/))

### Physical Climate Risks to Taiwan Fabs

**Typhoon Risk**: Taiwan averages 3–4 typhoons per year. TSMC's fabs are located on Taiwan's western coastal plain. Typhoon Gaemi in July 2024 caused severe flooding in central and southern Taiwan, but TSMC reported no significant production impact — reflecting effective fab disaster-resistant design. However, climate change is projected to intensify typhoon strength and increase extreme rainfall frequency.

**Earthquake Risk**: Taiwan sits on the Pacific Ring of Fire. The April 2024 Hualien M7.2 earthquake caused brief power outages at some TSMC facilities, but the company restored normal operations within hours — TSMC fabs are built to the highest seismic standards with automatic seismic isolation systems for critical equipment. The 1999 Chi-Chi earthquake (M7.3) historically caused multi-day power outages at Hsinchu Science Park, after which TSMC substantially upgraded power backup and structural seismic capabilities.

**Taiwan Energy Supply Vulnerability**: Taiwan imports **97%** of its energy and could sustain only approximately two weeks of energy supply under a blockade scenario. According to Global Taiwan Institute (2026), due to slow LNG terminal expansion, limited solar/wind land area, and nuclear phase-out, structural electricity supply shortage risk will continue to intensify before 2030 — a systemic risk not only for TSMC but for all its Taiwan fabs (~80% of capacity). ([Source: Global Taiwan Institute, 2026-02](https://globaltaiwan.org/2026/02/taiwans-bumpy-road-to-energy-resilience/))
