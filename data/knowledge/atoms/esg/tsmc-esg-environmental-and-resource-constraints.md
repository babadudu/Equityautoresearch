TSMC's environmental footprint is a critical operational constraint and a focal point for global ESG investors. As of 2026, the company maintains an MSCI AAA rating but faces structural challenges due to Taiwan's limited natural resources.

### Energy: The 9% Power Consumption Hurdle
TSMC is Taiwan's largest single electricity user. In 2023, it consumed 24.78 TWh, accounting for 8.9% of Taiwan's total power and over 41% of industrial power (Source: https://www.statista.com/statistics/1312965/tsmc-energy-consumption-by-source/). By 2030, this is projected to reach 24% of national electricity as advanced nodes (N2/A16) require 20-30% more power per wafer than N5. 
- **RE100 Commitment**: TSMC aims for 100% renewable energy by 2040. In 2024, its RE percentage was only 13-14%, constrained by Taiwan’s fossil-fuel-heavy grid (91% coal/gas). 
- **CPPA Dominance**: TSMC has signed the world’s largest renewable CPPA with Ørsted (920 MW from offshore wind) and buys ~99% of Taiwan's issued Renewable Energy Certificates (T-RECs).

### Water: 150,000 Tons Per Day
Chip manufacturing requires ultra-pure water (UPW) for rinsing wafers. TSMC’s daily water use in Taiwan has surged to ~150,000 tons, equivalent to the daily use of a city of 435,000 people. During the 2021 drought, the worst in 56 years, TSMC had to deploy water trucks from southern Taiwan to maintain production (Source: https://fortune.com/2021/06/12/chip-shortage-taiwan-drought-tsmc-water-usage/). S&P Global now considers water risk a direct credit rating factor for TSMC. The company aims for a 95% water recycling rate by 2030 and achieved "water positive" status at its Kumamoto plant by recharging groundwater through rice paddies.

### Carbon and SBTi
TSMC’s 2024 GHG emissions totaled 22.7 million tCO2e (Scope 1+2+3), a 10% YoY increase. The company has committed to the SBTi corporate net-zero standard, aiming for a "peak" in emissions by 2025. However, the N2/A16 ramp-up is expected to add 60-110 million tons of annual Scope 2 emissions by 2027, potentially pushing the peak further out unless renewable procurement accelerates significantly. TSMC counters this by claiming its high-efficiency chips save 6.39x more energy globally than is used in their production—a "net positive" claim that Greenpeace and other NGOs contest as being based on unverified life-cycle assumptions (Source: https://www.greenpeace.org/eastasia/press/7930/semiconductor-industry-electricity-consumption-to-more-than-double-by-2030-study/).
