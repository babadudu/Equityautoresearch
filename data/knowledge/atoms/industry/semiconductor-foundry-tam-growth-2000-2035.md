## Semiconductor Foundry TAM & Industry Growth Trends (2000–2035)

### Origins: The Pure-Play Foundry Model

The modern semiconductor foundry industry traces its origins to 1987, when Morris Chang (張忠謀) founded TSMC and pioneered the "pure-play foundry" business model — separating chip design from manufacturing and fundamentally restructuring the semiconductor industry's division of labor. Prior to this, most semiconductor companies operated as IDMs (Integrated Device Manufacturers), handling both design and fabrication in-house. TSMC's emergence catalyzed the fabless design company ecosystem (Qualcomm, NVIDIA, MediaTek, and others), transforming the industry from vertical integration toward horizontal specialization.

Morris Chang recalled his founding vision: "I wanted to establish a company that would be the foundry for the world's semiconductor industry. At that time, there was no such company." ([Source: Morris Chang, TSMC Founding Story](https://pr.tsmc.com/english/news/3210))

In retrospect, Chang has assessed the model's impact: "The foundry model has proven to be one of the most successful business model innovations in the history of the semiconductor industry." ([Source: TSMC PR](https://pr.tsmc.com/english/news/3210))

### Market Size Evolution: From Niche to Strategic Industry

The semiconductor foundry market barely existed when TSMC was founded in 1987 — global semiconductor revenue at the time was approximately $26 billion ([Source: SIA](https://www.semiconductors.org/)), with foundry representing only a negligible fraction. As fabless design companies proliferated through the 1990s, the foundry market expanded rapidly.

- **2025–2035 CAGR**: approximately 12.2% ([GM Insights](https://www.gminsights.com/industry-analysis/semiconductor-foundry-market))
- **2025–2034 CAGR**: approximately 5.8–8% depending on methodology ([Precedence Research](https://www.precedenceresearch.com/semiconductor-foundry-market); [Fortune Business Insights](https://www.fortunebusinessinsights.com/semiconductor-foundry-market-110068))
- **Advanced node monopoly**: TSMC holds >90% market share in sub-7nm process nodes ([Source: TSMC 2024 Annual Report](https://investor.tsmc.com/sites/ir/annual-report/2024/2024%20Annual%20Report_E.pdf)) — concentration is most extreme at the leading edge

### AI as the Structural Growth Driver

Artificial intelligence / HPC has supplanted smartphones as TSMC's largest revenue platform. In 2024, HPC surpassed 50% of TSMC's revenue mix; by Q2 2025, HPC reached 60% of revenue — a structural demand shift rather than a cyclical bump.

CEO C.C. Wei stated at the Q3 2025 earnings call: "AI demand has continued to be very strong and stronger than expected three months prior." ([Source: TSMC Q3 2025 Transcript](https://investor.tsmc.com/english/encrypt/files/encrypt_file/reports/2025-10/6860312f04fd291d0f26b46c1234f84e6332717e/TSMC%203Q25%20Transcript.pdf))

At the Q2 2024 earnings call, Wei elaborated on the convergence of ASIC and GPU AI workloads at TSMC: "Whether AI applications use ASICs or graphics processors, they all require very leading-edge technology and are working with TSMC." ([Source: TSMC Q2 2024 Transcript](https://investor.tsmc.com/english/encrypt/files/encrypt_file/reports/2024-08/5122725a56670882d777a8e8bfe0ed247cc55330/TSMC%202Q24%20Transcript.pdf))

In a July 2025 Fortune Global 500 profile, Wei described TSMC's elevated strategic position: "The AI boom has sent TSMC's importance to an entirely new level." ([Source: Fortune Asia, 2025-07-30](https://fortune.com/asia/2025/07/30/tsmc-ai-boom-cc-wei-wendell-huang-global-500/))

### Geographic Concentration

Asia-Pacific dominates global foundry capacity. In 2024, the region accounted for approximately 68% of global foundry market share ([Source: Custom Market Insights](https://www.custommarketinsights.com/report/semiconductor-foundry-market/)). This concentration is both a structural advantage (deep talent pools, supplier ecosystems, government support) and a geopolitical risk factor (Taiwan Strait exposure), with TSMC's Arizona and Japan fabs representing early-stage geographic diversification.

### Key Takeaways for Investors

1. **TAM compounding**: The foundry market has grown at ~10% CAGR over 25 years, with acceleration projected (12.2% CAGR through 2035) driven by AI.
2. **TSMC share gain within growth market**: TSMC's share rising from ~50% (2000) to ~64% (2025) within an expanding TAM is a rare combination — market growth plus share capture.
3. **Advanced node moat is near-absolute**: >90% sub-7nm share means TSMC is effectively the only commercial source for the most critical chips in the AI stack.
4. **Demand visibility**: AI infrastructure buildout (data centers, inference at edge) provides multi-year demand visibility that smartphone cycles never did — less seasonal, more strategic.
5. **Long-cycle secular trend**: Automotive semiconductors, IoT, and energy infrastructure are secondary TAM contributors that provide floor demand even if AI spending normalizes.
