## Cloud Computing TAM & AI-Driven Growth

### Historical Context

Cloud computing commercialization began in 2006 when Amazon Web Services launched S3 and EC2. Google followed in 2008 with App Engine, and Microsoft entered in 2010 with Azure. Over 15+ years, the market has matured from IaaS commodity storage to a full stack of managed services, data analytics, and AI/ML infrastructure.

### Market Size & Growth Projections

According to MarketsandMarkets (2025), the global cloud computing market is valued at **$1,294.9 billion in 2025** and is projected to reach **$2,281.1 billion by 2030**, at a CAGR of **12.0%** ([MarketsandMarkets](https://www.marketsandmarkets.com/Market-Reports/cloud-computing-market-234.html)).

Goldman Sachs Research offers a more bullish outlook driven by AI deployment: cloud TAM could reach **~$2 trillion by 2030** at a CAGR of approximately **22%** ([Goldman Sachs, 2025](https://www.goldmansachs.com/insights/articles/cloud-revenues-poised-to-reach-2-trillion-by-2030-amid-ai-rollout)).

### AI as a Structural Growth Accelerator

The divergence between MarketsandMarkets (12% CAGR) and Goldman Sachs (~22% CAGR) reflects the degree to which AI workload migration accelerates enterprise cloud adoption. Key factors:

- **Model training and inference** require GPU-dense cloud infrastructure at scale
- **Generative AI application deployment** is pulling enterprises from on-premise to cloud-native architectures
- **Data gravity**: as enterprises centralize data lakes in the cloud for AI training, adjacent workloads follow
- **Sovereign cloud** initiatives in regulated industries (finance, healthcare, government) represent incremental TAM

### Google Cloud's Position

Google Cloud Platform (GCP) is the third-largest cloud provider by market share, behind AWS and Azure. Alphabet reports Google Cloud as a separate segment; it achieved operating profitability in 2023 after years of investment losses. The cloud segment benefits from Google's leadership in AI research (DeepMind integration, Gemini model family) and infrastructure efficiency (TPU custom silicon).
