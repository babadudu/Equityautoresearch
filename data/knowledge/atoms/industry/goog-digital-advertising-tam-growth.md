## Digital Advertising TAM & Growth Drivers (Alphabet / GOOG)

### Historical Context

Modern digital advertising traces its origins to 1994 with the first web banner ad (AT&T on HotWired.com), but the true inflection point came in 2000 when Google launched AdWords — replacing display advertising with keyword-based bidding that monetized user **intent** as a business model. Over the subsequent two decades, digital advertising underwent three paradigm shifts:

1. **Search advertising** (2000s)
2. **Social advertising** (2010s)
3. **Short-form video / AI advertising** (2020s)

### Market Size & Growth Projections

According to Research and Markets (2025), the global digital advertising market is projected at **$763.6 billion in 2025**, growing to **$1,449.0 billion by 2030**, representing a CAGR of approximately **13.7%** ([source](https://marketingreport.one/news/global-digital-advertising-market-to-reach-$764-billion-by-2025-research.html)).

The US market stood at **$101.5 billion in 2024** and is projected to reach **$220.0 billion by 2030** (CAGR 13.6%) ([Grand View Research](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/horizon/outlook/digital-advertising-market/united-states)).

### AI's Expanding Role in Search Advertising

CEO Sundar Pichai expressed strong conviction about the durability of search advertising in a May 2025 All-In Podcast interview: "Search is very, very good at what it does empirically. People value it for what it does." He further emphasized that AI is **expanding** rather than replacing search: "The nature of queries has expanded. So there are whole new use cases coming into search." ([All-In Podcast, May 2025](https://singjupost.com/transcript-of-sundar-pichais-interview-on-the-all-in-podcast/))

At the Bloomberg Tech Summit (June 2025), Pichai framed the forward opportunity as larger than the past: "The opportunity ahead is bigger than the opportunity we had in the past." He also noted deepening user engagement: "People come back, people are curious, people are expanding their use cases and people do seek out sources." On AI's monetization impact, Pichai reported higher per-session engagement: "People are spending more time on average per click." ([Bloomberg Tech Summit, June 2025](https://singjupost.com/transcript-of-sundar-pichai-on-future-of-ai-antitrust-and-privacy/))

### Structural Tailwinds

Key structural drivers sustaining digital advertising growth:
- Continued shift of ad budgets from linear TV and print to digital channels
- AI-powered ad targeting and creative generation improving ROI for advertisers
- Expansion of query volume as AI assistants surface new search use cases
- Growth in connected TV and YouTube as premium video inventory
- Emerging market internet penetration still in early innings
