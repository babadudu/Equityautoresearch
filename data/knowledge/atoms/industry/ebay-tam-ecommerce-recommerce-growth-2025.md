## E-Commerce Origins & Evolution

Electronic commerce traces its roots to 1979, when British inventor Michael Aldrich created "teleshopping" — connecting televisions to transaction-processing computers via telephone lines. The modern era began in the mid-1990s: on August 11, 1994, Phil Brandenberger completed what is widely recognized as the first secure online retail transaction, purchasing a Sting CD on NetMarket via credit card. That same year, Jeff Bezos founded Amazon as an online bookstore. Pierre Omidyar launched AuctionWeb (later renamed eBay) on Labor Day 1995 as a side project while holding a full-time job — he did not anticipate explosive growth, yet the platform attracted massive user engagement even before charging fees. By mid-1997, eBay had 150,000 users, 794,000 items in auction daily, and average users spending 3.5 hours per month on the site ([Entrepreneur](https://www.entrepreneur.com/growing-a-business/pierre-omidyar/197554)).

The dot-com boom of the late 1990s drove the first e-commerce wave. After the 2000 bubble burst, survivors Amazon and eBay re-accelerated in the mid-2000s as broadband proliferated. The 2007 iPhone launch catalyzed the mobile commerce revolution — eBay was an early mover in mobile apps, with mobile now representing 60–64% of transactions. The COVID-19 pandemic (2020) was the most recent inflection point: global e-commerce penetration jumped from ~16% to ~21%, with some normalization since but irreversible behavioral shifts in online purchasing.

## Global E-Commerce Market Size (2025–2026)

The global e-commerce market reached **$6.86 trillion** in 2025, growing 8.4% year-over-year ([SellersCommerce, 2025](https://www.sellerscommerce.com/blog/ecommerce-statistics/)). For 2026, the market is projected to reach **$6.88 trillion** (+7.2% YoY), representing **21.1% of global retail sales** ([Shopify Global Ecommerce Sales Report, 2026](https://www.shopify.com/blog/global-ecommerce-sales)). China, the United States, and Western Europe are the three largest regions, with combined sales exceeding **$5.17 trillion** ([Shopify, 2026](https://www.shopify.com/enterprise/blog/global-ecommerce-statistics)).

The **marketplace model** (C2C/B2C intermediary platforms) accounts for approximately **67% of global e-commerce GMV** — making it the dominant transaction format globally.

eBay's estimated share of global e-commerce GMV is approximately **~1.2%** (derived: $79.6B FY2025 GMV / $6.86T global market).

## Recommerce & Secondhand: The High-Growth Sub-Market

The segment most relevant to eBay's strategic positioning is the C2C/B2C used and collectibles market — a structurally high-growth sub-market driven by generational preference shifts, sustainability consciousness, and digital platform maturation.

### Global Secondhand Apparel
- **2025 market size**: $198.6 billion
- **2031 projected size**: **$486.0 billion** (CAGR **16.1%**)
- **Key drivers**: Gen Z consumer preferences, rise of digital resale platforms, trade-in programs, demand for sustainable and affordable fashion
- Source: [GlobeNewsWire Secondhand Apparel Market Report, 2026-01-23](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/01/23/3224864/28124/en/Secondhand-Apparel-Market-Analysis-Report-2026-A-485-Billion-Market-by-2031-Driven-by-Gen-Z-Rise-of-Digital-Resale-Platforms-Trade-in-Programs-and-Demand-for-Sustainable-and-Afford.html)

### U.S. Online Resale
- Online resale accelerated **23% growth in 2024** — the strongest rate since 2021
- **2029 projected size**: **$40 billion** (CAGR **13%**)
- Source: [ThredUp 2025 Resale Report](https://www.thredup.com/resale)

### Consumer Sentiment
- **Nearly 80% of Gen Z and millennial consumers** self-identify as participants in the recommerce movement
- Source: [ThredUp Newsroom, 2025](https://newsroom.thredup.com/news/thredup-13th-resale-report)

## eBay's Position in the Recommerce TAM

eBay is the largest incumbent in the recommerce/resale category, and its strategic pivot under CEO Jamie Iannone has deepened this positioning.

### Recommerce Share of GMV
In the Q4 2025 earnings call, Iannone confirmed eBay's recommerce scale: *"Recommerce made up over 40% of GMV on the eBay Inc. platform in 2025"* ([eBay Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-18](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/18/ebay-ebay-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)). This represents a meaningful structural shift: in a 2025 Semafor interview, Iannone noted, *"We refocused on some of eBay's original strengths — used and refurbished items have grown from a third of our sales to more than 40%"* ([Semafor, 2025-10-09](https://www.semafor.com/article/10/09/2025/how-jamie-iannone-made-ebay-an-investor-collectible)).

### Untapped Household TAM
Management frames the addressable market in concrete household terms. Iannone in Q4 2024 earnings: *"The average household has $3,000 to $4,000 of possessions that they could sell on eBay"* ([eBay Q4 2024 Earnings, 2025-02-27](https://investors.ebayinc.com/investor-news/press-release-details/2025/eBay-Inc.-Reports-Fourth-Quarter-and-Full-Year-2024-Results/default.aspx)). He added a conversion funnel perspective: *"fewer than 20% of sellable items"* are currently listed on the platform — meaning **80%+ of household sellable inventory remains undigitized**, representing a large untapped TAM ([Semafor, 2025-10-09](https://www.semafor.com/article/10/09/2025/how-jamie-iannone-made-ebay-an-investor-collectible)).

### AI as TAM Unlock Mechanism
Iannone connected AI capabilities to TAM conversion in a 2025 Northeastern University fireside chat: *"All of a sudden, you can unlock this stuff in seconds"* ([Northeastern, 2025-10-28](https://news.northeastern.edu/2025/10/28/ebay-ceo-ai-leadership-fireside-chat-northeastern/)). The implication: AI-assisted listing (image recognition, auto-pricing, description generation) removes friction that kept items off-platform, converting latent supply into active GMV.

### Generational Demand Tailwind
In a 2022 Financial Times interview, Iannone framed recommerce as a generational alignment play: *"We're leaning into where Gen Z and millennials are... there's a bigger focus on sustainability, and 're-commerce'. eBay is really the pioneer... that gives us an opportunity"* ([Financial Times via PYMNTS, 2022-10-21](https://www.pymnts.com/news/ecommerce/2022/ebay-bids-on-luxury-recommerce-to-woo-young-shoppers/)).

### Cumulative Sustainability Impact
In Q4 2025 earnings, Iannone quantified eBay's recommerce leadership in sustainability terms: *"We estimate we achieved a cumulative positive impact of close to $25,000,000,000"* over five years — a figure eBay uses to position recommerce as a brand and regulatory asset ([eBay Q4 2025 Earnings Call, 2026-02-18](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/02/18/ebay-ebay-q4-2025-earnings-call-transcript/)).

## Focus Categories: Concentrated TAM Capture

eBay's TAM strategy is further operationalized through its "focus categories" — verticals with deep secondhand/collectibles demand where eBay holds structural advantages (authentication, enthusiast community, cross-border supply). By Q1 2025, these categories had reached critical mass:

*"Focus category GMV grew by over six percent in Q1 and now makes up more than one third of our total volume globally"* — Iannone, [eBay Q1 2025 Earnings Call, 2025-04-30](https://fortune.com/company/ebay/earnings/q1-2025/).

Focus categories include: Trading Cards, Sneakers, Luxury Handbags, Watches, Jewelry, Parts & Accessories, Refurbished Electronics.

---

## Cross-Border Advantage Expands Effective TAM

eBay's cross-border capabilities structurally expand its effective TAM beyond any single national market. Over **90% of eBay sellers export to international markets** (detailed in competitive moat analysis). This gives eBay a liquidity advantage in niche and collectible categories where no single domestic market has sufficient supply/demand depth. Iannone framed this as a tariff-environment advantage in Q1 2025: *"Our dynamic global supply and demand is an advantage in this environment"* ([eBay Q1 2025 Earnings Call, 2025-04-30](https://fortune.com/company/ebay/earnings/q1-2025/)).
