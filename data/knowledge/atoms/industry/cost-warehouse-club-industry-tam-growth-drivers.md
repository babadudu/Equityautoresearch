## Warehouse Club Industry: TAM Sizing, Growth Drivers, and Costco's Market Position

### Industry Origins and Structural Foundation

The warehouse club industry traces its roots to 1976, when Sol Price founded Price Club in San Diego — the world's first membership-based warehouse retailer. Price's core thesis: by screening members, buying in bulk, and applying minimal markups, the margins traditionally extracted by retail intermediaries could be returned directly to consumers. This model spread rapidly through the 1980s: Sam Walton founded Sam's Club in 1983 ([Walmart Corporate History](https://corporate.walmart.com/about/history)), and that same year Jim Sinegal and Jeff Brotman founded Costco in Seattle. The 1993 merger of Price Club and Costco cemented the modern warehouse club duopoly — Costco and Sam's Club together account for over 85% of the U.S. market.

The structural genius of the model, as articulated by Sinegal in a 2009 Seattle Times interview, is that membership fees function not as a profit center but as a subsidy pool for low prices: *"We use all the membership fees we charge to keep lower prices on the shelf."* ([The Seattle Times, 2009/11/15](https://www.seattletimes.com/business/complete-qa-with-costco-founder-ceo-jim-sinegal/)). This architecture — where fee revenue covers operating overhead and merchandise margins stay razor-thin — is the defining competitive barrier of the warehouse club format.

### Global Warehouse Club Market: TAM Estimates and Definitional Variance

Three major research firms size the global warehouse club market, with estimates varying significantly based on definitional scope:

| Research Firm | Base Year | Market Size | Forecast Year | Forecast Size | CAGR |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Verified Market Research | 2024 | $1.6T | 2032 | $2.4T | 5.2% |
| Proficient Market Insights | 2025 | $776B | 2034 | $1,347B | 5.14% |
| Cognitive Market Research | 2025 | ~$500B | 2033 | ~$850B | 7.0% |

Sources: [VMR](https://www.verifiedmarketresearch.com/product/warehouse-clubs-market/), [PMI](https://www.proficientmarketinsights.com/market-reports/warehouse-clubs-market-4162), [CMR](https://www.cognitivemarketresearch.com/warehouse-club-market-report)

**Resolving the Definitional Gap:**

- **VMR ($1.6T, broadest):** Aggregates warehouse clubs + supercenters + cash-and-carry formats globally. This includes Walmart Supercenter (~$420B revenue), Metro/Makro cash-and-carry operations (~$30B), and Asian large-format retail (Thailand's Makro, India's DMart) — none of which require annual membership fees. This is the least useful figure for Costco investors.

- **PMI ($776B, most relevant):** Focuses exclusively on membership-only warehouse clubs — Costco, Sam's Club, BJ's Wholesale, PriceSmart. Excludes supercenters and cash-and-carry. **This is the most relevant TAM for COST analysis.** Costco's FY2025 global revenue of ~$275B represents approximately **35% of this $776B market**, confirming its absolute dominance in the pure membership warehouse club segment.

- **CMR (~$500B, intermediate):** Covers major membership-format operators but excludes portions of emerging markets.

Despite the headline size differences, all three reports converge on a **5–7% CAGR outlook**, driven by the same underlying forces: middle-class expansion (especially in Asia) and a structural shift toward membership-based value consumption.

### U.S. Market: Government Statistical Validation

The U.S. Census Bureau tracks warehouse club sales via NAICS code 452311 (Warehouse Clubs and Supercenters) in its Monthly Retail Trade Survey. Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED) shows:

- **February 2025:** $57.6B (seasonally adjusted monthly)
- **January 2025:** $57.4B
- **Implied annualized run rate:** ~**$684B**

Source: [FRED - Retail Sales: Warehouse Clubs and Superstores](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/MRTSSM45291USS)

Important caveat: NAICS 452311 includes Walmart Supercenter (a non-membership format with ~$280B U.S. revenue). Stripping out supercenter formats, the pure membership warehouse club segment within this NAICS is approximately 55–60% of the total, or **$375–410B** — broadly consistent with PMI's membership-only estimate.

The NRF projects 2025 U.S. total retail at **$5.42–5.48 trillion**, with online and non-store sales growing 7–9% to $1.5–1.6T. Costco's ~$197B U.S. revenue represents **~5% of total U.S. retail** — an extraordinary concentration in a single retailer.

### Costco's Market Share Position

In the membership-only warehouse club format:
- **U.S. market share: 60%+** (vs. Sam's Club at ~30%, BJ's at ~10%)
- Costco's $197B U.S. revenue against NAICS 452311's $684B annualized = **~29%** share of the combined warehouse-club-plus-supercenter category
- Excluding Walmart Supercenter's ~$280B, Costco's implied share of pure membership warehouse clubs exceeds **60%**

At the global level, Costco's $275B FY2025 revenue against PMI's $776B membership-only market implies **~35% global market share**, making it the single largest operator by a wide margin.

### TAM Expansion Runway: International Markets

CEO Ron Vachris has articulated a concrete international expansion program. In a South Sound Biz interview, Vachris stated Costco sees *"good runway"* for **opening 24–25 warehouses annually over the next 5–10 years** ([South Sound Biz, 2024](https://www.southsoundbiz.com/profiles/ron-vachris-costco-interview/article_7c03e854-37d9-11ef-b403-1fab84c884aa.html)). From a base of 914 warehouses, this trajectory implies reaching approximately **1,150–1,160 locations by 2034** (+26%), with international markets — particularly Asia-Pacific (China, Japan, South Korea) and Europe — serving as the primary growth engine.

Vachris has also identified a digital dimension to TAM penetration: *"Digital and technology are important parts of our future growth, and we are investing to improve the member experience."* ([Fortune, 2025/06/04](https://fortune.com/2025/06/04/costco-ceo-ron-vachris-fortune-500-success/)). The dual-track strategy — physical warehouse expansion combined with digital member experience investment — defines Costco's TAM capture approach.

Importantly, Costco's expansion philosophy has consistently prioritized unit economics over headline store count. Sinegal established this discipline early: *"We don't have any grand scheme that says, for example, that we have to be in Latin America by the year 2015 or have 1000 Costco's in ten years. We're going to grow very sensibly."* ([Motley Fool - Jim Sinegal Interview, 2013/07/31](https://www.fool.com/investing/general/2013/07/31/an-interview-with-jim-sinegal-of-costco.aspx)). This "disciplined expansion" ethos — ensuring each new warehouse meets Costco's ~$280M annual revenue standard before proceeding — means the TAM penetration trajectory is calibrated to profitability, not volume.

### Secular Growth Drivers

Across all TAM estimates and forecasts, five structural growth drivers consistently appear:

1. **Middle-class expansion in Asia:** China, South Korea, and Southeast Asia represent the largest underpenetrated membership warehouse markets. Costco's 2019 Shanghai opening (record-breaking opening-day crowds requiring temporary closure) validated demand.

2. **Value-seeking consumer behavior:** Post-2022 inflation has structurally reinforced consumer preference for bulk-buy value formats, accelerating membership growth beyond pre-pandemic trends.

3. **Membership model stickiness:** Global renewal rates (Costco: ~93% in the U.S. and Canada) create predictable recurring revenue and natural resistance to competitive entry.

4. **Private label penetration (Kirkland Signature):** As Kirkland Signature grows as a percentage of total sales (currently ~30%+ of revenue), it expands the effective TAM by capturing share from both national brands and specialty retailers.

5. **E-commerce complement:** Warehouse clubs are structurally advantaged in e-commerce (high average order value, existing member relationships, integrated logistics) relative to traditional grocery or department store formats.

### Key Analytical Notes for Investors

- **Use PMI's $776B (2025) membership-only TAM** as the base case for Costco market share calculations. Costco's 35% share of this market, combined with a 5%+ industry CAGR, implies substantial organic growth simply from market expansion — before share gains.
- The FRED/Census NAICS 452311 data ($684B annualized, Feb 2025) provides quarterly-updatable ground truth for U.S. market sizing.
- Costco's per-warehouse average revenue of ~$280M/year is the single most important benchmark for evaluating international expansion quality — each new warehouse should be assessed against this threshold.
- The consistent 5–7% industry CAGR across multiple independent research firms spanning different methodologies provides high confidence in the growth trajectory, even if absolute size estimates vary.
