## C.C. Wei（魏哲家）— TSMC Chairman & CEO

### Background & Education

C.C. Wei was born in 1953 in Lugu Township, Nantou County, Taiwan. He attended Taichung First Senior High School, then earned his bachelor's and master's degrees in electrical engineering from National Chiao Tung University (國立交通大學). He subsequently earned a PhD in electrical engineering from Yale University (1985), with a dissertation titled "RF Plasma Damage in MOS Structures" under advisor Professor Tso-Ping Ma (馬佐平). ([Source: Wikipedia - Che-Chia Wei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Che-Chia_Wei))

### Early Career

After his doctorate, Wei held technical roles at Texas Instruments, STMicroelectronics, and Chartered Semiconductor (as VP of Technology), accumulating broad semiconductor manufacturing and technical management experience across multiple companies before joining TSMC.

### Rise Through TSMC

Wei joined TSMC in February 1998 as Vice President. His subsequent promotions:

- **March 2012**: Co-COO
- **November 2013**: President and Co-CEO (alongside Mark Liu)
- **June 2018**: CEO (following Morris Chang's formal retirement)
- **June 2024**: Chairman and CEO (succeeding Mark Liu as Chairman)

Morris Chang's assessment of Wei: "He went through all three departments—so I call him the best-prepared CEO." — referring to Chang's deliberate 2009 decision to rotate Wei from the technical division into business development, ensuring Wei had rotated through technology, manufacturing, and business development — TSMC's three core domains. ([Source: Wikipedia, CommonWealth Magazine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Che-Chia_Wei))

Wei's own account of why he chose to build his career at TSMC: "When looking for a job, you have to choose your boss first, and I had a great boss." — a direct reference to Morris Chang's long-term mentorship. ([Source: CommonWealth Magazine, 2024-06-03](https://english.cw.com.tw/article/article.action?id=3710))

### Key Achievements as CEO

- Revenue growth: TSMC NT$1 trillion (2018) → NT$3.8 trillion (2025), a 3.8× expansion over his tenure
- Led TSMC's global manufacturing diversification: Arizona (total committed investment ~$165 billion), Japan Kumamoto (JASM), Dresden Germany
- **September 2024**: Named to Time magazine's list of the 100 most influential people in AI
- **2024**: Received the SIA Robert N. Noyce Award, the semiconductor industry's highest honor ([Source: SIA](https://www.semiconductors.org/tsmcs-dr-c-c-wei-and-dr-mark-liu-to-receive-sias-highest-honor/))

### Management Philosophy

Wei's leadership style is described as pragmatic, low-key, and firm — shaped by his rural upbringing in Lugu Township. CommonWealth Magazine's 2024 leadership feature described him as having an "unorthodox leadership style," distinct from his predecessor's academic disposition. Wei wins team trust through directness and self-deprecating humor.

**On globalization strategy**: "Turning Taiwan's TSMC into the World's TSMC." — articulating his proactive transformation of TSMC from a Taiwan-centric company to a global institution under geopolitical pressure. ([Source: CommonWealth Magazine, 2025-07-10](https://english.cw.com.tw/article/article.action?id=4215))

**On U.S. talent (Yale speech, October 2023)**: "The U.S. is the country with the most talented and innovative people, especially for the Ivy League student." — delivered at Yale Science Building; Wei simultaneously flagged the real constraint on U.S. expansion: insufficient on-the-ground semiconductor engineers, which shaped TSMC's choice of Arizona as having a relatively larger technical talent pool. ([Source: Yale Daily News, 2023-10-05](https://yaledailynews.com/blog/2023/10/05/taiwan-semiconductor-ceo-c-c-wei-speaks-at-the-yale-science-building/))

**On geopolitical noise (2024, re: Trump comments on Taiwan)**: "We did not change any of our original plan of expansion of our overseas fab. We continue to expand in Arizona, in Kumamoto and maybe in future in Europe. No change to our strategy." ([Source: The Register, 2024-07-18](https://www.theregister.com/2024/07/18/tsmc_ceo_predicts_ai_chip/))

**On AI chip demand**: "Whether AI applications use ASICs or graphics processors, they all require very leading-edge technology and are working with TSMC." — Wei's articulation of TSMC's platform-agnostic indispensability in the AI era, regardless of whether the winning architecture is GPU or custom ASIC. ([Source: TSMC Q2 2024 Earnings Transcript](https://investor.tsmc.com/english/encrypt/files/encrypt_file/reports/2024-08/5122725a56670882d777a8e8bfe0ed247cc55330/TSMC%202Q24%20Transcript.pdf))

**On Intel Foundry competition (January 2026 earnings call)**: "In the development of semiconductor technology to this point, receiving investment has not helped to improve competitiveness, right?" — directly stating that capital alone cannot buy technology leadership. When asked whether he was worried about Intel's progress, Wei responded simply: "Not worried." ([Source: WCCFTech, 2026-01](https://wccftech.com/tsmcs-ceo-says-intel-foundry-wont-be-competitive-by-just-throwing-money/))

### Integrity & Governance Continuity

Following the leadership transition in June 2024, Wei explicitly committed to continuing TSMC's integrity governance: "no matter where TSMC conducts business in the world, it will abide by the highest standards of corporate governance, stick to the bottom line of integrity." — a signal to global customers that the pure-play foundry's IP security guarantee is foundational to TSMC's existence. ([Source: Ventron Chip, 2024](https://www.ventronchip.com/news/tsmc-completes-leadership-handover-c.-c.-wei-takes-over-as-chairman.html))

Institutional outcomes of this culture: TSMC ranked in the top 5% of Taiwan Stock Exchange corporate governance evaluations for 11 consecutive years (through 2025); 77,293 employees globally completed annual integrity and compliance training in 2025 with a 100% pass rate; an Ombudsman (whistleblower) system with senior executives and audit functions reporting directly to the Board of Directors. ([Source: TSMC IR Corporate Governance](https://investor.tsmc.com/english/corporate-governance-officer); [TSMC 2024 Annual Report Ch.7](https://investor.tsmc.com/static/annualReports/2024/english/pdf/2024_tsmc_ar_e_ch7.pdf))

### Key Challenges & Ongoing Proof Points

Wei inherited Morris Chang's legacy but faces a categorically different operating environment: geopolitical fragmentation, AI demand surge, and global factory buildout. The Arizona fabs represent TSMC's most complex management challenge in its history — cross-cultural management, cost control, and talent recruitment in an unfamiliar labor market. Early reports of cultural friction and schedule delays in Arizona mean Wei must continue demonstrating that TSMC can replicate its manufacturing discipline outside Taiwan.

On CapEx discipline (December 2022, Monte Jade Science and Technology Association, "New Challenges in the Semiconductor Industry" address): Wei acknowledged the complexity behind the 2022 CapEx reduction from $42 billion to $36 billion but declined to publicly disclose detailed rationale — demonstrating his characteristically pragmatic and cautious communication style under uncertainty. ([Source: Digitimes, 2022-12-22](https://www.digitimes.com/news/a20221222VL206/japan-tsmc.html&chid=12))
