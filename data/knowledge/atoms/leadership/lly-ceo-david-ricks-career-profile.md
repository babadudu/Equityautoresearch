## David A. Ricks — Career Profile

### Background & Education

David Alexander Ricks was born in 1967 and grew up in California ([Posse Foundation, 2019](https://www.possefoundation.org/news-and-events/ubben-posse-fellow-interviews-david-ricks)). He is not a typical pharmaceutical industry insider. In 1990 he earned a BS from Purdue University, then joined IBM as a business analyst, gaining exposure to large-enterprise systems thinking. He later pursued an MBA at Indiana University Kelley School of Business (received 1996) — coming to Indianapolis because "his girlfriend (now wife) was in medical school" ([Chief Executive, 2025](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/)).

In 1996 he joined Eli Lilly as a **business development associate** handling M&A, giving him early exposure to the pharmaceutical industry's global complexity ([Quartr, 2025](https://quartr.com/insights/business-philosophy/david-a-ricks-eli-lillys-chairman-and-ceo); [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_A._Ricks)).

### Career Trajectory at Eli Lilly

**1998–2005 — Marketing to Canada GM**: Promoted to marketing manager in 1998, then regional sales manager. In 2002 moved to the Canadian subsidiary as marketing director; by 2005 became General Manager of Canada, completing the critical transition from functional role to full P&L accountability ([Quartr, 2025](https://quartr.com/insights/business-philosophy/david-a-ricks-eli-lillys-chairman-and-ceo)).

**2008–2010 — China President**: Posted to Shanghai as President and General Manager of Eli Lilly China during a period of rapid growth in the Chinese pharmaceutical market. This assignment gave him first-hand understanding of emerging market regulatory complexity and growth opportunities. It also formed the empirical basis for his later hardline stance on Chinese counterfeit GLP-1 products: "Most of them are sourced out of Chinese labs that aren't even approved in China" ([Washington Reporter, 2025-03](https://washingtonreporter.news/p/interview-eli-lilly-ceo-david-ricks)).

**2010 — President, Eli Lilly USA**: Recalled to lead the largest market just two years after China deployment.

**2012 — President, Bio-Medicines**: Gained direct exposure to Lilly's most advanced R&D pipeline — neuroscience, pain management, and immunology — overseeing both drug development and commercialization.

**2016 — The tirzepatide "Eureka Moment"**: Just before assuming the CEO role, the research team delivered transformative Phase 2 data. Ricks described the moment: "We had to stop the study because people were losing too much weight to stay in it" ([Chief Executive, 2025](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/)). This single data readout defined his entire CEO tenure.

**January 2017 — CEO**: Became Lilly's 11th CEO in 150 years: "I'm the 11th CEO of the company in 150 years" ([T. Rowe Price, 2025](https://www.troweprice.com/en/us/insights/the-long-view-eli-lilly)). At the time of appointment, Lilly's market capitalization was approximately $70B.

**2017–2022 — Strategic Transformation**: Divested non-core assets, spun off the animal health division (Elanco), and concentrated resources on diabetes, obesity, Alzheimer's, and oncology.

**2022–2025 — Breakout Period**: Mounjaro approved (2022), Zepbound approved (2023), Kisunla approved (2024). Market capitalization grew from ~$70B to ~$770B — more than 10x growth over his tenure.

### Talent Philosophy

Ricks' approach to talent selection diverges sharply from conventional large-company practice. His core principle: hire for the most probable person to be great rather than the most qualified today — a philosophy reflected in his own career: "Time and time again, the company put me in jobs I had no business being in. But I was able to learn and then go to the next one" ([Bell Pioneer, 2025](https://www.bellpioneer.com/insights/the-better-version-of-nice-inside-ceo-david-ricks-leadership-philosophy-at-eli-lilly)).

He identifies four core qualities he seeks: **Curiosity, Humility, Hard Work, Teamwork** ([T. Rowe Price, 2025](https://www.troweprice.com/en/us/insights/the-long-view-eli-lilly)). Curiosity is ranked first: "I love learning new things. I could listen all day to young scientists talk about what they're working on — sometimes crazy and cutting-edge things" ([Chief Executive, 2025](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/)).

On career advice, he is consistently adventure-oriented: "Have a sense of adventure! Move around, and don't be afraid to change" and "Taking a 'Just say yes' approach to many things professionally can be good" ([Posse Foundation, 2019](https://www.possefoundation.org/news-and-events/ubben-posse-fellow-interviews-david-ricks)).

Promotion criteria prioritize mission over compensation: "Commitment to the cause matters, too. Really believing in what we do and wanting to work in a business where you can create solutions to the biggest health problems in the world being more important to you than compensation or title — those people get promoted" ([Chief Executive, 2025](https://chiefexecutive.net/ceo-of-the-year-a-conversation-with-eli-lillys-dave-ricks/)).

### Succession Risk & Bench Depth

- **Ricks age/tenure**: Born 1967 (~59 years old), CEO since 2017 (~9-year tenure), also serves as Chairman. Average large-pharma CEO tenure is 7–10 years; Ricks is near the upper bound.
- **Anat Ashkenazi** (former CFO): Previously considered the strongest succession candidate; departed in 2024 to become Google CFO — a significant bench loss.
- **Lucas Montarce** (EVP, President of Lilly International): Manages all non-US markets, overseeing 60%+ of global employees; deep international/emerging-market experience.
- **Patrik Jonsson** (EVP, President of Lilly USA): Manages the largest market (67% of revenue); central role in GLP-1 commercialization.
- **Daniel Skovronsky, M.D., Ph.D.** (EVP, Chief Scientific & Medical Officer): Leads R&D pipeline; architect of the Alzheimer's and GLP-1 scientific strategy. Primarily scientist-identity with limited commercial management experience.
- **Bench depth rating**: Medium-low. Ashkenazi's departure was a material loss; remaining candidates have complementary strengths but no clear designated successor. Ricks' highly personalized leadership style (138-person executive team, personally approves senior hires) elevates key-person risk above industry average. Mitigant: Lilly's 150-year institutional history across 11 CEO transitions suggests durable succession mechanisms exist.
- **Succession disclosure**: No public succession timeline. Ricks stated in 2019: "I'll know I did a good job because we found a successor who can do it better" ([Posse Foundation, 2019](https://www.possefoundation.org/news-and-events/ubben-posse-fellow-interviews-david-ricks)), signaling intent to transition but with no defined timeline.

### Paragon Intel Independent Assessment

Independent analysis firm [Paragon Intel](https://paragonintel.com/david-ricks-ceo-analysis-lly/) identifies Ricks' core strength as "proven strategic and commercial execution," making him "a credible steward." Three strategic tensions flagged for investor monitoring: (1) whether Ricks over-relies on external narrative management rather than internal accountability following manufacturing scale-up failures; (2) whether post-GLP-1 success he will prioritize transformative M&A vs. repairing critical manufacturing infrastructure; (3) balance between operational execution and strategic vision ([Paragon Intel, 2025](https://paragonintel.com/david-ricks-ceo-analysis-lly/)).
