The history and success of TSMC are inseparable from its leadership, characterized by a transition from the strategic foundationalism of Morris Chang to the pragmatic global expansionism of C.C. Wei.

### Morris Chang: The Architect
Morris Chang (born 1931) spent 25 years at Texas Instruments (TI), rising to Senior VP of global semiconductor operations, before founding TSMC at age 56. His management philosophy is built on "Integrity" and a strict 8% R&D spending rule—maintaining this fixed percentage regardless of economic recessions to ensure technological continuity (Source: https://www.acquired.fm/episodes/tsmc-founder-morris-changs-autobiography). Chang’s integrity-first approach includes a personal zero-tolerance policy for executive misconduct: "If there is any executive violating this very principle... I must personally handle the situation and ask him/her to resign." (Source: https://exampleof2015.wordpress.com/2016/03/03/integrity-we-should-carry-on-throughout-all-of-our-life/). He famously settled a dispute with NVIDIA in 2009 by personally calling Jensen Huang and offering a fair settlement within 48 hours, preserving a multi-billion dollar partnership.

### C.C. Wei: The Best-Prepared CEO
Dr. C.C. Wei (Yale PhD) joined TSMC in 1998 and underwent a unique "rotation" program designed by Chang, moving through technology, manufacturing, and business development. Chang calls him the "best-prepared CEO." Under Wei’s leadership since 2018, TSMC has navigated the AI explosion and the geopolitically forced transition from "Taiwan's TSMC" to the "World's TSMC." Wei is known for his candid, sometimes humorous style, once admitting he is "also very nervous" about spending $56B in CapEx, yet justifying it through direct verification of demand with cloud service providers: "I also double checked their financial status—they are very rich." (Source: https://www.nextplatform.com/compute/2026/01/16/tsmc-has-no-choice-but-to-trust-the-sunny-ai-forecasts-of-its-customers/4092173).

### Succession and Board Depth
As Wei (born 1953) enters his 70s, TSMC has institutionalized a "Dual Co-COO" structure to prepare the next generation, mirroring the path Wei himself took. 
- **Dr. Y.J. Mii (EVP & Co-COO)**: A 31-year veteran and UCLA PhD, Mii leads the R&D organization and is the technical architect of every node from 16nm to 2nm.
- **Y.P. Chyn (EVP & Co-COO)**: An original employee since 1987, Chyn leads worldwide fab operations and established the company's "Golden Spice" yield modeling standards.
This bench is supported by CFO Wendell Huang, who maintains strict financial discipline through his "six-factor" gross margin framework. Despite recent retirements of veterans like Wei-Jen Lo and Rick Cassidy, TSMC’s board continues to link executive incentives (Restricted Stock Awards) directly to ESG achievements, ensuring that leadership performance is measured beyond just quarterly earnings.
