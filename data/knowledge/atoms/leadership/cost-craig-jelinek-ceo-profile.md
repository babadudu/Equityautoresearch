## Craig Jelinek: Costco's Second CEO (2012–2023) — Low-Profile Steward of a $242B Retailer

### Profile

Craig Jelinek was Costco's second CEO, serving from 2012 to 2023. Like his predecessor Jim Sinegal and his successor Ron Vachris, Jelinek was a career "lifer" — he joined Costco from the ground level and worked under Sinegal for more than 30 years before ascending to the top role. His leadership style was notably more media-averse than Sinegal's; he rarely granted interviews and maintained a low public profile throughout his tenure.

### Tenure Performance (2012–2023)

Under Jelinek's stewardship, Costco's financial and operational profile expanded substantially:
- Revenue grew from ~$99B (FY2012) to ~$242B (FY2023), representing an 11-year CAGR of approximately 8.5%
- International warehouse count grew from ~150 locations to ~280
- Two membership fee increases (2017 and 2024) were implemented without meaningfully affecting renewal rates, demonstrating the strength of member loyalty cultivated under his watch

### The Hot Dog Commitment

In one of his most quoted statements — from a rare 2013 interview — Jelinek reaffirmed Costco's iconic $1.50 hot dog and drink price with characteristic bluntness: *"Trust me, that's never going to change. If I have to subsidize it personally, it's not going to change."* ([Motley Fool, 2013/08/01](https://www.fool.com/investing/general/2013/08/01/an-interview-with-costco-ceo-craig-jelinek.aspx)). The statement captured the Costco leadership ethos: long-term member value over short-term margin extraction.

### Economic Resilience View

In a 2022 CNBC interview — one of his rare extended media appearances — Jelinek articulated why Costco's model is structurally more resilient in economic downturns: consumers facing uncertainty preferentially seek "certain value," which plays directly to the warehouse club proposition ([CNBC, 2022/07/11](https://www.cnbc.com/2022/07/11/4-things-costco-ceo-craig-jelinek-said-in-a-rare-interview-on-the-economy-retail-trends-and-more.html)).

### High-Wage Advocacy

In 2022, Jelinek testified before the U.S. Senate Budget Committee, formally defending Costco's high-wage strategy as both ethically sound and economically rational — consistent with the philosophy Jim Sinegal institutionalized decades earlier ([U.S. Senate Budget Committee](https://www.budget.senate.gov/download/craig-jelinek-testimony)).

### Earnings Communication Style

In a September 2023 CNBC appearance with Jim Cramer following strong Q4 results, Jelinek attributed performance to *"持續的執行紀律和會員信任"* (sustained execution discipline and member trust) ([CNBC, 2023/09/27](https://www.cnbc.com/video/2023/09/27/costco-ceo-craig-jelinek-talks-q4-earnings-results-with-jim-cramer.html)) — language consistent with Costco's characteristically understated public communications.

### Significance in the CEO Lineage

Jelinek's 11-year tenure served as proof-of-concept that Costco's culture and model were not personality-dependent on Sinegal. Revenue more than doubled; member loyalty deepened; international expansion accelerated — all without the charismatic founder at the helm. His smooth transition from Sinegal and his own smooth handoff to Vachris in January 2024 validated Costco's internal succession architecture as a genuine competitive asset.
