## Amazon Leadership Succession and Executive Bench

### Current CEO

**Andy Jassy**, born 1967, has been Amazon CEO since July 2021 (~4.5 years as of early 2026). He was previously CEO of AWS from 2006 to 2021. At 58 years old he is in the middle range of typical large-cap tech CEO tenure.

### Succession Mechanism: The Shadow/Technical Advisor Program

Amazon's primary leadership development mechanism is the "Technical Advisor" (TA) or shadow program, in which a high-potential executive is assigned to shadow the CEO for a period of one to two years. Jeff Bezos used this role to develop future leaders; Jassy himself served in this capacity under Bezos (joining Amazon in 1997 and acting as Bezos's TA). The TA program functions as a de facto CEO apprenticeship and is one of the most direct pipelines to senior leadership in the company.

Amazon also operates through the **S-team (Senior Team)**, a small group of direct reports to the CEO who collectively own the major business units. S-team tenure and performance are the primary qualifiers for CEO succession.

### Key Next-Generation Executives

**Matt Garman — CEO, Amazon Web Services (appointed 2024)**

Garman replaced Adam Selipsky as AWS CEO in 2024. He is an AWS veteran who previously led EC2 (Amazon's core compute service) and later ran AWS Sales and Marketing. He holds a Harvard MBA. His combination of deep technical AWS roots and commercial leadership experience make him one of the most likely candidates for Amazon group CEO succession. He has more than 15 years at Amazon.

**Doug Herrington — CEO, Worldwide Amazon Stores**

Herrington leads global retail and fulfillment — the largest revenue segment by gross merchandise volume. He joined Amazon in 2005 and has more than 20 years with the company. His background spans consumer, hardlines, softlines, and logistics. As the steward of the e-commerce flywheel he represents a complementary profile to AWS-oriented succession candidates.

**Adam Selipsky — Former AWS CEO (2021–2024)**

Selipsky served as AWS CEO from 2021 to 2024 before being replaced by Garman. He has since departed Amazon. His tenure coincided with the early phase of Amazon's generative AI investments.

### Succession Risk Assessment

| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|------------|
| CEO age | Andy Jassy, 58 — moderate tenure remaining under typical assumptions |
| Bench depth | **High**: Amazon's S-team system and TA/shadow program produce well-prepared internal successors |
| Next-in-line depth | Matt Garman (AWS CEO) and Doug Herrington (Worldwide Stores CEO) both have 15–20+ years Amazon experience |
| External succession risk | Low: Amazon has historically promoted internally for CEO; Jassy himself was an internal candidate |
| Formal succession disclosure | None publicly disclosed, consistent with Amazon's practice of not releasing formal succession plans |
| Key-person concentration | Moderate: Jassy's deep AWS origins and personal relationships in AI investment ecosystem (e.g., Anthropic) are not easily transferred, but the S-team structure distributes operational authority |

### Structural Note on Bezos's Role

Jeff Bezos (born 1964) transitioned to Executive Chairman in July 2021. He retains a significant equity stake and board seat, providing cultural continuity and a backstop on major strategic decisions. His primary focus post-CEO is Blue Origin (commercial space) and the Day 1 Fund (philanthropic). His formal day-to-day influence on Amazon operations is limited but his presence as a reference architecture for the culture — via the Leadership Principles, shareholder letters, and institutional memory — remains significant.
